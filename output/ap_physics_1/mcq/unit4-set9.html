The second cart has a mass of $2.0 \text{ kg}$ and is at rest. After the collision, the first cart moves backward at $0.67 \text{ m/s}$ and the second cart moves forward at $1.33 \text{ m/s}$. Which of the following statements justifies that linear momentum is conserved for the system of two carts?
  • A. The total kinetic energy before the collision equals the total kinetic energy after the collision.
  • B. The initial velocity of the first cart equals the sum of the final velocities of both carts.
  • C. The sum of the initial momenta ($ (1.0 \text{ kg})(2.0 \text{ m/s}) + (2.0 \text{ kg})(0 \text{ m/s}) = 2.0 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$) equals the sum of the final momenta ($ (1.0 \text{ kg})(-0.67 \text{ m/s}) + (2.0 \text{ kg})(1.33 \text{ m/s}) \approx -0.67 + 2.66 \approx 1.99 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$).
  • D. The magnitude of the impulse on the first cart is equal to the magnitude of the impulse on the second cart.
