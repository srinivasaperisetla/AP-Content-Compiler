AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 7)

FRQ 1

A marketing firm wants to estimate the proportion of adults in a certain city who regularly use a new social media app. They conduct a random sample of 500 adults, and 120 report using the app regularly.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the true proportion of adults in the city who regularly use the app.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of adults in the city who regularly use the app.
c. Interpret the 95% confidence interval in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample Z-interval for proportion (1pt); Verifies conditions (Random, 10% rule, Large Counts) (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct calculation of sample proportion and standard error (1pt); Calculates correct interval (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context (1pt).

FRQ 2

A company claims that 70% of its customers are satisfied with its new product. A consumer advocacy group believes the satisfaction rate is lower. They survey 200 randomly selected customers and find that 128 are satisfied.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this situation.
b. Identify the appropriate inference method and verify the conditions for performing a significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value, interpret its meaning in the context of the problem and state your conclusion at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states null and alternative hypotheses with parameter defined (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies one-sample Z-test for proportion and verifies conditions (Random, 10% rule, Large Counts) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates correct test statistic (1pt); Calculates correct p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (2pts): Interprets p-value in context (1pt); States correct conclusion with justification based on p-value and significance level (1pt).

FRQ 3

Researchers are comparing the effectiveness of two different fertilizers, Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B, on a particular type of plant. They randomly select 150 plants for Fertilizer A and 180 plants for Fertilizer B. After a month, 120 of the plants treated with Fertilizer A showed significant growth, while 135 of the plants treated with Fertilizer B showed significant growth.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions of plants that show significant growth between Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B.
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions of plants that show significant growth (proportion A - proportion B).
c. Based on the confidence interval, does there appear to be a significant difference in the effectiveness of the two fertilizers? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies two-sample Z-interval for proportions (1pt); Verifies conditions for both samples (Random, 10% rule, Large Counts for both groups) (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates correct sample proportions and standard error for the difference (1pt); Calculates correct interval (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): States whether there is a significant difference (1pt); Justifies based on whether the interval contains 0 (1pt).

FRQ 5

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug designed to reduce allergic reactions. The company claims the drug is effective if more than 60% of patients experience a reduction in symptoms. They plan to conduct a significance test with null hypothesis $H_0: p \le 0.60$ and alternative hypothesis $H_a: p > 0.60$, where $p$ is the true proportion of patients who experience symptom reduction.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this problem.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this problem.
c. Explain a consequence of making a Type I error for the pharmaceutical company and for patients.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly describes a Type I error in context (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly describes a Type II error in context (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Explains a consequence for the company (1pt); Explains a consequence for patients (1pt).
