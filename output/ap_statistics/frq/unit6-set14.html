AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 14)

FRQ 1

A polling organization wants to estimate the proportion of adults in a large city who support a new public transportation initiative. A random sample of 500 adults from the city was surveyed, and 280 of them indicated support for the initiative.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure to construct a confidence interval for the true proportion of adults in the city who support the initiative.
b. Verify the conditions for constructing this confidence interval.
c. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of adults in the city who support the initiative.
d. Interpret the 95% confidence interval in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a one-sample z-interval for a population proportion.
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies random sampling (stated), checks normality ($n\hat{p} = 280 \ge 10$ and $n(1-\hat{p}) = 220 \ge 10$), and checks independence ($500 < 10\%$ of all adults in a large city).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $\hat{p} = 280/500 = 0.56$ and $SE_{\hat{p}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.56(0.44)}{500}} \approx 0.0221$. Interval: $0.56 \pm 1.96(0.0221) \Rightarrow (0.5166, 0.6034)$.
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly interprets the interval: "We are 95% confident that the true proportion of adults in the city who support the new public transportation initiative is between 0.5166 and 0.6034."

FRQ 2

A manufacturer claims that less than 5% of their products are defective. A quality control manager wants to test this claim. A random sample of 200 products is inspected, and 12 of them are found to be defective.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test to investigate the manufacturer's claim. Define any parameters used.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value calculated in part (c), and using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the manager make? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): $H_0: p = 0.05$, $H_a: p < 0.05$, where $p$ is the true proportion of defective products.
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies random sampling (stated), checks normality ($np_0 = 200(0.05) = 10 \ge 10$ and $n(1-p_0) = 200(0.95) = 190 \ge 10$), and checks independence ($200 < 10\%$ of all products).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $\hat{p} = 12/200 = 0.06$. Test statistic $z = \frac{0.06 - 0.05}{\sqrt{\frac{0.05(0.95)}{200}}} \approx 0.65$. P-value $P(Z < 0.65) \approx 0.7422$.
  • Part d (1pt): Since the p-value ($0.7422$) is greater than $\alpha = 0.05$, we fail to reject $H_0$. There is not convincing statistical evidence to support the claim that less than 5% of products are defective.

FRQ 3

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug designed to reduce blood pressure. The null hypothesis, $H_0$, is that the drug has no effect, meaning the proportion of patients whose blood pressure decreases is $p=0.5$. The alternative hypothesis, $H_a$, is that the drug is effective, meaning $p > 0.5$.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this problem.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this problem.
c. Explain a potential consequence of making a Type I error for the pharmaceutical company.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): A Type I error occurs if the company concludes the drug is effective (rejects $H_0$) when, in reality, it has no effect ($p=0.5$).
  • Part b (1pt): A Type II error occurs if the company concludes the drug has no effect (fails to reject $H_0$) when, in reality, it is effective ($p>0.5$).
  • Part c (1pt): A consequence of a Type I error would be that the company might invest heavily in marketing and production of an ineffective drug, leading to financial losses and possibly harming its reputation.

FRQ 4

To compare the effectiveness of two different online learning platforms for a coding course, a researcher randomly assigned 150 students to Platform A and another 160 students to Platform B. At the end of the course, 120 students from Platform A successfully completed a certification exam, while 135 students from Platform B successfully completed the exam.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure to construct a confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions of students who successfully complete the certification exam between the two platforms.
b. Verify the conditions for constructing this confidence interval.
c. Construct and interpret a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions ($p_A - p_B$).
d. Based on your interval in part (c), is there convincing statistical evidence that one platform leads to a higher proportion of successful completions than the other? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a two-sample z-interval for a difference between population proportions.
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies random assignment (stated). Checks normality for both groups: $n_A\hat{p}_A = 120 \ge 10$, $n_A(1-\hat{p}_A) = 30 \ge 10$, $n_B\hat{p}_B = 135 \ge 10$, $n_B(1-\hat{p}_B) = 25 \ge 10$. Assumes independence between groups due to random assignment.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $\hat{p}_A = 120/150 = 0.8$, $\hat{p}_B = 135/160 = 0.84375$. $SE = \sqrt{\frac{0.8(0.2)}{150} + \frac{0.84375(0.15625)}{160}} \approx 0.0441$. Interval: $(0.8 - 0.84375) \pm 1.645(0.0441) \Rightarrow -0.04375 \pm 0.0725 \Rightarrow (-0.1162, 0.0288)$. Interpretation: "We are 90% confident that the true difference in proportions of successful completions (Platform A - Platform B) is between -0.1162 and 0.0288."
  • Part d (1pt): Since the interval contains 0, there is not convincing statistical evidence that one platform leads to a higher proportion of successful completions than the other. The true difference could be zero, or positive, or negative.

FRQ 5

A university wants to determine if there is a difference in the proportion of male and female students who graduate within four years. A random sample of 250 male students showed that 180 graduated within four years. A separate random sample of 300 female students showed that 230 graduated within four years.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test if there is a significant difference in the true proportions of male and female students who graduate within four years. Define any parameters used.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value calculated in part (c), and using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what conclusion should the university make? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): $H_0: p_M = p_F$ (or $p_M - p_F = 0$), $H_a: p_M \ne p_F$ (or $p_M - p_F \ne 0$), where $p_M$ is the true proportion of male students graduating within four years and $p_F$ is the true proportion for female students.
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies random samples (stated). Checks normality for both groups (using pooled proportion): $\hat{p}_M = 180/250 = 0.72$, $\hat{p}_F = 230/300 \approx 0.7667$. Pooled $\hat{p}_c = (180+230)/(250+300) = 410/550 \approx 0.745$. $n_M\hat{p}_c = 250(0.745) \approx 186.25 \ge 10$, $n_M(1-\hat{p}_c) = 250(0.255) \approx 63.75 \ge 10$, $n_F\hat{p}_c = 300(0.745) \approx 223.5 \ge 10$, $n_F(1-\hat{p}_c) = 300(0.255) \approx 76.5 \ge 10$. Checks independence ($250 < 10\%$ of male students and $300 < 10\%$ of female students).
  • Part c (2pts): $z = \frac{(\hat{p}_M - \hat{p}_F) - 0}{\sqrt{\hat{p}_c(1-\hat{p}_c)(\frac{1}{n_M} + \frac{1}{n_F})}} = \frac{(0.72 - 0.7667)}{\sqrt{0.745(0.255)(\frac{1}{250} + \frac{1}{300})}} \approx \frac{-0.0467}{\sqrt{0.189975(0.004 + 0.00333)}} \approx \frac{-0.0467}{\sqrt{0.001399}} \approx \frac{-0.0467}{0.0374} \approx -1.249$. P-value $2P(Z < -1.249) \approx 2(0.1058) \approx 0.2116$.
  • Part d (1pt): Since the p-value ($0.2116$) is greater than $\alpha = 0.01$, we fail to reject $H_0$. There is not convincing statistical evidence to conclude that there is a significant difference in the true proportions of male and female students who graduate within four years.
