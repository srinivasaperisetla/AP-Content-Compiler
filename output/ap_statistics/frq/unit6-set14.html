AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 14)

FRQ 1

A company manufactures computer chips. They want to estimate the proportion of chips that pass a rigorous quality control test. A random sample of 250 chips is selected, and 210 of them pass the test.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure for constructing a confidence interval for the true proportion of chips that pass the quality control test and verify the conditions for this procedure.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of chips that pass the quality control test. Interpret the interval in the context of the problem.
c. The company claims that at least 88% of their chips pass the quality control test. Based on your confidence interval in part (b), does this claim seem plausible? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample z-interval for a population proportion (1pt); Verifies conditions (Random, 10% condition, Large Counts) (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates a correct 95% confidence interval for p (1pt); Interprets the interval in context (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): States whether the claim is plausible and provides a correct justification based on the confidence interval (1pt)

FRQ 2

A health organization claims that the proportion of adults in a certain city who exercise regularly is 60%. A fitness enthusiast believes this proportion is lower. A random sample of 150 adults from the city is surveyed, and 81 report that they exercise regularly.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test to investigate the fitness enthusiast's belief. Identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for the test.
c. Interpret the p-value in the context of the problem. Based on your p-value, what conclusion should be made at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses (0.5pt); Identifies one-sample z-test for a population proportion (0.5pt)
  • Part b (3pts): Verifies conditions (Random, 10% condition, Large Counts using hypothesized proportion) (1pt); Calculates correct test statistic (1pt); Calculates correct p-value (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the p-value in context (1pt); States a correct conclusion in context and provides a correct justification based on the p-value (1pt)

FRQ 3

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug designed to reduce allergic reactions. They conduct a significance test to see if the proportion of people experiencing allergic reactions is significantly lower when taking the new drug compared to the current standard treatment. The null hypothesis is that the proportion of allergic reactions is the same for both treatments, and the alternative hypothesis is that it is lower for the new drug.
a. Describe a Type I error and a Type II error in the context of this problem and their potential consequences.
b. If the company increases the sample size for the study, how would this affect the probability of a Type II error, assuming all other factors remain constant? Explain.
c. Suppose the company decides to use a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$ instead of $\alpha = 0.05$. How would this change affect the probability of a Type I error and the probability of a Type II error, assuming all other factors remain constant?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Describes a Type I error in context with a plausible consequence (1pt); Describes a Type II error in context with a plausible consequence (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Explains how increasing sample size affects the probability of a Type II error (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Explains how changing the significance level affects the probabilities of Type I and Type II errors (1pt)

FRQ 4

A consumer advocacy group wants to compare the proportion of defective items produced by two different manufacturers, A and B. They randomly select 300 items from Manufacturer A's production and find 18 defective items. They also randomly select 250 items from Manufacturer B's production and find 20 defective items.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure for constructing a confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions of defective items (Manufacturer A - Manufacturer B). Verify the conditions for this procedure.
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions of defective items (Manufacturer A - Manufacturer B). Interpret the interval in the context of the problem.
c. Based on your confidence interval in part (b), does there appear to be a significant difference in the proportion of defective items produced by Manufacturer A and Manufacturer B? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies two-sample z-interval for a difference of population proportions (1pt); Verifies conditions (Random, 10% condition, Large Counts for both samples) (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates a correct 90% confidence interval for $p_A - p_B$ (1pt); Interprets the interval in context (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): States whether there is a significant difference and provides a correct justification based on the confidence interval (1pt)

FRQ 5

A university is investigating whether there is a difference in the proportion of students who graduate within four years based on their enrollment status (in-state vs. out-of-state). A random sample of 200 in-state students found 124 graduated within four years. A separate random sample of 150 out-of-state students found 81 graduated within four years.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test to determine if there is a difference in the true proportion of students who graduate within four years between in-state and out-of-state students. Identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for the test.
c. Interpret the p-value in the context of the problem. Based on your p-value, what conclusion should be made at the $\alpha = 0.01$ significance level? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses (0.5pt); Identifies two-sample z-test for a difference of population proportions (0.5pt)
  • Part b (3pts): Verifies conditions (Random, 10% condition, Large Counts using pooled proportion for both samples) (1pt); Calculates correct test statistic (1pt); Calculates correct p-value (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the p-value in context (1pt); States a correct conclusion in context and provides a correct justification based on the p-value (1pt)
