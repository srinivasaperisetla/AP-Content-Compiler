AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 4: Probability, Random Variables, and Probability Distributions (Set 20)

FRQ 1

A survey was conducted among high school seniors about their plans after graduation. Of the surveyed students, 60% plan to attend a 4-year college, 25% plan to attend a 2-year college, and the remaining plan to enter the workforce directly. Of those planning to attend a 4-year college, 70% plan to study STEM fields. Of those planning to attend a 2-year college, 40% plan to study STEM fields.
a. What is the probability that a randomly selected senior plans to enter the workforce directly?
b. Are the events "plans to attend a 4-year college" and "plans to attend a 2-year college" mutually exclusive? Justify your answer.
c. What is the probability that a randomly selected senior plans to study STEM fields, given that they plan to attend a 4-year college?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates $1 - (0.60 + 0.25) = 0.15$.
  • Part b (1pt): States yes and explains that a student cannot attend both a 4-year and a 2-year college simultaneously, meaning their intersection is empty or probability is 0.
  • Part c (1pt): Identifies the given probability as $P(\text{STEM}
  • \text{4-year college}) = 0.70$.

FRQ 2

A carnival game involves rolling a fair six-sided die. If a player rolls a 1 or a 2, they win \$5. If they roll a 3, 4, or 5, they win \$1. If they roll a 6, they lose \$10. Let $X$ be the random variable representing the amount of money won or lost by a player in a single roll.
a. Construct the probability distribution for $X$.
b. Calculate the expected value of $X$. Show your work.
c. Interpret the expected value of $X$ in the context of this carnival game.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly lists all possible values of $X$ and their corresponding probabilities in a table or equivalent representation. ($P(X=5) = 2/6$, $P(X=1) = 3/6$, $P(X=-10) = 1/6$).
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct calculation of $E(X) = 5(\frac{2}{6}) + 1(\frac{3}{6}) + (-10)(\frac{1}{6}) = \frac{10+3-10}{6} = \frac{3}{6} = 0.50$.
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets $E(X)$ as the average amount of money a player can expect to win (or lose) per game in the long run (e.g., "On average, a player can expect to win \$0.50 per game in the long run if they play this game many times.").

FRQ 3

A company manufactures two types of light bulbs, Type A and Type B. The lifespan of Type A bulbs (in hours), $L_A$, has a mean of 1000 hours and a standard deviation of 150 hours. The lifespan of Type B bulbs, $L_B$, has a mean of 1200 hours and a standard deviation of 200 hours. Assume $L_A$ and $L_B$ are independent.
a. A new "super bulb" is created by combining the materials of Type A and Type B bulbs, such that its lifespan is $L_S = 0.8L_A + 0.2L_B$. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of $L_S$.
b. What is the mean and standard deviation of the difference in lifespans, $L_B - L_A$?
c. Describe how a linear transformation $Y = aX + b$ affects the mean and standard deviation of a random variable $X$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates $E(L_S) = 0.8E(L_A) + 0.2E(L_B) = 0.8(1000) + 0.2(1200) = 1040$ hours. Calculates $SD(L_S) = \sqrt{(0.8)^2 SD(L_A)^2 + (0.2)^2 SD(L_B)^2} = \sqrt{0.64(150^2) + 0.04(200^2)} = \sqrt{16000} \approx 126.49$ hours.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $E(L_B - L_A) = E(L_B) - E(L_A) = 1200 - 1000 = 200$ hours. Calculates $SD(L_B - L_A) = \sqrt{SD(L_B)^2 + SD(L_A)^2} = \sqrt{200^2 + 150^2} = 250$ hours.
  • Part c (1pt): States that the mean is transformed by $E(Y) = aE(X) + b$ and the standard deviation is transformed by $SD(Y) =
  • a
  • SD(X)$, explaining that adding/subtracting a constant does not change the standard deviation, and multiplying by a constant multiplies the standard deviation by the absolute value of that constant.

FRQ 4

A survey indicates that 30% of adults in a certain city prefer to get their news from online sources. A random sample of 15 adults from this city is selected. Assume that the preferences of these adults are independent.
a. Explain why a binomial distribution is an appropriate model for the number of adults in the sample who prefer online news sources.
b. Calculate the probability that exactly 4 adults in the sample prefer online news sources. Show your work.
c. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the number of adults in the sample who prefer online news sources.
d. Interpret the mean calculated in part (c) in the context of this situation.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Explains the BINs conditions: Binary (online vs not online), Independent (given), Fixed number of trials ($n=15$), Same probability of success ($p=0.30$).
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates $P(X=4) = \binom{15}{4} (0.30)^4 (0.70)^{11} \approx 0.2186$.
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates mean $\mu = np = 15(0.30) = 4.5$ and standard deviation $\sigma = \sqrt{np(1-p)} = \sqrt{15(0.30)(0.70)} = \sqrt{3.15} \approx 1.775$.
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets the mean as the average number of adults (4.5) who prefer online news sources in a sample of 15, if many such samples were taken.

FRQ 5

A quality control inspector is checking a batch of electronic components. The probability that a randomly selected component is defective is 0.08. The inspector examines components one by one until the first defective component is found. Assume that the components are independent.
a. Explain why a geometric distribution is an appropriate model for the number of components examined until the first defective one is found.
b. Calculate the probability that the first defective component is found on the 5th component examined. Show your work.
c. Calculate the expected number of components the inspector must examine to find the first defective one, and interpret this value in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Explains the conditions for a geometric distribution: Binary (defective vs not defective), Independent trials (given), Same probability of success ($p=0.08$), and interested in the number of trials until the first success (first defective component).
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates $P(X=5) = (1-0.08)^{5-1} (0.08) = (0.92)^4 (0.08) \approx 0.0573$.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the expected value $E(X) = 1/p = 1/0.08 = 12.5$. Interprets this as, on average, the inspector must examine 12.5 components to find the first defective one, if many such inspections were performed.
