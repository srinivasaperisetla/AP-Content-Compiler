AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 2: Exploring Two-Variable Data (Set 1)

FRQ 1

A researcher collected data on the number of hours spent studying for an exam ($x$) and the score obtained on the exam ($y$) for 15 students. The scatterplot shows a generally upward trend. The correlation coefficient was calculated as $r = 0.85$.
a. Describe the key characteristics of the scatterplot relating study hours to exam scores.
b. Interpret the value of the correlation coefficient $r = 0.85$ in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Describes direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (strong). Mentions context.
  • Part b (2pts): States that there is a strong, positive linear association between hours studied and exam scores. Mentions context.

FRQ 3

A linear regression model was developed to predict the height of a tree (in meters) based on its age (in years). The least-squares regression line is given by $\hat{Height} = 1.2 + 0.35 \times Age$.
a. Use the regression model to predict the height of a tree that is 10 years old.
b. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
c. Interpret the $y$-intercept of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates $\hat{Height} = 1.2 + 0.35(10) = 4.7$ meters.
  • Part b (2pts): Explains that for every additional year in age, the predicted height of the tree increases by 0.35 meters.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that a tree with an age of 0 years is predicted to be 1.2 meters tall. Acknowledges if extrapolation is involved or if the intercept lacks practical meaning.

FRQ 5

A scatterplot shows the relationship between the number of hours a student sleeps ($x$) and their score on a short quiz ($y$). Most students sleep between 6 and 9 hours and score between 70 and 90. One student reported sleeping 2 hours and scored 85.
a. Identify the influential point in this data set. Explain why it is considered an influential point.
b. Describe how removing this influential point would likely affect the correlation coefficient between hours slept and quiz score.
c. Does the presence of a strong correlation between hours slept and quiz score imply that sleeping more hours *causes* higher quiz scores? Explain why or why not.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies the point (2 hours, 85 score) as influential because it has a substantially different x-value (far from the mean of x-values) and pulling the regression line towards itself.
  • Part b (1pt): States that removing the influential point would likely make the correlation coefficient stronger (closer to 1 or -1) if the original point was weakening the relationship, or if it was pulling the line in a direction opposite to the main cluster.
  • Part c (2pts): Explains that correlation does not imply causation. There could be confounding variables (e.g., study habits, stress levels) or it could be by chance.
