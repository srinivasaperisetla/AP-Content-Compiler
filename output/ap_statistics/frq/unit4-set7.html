FRQ 2

A carnival game involves rolling a fair six-sided die. If the die shows a 6, you win \$5. If it shows a 5, you win \$2. Otherwise, you lose \$1. Let $X$ be the random variable representing the net amount of money won or lost in one play of the game.
a. Construct the probability distribution for $X$.
b. Calculate the expected value of $X$.
c. Interpret the expected value of $X$ in the context of the carnival game.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies possible values of $X$ ($5, 2, -1$) and their probabilities $P(X=5) = \frac{1}{6}$, $P(X=2) = \frac{1}{6}$, $P(X=-1) = \frac{4}{6}$ (or $\frac{2}{3}$). Table or list format is acceptable.
  • Part b (1pt): Correct calculation $E(X) = (5)(\frac{1}{6}) + (2)(\frac{1}{6}) + (-1)(\frac{4}{6}) = \frac{5+2-4}{6} = \frac{3}{6} = \$0.50$.
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets $E(X)$ as the average amount won per game over many plays.

FRQ 3

An electronics company produces two types of resistors, Type A and Type B. The resistance of Type A resistors has a mean of $\mu_A = 100$ ohms and a standard deviation of $\sigma_A = 5$ ohms. The resistance of Type B resistors has a mean of $\mu_B = 200$ ohms and a standard deviation of $\sigma_B = 10$ ohms. Assume the resistances are independent.
a. A circuit requires two Type A resistors in series. The total resistance $R_1 = A_1 + A_2$. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of $R_1$.
b. Another circuit requires one Type A resistor and one Type B resistor in series. The total resistance $R_2 = A + B$. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of $R_2$.
c. Suppose the company manufactures a new resistor by taking a Type A resistor and increasing its resistance by a factor of 1.5, then adding 10 ohms. Let $R_3 = 1.5A + 10$. Describe how the mean and standard deviation of $R_3$ relate to the mean and standard deviation of Type A resistors.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Mean $E(R_1) = \mu_A + \mu_A = 100 + 100 = 200$ ohms. Standard deviation $\sigma_{R_1} = \sqrt{\sigma_A^2 + \sigma_A^2} = \sqrt{5^2 + 5^2} = \sqrt{50} \approx 7.071$ ohms.
  • Part b (2pts): Mean $E(R_2) = \mu_A + \mu_B = 100 + 200 = 300$ ohms. Standard deviation $\sigma_{R_2} = \sqrt{\sigma_A^2 + \sigma_B^2} = \sqrt{5^2 + 10^2} = \sqrt{25 + 100} = \sqrt{125} \approx 11.180$ ohms.
  • Part c (2pts): The mean $E(R_3) = 1.5E(A) + 10 = 1.5(100) + 10 = 160$ ohms. The standard deviation $\sigma_{R_3} =
  • 1.5
  • \sigma_A = 1.5(5) = 7.5$ ohms. Explains that adding a constant shifts the mean but not the standard deviation, while multiplying by a constant scales both.

FRQ 4

A large manufacturer of light bulbs states that 90% of its bulbs last at least 1,000 hours. A quality control inspector randomly selects 12 bulbs from a large batch for testing.
a. Verify that the conditions for a binomial distribution are met for the number of bulbs that last at least 1,000 hours in the sample.
b. Calculate the probability that exactly 10 of the 12 selected bulbs last at least 1,000 hours.
c. Calculate the mean (expected number) of bulbs out of 12 that would last at least 1,000 hours.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States fixed number of trials ($n=12$), two outcomes (last $\ge$ 1000 hrs or not), independent trials (large batch implies sampling without replacement is approximately independent), and constant probability of success ($p=0.9$).
  • Part b (1pt): Correct calculation $P(X=10) = \binom{12}{10}(0.9)^{10}(0.1)^2 \approx 0.232$.
  • Part c (1pt): Correct calculation $E(X) = np = 12 \times 0.9 = 10.8$ bulbs.

FRQ 5

A customer service hotline has a 15% chance of connecting a caller to an agent within the first minute. Assume each call attempt is independent. A person keeps calling until they connect to an agent within the first minute.
a. What is the probability that the person connects to an agent within the first minute on their third call?
b. What is the probability that the person needs more than 4 calls to connect to an agent within the first minute?
c. Calculate the expected number of calls needed to connect to an agent within the first minute.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correct calculation $P(X=3) = (1-0.15)^{3-1}(0.15) = (0.85)^2(0.15) \approx 0.108375$.
  • Part b (2pts): Correct calculation $P(X>4) = (1-0.15)^4 = (0.85)^4 \approx 0.5220$. Alternatively, $P(X>4) = 1 - P(X \le 4) = 1 - (P(X=1) + P(X=2) + P(X=3) + P(X=4))$.
  • Part c (1pt): Correct calculation $E(X) = \frac{1}{p} = \frac{1}{0.15} \approx 6.67$ calls.
