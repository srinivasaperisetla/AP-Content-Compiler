FRQ 1

A study investigated the relationship between a student's preferred learning style (Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic) and their performance on a standardized test (Pass, Fail). A random sample of 200 students was classified, and the results are summarized in the table below.
Learning StylePassFailTotal
Visual552580
Auditory451560
Kinesthetic303060
Total13070200
a. Calculate the proportion of students who passed the standardized test for each learning style (Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic).
b. Compare the proportions calculated in part (a). Based on these proportions, does there appear to be an association between preferred learning style and test performance? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correct calculation of proportions for all three learning styles ($\frac{55}{80} = 0.6875$, $\frac{45}{60} = 0.75$, $\frac{30}{60} = 0.50$)
  • Part b (2pts): Correct comparison of the proportions (e.g., Auditory has the highest pass rate, Kinesthetic the lowest) AND a clear justification regarding association based on the comparison (e.g., since the proportions are different across learning styles, there appears to be an association).

FRQ 2

A meteorologist is studying the relationship between the daily high temperature (in degrees Fahrenheit) and the amount of electricity consumed (in megawatts) in a small town. Over 10 randomly selected days, she recorded the following data. Day 1: (70, 45), Day 2: (72, 47), Day 3: (75, 52), Day 4: (68, 42), Day 5: (80, 58), Day 6: (65, 38), Day 7: (78, 55), Day 8: (73, 49), Day 9: (82, 60), Day 10: (69, 43)
a. Calculate the correlation coefficient for these data. Show your work or state the formula used and the relevant summary statistics if using a calculator.
b. Interpret the value of the correlation coefficient in the context of this problem.
c. Suppose on day 11, the high temperature was $95^\circ F$ and the electricity consumed was $70$ megawatts. Explain how including this new data point might affect the correlation coefficient.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correct calculation of $r \approx 0.98$ (using technology) OR correct formula with relevant summary statistics (e.g., means, standard deviations, sum of products of z-scores) AND correct numerical answer.
  • Part b (1pt): Correct interpretation of $r$ in context (e.g., There is a strong, positive linear relationship between the daily high temperature and the amount of electricity consumed).
  • Part c (2pts): Correct explanation that the new data point $(95^\circ F, 70 \text{ MW})$ extends the range of both temperature and electricity consumption while maintaining the strong positive linear pattern observed in the original data. This would likely strengthen (increase) the correlation coefficient $r$.

FRQ 3

A car manufacturer wants to model the relationship between the age of a car (in years) and its selling price (in thousands of dollars). Based on a sample of cars, the least-squares regression line relating selling price ($\hat{y}$) to age ($x$) is given by $\hat{y} = 28.5 - 2.1x$.
a. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
b. Interpret the y-intercept of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem. If it is not appropriate to interpret, explain why not.
c. Predict the selling price of a 5-year-old car using this model.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correct interpretation of the slope: For each additional year of a car's age, the predicted selling price decreases by $2.1$ thousand dollars.
  • Part b (2pts): Correct interpretation of the y-intercept: A brand new car (0 years old) is predicted to sell for $28.5$ thousand dollars. AND a correct justification for its appropriateness (e.g., 0 years is within or close to the range of ages typically observed for cars, so it is a reasonable value to interpret).
  • Part c (1pt): Correct calculation of the predicted selling price: $\hat{y} = 28.5 - 2.1(5) = 28.5 - 10.5 = 18$ thousand dollars.

FRQ 4

A researcher collected data on daily maximum temperature ($^\circ$C) and the number of ice cream units sold at a beachside stand. The data for 8 randomly selected days are shown below: \nTemperature ($^\circ$C): 14, 16, 18, 20, 22, 24, 26, 28 \nSales (units): 185, 210, 230, 260, 280, 305, 330, 350
a. Construct a scatterplot of the data, with temperature on the horizontal axis and sales on the vertical axis.
b. Describe the relationship between daily maximum temperature and ice cream sales shown in the scatterplot, including strength, direction, and form.
c. The correlation coefficient for this data is $r \approx 0.98$. Interpret this value in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly plots all 8 points with labeled axes (1pt for axes, 1pt for points).
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the relationship as strong, positive, and linear (1pt for direction/strength, 1pt for form).
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets $r=0.98$ as a strong positive linear association between temperature and ice cream sales.

FRQ 5

A school guidance counselor is investigating the relationship between the number of hours a student spends studying per week ($x$) and their score on a standardized exam ($y$). The least-squares regression line calculated from a sample of students is $\hat{y} = 2.5 + 12x$.
a. Calculate the predicted exam score for a student who studied for 5 hours per week.
b. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
c. Interpret the $y$-intercept of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correct calculation $\hat{y} = 2.5 + 12(5) = 62.5$.
  • Part b (2pts): For each additional hour studied per week, the predicted exam score increases by 12 points.
  • Part c (1pt): A student who studies 0 hours per week is predicted to score 2.5 points on the exam. Acknowledges that this interpretation may not be reasonable in context.
