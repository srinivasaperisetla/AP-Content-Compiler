AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 2: Exploring Two-Variable Data (Set 18)

FRQ 1

A researcher is studying the relationship between the number of hours students spend studying per week and their score on a standardized math test. Data were collected from 15 students, and a scatterplot of the data is shown below. The correlation coefficient was calculated as $r = 0.85$.
a. Describe the relationship between study hours and test scores shown in the scatterplot.
b. Interpret the meaning of the correlation coefficient $r = 0.85$ in the context of this study.
c. Does a strong positive correlation imply that studying more hours *causes* higher test scores? Explain your reasoning.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Describes direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (strong) in context, and mentions unusual points if any.
  • Part b (1pt): Interprets $r$ as a strong positive linear association between study hours and test scores in context.
  • Part c (1pt): States that correlation does not imply causation AND provides a brief explanation (e.g., lurking variables).

FRQ 2

A company that sells custom-made t-shirts records the number of t-shirts ordered ($x$) and the total cost ($y$, in dollars) for each order. A least-squares regression analysis was performed, and the computer output is provided below.
PredictorCoefSE CoefTP
Constant15.502.107.380.000
T-shirts8.250.2533.000.000
S = 3.82R-Sq = 97.2%R-Sq(adj) = 97.0%
a. Write the equation of the least-squares regression line to predict the total cost from the number of t-shirts ordered.
b. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
c. Interpret the $y$-intercept of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem. Does this value make sense in context?
d. Predict the total cost for an order of 30 t-shirts.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly writes the LSRL equation, e.g., $\hat{y} = 15.50 + 8.25x$.
  • Part b (1pt): Interprets the slope (8.25) as an increase of $8.25 in total cost for each additional t-shirt ordered.
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the y-intercept (15.50) as the estimated fixed cost or base price for an order of 0 t-shirts AND comments on its contextual reasonableness (e.g., setup fee).
  • Part d (1pt): Calculates the predicted cost for 30 t-shirts using the LSRL, showing $\hat{y} = 15.50 + 8.25(30) = 263$.

FRQ 3

A survey was conducted to investigate the relationship between a person's preferred method of transportation for daily commuting (Car, Public Transit, Bike) and their age group (Under 30, 30-50, Over 50). The results from a random sample of 200 commuters are summarized in the two-way table below.
Preferred MethodUnder 3030-50Over 50Total
Car20452590
Public Transit30351580
Bike1051530
Total608555200
a. Calculate the proportion of commuters who prefer Public Transit.
b. Calculate the proportion of commuters who are Under 30 AND prefer Public Transit.
c. Based on these data, does there appear to be an association between age group and preferred method of transportation? Justify your answer by comparing appropriate conditional proportions.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates marginal proportion: $(30+35+15)/200 = 80/200 = 0.40$.
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates joint proportion: $30/200 = 0.15$.
  • Part c (2pts): Compares at least two conditional proportions (e.g., proportion of Under 30 who prefer Public Transit ($30/60 = 0.50$) vs. proportion of Over 50 who prefer Public Transit ($15/55 \approx 0.273$)) AND correctly states whether there appears to be an association based on the comparison (e.g., proportions are different, indicating association).

FRQ 4

A study investigated the relationship between the age of a car (in years, $x$) and its selling price (in thousands of dollars, $y$). Data from 10 cars were collected, and a scatterplot is shown below. An initial least-squares regression line was calculated to be $\hat{y} = 25 - 2.5x$. However, a specific car (Car K) with an age of 12 years and a selling price of 5 thousand dollars was noted.
a. Based on the scatterplot, identify Car K as an outlier, an influential point, or both. Justify your answer.
b. Explain how the exclusion of Car K would likely affect the slope of the least-squares regression line. Do not perform any calculations.
c. Suppose that for a different set of cars, a transformation of the response variable was used, resulting in the model $\widehat{\log_{10}(y)} = 1.3 - 0.05x$. Predict the selling price (in thousands of dollars) for a car that is 8 years old using this transformed model.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies Car K as an influential point AND justifies by noting its extreme x-value relative to other data and its significant vertical distance from the original regression line (or its potential to significantly change the slope/intercept).
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that removing Car K (which pulls the line down) would likely make the slope less steep (closer to zero or less negative).
  • Part c (2pts): Uses the transformed equation to find $\widehat{\log_{10}(y)} = 1.3 - 0.05(8) = 0.9$. Then correctly back-transforms to find predicted price: $\hat{y} = 10^{0.9} \approx 7.94$ thousand dollars.
