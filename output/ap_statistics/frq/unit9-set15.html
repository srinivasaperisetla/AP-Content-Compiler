AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data: Slopes (Set 15)

FRQ 1

A research team studied the relationship between the age of a car (in years) and its selling price (in thousands of dollars). They collected data from a random sample of 20 cars of a specific model. A linear regression analysis was performed, yielding a sample slope of $b = -2.5$ with a standard error of the slope $SE_b = 0.4$. The degrees of freedom for this inference are $18$.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure for constructing a confidence interval for the true slope and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the slope of the true regression line.
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in the context of this problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): (1pt) Identifies t-interval for slope. (1pt) States/verifies appropriate conditions (Linearity, Independence, Normal residuals, Equal variance).
  • Part b (2pts): (1pt) Correctly calculates $t^*$ value for $df=18$ at 95% confidence (e.g., $t^* = 2.101$). (1pt) Correct calculation of interval: $-2.5 \pm 2.101(0.4) \implies (-3.3404, -1.6596)$.
  • Part c (1pt): Correct interpretation in context (e.g., "We are 95% confident that for each additional year in age, the car's selling price decreases by between \$1,660 and \$3,340.").

FRQ 2

A nutritionist investigates whether there is a linear relationship between a person's daily sugar intake (in grams) and their body mass index (BMI). Data from a random sample of 30 individuals are collected.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test to determine if there is a positive linear relationship between daily sugar intake and BMI. Define any parameters used.
b. Identify the appropriate inference method for this significance test and verify the conditions for its use.
c. The nutritionist performed the test and obtained a test statistic of $t = 2.87$ and a P-value of $0.0039$. Interpret the P-value in the context of this study.
d. Using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what conclusion should the nutritionist draw from the test? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): (1/2pt) $H_0: \beta = 0$, $H_a: \beta > 0$ where $\beta$ is the true slope of the regression line relating daily sugar intake to BMI. (1/2pt) Correctly defines $\beta$.
  • Part b (2pts): (1pt) Identifies a t-test for the slope of a regression line. (1pt) States appropriate conditions (Linearity, Independence, Normal residuals, Equal variance) and mentions their verification.
  • Part c (1pt): Correct interpretation of the P-value (e.g., "Assuming there is no linear relationship between daily sugar intake and BMI, there is a 0.39% chance of observing a sample slope as extreme or more extreme than the one observed, purely by random variation.").
  • Part d (1pt): (1/2pt) Compares P-value ($0.0039$) to $\alpha$ ($0.01$) and concludes P-value < $\alpha$. Rejects $H_0$. (1/2pt) Concludes there is convincing evidence of a positive linear relationship between daily sugar intake and BMI.

FRQ 3

A company manufacturing tires wants to investigate the relationship between the amount of rubber (in kg) used in a tire and the tire's lifespan (in thousands of miles). They plan to collect data from a sample of tires.
a. Describe how increasing the sample size from $n=15$ to $n=30$ (while keeping other statistics, like the sample slope and standard error of the slope, approximately constant) would affect the width of a 95% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line. Justify your answer.
b. Explain why it is important to verify that the residuals are approximately normally distributed before constructing a confidence interval for the slope of a regression model.
c. Describe how a scatterplot of the original data could be used to check the linearity condition for inference.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): (1/2pt) States that the width of the confidence interval would decrease. (1/2pt) Justifies by explaining that a larger sample size leads to more degrees of freedom, which results in a smaller critical $t^*$ value, and/or a smaller standard error for the slope, both of which reduce the margin of error and thus the interval width.
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that the t-distribution used for inference on the slope relies on the assumption of normal residuals for its validity, especially for smaller sample sizes.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that a scatterplot should show a roughly linear pattern without obvious curvature, indicating that a linear model is appropriate.

FRQ 4

A psychologist investigates the relationship between the amount of time (in minutes) spent meditating daily and a person's reported stress level (on a scale of 1 to 100). Data from a random sample of 22 participants is collected.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test to determine if there is a negative linear relationship between meditation time and stress level. Define any parameters used.
b. A regression analysis was performed, and the sample slope for meditation time was $b = -0.75$ with a standard error of the slope $SE_b = 0.20$. Calculate the test statistic for this hypothesis test.
c. The degrees of freedom for this test are $20$. Using this information, interpret a P-value of $0.001$ in the context of this study.
d. Using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the psychologist draw from this test? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): (1/2pt) $H_0: \beta = 0$, $H_a: \beta < 0$ where $\beta$ is the true slope of the regression line relating meditation time to stress level. (1/2pt) Correctly defines $\beta$.
  • Part b (1pt): Correct calculation of test statistic: $t = \frac{b - 0}{SE_b} = \frac{-0.75 - 0}{0.20} = -3.75$.
  • Part c (1pt): Correct interpretation of the P-value (e.g., "Assuming there is no linear relationship between meditation time and stress level, there is a 0.1% chance of observing a sample slope of -0.75 or more extreme (more negative) purely by random variation.").
  • Part d (1pt): (1/2pt) Compares P-value ($0.001$) to $\alpha$ ($0.05$) and concludes P-value < $\alpha$. Rejects $H_0$. (1/2pt) Concludes there is convincing evidence of a negative linear relationship between meditation time and stress level.

FRQ 5

A meteorologist observes a scatter plot showing the relationship between the daily maximum temperature (in degrees Celsius) and the number of hours of sunshine for 30 consecutive days in a city. The scatter plot displays a generally positive linear trend.
a. Identify a statistical question about the relationship between daily maximum temperature and hours of sunshine that could be answered using inference for the slope of a regression model.
b. Describe what the true slope of the regression line would represent in the context of this problem.
c. If the meteorologist wants to estimate the true slope with a 99% confidence interval, identify the appropriate inference procedure by name and provide the general formula for the interval.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a question such as "Is there a significant linear relationship between daily maximum temperature and hours of sunshine?" or "What is the average change in temperature for each additional hour of sunshine?".
  • Part b (1pt): Describes the true slope as the average change in daily maximum temperature for each additional hour of sunshine in the population.
  • Part c (1pt): (1/2pt) Identifies "t-interval for the slope of a regression line" or "linear regression t-interval". (1/2pt) Provides the general formula: $b \pm t^*(SE_b)$.
