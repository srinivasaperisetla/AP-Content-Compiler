AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 20)

FRQ 1

A market researcher collected data on the favorite type of hot drink among a sample of 100 people at a local coffee shop. The results were: Coffee (45), Tea (30), Hot Chocolate (15), Other (10).
a. Construct a relative frequency table for these data.
b. Create a bar chart to display the distribution of favorite hot drinks.
c. Describe one important feature of the distribution of favorite hot drinks.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates relative frequencies and constructs a table with labels
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly constructs a bar chart with labeled axes and categories
  • Part c (1pt): Describes the most popular category or provides a clear insight from the distribution in context

FRQ 2

A fitness tracker company recorded the number of steps taken in a day by 20 randomly selected users. The data are as follows (in thousands of steps): 5.2, 6.1, 7.5, 8.0, 8.1, 8.3, 8.5, 9.0, 9.2, 9.5, 9.6, 10.0, 10.1, 10.5, 10.8, 11.2, 11.5, 12.0, 12.5, 18.2.
a. Construct a stemplot of the number of steps taken by these users.
b. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of steps taken. Are there any apparent outliers?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly constructs a stemplot with a key
  • Part b (3pts): Describes shape (e.g., skewed right), center (median or mean value), and spread (e.g., range/IQR) in context; Identifies and justifies any apparent outliers (e.g., visually based on gap)

FRQ 3

A statistics student collected data on the number of text messages sent by 15 of her friends in one day. The data are: 25, 30, 32, 35, 38, 40, 42, 45, 50, 55, 60, 65, 70, 75, 120.
a. Calculate the mean, median, interquartile range (IQR), and standard deviation for this data set.
b. Which measure of center (mean or median) would be a more appropriate choice to describe the typical number of text messages sent? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (3pts): Correctly calculates the mean ($\bar{x}$), median, IQR, and standard deviation ($s_x$)
  • Part b (2pts): Selects the median as more appropriate; Justifies based on the presence of skewness or outliers in the data

FRQ 4

The heights of adult women in a certain country are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 64$ inches and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 2.5$ inches.
a. Calculate the $z$-score for an adult woman who is 68 inches tall. Interpret this $z$-score in context.
b. What proportion of adult women in this country are taller than 68 inches?
c. If an adult woman's height is at the 10th percentile, what is her height in inches?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates $z$-score $\frac{68 - 64}{2.5} = 1.6$; Interprets the $z$-score as 1.6 standard deviations above the mean height
  • Part b (2pts): Uses normal distribution to find proportion, $P(Z > 1.6) \approx 0.0548$; States the proportion in context
  • Part c (2pts): Finds the $z$-score for the 10th percentile (e.g., $z \approx -1.28$); Calculates height: $64 + (-1.28)(2.5) = 60.8$ inches

FRQ 5

A survey asked 100 students about their favorite type of movie. The results were: Action (35), Comedy (40), Drama (15), Sci-Fi (10).
a. Construct a bar chart to display the distribution of favorite movie types. Be sure to label your axes.
b. Describe two key features of the distribution of favorite movie types based on your bar chart.
c. Suppose a similar survey was conducted among 100 faculty members, and 60% preferred Drama. Briefly compare the movie preferences of students and faculty members for Drama movies.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly constructs a bar chart with all categories, accurate heights, and labeled axes (e.g., "Movie Type" and "Number of Students" or "Frequency").
  • Part b (1pt): Describes two features, such as "Comedy is the most preferred category" and "Sci-Fi is the least preferred category" or "More students prefer Action or Comedy than Drama or Sci-Fi".
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly compares the preferences, stating that a higher proportion/percentage of faculty (60%) preferred Drama compared to students (15%).
