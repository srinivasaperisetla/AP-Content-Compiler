AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data: Slopes (Set 7)

FRQ 1

A researcher is studying the relationship between the number of hours a student spends studying for a test (x) and the score they receive on the test (y). A random sample of 25 students was taken, and the least-squares regression line for their data was calculated as $\hat{y} = 55.3 + 1.85x$. The standard error of the slope, $s_b$, was $0.32$.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure for the slope of the regression model. (Do not perform calculations.)
b. Verify the conditions necessary for constructing this confidence interval. Assume a residual plot shows no clear pattern and the residuals appear approximately normal.
c. Calculate and interpret a 95% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly identifies a t-interval for the slope of a regression line
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies all four conditions: Random sample, Independence (10% rule: $25 < 0.10N$), Linearity (residual plot no pattern), Equal standard deviation (residual plot no fan shape), and Normality of residuals (normal probability plot or histogram of residuals, as assumed)
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the 95% confidence interval using $df=n-2=23$ and $t^*=2.069$, showing all values. Correctly interprets the interval in the context of the problem, linking study hours and test scores.

FRQ 2

A consumer advocacy group is investigating the relationship between the age of a light bulb (in hundreds of hours, $x$) and its brightness (in lumens, $y$). They want to determine if there is a significant negative linear relationship between these two variables. They collect data from a random sample of 30 bulbs. A regression analysis yields a sample slope $b = -2.5$ lumens per hundred hours, a standard error of the slope $s_b = 1.0$, and $n=30$.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test to investigate if there is a significant negative linear relationship.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test. Assume that scatterplot and residual plots support linearity, constant variance, and normality.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test. Show all work.
d. Interpret the p-value in context and use it to justify a conclusion about whether there is a significant negative linear relationship at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States $H_0: \beta = 0$ and $H_a: \beta < 0$, defining $\beta$ as the true slope of the linear relationship between light bulb age and brightness.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies all necessary conditions: Random sample, Independence ($30 < 0.10N$), Linearity, Equal standard deviation, and Normality of residuals (by assumption in prompt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates test statistic $t = \frac{b - 0}{s_b} = \frac{-2.5}{1.0} = -2.5$. Calculates p-value for $t = -2.5$ with $df = 30-2=28$ (approx $0.008$).
  • Part d (2pts): Correctly interprets the p-value (e.g., "Assuming there is no linear relationship between age and brightness, there is a 0.8% chance of observing a sample slope as extreme or more extreme than -2.5."). Justifies conclusion: since $p < 0.05$, reject $H_0$. Concludes there is convincing evidence of a significant negative linear relationship between bulb age and brightness.

FRQ 3

A small independent music artist is interested in the relationship between the number of plays a song receives on a streaming service and the number of downloads it gets. Data for 15 of their songs are collected and plotted in a scatterplot, along with the least-squares regression line.
a. Describe the overall pattern of the relationship shown in the scatterplot. (Do not calculate the regression equation.)
b. Based on the scatterplot, identify a question about the variation in the data that could be further investigated using statistical inference.
c. Suppose the artist collects data for 100 songs instead of 15. How would this larger sample size likely affect the width of a confidence interval for the true slope of the relationship between plays and downloads?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Describes the direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (moderately strong to strong) of the relationship, and mentions context.
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies a question related to variation, e.g., "Is the linear relationship between plays and downloads statistically significant?" or "Is the slope of the relationship significantly different from zero?"
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that a larger sample size ($n=100$) would decrease the standard error of the slope ($s_b$), which would lead to a smaller margin of error and thus a narrower confidence interval for the true slope.

FRQ 4

A health scientist is studying the effect of daily exercise time (in minutes, $x$) on a person's resting heart rate (in beats per minute, $y$). They believe that increased exercise time leads to a decrease in resting heart rate. A linear regression analysis was performed on data from a random sample of 50 individuals. The test statistic for the slope was calculated as $t = -2.85$, and the corresponding p-value was $0.003$. Assume all conditions for inference are met.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure for investigating the scientist's belief.
b. State the null and alternative hypotheses for this test, defining any parameters used.
c. Based on the calculated p-value, interpret what this p-value means in the context of this study and state a conclusion at the $\alpha = 0.01$ significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a t-test for the slope of a regression model.
  • Part b (1pt): States $H_0: \beta = 0$ and $H_a: \beta < 0$, where $\beta$ is the true slope of the linear relationship between daily exercise time and resting heart rate.
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the p-value: "Assuming there is no linear relationship between daily exercise time and resting heart rate ($\beta=0$), there is a 0.3% chance of observing a sample slope as extreme as or more extreme than what was found." States conclusion: Since $p=0.003 < \alpha=0.01$, reject $H_0$. Concludes there is convincing evidence that increased daily exercise time leads to a decrease in resting heart rate.

a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure for estimating the true slope of the relationship between fertilizer amount and plant growth.
b. Verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the slope of the regression line.
c. Calculate and interpret a 95% confidence interval for the slope of the regression line.
d. Based on your confidence interval in part (c), justify whether there is convincing evidence of a positive linear relationship between fertilizer amount and plant growth.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly identifies a t-interval for the slope of a regression line (or similar phrasing).
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies linearity (residual plot shows no pattern, implied from context), independence (random sample or assignment), normality (residuals approximately normal/histogram, assumed or checked from data not given), equal variance (residual plot shows constant scatter, assumed or checked). (1pt for 2-3 conditions, 2pts for all 4).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the interval using $\text{df} = 23$ ($n-2$), $t^* = 2.069$ (from t-table for 95% CI, 2-sided), and computes $0.75 \pm 2.069 \times 0.12 = (0.5017, 0.9983)$.
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the interval in context (e.g., "We are 95% confident that for each additional gram of fertilizer, the true mean plant growth increases by between $0.5017$ cm and $0.9983$ cm.").
  • Part d (1pt): States that since the interval $(0.5017, 0.9983)$ contains only positive values, there is convincing evidence of a positive linear relationship between fertilizer amount and plant growth.
