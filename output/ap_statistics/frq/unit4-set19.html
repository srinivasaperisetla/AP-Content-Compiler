FRQ 1

A local coffee shop offers a loyalty program where customers receive a free coffee after accumulating 5 stamps. Each purchase earns one stamp. The probability that a customer makes a purchase on any given day is $0.6$. The shop wants to estimate the probability that a customer will earn a free coffee within 8 purchases.
a. Describe a simulation using a random number generator that could be used to estimate the probability that a customer earns a free coffee within 8 purchases. Clearly state what constitutes one trial, what outcomes are recorded, and how the random numbers would be assigned.
b. Conduct three trials of your simulation using the following list of random digits: $29317\ 95076\ 12399\ 54310\ 07354\ 81961$. Record the number of purchases needed to earn a free coffee for each trial and whether a free coffee was earned within 8 purchases.
c. Based on your three trials, estimate the probability that a customer earns a free coffee within 8 purchases.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a random process for one purchase (e.g., digits 0-5 for purchase, 6-9 for no purchase)
  • Describes how to count purchases and stamps for one trial, and determine if 5 stamps are earned within 8 purchases
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly performs three trials according to description
  • Correctly records outcome for each trial (e.g., number of purchases, yes/no free coffee within 8 purchases)
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates the estimated probability as the number of successful trials divided by 3

FRQ 2

In a large high school, $40\%$ of students participate in sports, and $25\%$ of students are involved in music programs. Of those students who participate in sports, $10\%$ are also involved in music programs.
a. What is the probability that a randomly selected student participates in both sports and music programs? Show your work.
b. What is the probability that a randomly selected student participates in music programs, given that they do NOT participate in sports? Show your work.
c. Are the events "participates in sports" and "is involved in music programs" mutually exclusive? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates $P(\text{Sports AND Music}) = P(\text{Music }
  • \text{ Sports}) \times P(\text{Sports}) = 0.10 \times 0.40 = 0.04$.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates $P(\text{Not Sports}) = 1 - P(\text{Sports}) = 1 - 0.40 = 0.60$.
  • Correctly calculates $P(\text{Music AND Not Sports}) = P(\text{Music}) - P(\text{Music AND Sports}) = 0.25 - 0.04 = 0.21$.
  • Correctly calculates $P(\text{Music }
  • \text{ Not Sports}) = \frac{P(\text{Music AND Not Sports})}{P(\text{Not Sports})} = \frac{0.21}{0.60} = 0.35$.
  • Part c (1pt): States that the events are NOT mutually exclusive.
  • Provides correct justification, e.g., $P(\text{Sports AND Music}) = 0.04 \ne 0$ or by showing overlap.

FRQ 4

A factory manufactures light bulbs, and $5\%$ of the bulbs produced are defective. A quality control inspector randomly selects 15 bulbs for inspection.
a. Verify that the conditions for a binomial distribution are met for the number of defective bulbs in the sample.
b. Calculate the probability that exactly 2 of the 15 selected bulbs are defective. Show your work.
c. Calculate the expected number of defective bulbs in a sample of 15. Interpret this value in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States fixed number of trials ($n=15$), independent trials (random selection), two outcomes (defective/not defective), and constant probability of success ($p=0.05$).
  • Part b (1pt): Uses binomial probability formula $P(X=k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k}$ or binomial PDF function with $n=15, k=2, p=0.05$ to get $P(X=2) = \binom{15}{2} (0.05)^2 (0.95)^{13} \approx 0.1348$.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $E(X) = n \cdot p = 15 \cdot 0.05 = 0.75$.
  • Interprets $0.75$ as the average number of defective bulbs in many samples of 15 bulbs.
