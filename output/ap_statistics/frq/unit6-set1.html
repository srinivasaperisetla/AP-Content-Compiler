AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 1)

FRQ 1

A polling organization wants to estimate the proportion of registered voters who support a new environmental bill. They conduct a random sample of 800 registered voters and find that 456 support the bill.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the population proportion of registered voters who support the new environmental bill.
b. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of registered voters who support the new environmental bill. Show all work.
c. Interpret the 95% confidence interval in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample z-interval for proportion (1pt); Verifies random sample, 10% condition (800 < 10% of all voters), and Large Counts condition (np=456>=10, n(1-p)=344>=10) (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates point estimate and standard error (1pt); Calculates correct interval (e.g., (0.536, 0.604)) (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets the confidence interval correctly in context (e.g., "We are 95% confident that the true proportion of all registered voters who support the new environmental bill is between 0.536 and 0.604") (1pt)

FRQ 2

A company claims that 75% of its customers are satisfied with their service. A consumer advocacy group suspects this claim is too high. They conduct a random survey of 200 customers and find that 138 are satisfied.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test to investigate the advocacy group's suspicion. Define any parameters used.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value, what conclusion should the advocacy group make at the significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States H0: p=0.75, Ha: p<0.75 and defines p as the true proportion of satisfied customers (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random sample, 10% condition (200 < 10% of all customers), and Large Counts condition (np0=200*0.75=150>=10, n(1-p0)=200*0.25=50>=10) (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates test statistic (z = -1.96) (1pt); Calculates p-value (P(Z < -1.96) = 0.025) (1pt)
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to alpha and states conclusion to reject H0 (0.5pt); Justifies conclusion in context (0.5pt)

FRQ 3

A researcher wants to compare the effectiveness of two different medications for treating a certain allergy. In a random sample of 150 patients treated with Medication A, 105 reported improvement. In an independent random sample of 160 patients treated with Medication B, 96 reported improvement.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the difference in the proportion of patients who report improvement with Medication A versus Medication B.
b. Construct a 90% confidence interval for the difference in proportions ($p_A - p_B$). Show all work.
c. Based on your confidence interval, is there convincing evidence that Medication A is more effective than Medication B? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies two-sample z-interval for difference of proportions (1pt); Verifies random samples (independent), 10% condition for both samples, and Large Counts condition for both samples (n_A*p_A=105>=10, n_A*(1-p_A)=45>=10, n_B*p_B=96>=10, n_B*(1-p_B)=64>=10) (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates point estimates and standard error for the difference (1pt); Calculates correct interval (e.g., (0.007, 0.193)) (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): States whether there is convincing evidence (Yes) (0.5pt); Justifies based on the interval being entirely above 0 (0.5pt)

FRQ 4

A school administrator wants to determine if there is a difference in the proportion of male versus female students who participate in extracurricular sports. A random sample of 120 male students found 72 participate in sports. An independent random sample of 150 female students found 75 participate in sports.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a test to determine if there is a difference in the proportion of male versus female students who participate in extracurricular sports. Define any parameters used.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the pooled proportion, the test statistic, and the p-value for this test.
d. Interpret the p-value in the context of this study.
e. Based on your conclusion from part (c) and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what should the administrator conclude? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States H0: p_M = p_F, Ha: p_M != p_F and defines p_M and p_F as true proportions for males and females (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random samples (independent), 10% condition for both, and Large Counts for both using pooled proportion (n_M*p_pooled, n_M*(1-p_pooled), n_F*p_pooled, n_F*(1-p_pooled) all >= 10) (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates pooled proportion (p_c = 0.544) (0.5pt); Calculates test statistic (z = 1.62) (1pt); Calculates p-value (p = 0.1052) (0.5pt)
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets the p-value correctly in context (e.g., "Assuming there is no difference in the true proportions of male and female students participating in sports, there is a 0.1052 probability of observing a sample difference as extreme or more extreme than the one observed.") (1pt)
  • Part e (1pt): States conclusion to fail to reject H0 (p-value 0.1052 > 0.01) (0.5pt); Justifies based on p-value and states there is no significant evidence of a difference in proportions (0.5pt)

FRQ 5

A pharmaceutical company is developing a new drug to prevent migraines. They claim the drug is effective for more than 60% of patients. In a preliminary study, 80 out of 120 randomly selected patients experienced a reduction in migraine frequency after taking the new drug.
a. Verify the conditions for and construct a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of patients who experience a reduction in migraine frequency. Show all work.
b. Based on the confidence interval from part (a), does this study provide convincing evidence that the drug is effective for more than 60% of patients? Justify your answer.
c. Suppose the company decides to conduct a significance test with hypotheses $H_0: p = 0.60$ versus $H_a: p > 0.60$. Calculate the test statistic for this test using the sample data. (You do not need to calculate the p-value or verify conditions again).
d. Describe a Type I error in the context of this problem and explain the consequence of making such an error.
e. If the true proportion of patients for whom the drug is effective is actually $p = 0.70$, describe a Type II error in this context. Without performing calculations, explain how increasing the sample size would affect the probability of making a Type II error, assuming all else remains constant.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Verifies random sample, 10% condition, and Large Counts condition (np=80>=10, n(1-p)=40>=10) (1pt); Constructs correct interval (e.g., (0.584, 0.750)) (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): States no convincing evidence (0.5pt); Justifies that 0.60 is contained within the interval or the lower bound is not greater than 0.60 (0.5pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates test statistic (z = 1.49) (1pt)
  • Part d (1pt): Describes Type I error correctly (0.5pt); Explains consequence (0.5pt)
  • Part e (1pt): Describes Type II error correctly (0.5pt); Explains increasing sample size decreases the probability of a Type II error (0.5pt)
