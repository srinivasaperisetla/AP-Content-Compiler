FRQ 1

A survey was conducted among 200 high school students about their preferred mode of transportation to school (Bus, Car, Walk) and their grade level (9th, 10th, 11th, 12th). The results are summarized in the table below.
Mode \ Grade9th10th11th12thTotal
Bus2520151070
Car15253035105
Walk1055525
Total50505050200
a. What is the probability that a randomly selected student is in 10th grade and prefers to travel by bus?
b. Given that a randomly selected student prefers to travel by car, what is the probability that the student is in 12th grade?
c. Are the events "prefers to travel by bus" and "is in 9th grade" independent? Justify your answer using probabilities.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correct calculation of joint probability $P(\text{10th and Bus}) = 20/200 = 0.10$.
  • Part b (1pt): Correct calculation of conditional probability $P(\text{12th
  • Car}) = 35/105 \approx 0.333$.
  • Part c (2pts): Correct calculation of $P(\text{Bus}) = 70/200 = 0.35$ and $P(\text{9th}) = 50/200 = 0.25$. Correct calculation of $P(\text{Bus and 9th}) = 25/200 = 0.125$. Justification that $P(\text{Bus and 9th}) \ne P(\text{Bus}) \times P(\text{9th})$ ($0.125 \ne 0.35 \times 0.25 = 0.0875$), so they are not independent. OR Comparison of $P(\text{Bus
  • 9th}) = 25/50 = 0.5$ with $P(\text{Bus}) = 0.35$, showing they are not equal.

FRQ 2

A small company sells custom-designed t-shirts. The number of t-shirts ordered by a customer, $X$, and the profit, $Y$, for each order are random variables. The probability distribution for the number of t-shirts ordered is given in the table below. The profit for an order is given by the formula $Y = 5X - 10$ dollars, reflecting a fixed setup cost and a per-shirt profit.
Number of T-shirts ($x$)Probability $P(X=x)$
10.20
20.35
30.30
40.10
50.05
a. Calculate the expected number of t-shirts ordered per customer, $E[X]$.
b. Calculate the expected profit per order, $E[Y]$, and the standard deviation of the profit per order, $\sigma_Y$.
c. A customer places two independent orders. Let $Y_1$ be the profit from the first order and $Y_2$ be the profit from the second order. Calculate the expected total profit for the two orders, $E[Y_1 + Y_2]$, and the standard deviation of the total profit, $\sigma_{Y_1 + Y_2}$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correct calculation of $E[X] = \sum x \cdot P(X=x) = 1(0.20) + 2(0.35) + 3(0.30) + 4(0.10) + 5(0.05) = 2.45$ t-shirts.
  • Part b (2pts): Correct calculation of $E[Y] = E[5X - 10] = 5E[X] - 10 = 5(2.45) - 10 = 12.25 - 10 = 2.25$ dollars. Correct calculation of $\sigma_Y = \sqrt{\text{Var}(5X-10)} = \sqrt{5^2 \text{Var}(X)} = 5\sigma_X$. $\text{Var}(X) = \sum (x - E[X])^2 P(X=x) = (1-2.45)^2(0.2) + \dots = 1.0475$, so $\sigma_X = \sqrt{1.0475} \approx 1.0235$. Thus $\sigma_Y = 5(1.0235) \approx 5.1175$ dollars.
  • Part c (2pts): Correct calculation of $E[Y_1 + Y_2] = E[Y_1] + E[Y_2] = 2.25 + 2.25 = 4.50$ dollars. Correct calculation of $\sigma_{Y_1 + Y_2} = \sqrt{\text{Var}(Y_1) + \text{Var}(Y_2)} = \sqrt{\sigma_Y^2 + \sigma_Y^2} = \sqrt{2 \sigma_Y^2} = \sigma_Y \sqrt{2} \approx 5.1175 \sqrt{2} \approx 7.237$ dollars.

FRQ 3

A survey of high school students found that 60% play a sport, 25% are in a club, and 15% do both.
a. What is the probability that a randomly chosen student plays a sport or is in a club?
b. Are playing a sport and being in a club mutually exclusive events? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correct calculation $P(S \cup C) = P(S) + P(C) - P(S \cap C) = 0.60 + 0.25 - 0.15 = 0.70$.
  • Part b (2pts): No, because $P(S \cap C) = 0.15 \ne 0$. Mutually exclusive events have an intersection probability of 0.

FRQ 4

A company categorizes its employees by gender and job type (admin or technical). The data for 200 employees are summarized in the following table:
AdminTechnicalTotal
Male3070100
Female5050100
Total80120200
a. What is the probability that a randomly selected employee is female AND works in an administrative role?
b. Given that an employee is technical, what is the probability that the employee is male?
c. Are the events "being male" and "working in a technical role" independent? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): $P(\text{Female and Admin}) = 50/200 = 0.25$.
  • Part b (2pts): $P(\text{Male
  • Technical}) = P(\text{Male and Technical}) / P(\text{Technical}) = (70/200) / (120/200) = 70/120 = 7/12 \approx 0.583$.
  • Part c (2pts): $P(\text{Male}) = 100/200 = 0.5$. $P(\text{Male
  • Technical}) = 70/120 \approx 0.583$. Since $P(\text{Male}) \ne P(\text{Male
  • Technical})$, the events are not independent.

FRQ 5

A carnival game involves rolling a standard six-sided die. If you roll a 6, you win \$5. If you roll a 5, you win \$1. Otherwise, you win nothing. Let $X$ be the random variable representing the amount of money won in one roll.
a. Construct a probability distribution table for $X$.
b. Calculate the expected value of $X$, $E(X)$.
c. Interpret the expected value of $X$ in the context of the carnival game.
