AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 8)

FRQ 1

A polling organization wants to estimate the proportion of registered voters who support a particular bond measure. They randomly sample 600 registered voters and find that 318 of them support the bond measure.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of registered voters who support the bond measure.
c. Interpret the 95% confidence interval in the context of the problem.
d. The local newspaper reported that 50% of registered voters support the bond measure. Based on your confidence interval, does this report seem plausible? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample z-interval for proportion and checks Random, 10%, and Large Counts conditions.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the 95% confidence interval for the proportion.
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the confidence interval in context.
  • Part d (1pt): States whether the report is plausible and justifies the answer based on the confidence interval.

FRQ 2

A company claims that 75% of its new "eco-friendly" light bulbs last longer than 1000 hours. A consumer advocacy group suspects this claim is too high. They test a random sample of 200 light bulbs and find that 140 of them lasted longer than 1000 hours.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this situation. Identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your results, what conclusion should the consumer advocacy group make at the significance level α = 0.05? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses and identifies one-sample z-test for proportion.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies Random, 10%, and Large Counts conditions for the test.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic and p-value.
  • Part d (1pt): States a correct conclusion based on the p-value and α, and justifies it in context.

FRQ 4

A company manufactures two types of smartphone batteries, Standard and Extended-Life. They want to determine if there is a significant difference in the proportion of defective batteries between the two types. A random sample of 500 Standard batteries found 20 defectives, while a random sample of 600 Extended-Life batteries found 18 defectives.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this situation. Identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your results, what conclusion should the company make at the significance level α = 0.01? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses and identifies two-sample z-test for difference in proportions.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies Random, 10%, and Large Counts conditions for both groups (using pooled proportion for expected counts).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic and p-value.
  • Part d (1pt): States a correct conclusion based on the p-value and α, and justifies it in context.

FRQ 5

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug for its effectiveness in reducing symptoms of a chronic illness. The current standard treatment has a known success rate of 60%. The company wants to determine if the new drug has a higher success rate. They plan to conduct a significance test with the null hypothesis H₀: p = 0.60 and the alternative hypothesis Hₐ: p > 0.60.
a. Describe a Type I error and a Type II error in the context of this problem.
b. Explain a potential consequence for the pharmaceutical company if they make a Type I error. Explain a potential consequence if they make a Type II error.
c. If the company decides to reduce the significance level (α) from 0.05 to 0.01, how would this affect the probability of a Type I error and the probability of a Type II error?
d. Describe one way the company could increase the power of their test, assuming the true success rate for the new drug is actually higher than 0.60.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes Type I and Type II errors in context.
  • Part b (1pt): Explains a plausible consequence for both Type I and Type II errors.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains the effect of reducing α on the probabilities of Type I and Type II errors.
  • Part d (1pt): Describes a correct way to increase power (e.g., increase sample size, increase α, increase difference between hypothesized and true proportion).
