AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 2: Exploring Two-Variable Data (Set 2)

FRQ 1

A researcher is investigating the relationship between the number of hours a student spends studying for a statistics exam and the score they achieve on the exam. Data was collected from 20 students, and a scatterplot of the hours studied versus exam score is shown below.
a. Describe the overall pattern of the relationship between hours studied and exam score shown in the scatterplot, including its direction, form, and strength.
b. Explain what the correlation coefficient $r = 0.85$ indicates about the relationship between hours studied and exam score in this context.
c. Does a high positive correlation (like $r=0.85$) necessarily mean that increased study time causes higher exam scores? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (strong). Mentions context for each aspect.
  • Part b (1pt): States that there is a strong, positive linear association between hours studied and exam scores. Mentions context.
  • Part c (1pt): States that correlation does not imply causation. Justifies by mentioning lurking variables or experimental design.

FRQ 2

A real estate agent wants to predict the selling price of a house based on its size (in square feet). A random sample of houses sold in a particular neighborhood yielded the least-squares regression line $\hat{y} = 50,000 + 75x$, where $x$ is the size of the house in square feet and $\hat{y}$ is the predicted selling price in dollars. The coefficient of determination is $r^2 = 0.81$.
a. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
b. Interpret the $y$-intercept of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
c. Predict the selling price of a house that is 2,200 square feet.
d. The actual selling price of the 2,200 square foot house was $\$210,000$. Calculate the residual for this house.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly interprets the slope (for every additional square foot, the predicted selling price increases by $\$75$) in context.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly interprets the $y$-intercept (a house with 0 square feet is predicted to sell for $\$50,000$) and comments on its practical meaning/lack thereof.
  • Part c (1pt): Shows calculation $\hat{y} = 50,000 + 75(2200) = 215,000$ and states the predicted price is $\$215,000$.
  • Part d (1pt): Shows calculation Residual = Actual - Predicted = $\$210,000 - \$215,000 = -\$5,000$ and states the residual.

FRQ 4

A company investigates the relationship between its advertising expenditure (in thousands of dollars) and its monthly sales (in thousands of dollars). Data for 11 months are collected and plotted in a scatterplot. One month had an exceptionally high advertising expenditure and high sales compared to the other months.
a. On a scatterplot, describe the characteristics of a point that would be considered an influential point.
b. Suppose the point representing the month with exceptionally high advertising expenditure and high sales is $(50, 400)$ while most other points are clustered between advertising expenditure of $5$ to $15$ and sales of $100$ to $150$. If this influential point were removed, describe the expected changes in the least-squares regression line's slope and the correlation coefficient $r$. Justify your answer.
c. Distinguish between an outlier in the $y$-direction (a residual outlier) and an influential point, explaining how each impacts the least-squares regression line differently.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Describes an influential point as one that, if removed, would significantly change the slope and/or $y$-intercept of the least-squares regression line. Often has a large $x$-value (high leverage).
  • Part b (2pts): States that removing the point $(50, 400)$ would likely decrease the slope and increase the correlation coefficient. Justifies that the point pulls the line towards itself, making the slope steeper, and its removal would make the remaining points show a stronger linear relationship since it's far out in $x$ and aligned with the trend.
  • Part c (2pts): Explains that an outlier in the $y$-direction (large residual) has a large vertical distance from the line but may not significantly change the slope if its $x$-value is near the mean. An influential point has an extreme $x$-value (high leverage) and may or may not have a large residual, but its removal significantly changes the line's slope and/or intercept.

FRQ 5

A researcher is studying the decay of a radioactive substance. The initial amount of the substance is 100 grams. The amount remaining (in grams) is measured at various times (in hours). A scatterplot of the amount remaining versus time shows a curved, decreasing pattern, indicating a nonlinear relationship. To linearize the relationship, the natural logarithm of the amount remaining was taken. The least-squares regression line for $\ln(\text{amount})$ versus time is $\widehat{\ln(\text{amount})} = 4.60 - 0.15(\text{time})$.
a. Explain why a linear model using the original variables (amount remaining versus time) would not be appropriate for these data based on the description.
b. If a scatterplot of $\ln(\text{amount})$ versus time showed a strong, linear, decreasing pattern with no obvious curvature in its residual plot, explain how this indicates that the transformation was successful.
c. Use the given regression equation for the transformed data to predict the amount of the radioactive substance remaining after 10 hours. Show your work.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States that a linear model is not appropriate because the original scatterplot shows a curved, decreasing pattern, violating the linearity assumption for linear regression.
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that a linear relationship in the transformed data, combined with random scatter in the residual plot, indicates that the original relationship can be modeled by an exponential function, which is linearized by the log transformation.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $\widehat{\ln(\text{amount})} = 4.60 - 0.15(10) = 4.60 - 1.50 = 3.10$. Then exponentiates to find the predicted amount: $\text{amount} = e^{3.10} \approx 22.198$ grams. Shows both steps.
