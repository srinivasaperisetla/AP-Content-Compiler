AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 15)

FRQ 1

A class of 30 students was asked to choose their favorite type of movie from four categories: Action, Comedy, Drama, or Sci-Fi. The results are shown in the frequency table below.
Movie TypeFrequency
Action8
Comedy12
Drama4
Sci-Fi6
a. Construct a relative frequency table for these data.
b. Construct a bar chart to display the distribution of favorite movie types. Label your axes clearly.
c. Based on your bar chart, describe the distribution of favorite movie types among the students.
d. Suppose a different class of 25 students had 10 students choose Comedy. Compare the proportion of students who prefer Comedy in this new class to the proportion in the original class.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates and lists relative frequencies for all categories.
  • Part b (1pt): Constructs a correct bar chart with labeled axes and categories.
  • Part c (1pt): Describes the mode (most frequent category) and least frequent category in context.
  • Part d (1pt): Calculates both proportions and makes a correct comparison in context.

FRQ 2

A researcher recorded the reaction times (in milliseconds) of 25 participants to a visual stimulus. The reaction times are: 185, 192, 198, 201, 203, 205, 205, 207, 210, 212, 215, 218, 220, 221, 223, 225, 228, 230, 233, 235, 240, 242, 245, 250, 265.
a. Construct a stemplot to display the distribution of reaction times. Be sure to include a key.
b. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of reaction times based on your stemplot.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly constructs a stemplot for the given data, including a key and appropriate stem/leaf splits (e.g., stems representing hundreds/tens, leaves representing units).
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the shape (e.g., "skewed right" or "approximately symmetric"), approximate center (e.g., median around 220 ms), and approximate spread (e.g., range from 185 to 265 ms) of the distribution in context.

FRQ 3

A store manager wants to analyze the daily sales (in hundreds of dollars) for their main product over a 15-day period. The sales data are: 12.5, 15.0, 11.8, 13.2, 16.1, 14.5, 10.9, 17.3, 13.8, 15.2, 11.5, 14.9, 16.0, 12.0, 18.5.
a. Calculate the mean and median daily sales for this period.
b. Calculate the interquartile range (IQR) for the daily sales.
c. Based on your calculations, would the mean or the median be a more appropriate measure of center for these data? Justify your choice.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the mean ($\bar{x}$) and median daily sales in context, showing work.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the IQR ($Q_3 - Q_1$) for the daily sales in context.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains whether the mean or median is more appropriate by considering the distribution's shape (or potential outliers) and justifies the choice in context.

FRQ 4

The lifespan of a certain brand of light bulb is approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 800$ hours and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 50$ hours.
a. Calculate the proportion of light bulbs that will last between 750 hours and 850 hours.
b. The manufacturer wants to offer a warranty such that only 1% of light bulbs would need to be replaced. Determine the minimum lifespan (in hours) a light bulb must have to qualify for this warranty. Round your answer to the nearest hour.
c. Suppose another brand of light bulbs has a mean lifespan of 820 hours and a standard deviation of 60 hours. A light bulb from the first brand lasts 870 hours, and a light bulb from the second brand lasts 900 hours. Compare the relative positions of these two light bulbs within their respective distributions.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates the z-scores for 750 hours ($z = -1$) and 850 hours ($z = 1$) and determines the proportion of bulbs within this range (approximately 0.6827).
  • Part b (2pts): Finds the z-score corresponding to the 1st percentile (approx. $z = -2.33$) and uses it to calculate the minimum lifespan in hours ($X = \mu + z\sigma = 800 + (-2.33)(50) \approx 683.5$ hours, rounded to 684 hours). Shows all steps.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the z-score for each light bulb ($z_{Brand1} = \frac{870-800}{50} = 1.4$, $z_{Brand2} = \frac{900-820}{60} \approx 1.33$). Compares and interprets the z-scores to explain which light bulb has a relatively longer lifespan compared to its brand's average.

FRQ 5

A survey asked 20 high school students about their favorite sport among Basketball, Soccer, Tennis, and Volleyball. The responses were: Basketball, Soccer, Soccer, Tennis, Basketball, Volleyball, Soccer, Basketball, Tennis, Soccer, Basketball, Volleyball, Soccer, Soccer, Basketball, Tennis, Basketball, Soccer, Volleyball, Basketball.
a. Construct a frequency bar chart for the favorite sport data.
b. Describe the distribution of favorite sports based on your graph.
c. If another class of 20 students had significantly more students choosing Tennis, how would their distribution of favorite sports compare to this one?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly labels axes, uses appropriate scale, and draws bars to correct heights for each category.
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies the mode (Basketball) and notes the least frequent categories (Tennis, Volleyball).
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that the peak for Tennis would be higher and potentially the mode for the other class, showing an understanding of comparison.
