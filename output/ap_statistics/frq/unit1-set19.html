AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 19)

FRQ 1

A high school principal wants to understand students' favorite ice cream flavors to decide which flavors to stock in the cafeteria. A random sample of 100 students was asked for their favorite flavor. The results were: Vanilla (25), Chocolate (35), Strawberry (15), Mint Chip (10), Other (15).
a. Construct a relative frequency table for the ice cream flavors.
b. Create a bar chart to display the distribution of favorite ice cream flavors. Be sure to label your axes.
c. Based on your bar chart, describe the most popular flavor among the sampled students.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates relative frequencies for all categories (1pt); Constructs a table with correct labels/columns (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Constructs a bar chart with correct bars/heights (1pt); Labels both axes appropriately (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Identifies Chocolate as the most popular flavor and refers to its height/frequency in the bar chart.

FRQ 4

The lifespan of a particular brand of refrigerator is known to be approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 12$ years and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 1.5$ years.
a. Calculate the proportion of refrigerators that last longer than 15 years.
b. The manufacturer offers a 10-year warranty. What proportion of refrigerators are expected to fail before the warranty expires?
c. A rival company claims that their refrigerators last longer, with 90% lasting at least 11 years. Assuming their lifespans are also normally distributed with the same standard deviation $\sigma = 1.5$ years, calculate the mean lifespan of the rival company's refrigerators that would support this claim.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the z-score for 15 years (1pt); Uses the z-score to find the correct proportion (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the proportion of refrigerators lasting less than 10 years (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Determines the z-score corresponding to the 10th percentile (1pt); Uses the z-score, standard deviation, and given percentile value to solve for the mean (1pt).
