FRQ 3

A company records the commute times (in minutes) for a random sample of 20 employees. The data are as follows: 15, 20, 25, 25, 30, 30, 30, 35, 40, 40, 45, 50, 50, 55, 60, 60, 65, 70, 75, 120.
a. Calculate the mean and median commute times for these employees. Show your work.
b. Identify any outliers in the data using the $1.5 \times IQR$ rule. Show your work.
c. Explain which measure of center (mean or median) would be more appropriate to describe the typical commute time for these employees and justify your choice.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the mean ($\bar{x} = 47.75$ minutes) and median (47.5 minutes), showing formulas or sufficient work.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates $Q_1$, $Q_3$, and $IQR$. Determines lower and upper fences. Correctly identifies any outliers. ($Q_1 = 30$, $Q_3 = 60$, $IQR = 30$. Lower fence: $30 - 1.5(30) = -15$. Upper fence: $60 + 1.5(30) = 105$. Outlier: 120 minutes).
  • Part c (2pts): Identifies the median as more appropriate. Justifies based on the presence of an outlier (120 minutes) affecting the mean, making the median more resistant to extreme values.

FRQ 4

The lifespan of a certain brand of light bulb is approximately normally distributed with a mean of 1500 hours and a standard deviation of 150 hours.
a. What proportion of light bulbs from this brand are expected to last between 1400 hours and 1650 hours? Show your work.
b. A consumer organization wants to guarantee that at least 95% of their light bulbs will last longer than a specified number of hours. Determine the minimum lifespan (in hours) they should guarantee. Show your work.
c. Suppose another brand of light bulb has a mean lifespan of 1600 hours and a standard deviation of 100 hours. If a light bulb from the first brand lasts 1700 hours and a light bulb from the second brand lasts 1750 hours, which light bulb has a relatively longer lifespan compared to its own brand? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates the $z$-scores for 1400 and 1650 hours. Uses the Normal distribution to find the correct proportion, showing calculation ($P(1400 < X < 1650) \approx 0.588$). ($z_{1400} = \frac{1400-1500}{150} \approx -0.67$. $z_{1650} = \frac{1650-1500}{150} = 1.00$. $P(-0.67 < Z < 1.00) \approx 0.588$).
  • Part b (2pts): Finds the $z$-score corresponding to the 5th percentile (or 95th percentile from the left). Uses the $z$-score to calculate the minimum lifespan, showing calculation. ($z \approx -1.645$. Minimum lifespan $\approx 1500 + (-1.645)(150) \approx 1253.25$ hours).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the $z$-score for each light bulb. Compares the $z$-scores and states which light bulb has a relatively longer lifespan. ($z_{\text{Brand 1}} = \frac{1700-1500}{150} \approx 1.33$. $z_{\text{Brand 2}} = \frac{1750-1600}{100} = 1.50$. Brand 2 light bulb has a relatively longer lifespan because its $z$-score (1.50) is higher than Brand 1's $z$-score (1.33)).

FRQ 5

A survey asked 50 students about their favorite color among blue, green, red, and yellow. The results were: Blue (18 students), Green (12 students), Red (10 students), Yellow (10 students).
a. Construct a relative frequency bar chart for the favorite colors.
b. Describe one characteristic of the distribution of favorite colors.
c. Another school surveyed 50 students and found 25 preferred blue, 15 preferred green, 5 preferred red, and 5 preferred yellow. Compare the distribution of favorite colors between the two schools.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly labeled axes (colors, relative frequency) and correctly scaled bars for each color, showing relative frequencies (e.g., Blue: 0.36, Green: 0.24, Red: 0.20, Yellow: 0.20).
  • Part b (1pt): Describes mode (blue is most popular) or notes that red and yellow are equally preferred.
  • Part c (2pts): Compares center (e.g., blue is the most popular in both schools, but more strongly in the second school) and spread/shape (e.g., the second school's distribution is more skewed towards blue, while the first school has a more even distribution among the other colors).
