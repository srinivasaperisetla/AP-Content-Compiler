AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 1)

FRQ 1

A survey was conducted at a local high school to determine students' favorite type of social media platform. The results from a random sample of 200 students are summarized below.
PlatformNumber of Students
A70
B50
C40
D25
Other15
a. Construct a relative frequency table for these data.
b. Construct a bar chart to represent the distribution of favorite social media platforms.
c. Describe the distribution of favorite social media platforms for these students.
d. Suppose the same survey was conducted at a different high school, and it was found that 40% of students preferred Platform B. Compare this to the preference for Platform B at the first high school.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly constructs a relative frequency table including categories, frequencies, and relative frequencies.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly constructs a bar chart with labeled axes and appropriate scales.
  • Part c (1pt): Describes the mode and mentions other categories, addressing UNC-1.D.
  • Part d (1pt): Calculates the relative frequency for Platform B from the given data and compares it to 40%, identifying which school has a higher preference.

FRQ 2

A teacher recorded the number of minutes each of 25 students spent completing a statistics assignment. The times, in minutes, are as follows: 25, 30, 32, 28, 35, 30, 29, 31, 27, 26, 33, 38, 30, 28, 31, 34, 30, 29, 36, 27, 32, 29, 30, 28, 31.
a. Construct a stemplot of the assignment completion times.
b. Describe the distribution of assignment completion times, including shape, center, and spread.
c. Identify any potential outliers in the distribution.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly constructs a stemplot with appropriate stems, leaves, and a key, addressing UNC-1.G.
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the shape (e.g., roughly symmetric/unimodal), center (approx. median/mean), and spread (range/IQR) in context, addressing UNC-1.H.
  • Part c (1pt): Identifies potential outliers based on visual inspection or calculation (e.g., $1.5 \times IQR$ rule) and explains reasoning.

FRQ 3

The annual salaries (in thousands of dollars) for a small startup company's employees are given as: 50, 52, 55, 58, 60, 62, 65, 70, 75, 150.
a. Calculate the mean and median annual salary for these employees.
b. Calculate the standard deviation and interquartile range (IQR) for these annual salaries.
c. Which measure of center (mean or median) would be more appropriate to describe these salaries? Justify your choice.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates both the mean and median.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates both the standard deviation and IQR.
  • Part c (2pts): Identifies the median as more appropriate and justifies the choice by explaining the effect of the outlier(s) on the mean, addressing UNC-1.K.

FRQ 4

Two different brands of light bulbs, Brand X and Brand Y, are tested for their lifespan (in hours). A random sample of 20 bulbs from each brand was selected, and the results are displayed in the parallel boxplots below.
a. Compare the medians of the lifespans for Brand X and Brand Y light bulbs.
b. Compare the variability in lifespans for Brand X and Brand Y light bulbs.
c. Based on these boxplots, which brand would you recommend for a customer seeking a light bulb with a consistently long lifespan? Justify your recommendation.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Accurately compares the medians, stating which is higher and providing numerical values or estimates, addressing UNC-1.N.
  • Part b (1pt): Compares the variability using the IQR or range for both brands and determines which is more variable, addressing UNC-1.N.
  • Part c (2pts): Recommends a brand and justifies the choice based on both measures of center and spread discussed in parts (a) and (b), in context, addressing UNC-1.O.

FRQ 5

The weight of a certain type of apple harvested from an orchard is approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 180$ grams and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 15$ grams.
a. Calculate the proportion of apples that weigh more than 200 grams.
b. An apple is considered "premium" if its weight is in the top 10% of all apples. Determine the minimum weight, in grams, for a premium apple.
c. Suppose a different type of fruit, a pear, has weights that are also approximately normally distributed with a mean of 150 grams and a standard deviation of 10 grams. If an apple weighs 195 grams and a pear weighs 165 grams, which fruit is relatively heavier compared to others of its kind? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates the z-score for 200 grams and uses it to find the proportion (or uses calculator function) and states the proportion, addressing VAR-2.B.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly uses the inverse normal function to find the z-score for the 90th percentile and calculates the corresponding weight, addressing VAR-2.B.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the z-score for both the apple and the pear, compares them, and justifies which fruit is relatively heavier by explaining that a larger z-score indicates a relatively heavier fruit, addressing VAR-2.C.
