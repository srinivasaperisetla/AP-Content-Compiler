AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 16)

FRQ 1

A high school surveyed 200 students about their preferred genre of music for an upcoming school dance. The results are summarized in the table below.
Music GenreNumber of Students
Pop80
Hip-Hop50
Rock35
Electronic25
Other10
a. Construct a relative frequency table for these data.
b. Construct a bar chart to display the distribution of music preferences.
c. Describe one advantage of using a relative frequency table over a frequency table in this context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates relative frequencies for all categories (1pt) and presents them in a well-labeled table (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Constructs a bar chart with correctly labeled axes and bars proportional to relative frequencies (1pt), and includes a title (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that relative frequencies allow for easier comparison of proportions, especially if comparing to another group with a different total number of students, or understanding the part-to-whole relationship directly.

FRQ 2

A company recorded the daily commute times, in minutes, for a random sample of 30 employees. The commute times are: 15, 18, 20, 20, 21, 22, 22, 25, 25, 26, 28, 28, 28, 30, 30, 32, 32, 35, 35, 38, 38, 39, 39, 40, 40, 42, 45, 48, 50, 65.
a. Construct a histogram to display the distribution of commute times for these employees. Make sure to label your axes.
b. Based on your histogram, describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of commute times. Are there any apparent outliers?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Constructs a histogram with correctly labeled axes (0.5pt), appropriate bin widths and scales (1pt), and correct bar heights (0.5pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly describes the shape (e.g., skewed right, unimodal) (0.5pt), identifies an approximate center (0.5pt), describes spread (e.g., range or variability) (0.5pt), and identifies potential outliers if present (0.5pt).

FRQ 3

A teacher administered a 50-point quiz to two different sections of an AP Statistics class. The scores for Section A are: 38, 42, 35, 45, 40, 39, 41, 37, 43, 44. The scores for Section B are: 25, 30, 40, 45, 50, 20, 35, 42, 48, 32.
a. Calculate the median and the interquartile range (IQR) for Section A scores.
b. Calculate the mean and standard deviation for Section B scores.
c. Based on the calculated statistics, which measure of center and measure of variability would be more appropriate to use for Section B, and why?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the median for Section A (1pt) and the IQR for Section A (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the mean for Section B (1pt) and the standard deviation for Section B (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Identifies the median as the more appropriate measure of center for Section B (0.5pt), identifies the IQR as the more appropriate measure of variability for Section B (0.5pt), and provides a correct justification based on the presence of outliers or skewness (1pt).

FRQ 4

The heights of adult males in a certain city are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 69.5$ inches and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 2.5$ inches.
a. What proportion of adult males in this city are taller than 72 inches? Show your work.
b. A building has a doorway that is 66 inches high. What proportion of adult males in this city would have to duck to enter this doorway?
c. An adult male is 71 inches tall. Calculate his z-score and interpret what it means in the context of male heights in this city.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates the correct z-score for 72 inches (1pt) and uses it to find the correct proportion (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct z-score for 66 inches (1pt) and uses it to find the correct proportion (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct z-score for 71 inches (1pt) and provides a correct interpretation of the z-score in context (1pt).

FRQ 5

The following are the scores on a recent chemistry exam for 20 students: 65, 78, 80, 72, 91, 85, 68, 75, 83, 90, 79, 81, 70, 77, 86, 95, 63, 74, 82, 88.
a. Construct a stem-and-leaf plot for the exam scores.
b. Calculate the median, interquartile range (IQR), and standard deviation for these exam scores.
c. If there were an extreme outlier score of 20 instead of 63, explain whether the mean or the median would be a more appropriate measure of the center for the distribution of exam scores.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly constructs a stem-and-leaf plot with a key.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the median ($79.5$), IQR ($12.5$), and standard deviation (approx. $8.76$).
  • Part c (2pts): Explains that the median would be more appropriate because it is resistant to outliers, while the mean is pulled towards the outlier.
