AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 5)

FRQ 1

A survey asked 200 randomly selected high school students about their preferred method of transportation to school. The results are summarized in the following table.
MethodNumber of Students
Car80
Bus60
Walk35
Bicycle15
Other10
a. Construct a bar chart to display the distribution of preferred transportation methods.
b. Describe the distribution of preferred transportation methods among these students.
c. If a similar survey was conducted 10 years ago and "Car" was preferred by $40\%$ of students, "Bus" by $30\%$, "Walk" by $20\%$, "Bicycle" by $5\%$, and "Other" by $5\%$, compare the current distribution to the historical distribution.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly labeled axes with categories and frequencies/relative frequencies, and correctly drawn bars for each category.
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies the most and least preferred methods (e.g., Car is most preferred, Other is least preferred).
  • Part c (2pts): Compares at least two categories quantitatively (e.g., car preference increased from $40\%$ to $40\%$, walk preference decreased from $20\%$ to $17.5\%$) or qualitatively, providing evidence from the data.

FRQ 2

A researcher recorded the number of text messages sent by 25 teenagers in a 24-hour period. The data are as follows: $54, 120, 78, 92, 65, 110, 88, 70, 95, 60, 105, 75, 82, 115, 68, 90, 85, 98, 72, 100, 125, 50, 130, 45, 150$.
a. Calculate the mean, median, and interquartile range (IQR) for the number of text messages sent.
b. Based on the data, would the mean or the median be a more appropriate measure of the typical number of text messages sent? Justify your answer.
c. Identify any outliers in the data set using the $1.5 \times IQR$ rule. Show your work.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (3pts): Correct calculation of the mean, median, and IQR.
  • Part b (1pt): States either mean or median and provides a valid justification based on the presence/absence of skewness or outliers in the data.
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the lower and upper bounds for outliers using the $1.5 \times IQR$ rule and identifies any values outside these bounds.

FRQ 3

The weights (in pounds) of 20 newborn babies at a hospital were recorded: $6.8, 7.2, 8.1, 6.5, 7.9, 8.5, 7.0, 7.5, 6.9, 8.0, 7.3, 7.7, 9.1, 6.2, 7.1, 8.3, 7.6, 6.7, 7.4, 8.8$.
a. Construct a stem-and-leaf plot for the weights of the newborn babies. Be sure to include a key.
b. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of newborn baby weights.
c. One baby weighed $9.1$ pounds. Explain what this value suggests about the baby's weight relative to the other newborn babies in this sample.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correct stem-and-leaf plot with stems, ordered leaves, and a clear key.
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the shape (e.g., roughly symmetric, skewed), an approximate center (e.g., around $7.5$ pounds), and the spread (e.g., range from $6.2$ to $9.1$ pounds) of the distribution.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that $9.1$ pounds is among the highest or an unusually high weight in this sample, or provides context for its relative position.

FRQ 4

Researchers wanted to compare the effectiveness of two different diets (Diet A and Diet B) on weight loss over a 3-month period. They randomly assigned 50 participants to Diet A and 50 to Diet B. The boxplots below summarize the weight loss (in pounds) for participants on each diet.
a. Compare the distributions of weight loss for Diet A and Diet B.
b. Based on these boxplots, which diet appears to be more effective for weight loss? Justify your answer using specific evidence from the plots.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (3pts): Compares the distributions by addressing shape (e.g., skewness), center (e.g., medians), and spread (e.g., IQR, range), and any outliers for both diets in context.
  • Part b (1pt): States which diet appears more effective and provides specific justification from the boxplots (e.g., Diet A has a higher median weight loss or Diet B shows greater variability).

FRQ 5

The weights of adult male grizzly bears in a certain region are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 500$ pounds and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 50$ pounds.
a. Calculate the proportion of adult male grizzly bears in this region that weigh between $450$ and $575$ pounds.
b. A wildlife biologist wants to identify the heaviest $10\%$ of bears for a special study. Determine the minimum weight a bear must have to be included in this study.
c. One grizzly bear is observed to weigh $620$ pounds. Compare this bear's weight to the distribution of weights for all adult male grizzly bears in this region.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the $z$-scores for $450$ and $575$ pounds, and then uses these $z$-scores to find the correct proportion.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly finds the $z$-score corresponding to the 90th percentile and uses it to calculate the minimum weight.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the $z$-score for a $620$-pound bear and interprets its meaning in context (e.g., how many standard deviations above the mean, indicating it's an unusually heavy bear).
