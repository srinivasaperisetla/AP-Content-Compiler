AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 5: Sampling Distributions (Set 17)

FRQ 1

A large company is concerned about employee satisfaction. Historically, $70\%$ of its employees report being "very satisfied" with their job. A new HR initiative is implemented, and the company plans to survey a random sample of $150$ employees to see if satisfaction has changed.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of the sample proportion of employees who report being "very satisfied" if the true proportion remains $0.70$. Include its shape, mean, and standard deviation.
b. Calculate the probability that in a random sample of $150$ employees, $65\%$ or fewer report being "very satisfied" if the true proportion is still $0.70$. Show your work.
c. Interpret the probability calculated in part (b) in the context of the new HR initiative.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (3pts): Shape is approximately normal because conditions $n\hat{p} \ge 10$ and $n(1-\hat{p}) \ge 10$ are met ($150 \times 0.70 = 105 \ge 10$ and $150 \times 0.30 = 45 \ge 10$). Mean is $0.70$. Standard deviation is $\sqrt{\frac{0.70(1-0.70)}{150}} \approx 0.0374$.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $z$-score $\frac{0.65 - 0.70}{0.0374} \approx -1.33$. Uses $P(Z \le -1.33)$ to find probability $\approx 0.0918$.
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets $0.0918$ as the chance of observing $65\%$ or fewer satisfied employees if the HR initiative had no effect and the true proportion is still $70\%$.

FRQ 2

The weight of adult male grizzly bears in a certain region is approximately normally distributed with a mean of $790$ pounds and a standard deviation of $105$ pounds. Wildlife biologists plan to capture and weigh a random sample of $36$ adult male grizzly bears.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of the sample mean weight for samples of $36$ adult male grizzly bears. Include its shape, mean, and standard deviation.
b. Calculate the probability that the sample mean weight of $36$ bears will be less than $750$ pounds. Show your work.
c. A wildlife sanctuary has claimed that the average weight of male grizzly bears in their care is significantly lower than the regional average. Based on your answer in part (b), would a sample mean weight of $750$ pounds for $36$ bears provide strong evidence to support this claim? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (3pts): Shape is approximately normal because the population is normally distributed. Mean is $790$ pounds. Standard deviation is $\frac{105}{\sqrt{36}} = \frac{105}{6} = 17.5$ pounds.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $z$-score $\frac{750 - 790}{17.5} \approx -2.29$. Uses $P(Z \le -2.29)$ to find probability $\approx 0.0110$.
  • Part c (1pt): Yes, a probability of $0.0110$ is quite small, indicating that a sample mean of $750$ pounds would be unusual if the true mean were $790$ pounds, thus providing strong evidence against the claim that the sanctuary bears are similar to the regional average.

FRQ 3

A national survey reported that $52\%$ of adults aged $18-29$ use social media daily, while $40\%$ of adults aged $30-49$ use social media daily. A researcher plans to take a random sample of $200$ adults from the $18-29$ age group and an independent random sample of $150$ adults from the $30-49$ age group.
a. Determine the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions ($\hat{p}_{18-29} - \hat{p}_{30-49}$).
b. Verify that the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions can be approximated by a normal distribution.
c. Calculate the probability that the proportion of daily social media users in the younger age group's sample is at least $10$ percentage points higher than in the older age group's sample. Show your work.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Mean of $\hat{p}_1 - \hat{p}_2$ is $p_1 - p_2 = 0.52 - 0.40 = 0.12$. Standard deviation is $\sqrt{\frac{p_1(1-p_1)}{n_1} + \frac{p_2(1-p_2)}{n_2}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.52(0.48)}{200} + \frac{0.40(0.60)}{150}} \approx \sqrt{0.001248 + 0.0016} \approx \sqrt{0.002848} \approx 0.0534$.
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies conditions for both samples: $n_1 p_1 = 200(0.52) = 104 \ge 10$, $n_1(1-p_1) = 200(0.48) = 96 \ge 10$. $n_2 p_2 = 150(0.40) = 60 \ge 10$, $n_2(1-p_2) = 150(0.60) = 90 \ge 10$. All are $\ge 10$, so approx. normal is appropriate.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $z$-score for a difference of $0.10$: $Z = \frac{0.10 - 0.12}{0.0534} \approx -0.37$. Probability $P(Z \ge -0.37) = 1 - P(Z < -0.37) \approx 1 - 0.3557 \approx 0.6443$.

FRQ 4

A polling organization wants to estimate the proportion of registered voters who support a certain ballot measure. They take a simple random sample of $400$ registered voters and find that $220$ of them support the measure.
a. Calculate the point estimate for the proportion of all registered voters who support the ballot measure.
b. Explain what it means for an estimator to be unbiased in the context of estimating the population proportion.
c. The organization considers using a biased estimator, which consistently overestimates the true proportion by $0.03$. Describe a potential consequence of using such a biased estimator for repeated sampling.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): The point estimate for the population proportion $p$ is the sample proportion $\hat{p} = \frac{220}{400} = 0.55$.
  • Part b (2pts): An estimator is unbiased if, in the long run, the mean of its sampling distribution is equal to the true population parameter being estimated. For the sample proportion $\hat{p}$, this means that the expected value of $\hat{p}$ is equal to the population proportion $p$.
  • Part c (2pts): If the estimator consistently overestimates the true proportion by $0.03$, then in repeated sampling, the average of the estimates would be $p+0.03$ instead of $p$. This would lead to systematically incorrect conclusions about the true population proportion, consistently suggesting higher support for the measure than actually exists.

FRQ 5

The amount of soda dispensed by a vending machine is normally distributed with a mean of $16.2$ ounces and a standard deviation of $0.15$ ounces. The machine is set to dispense $16$ ounces per cup. A student runs a simulation by taking $1000$ random samples of $5$ cups each and calculating the mean amount dispensed for each sample.
a. Calculate the probability that a single cup dispenses less than $16$ ounces of soda.
b. Describe how the sampling distribution of the sample mean for samples of size $5$ would differ from the distribution of individual cup amounts. Focus on shape, mean, and standard deviation.
c. Based on the information provided, explain why a simulation might be used to estimate the sampling distribution of the sample mean in this context, even though we know the population distribution.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates $z$-score for a single cup: $Z = \frac{16 - 16.2}{0.15} \approx -1.33$. Probability $P(X < 16) \approx P(Z < -1.33) \approx 0.0918$.
  • Part b (3pts): The shape of the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}$ would also be normal because the population is normal, even for $n=5$. The mean of the sampling distribution would be the same as the population mean, $\mu_{\bar{x}} = 16.2$ ounces. The standard deviation of the sampling distribution would be smaller than the population standard deviation, $\sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{0.15}{\sqrt{5}} \approx 0.067$ ounces.
  • Part c (1pt): A simulation might be used to confirm theoretical properties of the sampling distribution, especially for smaller sample sizes or when the population distribution might be unknown or non-normal, to see how the sample means behave. While the population is normal here, simulation can reinforce understanding or explore scenarios where the theory is less straightforward.
