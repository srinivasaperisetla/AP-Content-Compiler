AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 3)

FRQ 1

A local city council wants to estimate the proportion of residents who support a new initiative to expand public transportation. They survey 300 randomly selected residents, and 189 of them express support for the initiative.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of city residents who support the initiative. Show your work.
c. Interpret the confidence interval calculated in part (b) in the context of the problem.
d. If the city council wanted to conduct a new survey to estimate this proportion with a margin of error of no more than 3% at a 95% confidence level, what is the minimum number of residents they would need to survey?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly identifies a one-sample z-interval for a proportion and verifies all conditions (random, 10% condition, large counts).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the 95% confidence interval for the proportion, showing formula, correct values plugged in, and the resulting interval. (e.g., (0.574, 0.686))
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the confidence interval in context, referencing 95% confidence and the true proportion of residents supporting the initiative.
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly calculates the minimum sample size required, showing appropriate formula or setup. (e.g., n=1067 or n=1068)

FRQ 2

A company claims that 75% of its customers are satisfied with their new product. An independent consumer group wants to test this claim. They survey a random sample of 200 customers and find that 140 of them are satisfied.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test the company's claim. Identify the appropriate inference procedure.
b. Verify that the conditions for performing this significance test are met.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your results in part (c), what conclusion should the consumer group draw about the company's claim? Use a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states null ($p=0.75$) and alternative ($p \neq 0.75$) hypotheses in terms of the true proportion of satisfied customers and identifies a one-sample z-test for proportions.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies all conditions for the test (random, 10% condition, large counts based on null hypothesis).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic (z = -1.63) and p-value (0.1032), showing work.
  • Part d (1pt): Draws a correct conclusion in context, comparing the p-value to alpha and stating whether to reject or fail to reject the null hypothesis.

FRQ 4

A researcher wants to investigate if there is a difference in the proportion of males and females who prefer online learning over traditional classroom learning. A random sample of 120 male students found 78 preferred online learning, while a random sample of 150 female students found 81 preferred online learning.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test if there is a difference in the proportions of males and females who prefer online learning. Identify the appropriate inference procedure.
b. Verify that the conditions for performing this significance test are met.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your results in part (c), what conclusion should the researcher draw about the preference for online learning between male and female students? Use a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states null ($p_M = p_F$) and alternative ($p_M \neq p_F$) hypotheses in terms of the true proportions and identifies a two-sample z-test for proportions.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies all conditions for the test (random samples, 10% condition for each, large counts for each sample using pooled proportion).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic (z = 2.45) and p-value (0.0143), showing work.
  • Part d (1pt): Draws a correct conclusion in context, comparing the p-value to alpha and stating whether to reject or fail to reject the null hypothesis.

FRQ 5

A pharmaceutical company is developing a new drug to reduce the severity of allergy symptoms. They want to test if the proportion of patients experiencing significant symptom reduction (p) with the new drug is greater than 0.60, which is the known proportion for the current leading drug. They plan to conduct a significance test with null hypothesis $H_0: p = 0.60$ and alternative hypothesis $H_a: p > 0.60$.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this study and state a potential consequence of making this error.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this study and state a potential consequence of making this error.
c. If the pharmaceutical company decides to increase the sample size for their study, how would this affect the power of the test, assuming the significance level remains the same? Explain your reasoning.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly describes a Type I error as concluding the new drug is better when it is not, and provides a plausible consequence (e.g., costly production of an ineffective drug).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly describes a Type II error as failing to conclude the new drug is better when it actually is, and provides a plausible consequence (e.g., missing out on a beneficial drug).
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly states that increasing sample size increases the power of the test and provides a correct explanation (e.g., larger sample size reduces standard error, making it easier to detect a true difference).
