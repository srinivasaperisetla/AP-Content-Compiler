AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 9)

FRQ 1

A local pizza shop claims that 70% of its customers prefer their new thin-crust pizza over the traditional thick-crust. To investigate this claim, a random sample of 120 customers was selected, and 78 stated a preference for the thin-crust pizza.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of customers who prefer the thin-crust pizza.
c. Interpret the confidence interval calculated in part (b) in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a one-sample z-interval for a proportion (1pt); Verifies random sampling, 10% condition (120 < 10% of all customers), and Normal condition (np̂=78 >= 10, n(1-p̂)=42 >= 10) (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the sample proportion p̂ (0.65) and standard error (1pt); Calculates the correct confidence interval (0.567, 0.733) (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context, stating that we are 95% confident that the true proportion of customers preferring thin-crust pizza is between 0.567 and 0.733 (1pt).

FRQ 2

A national health organization claims that the proportion of adults who regularly exercise is greater than 0.45. A researcher wants to test this claim and collects data from a random sample of 250 adults, finding that 120 of them exercise regularly.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this test and identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for conducting this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Interpret the p-value and state a conclusion in context at the α = 0.05 significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States H0: p = 0.45 and Ha: p > 0.45 (1/2 pt); Identifies a one-sample z-test for a population proportion (1/2 pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random sampling, 10% condition (250 < 10% of all adults), and Normal condition (np0=250*0.45=112.5 >= 10, n(1-p0)=250*0.55=137.5 >= 10) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the sample proportion p̂ (0.48) and the correct test statistic z = 0.95 (1pt); Calculates the correct p-value (0.171) (1pt).
  • Part d (2pts): Interprets the p-value correctly (the probability of observing a sample proportion of 0.48 or greater, assuming the true proportion is 0.45, is 0.171) (1pt); States a correct conclusion comparing p-value to α and fails to reject H0, concluding there is not sufficient evidence to support the claim that the proportion is greater than 0.45 (1pt).

FRQ 3

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug designed to reduce cholesterol. The company wants to test if the proportion of patients who experience a significant reduction in cholesterol is greater than 0.60. They plan to conduct a significance test at the α = 0.05 level.
a. Describe a Type I error and a Type II error in the context of this study.
b. Explain a potential consequence of making a Type I error in this situation.
c. If the company wanted to reduce the probability of a Type I error, how would that affect the probability of a Type II error, assuming the sample size remains the same?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes Type I error (rejecting H0 when H0 is true - concluding the drug is effective when it is not) in context (1pt); Correctly describes Type II error (failing to reject H0 when Ha is true - concluding the drug is not effective when it actually is) in context (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Explains a plausible consequence, such as the company marketing an ineffective drug, wasting resources, or patients relying on an ineffective treatment (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): States that reducing the probability of a Type I error (e.g., by lowering α) would increase the probability of a Type II error (1pt).

FRQ 4

A health study investigated whether there is a difference in the proportion of people who prefer morning exercise versus evening exercise between two age groups: young adults (18-30 years) and middle-aged adults (31-50 years). A random sample of 150 young adults found 78 preferred morning exercise. A separate random sample of 120 middle-aged adults found 54 preferred morning exercise.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the proportion of young adults and middle-aged adults who prefer morning exercise.
c. Interpret the confidence interval in context and use it to justify whether there is a significant difference in preference for morning exercise between the two age groups.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a two-sample z-interval for a difference in proportions (1pt); Verifies random sampling for both groups, 10% condition for both samples, and Normal condition for both samples (n1p̂1=78>=10, n1(1-p̂1)=72>=10, n2p̂2=54>=10, n2(1-p̂2)=66>=10) (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates sample proportions (p̂1=0.52, p̂2=0.45) and the standard error for the difference (1pt); Calculates the correct 90% confidence interval (-0.030, 0.170) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the confidence interval in context, stating we are 90% confident that the true difference in proportions is between -0.030 and 0.170 (1pt); Justifies that since the interval contains 0, there is no significant evidence of a difference in preference between the two groups at the 90% confidence level (1pt).

FRQ 5

A tutoring center claims that its new online program is more effective than its traditional in-person program. They believe the proportion of students passing a standardized test after using the online program is higher than for those using the in-person program. A random sample of 100 students using the online program had 85 pass the test. A separate random sample of 120 students using the in-person program had 90 pass the test.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test the center's claim and identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for conducting this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Interpret the p-value and state a conclusion in context at the α = 0.01 significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States H0: p_online = p_in-person (or p_online - p_in-person = 0) and Ha: p_online > p_in-person (or p_online - p_in-person > 0) (1/2 pt); Identifies a two-sample z-test for a difference in population proportions (1/2 pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random sampling for both groups, 10% condition for both samples, and Normal condition using pooled proportion (p̂_pooled = (85+90)/(100+120) = 175/220 ≈ 0.795, n1p̂_pooled=79.5>=10, n1(1-p̂_pooled)=20.5>=10, n2p̂_pooled=95.4>=10, n2(1-p̂_pooled)=24.6>=10) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates sample proportions (p̂_online=0.85, p̂_in-person=0.75) and the pooled proportion; Calculates the correct test statistic z = 1.95 (1pt); Calculates the correct p-value (0.0256) (1pt).
  • Part d (2pts): Interprets the p-value correctly (the probability of observing a difference in sample proportions as extreme or more extreme than 0.10, assuming no true difference, is 0.0256) (1pt); States a correct conclusion comparing p-value to α and fails to reject H0 (p-value 0.0256 > α 0.01), concluding there is not sufficient evidence to support the claim that the online program is more effective (1pt).
