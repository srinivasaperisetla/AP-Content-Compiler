FRQ 2

a. Describe the characteristics of the distribution of battery life for Brand X, including shape, center, spread, and any unusual features.
b. Compare the distributions of battery life for Brand X and Brand Y.
c. Based on these boxplots, which brand would you recommend for a user who values consistent, long battery life? Justify your recommendation.

Scoring Guidelines

FRQ 4

A small bookstore tracked the number of books sold each day for 12 days: 15, 22, 18, 25, 10, 20, 19, 23, 17, 16, 8, 50.
a. Calculate the mean and median number of books sold per day.
b. Determine if there are any outliers in this dataset using the $1.5 \times IQR$ rule. Show your work.
c. Based on your findings in parts (a) and (b), describe the shape of the distribution of books sold per day. Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates the mean ($\bar{x} = 22.75$) and median ($18.5$) books sold per day.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $Q_1$, $Q_3$, IQR, and upper/lower fences, then correctly identifies any outliers ($50$ is an outlier). Shows all calculations.
  • Part c (1pt): Describes the shape as skewed right and justifies by noting the relationship between mean and median ($\bar{x} > \text{median}$) and/or the presence of an outlier on the upper end.

FRQ 5

The scores on a national standardized test are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 500$ and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 100$.
a. Calculate the proportion of test-takers who score above 650 on this test. Show your work.
b. A college is considering admitting students who score in the top 15% on this test. Determine the minimum score a student needs to achieve to be admitted to this college.
c. A student scores 650 on the national standardized test. On another standardized test, which is also approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 60$ and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 12$, a different student scores 70. Compare the relative performance of these two students on their respective tests.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates the z-score for 650 ($z = (650-500)/100 = 1.5$) and uses it to find the proportion ($P(Z > 1.5) \approx 0.0668$). Shows work.
  • Part b (2pts): Finds the z-score corresponding to the 85th percentile (approximately $z=1.04$) and uses it to calculate the minimum score ($X = 500 + 1.04 \times 100 = 604$). Shows work.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the z-score for the first student on the national test ($z_1 = (650-500)/100 = 1.5$) and for the second student on the other test ($z_2 = (70-60)/12 \approx 0.83$). Compares the z-scores to conclude which student performed relatively better.
