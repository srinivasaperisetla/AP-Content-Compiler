AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 2: Exploring Two-Variable Data (Set 3)

FRQ 1

A marine biologist is studying the relationship between the length (in cm) and weight (in grams) of a certain species of fish. Data was collected from 20 fish, and a scatterplot was generated. The correlation coefficient for this data is $r = 0.88$.
a. Describe the direction, form, and strength of the relationship between fish length and weight as shown in the scatterplot.
b. Interpret the value of the correlation coefficient $r = 0.88$ in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes the direction as positive, the form as linear, and the strength as strong, with reference to the scatterplot.
  • Part b (2pts): Explains that $r=0.88$ indicates a strong, positive linear association between fish length and weight, meaning that longer fish tend to be heavier.

FRQ 2

A student conducted an experiment to investigate the relationship between the amount of fertilizer applied (in grams) and the yield of a certain plant (in kg). A linear regression model was fit to the data, and the residual plot is shown below. For a plant that received 10 grams of fertilizer, the actual yield was 2.5 kg, and the predicted yield from the model was 2.8 kg.
a. Calculate the residual for the plant that received 10 grams of fertilizer.
b. Based on the residual plot, is a linear model appropriate for describing the relationship between the amount of fertilizer and plant yield? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates the residual as Actual - Predicted = $2.5 - 2.8 = -0.3$ kg.
  • Part b (2pts): States that a linear model is NOT appropriate (or is questionable). Justifies by explaining that there is a clear pattern (e.g., curved pattern, U-shape, or fan-shape) in the residual plot, indicating that the linear model does not capture all of the systematic relationship.

FRQ 5

A fitness tracker company is studying the relationship between the number of steps a person takes per day ($x$) and the number of calories they burn ($y$). For a random sample of 10 individuals, they recorded the following summary statistics: $\bar{x} = 8000$ steps, $s_x = 1500$ steps, $\bar{y} = 2500$ calories, $s_y = 400$ calories. The sum of the products of the z-scores for each individual was $\sum z_x z_y = 8.5$.
a. Calculate the correlation coefficient ($r$) between the number of steps and calories burned.
b. Interpret the value of the correlation coefficient in the context of this study.
c. Explain what it means if the correlation coefficient was close to $0$ in this context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates $r = \frac{\sum z_x z_y}{n-1} = \frac{8.5}{10-1} = \frac{8.5}{9} \approx 0.944$.
  • Part b (2pts): States that there is a strong, positive, linear relationship between steps taken and calories burned (1pt for strength/direction, 1pt for context and mentioning linearity).
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that a correlation near $0$ would indicate a very weak or no linear relationship between steps taken and calories burned, meaning steps are not a good linear predictor of calories burned.
