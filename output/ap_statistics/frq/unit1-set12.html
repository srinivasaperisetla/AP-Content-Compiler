FRQ 2

A botanist measured the heights (in centimeters) of a sample of 15 mature sunflower plants grown under specific conditions. The measurements are: 165, 172, 168, 175, 160, 180, 170, 178, 162, 173, 169, 171, 176, 163, 174.
a. Calculate the mean, median, standard deviation, and interquartile range (IQR) for the plant heights.
b. Describe the distribution of sunflower plant heights, including its shape, center, spread, and any unusual features.
c. Based on your calculations in part (a) and description in part (b), explain which measures of center and variability would be most appropriate to describe this distribution. Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the mean and median ($\bar{x} \approx 170.47$ cm, Median $= 171$ cm)
  • Correctly calculates the standard deviation and IQR ($s \approx 6.47$ cm, IQR $= 174 - 165 = 9$ cm)
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the shape (approximately symmetric/unimodal)
  • Describes the center (around $170-171$ cm)
  • Describes the spread (range $160-180 = 20$ cm or IQR/SD)
  • Mentions absence of clear outliers or any unusual features
  • Part c (1pt): States that mean and standard deviation are appropriate
  • Justifies choice by referencing the roughly symmetric shape and absence of outliers.

FRQ 4

The lifespan of a particular brand of light bulb is approximately normally distributed with a mean of 1200 hours and a standard deviation of 150 hours.
a. What proportion of these light bulbs are expected to last for more than 1350 hours? Show your work.
b. The manufacturer wants to offer a warranty such that only the longest-lasting 10% of bulbs are covered. What is the minimum lifespan (in hours) a bulb must have to qualify for this warranty? Show your work.
c. Suppose another brand of light bulb has a mean lifespan of 1100 hours and a standard deviation of 100 hours. A bulb from the first brand lasts 1300 hours, and a bulb from the second brand lasts 1250 hours. Compare the relative longevity of these two bulbs using appropriate statistical measures.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates the correct z-score for 1350 hours ($z = \frac{1350-1200}{150} = 1$)
  • Calculates the correct proportion ($P(Z > 1) = 0.1587$ or $\approx 15.87\%$)
  • Part b (2pts): Identifies the correct z-score for the 90th percentile (e.g., $z \approx 1.28$)
  • Calculates the corresponding lifespan value (Lifespan $= 1200 + 1.28 \times 150 = 1392$ hours)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the z-score for the 1300-hour bulb from the first brand ($z_1 = \frac{1300-1200}{150} \approx 0.67$)
  • Calculates the z-score for the 1250-hour bulb from the second brand ($z_2 = \frac{1250-1100}{100} = 1.5$)
  • Compares the two z-scores and concludes that the second bulb has a relatively longer lifespan since $1.5 > 0.67$.

FRQ 5

A teacher is comparing the midterm exam scores of two different AP Statistics classes, Class A and Class B. Both classes took the same exam. The scores for Class A are represented by the dot plot below. For Class B, the scores had a mean of 78, a median of 80, a standard deviation of 10, and an IQR of 15. The range of scores for Class B was 55 to 95.
a. Describe the distribution of midterm exam scores for Class A, including its shape, center, spread, and any unusual features.
b. Compare the distributions of midterm exam scores for Class A and Class B. Refer to specific features of each distribution.
c. One student in Class A scored 60. Another student in Class A scored 85. Calculate the percentile for the student who scored 60 and for the student who scored 85 in Class A.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Describes shape of Class A (e.g., skewed left, unimodal)
  • Estimates center (e.g., median around 80-82)
  • Estimates spread (range from 50 to 95, so 45)
  • Mentions no clear outliers for Class A
  • Part b (2pts): Compares shapes (e.g., Class A skewed left, Class B possibly symmetric/slightly skewed based on mean/median relationship)
  • Compares centers (e.g., Class B's median/mean slightly higher than Class A's estimated center)
  • Compares spreads (e.g., Class A's range of 45 is larger than Class B's range of 40 ($95-55=40$), or compares estimated IQR for A with B's IQR of 15)
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the percentile for the score of 60 in Class A ($3/20 = 15\%$)
  • Correctly calculates the percentile for the score of 85 in Class A ($13/20 = 65\%$).
