AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 19)

FRQ 1

A government agency wants to estimate the proportion of eligible voters in a certain city who support a proposed new environmental policy. A random sample of 500 eligible voters was surveyed, and 280 of them indicated support for the policy.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of eligible voters in the city who support the new environmental policy.
c. Interpret the confidence interval in the context of this problem.
d. The agency claims that more than half of the eligible voters support the policy. Based on your interval, does the data support this claim? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies a one-sample z-interval for proportions and verifies the random sampling, nearly normal (large counts), and independence conditions.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the 95% confidence interval for the proportion.
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the confidence interval in context, mentioning the confidence level and the parameter being estimated.
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly states whether the data supports the claim and provides a justification based on the confidence interval.

FRQ 2

A company that produces light bulbs claims that no more than 10% of their bulbs are defective. A consumer advocacy group suspects that the true proportion of defective bulbs is higher than 10%. They take a random sample of 400 bulbs and find that 52 of them are defective.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses and identify the correct inference procedure for this situation.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this inference procedure.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value, what conclusion should the consumer advocacy group draw at the significance level of alpha = 0.05? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses and identifies a one-sample z-test for proportions.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies the random sampling, nearly normal (large counts), and independence conditions.
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates the correct test statistic (z-score) and p-value.
  • Part d (2pts): Compares p-value to alpha, states correct conclusion (reject/fail to reject H0), and justifies it in context regarding the company's claim.

FRQ 3

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug designed to reduce the severity of allergy symptoms. They plan to conduct a significance test to see if the proportion of patients experiencing reduced symptoms with the new drug (p) is greater than the proportion with the old drug (p0 = 0.60). They set their null hypothesis as H0: p = 0.60 and alternative hypothesis as Ha: p > 0.60.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this problem and explain a potential consequence.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this problem and explain a potential consequence.
c. Without performing any calculations, describe one way to increase the power of the test.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes Type I error (rejecting true H0) in context and states a reasonable consequence.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly describes Type II error (failing to reject false H0) in context and states a reasonable consequence.
  • Part c (1pt): Describes a correct way to increase power (e.g., increase sample size, increase alpha, increase effect size).

FRQ 4

Researchers are investigating the effectiveness of two different fertilizers, Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B, on crop yield. They randomly select 200 plants for Fertilizer A and 250 plants for Fertilizer B. For Fertilizer A, 150 plants showed increased yield, while for Fertilizer B, 160 plants showed increased yield.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Construct a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions of plants showing increased yield between Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B (pA - pB).
c. Interpret the confidence interval in context. Based on this interval, is there convincing evidence of a difference in the proportions of plants showing increased yield between the two fertilizers? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a two-sample z-interval for proportions and verifies random sampling, nearly normal (large counts for both groups), and independence conditions.
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates the correct 90% confidence interval for the difference in proportions.
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the confidence interval in context AND correctly concludes whether there is convincing evidence of a difference, justifying based on whether 0 is included in the interval.
