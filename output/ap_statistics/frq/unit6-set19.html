AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 19)

FRQ 1

A market research company wants to estimate the proportion of adults in a certain city who regularly use public transportation. They survey a random sample of 500 adults from the city and find that 180 of them regularly use public transportation.
a. Identify the population and the parameter of interest.
b. Verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the population proportion of adults who regularly use public transportation.
c. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the population proportion of adults in the city who regularly use public transportation. Show your work.
d. Interpret the confidence interval you constructed in part (c) in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly identifies population and parameter.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random sample, normal condition ($n\hat{p} \ge 10$ and $n(1-\hat{p}) \ge 10$), and independence (10% condition).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates $\hat{p}$, standard error, and interval. Shows formula or calculator syntax.
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly interprets the confidence interval in context.

FRQ 2

A food company claims that 30% of its snack bars contain peanuts. A consumer advocacy group suspects this claim is too low and that the actual proportion is higher. They take a random sample of 200 snack bars and find that 70 of them contain peanuts.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this investigation.
b. Verify the conditions for performing a one-sample $z$-test for a population proportion.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the $p$-value for this test. Show your work.
d. Based on your $p$-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the consumer advocacy group draw? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0: p=0.30$ and $H_a: p>0.30$ with $p$ defined.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random sample, normal condition ($np \ge 10$ and $n(1-p) \ge 10$), and independence (10% condition).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates correct test statistic $z$ and $p$-value. Shows formula or calculator syntax.
  • Part d (1pt): Compares $p$-value to $\alpha$ and draws a correct conclusion in context with justification.

FRQ 3

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug designed to reduce the proportion of patients experiencing a certain side effect. The standard drug has a side effect rate of 25%. The company wants to test if the new drug has a lower side effect rate. They set up hypotheses $H_0: p = 0.25$ and $H_a: p < 0.25$, where $p$ is the true proportion of patients experiencing the side effect with the new drug.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this problem and state a potential consequence.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this problem and state a potential consequence.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes Type I error (reject $H_0$ when $H_0$ is true) in context and provides a relevant consequence.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly describes Type II error (fail to reject $H_0$ when $H_a$ is true) in context and provides a relevant consequence.

FRQ 4

Researchers want to compare the effectiveness of two different fertilizers, Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B, on the germination rate of a particular type of seed. They randomly select 300 seeds and treat 150 with Fertilizer A and the remaining 150 with Fertilizer B. For Fertilizer A, 120 seeds germinate, while for Fertilizer B, 105 seeds germinate.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure to construct a confidence interval for the difference in germination rates and define the parameters of interest.
b. Verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the difference between the two population proportions.
c. Construct a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the germination rates ($p_A - p_B$) between seeds treated with Fertilizer A and seeds treated with Fertilizer B. Show your work.
d. Interpret the 90% confidence interval you constructed in part (c) in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a two-sample $z$-interval for a difference in proportions and defines $p_A$ and $p_B$.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random assignment/sampling, normal condition for both groups ($n_A\hat{p}_A, n_A(1-\hat{p}_A), n_B\hat{p}_B, n_B(1-\hat{p}_B) \ge 10$), and independence (10% condition or random assignment implies independence).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates correct $\hat{p}_A$, $\hat{p}_B$, standard error, and interval. Shows formula or calculator syntax.
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly interprets the confidence interval for the difference in proportions in context.

FRQ 5

A public health organization wants to determine if there is a difference in the proportion of adults who smoke in two different rural counties, County X and County Y. A random sample of 300 adults from County X found 60 smokers. A separate random sample of 250 adults from County Y found 40 smokers.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test if there is a significant difference in the proportion of adults who smoke between the two counties.
b. Verify the conditions for performing a two-sample $z$-test for a difference in population proportions.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the $p$-value for this test. Show your work.
d. Based on your $p$-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what conclusion should the public health organization draw? Justify your answer in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0: p_X = p_Y$ or $H_0: p_X - p_Y = 0$ and $H_a: p_X \ne p_Y$ or $H_a: p_X - p_Y = 0$ with $p_X, p_Y$ defined.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies two independent random samples, normal conditions for both groups using pooled proportion ($n_X\hat{p}_c, n_X(1-\hat{p}_c), n_Y\hat{p}_c, n_Y(1-\hat{p}_c) \ge 10$), and independence within each sample (10% condition).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates correct pooled proportion $\hat{p}_c$, test statistic $z$, and $p$-value. Shows formula or calculator syntax.
  • Part d (1pt): Compares $p$-value to $\alpha$ and draws a correct conclusion in context with justification.
