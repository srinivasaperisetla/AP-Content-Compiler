FRQ 1

A survey was conducted at a high school to determine students' favorite types of fruit. Students from Grade 9 and Grade 12 were asked to choose their favorite from Apples, Bananas, Oranges, and Grapes. The results are summarized in the table below.
Favorite FruitGrade 9 CountGrade 12 Count
Apples6040
Bananas4550
Oranges3070
Grapes1540
Total150200
a. Construct a relative frequency table for the favorite fruit choices of Grade 9 students.
b. Construct a segmented bar graph to display the distribution of favorite fruit choices for both Grade 9 and Grade 12 students.

FRQ 2

A biologist recorded the lengths (in cm) of 20 newly hatched fish from a particular species. The data are as follows: 2.1, 2.5, 2.2, 2.8, 2.3, 2.5, 2.6, 2.1, 2.4, 2.7, 2.3, 2.0, 2.6, 2.9, 2.2, 2.4, 2.7, 2.5, 2.3, 2.8.
a. Classify the variable "length of newly hatched fish" as categorical or quantitative. If quantitative, further classify it as discrete or continuous. Justify your classification.
b. Construct a stem-and-leaf plot for the lengths of the newly hatched fish.
c. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of fish lengths based on your stem-and-leaf plot from part (b).

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly classifies variable as quantitative (0.5pt) and continuous (0.5pt) and provides a clear justification (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Constructs a correct stem-and-leaf plot, including a key (1pt for correct stems/leaves, 1pt for key).
  • Part c (2pts): Describes the shape (e.g., roughly symmetric, skewed, bimodal) (0.5pt), center (e.g., median or mean) (0.5pt), and spread (e.g., range or IQR) (0.5pt) in context (0.5pt).

FRQ 3

A small company wants to analyze the daily commute times (in minutes) of its 10 employees. The recorded times are: 15, 20, 25, 20, 30, 80, 22, 18, 20, 25.
a. Calculate the mean and median commute times for these employees.
b. Calculate the standard deviation and the interquartile range (IQR) for these commute times.
c. Identify any potential outliers in the data using the $1.5 \times IQR$ rule. Show your work.
d. Explain which measure of center (mean or median) and which measure of spread (standard deviation or IQR) would be more appropriate to describe this distribution. Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates mean (0.5pt) and median (0.5pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates standard deviation (1pt) and IQR (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly calculates fences ($Q_1 - 1.5 \times IQR$, $Q_3 + 1.5 \times IQR$) (0.5pt) and identifies any outliers (0.5pt).
  • Part d (2pts): Correctly identifies median as appropriate measure of center (0.5pt) and IQR as appropriate measure of spread (0.5pt) with a clear justification based on the presence of outliers/skewness (1pt).

FRQ 4

The heights of adult males in a certain country are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 175$ cm and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 7$ cm.
a. Calculate the $z$-score for an adult male who is 185 cm tall. Interpret this $z$-score in context.
b. What proportion of adult males in this country are taller than 185 cm? Show your work.
c. An adult male is considered "short" if his height is in the lowest 10% of the distribution. Determine the height (in cm) that corresponds to this cutoff.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the $z$-score (1pt) and interprets it in context (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct calculation using normal distribution (e.g., sketch, probability statement, calculator command) (1pt) and provides the correct proportion (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Shows correct calculation to find the height corresponding to the 10th percentile (e.g., using inverse normal) (1pt) and provides the correct height (1pt).
