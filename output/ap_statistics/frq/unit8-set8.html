FRQ 1

A bag of Skittles claims to have the following color distribution: 20% Red, 20% Orange, 20% Yellow, 20% Green, and 20% Purple. A statistics student opened a large bag and counted the following number of candies:
ColorCount
Red110
Orange95
Yellow88
Green105
Purple102
Total500
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a chi-square goodness-of-fit test to determine if the observed distribution differs from the claimed distribution.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the expected count for each color of Skittles. Show your work for at least one color.
d. The chi-square test statistic for this sample was calculated as $\chi^2 = 7.25$. Determine the p-value for this test. Interpret the p-value in context and, using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, justify your claim about the population based on the results.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses in context.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random sampling and expected count conditions (all expected counts $\ge 5$).

  • Part d (2pts): Determines the correct p-value for $\chi^2 = 7.25$ with $df=4$ (1pt) AND correctly interprets the p-value in context AND states the correct conclusion regarding the claimed distribution, linked to the p-value and $\alpha$ (1pt).

FRQ 4

The chi-square distribution is a family of distributions used in statistical inference for categorical data. Its shape depends on a single parameter, the degrees of freedom.
a. Describe two key characteristics of any chi-square distribution.
b. Explain why the condition that all expected counts must be at least 5 is important for performing a chi-square test.
c. A chi-square test for goodness-of-fit is conducted for a six-sided die. The observed counts show a noticeable difference from the expected counts for certain faces. Briefly describe what question this variation suggests to a statistician.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Describes two characteristics such as: skewed right, non-negative values, shape determined by degrees of freedom, approaches normal as df increases.
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that the condition ensures the chi-square distribution is a good approximation of the sampling distribution of the test statistic, preventing inaccurate p-values.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that the variation suggests questioning whether the die is fair or if the observed differences are due to chance alone.

FRQ 5

A company manufactures three types of smartphone screens: Gorilla Glass, Ceramic Shield, and Sapphire Crystal. The quality control department wants to investigate two different issues: 1. Whether the proportion of defective screens is the same across the three manufacturing shifts (Day, Evening, Night). 2. Whether the overall distribution of screen types produced in a week matches a target distribution of 40% Gorilla Glass, 35% Ceramic Shield, and 25% Sapphire Crystal. They collect data on 1000 randomly selected screens for the first issue, and a random sample of 500 screens for the second issue.
a. For the first issue (defective screens across shifts), identify the appropriate chi-square test to use. State the null and alternative hypotheses for this test. Briefly explain your reasoning for choosing this specific chi-square test.
b. For the second issue (distribution of screen types), identify the appropriate chi-square test. State the null and alternative hypotheses for this test. Calculate the expected number of Sapphire Crystal screens if the target distribution is met and 500 screens are sampled.
c. Suppose for the second issue, the chi-square test statistic was $\chi^2 = 8.5$ with 2 degrees of freedom, and the p-value was $0.014$. Using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion can be drawn about the company's target distribution? Justify your answer.
d. Based on the conclusion in part (c), if the company wants to investigate which specific screen types are deviating from the target, what should they examine next?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies Chi-Square Test for Homogeneity (1pt) AND states correct H_0 and H_a in context for homogeneity AND provides a clear reason for choosing homogeneity (comparing distributions of a single categorical variable across populations).
  • Part b (2pts): Identifies Chi-Square Test for Goodness-of-Fit (1pt) AND states correct H_0 and H_a in context for goodness-of-fit (0.5pt) AND calculates expected count for Sapphire Crystal ($500 \times 0.25 = 125$) (0.5pt).
  • Part c (1pt): States the correct conclusion (reject $H_0$, the distribution does not match the target distribution) and justifies it using the p-value ($0.014 < 0.05$).
  • Part d (1pt): Suggests examining the contributions to the chi-square statistic from each cell or comparing the observed versus expected counts for individual screen types.
