AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data: Slopes (Set 9)

FRQ 1

A teacher wants to investigate the relationship between the number of hours a student spends studying for a statistics exam and the score they receive on the exam. The teacher collects data from a random sample of students and creates the scatterplot below.
a. Based on the scatterplot, identify one question that could be investigated about the relationship between study hours and exam scores.
b. Suppose the teacher wants to determine if there is a statistically significant positive linear relationship between study hours and exam scores. Identify the appropriate inference method to address this question.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a question related to variation in the scatterplot, such as "Is there a positive association between study hours and exam scores?" or "How much does the exam score increase for each additional hour of study?"
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies the appropriate method as a t-test for the slope of the population regression line.

FRQ 2

A study investigated the relationship between the number of hours a student studies per week ($x$) and their GPA ($y$). A least-squares regression line was calculated, and a 95% confidence interval for the true slope $\beta$ was found to be $(0.15, 0.28)$.
a. Interpret the 95% confidence interval for the slope in the context of this study.
b. Based on this confidence interval, is there convincing statistical evidence of a positive linear relationship between hours studied and GPA? Justify your answer.
c. If the sample size was significantly larger, but the sample slope and standard error remained roughly the same, how would the width of the confidence interval change?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correct interpretation of the interval for the slope in context (e.g., "We are 95% confident that for each additional hour studied per week, the mean GPA increases by between 0.15 and 0.28 points").
  • Part b (1pt): Yes, because 0 is not included in the interval and all values are positive, indicating a positive relationship.
  • Part c (1pt): The interval would become narrower because a larger sample size generally leads to a smaller margin of error.

FRQ 3

A researcher is investigating whether there is a linear relationship between the amount of fertilizer applied to a plant (in grams, $x$) and the plant's height after one month (in cm, $y$). They collected data from 30 plants.
a. Identify the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test to determine if there is a linear relationship between fertilizer amount and plant height. Define any parameters used.
b. Identify the name of the appropriate inference method for this situation.
c. Before conducting the test, the researcher needs to verify conditions. Describe two conditions for inference for the slope of a regression model and how you would check them.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): $H_0: \beta = 0$ and $H_a: \beta \ne 0$, where $\beta$ is the true slope of the regression line relating fertilizer amount to plant height.
  • Part b (1pt): Linear regression $t$-test (or $t$-test for the slope of a regression line).
  • Part c (2pts): For each of two conditions (e.g., Linearity: check residual plot for random scatter; Independence: check if observations are independent, $10\%$ condition if sampling without replacement; Normal residuals: check histogram/Normal probability plot of residuals for normality; Equal variance: check residual plot for constant variability), correctly describe the condition and how to check it.

FRQ 4

A study investigated the relationship between the age of a car (in years, $x$) and its selling price (in thousands of dollars, $y$). Data from a random sample of 25 cars were collected. A computer output for the least-squares regression analysis is provided below.
PredictorCoefSE CoefTP
Constant35.21.523.470.000
Age (years)-1.350.18-7.500.000
$S = 2.15$$R-Sq = 71.1\%$$R-Sq(adj) = 69.9\%$
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure for the slope of the regression model.
b. Verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the slope in this situation, assuming residual plots showed no obvious patterns and a histogram of residuals was approximately normal.
c. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the slope of the regression line. Show your work.
d. Interpret the calculated confidence interval in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): $t$-interval for the slope (or linear regression $t$-interval).
  • Part b (2pts): Linearity: Assumed met as residual plots showed no obvious patterns. Independence: Random sample of 25 cars, $25 \le 10\%$ of all cars. Normal residuals: Assumed met as histogram of residuals was approximately normal. Equal variance: Assumed met as residual plots showed no obvious patterns.
  • Part c (2pts): Correct calculation using $\hat{\beta} \pm t^*SE_{\hat{\beta}}$. Degrees of freedom $df = n-2 = 25-2=23$. For 95% confidence, $t^* = 2.069$. Calculation: $-1.35 \pm 2.069(0.18) = -1.35 \pm 0.37242$. Interval: $(-1.722, -0.978)$.
  • Part d (1pt): We are 95% confident that for each additional year in age, the mean selling price of a car decreases by between \$978 and \$1722.

FRQ 5

A coffee shop owner wants to determine if there is a linear relationship between the daily outdoor temperature (in degrees Celsius, $x$) and the number of hot coffees sold ($y$). Data for 20 randomly selected days yielded the following regression output.
PredictorCoefSE CoefTP
Constant120.515.37.880.000
Temperature-2.50.82-3.050.007
$S = 8.1$$R-Sq = 34.0\%$$R-Sq(adj) = 30.5\%$
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for a test to determine if there is a negative linear relationship between temperature and hot coffee sales. Define any parameters used.
b. Verify the conditions for conducting a significance test for the slope of the regression line, given that residual plots showed random scatter and constant variance, and a normal probability plot of residuals was approximately linear.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for the test.
d. Based on your results and using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion can be drawn? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): $H_0: \beta = 0$ and $H_a: \beta < 0$, where $\beta$ is the true slope of the regression line relating daily outdoor temperature to the number of hot coffees sold.
  • Part b (2pts): Linearity: Assumed met as residual plots showed random scatter. Independence: Randomly selected days, and 20 days is less than 10% of all possible days. Normal residuals: Assumed met as normal probability plot of residuals was approximately linear. Equal variance: Assumed met as residual plots showed constant variance.
  • Part c (2pts): Test statistic $t = \frac{\hat{\beta} - \beta_0}{SE_{\hat{\beta}}} = \frac{-2.5 - 0}{0.82} \approx -3.049$. Degrees of freedom $df = n-2 = 20-2 = 18$. P-value for $t = -3.049$ with $df = 18$ (one-sided for $H_a: \beta < 0$) is approximately $0.003$.
  • Part d (1pt): Since p-value ($0.003$) $< \alpha (0.05)$, we reject $H_0$. There is convincing evidence of a negative linear relationship between daily outdoor temperature and the number of hot coffees sold.
