AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 16)

FRQ 1

A random sample of 150 high school students was surveyed, and 96 of them reported using social media for more than 3 hours per day.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure for estimating the true proportion of all high school students who use social media for more than 3 hours per day. Verify the conditions for constructing this confidence interval.
b. Construct a 90% confidence interval for the true proportion of high school students who use social media for more than 3 hours per day.
c. Interpret the confidence interval you constructed in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample Z interval for proportions and verifies random sampling, independence (10% condition), and Normal/Large Counts condition.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the 90% confidence interval, showing work (formula or values).
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context.

FRQ 2

A school administrator claims that 80% of students at their high school plan to attend a 4-year college. A random sample of 200 students was taken, and 140 of them stated they plan to attend a 4-year college.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test the administrator's claim. Identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your p-value, what conclusion should be made at the α = 0.05 significance level? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States correct null and alternative hypotheses and identifies one-sample Z test for proportions.
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies random sampling, independence (10% condition), and Normal/Large Counts condition using hypothesized proportion.
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the test statistic and p-value.
  • Part d (2pts): Makes a correct conclusion based on the p-value and α, and justifies it in context.

FRQ 3

A company manufactures light bulbs and wants to ensure that no more than 5% of their bulbs are defective. They regularly test a random sample of bulbs. The hypotheses for their quality control test are H0: p = 0.05 versus Ha: p > 0.05, where p is the true proportion of defective bulbs.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this problem and state a possible consequence.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this problem and state a possible consequence.
c. Suppose the company decides to decrease the significance level (α) of their test. Explain how this change would affect the probability of a Type I error and the probability of a Type II error.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes a Type I error (reject H0 when H0 is true) in context and states a reasonable consequence.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly describes a Type II error (fail to reject H0 when H0 is false) in context and states a reasonable consequence.
  • Part c (2pts): Explains that decreasing α decreases the probability of a Type I error and increases the probability of a Type II error.

FRQ 4

A researcher wants to compare the proportion of adults who prefer online news sources in two different age groups: 18-30 years old and 50-65 years old. A random sample of 120 adults aged 18-30 found 78 prefer online news. A separate random sample of 100 adults aged 50-65 found 45 prefer online news.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure for estimating the difference in proportions of adults who prefer online news sources between the two age groups. Verify the conditions for constructing this confidence interval.
b. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the difference in proportions (p_18-30 - p_50-65).
c. Interpret the confidence interval in context. Based on this interval, is there convincing evidence of a difference in preference for online news sources between the two age groups? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies two-sample Z interval for proportions and verifies random sampling, independence (10% condition for each group), and Normal/Large Counts condition for each group.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the 95% confidence interval, showing work (formula or values).
  • Part c (2pts): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context and justifies the conclusion about the difference based on whether 0 is included in the interval.

FRQ 5

A company introduced a new marketing campaign for one of its products. Before the campaign, 25% of customers were aware of the product. After the campaign, a random sample of 300 customers found 87 were aware of the product. A separate random sample of 250 customers from before the campaign found 50 were aware.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test if the new marketing campaign increased product awareness. Identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your p-value, what conclusion should be made at the α = 0.01 significance level? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States correct null and alternative hypotheses (H0: p_after = p_before, Ha: p_after > p_before) and identifies two-sample Z test for proportions.
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies random sampling, independence (10% condition for each sample), and Normal/Large Counts condition using pooled proportion.
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the pooled sample proportion, test statistic, and p-value.
  • Part d (2pts): Makes a correct conclusion based on the p-value and α, and justifies it in context.
