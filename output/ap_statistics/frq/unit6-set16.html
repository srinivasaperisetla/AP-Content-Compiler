AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 16)

FRQ 1

A marketing firm wants to estimate the proportion of adult internet users who prefer to shop online using a mobile device. In a random sample of 500 adult internet users, 280 reported preferring a mobile device for online shopping.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure for this situation and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Construct a 95 percent confidence interval for the proportion of all adult internet users who prefer to shop online using a mobile device.
c. Interpret the confidence interval you constructed in part (b) in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a one-sample z-interval for a proportion (1pt); Verifies random sampling, $n\hat{p} \ge 10$, and $n(1-\hat{p}) \ge 10$ (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the sample proportion $\hat{p}$ and standard error (1pt); Calculates the confidence interval (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets the confidence interval in context, stating the confidence level and interval.

FRQ 2

A company claims that at least 85 percent of their newly manufactured light bulbs last for more than 1,000 hours. A consumer advocacy group suspects this claim is too high. They test a random sample of 200 light bulbs and find that 162 of them last for more than 1,000 hours.
a. State appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to address the consumer group's suspicion. Define any parameters used.
b. Verify the conditions for performing a significance test for a population proportion.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value calculated in part (c), what conclusion should the consumer advocacy group make at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct $H_0: p \ge 0.85$ and $H_a: p < 0.85$ and defines $p$ as the true proportion of light bulbs that last for more than 1,000 hours (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random sample, $np_0 \ge 10$ ($200 \times 0.85 = 170 \ge 10$), and $n(1-p_0) \ge 10$ ($200 \times 0.15 = 30 \ge 10$) (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the test statistic $z = \frac{\hat{p} - p_0}{\sqrt{\frac{p_0(1-p_0)}{n}}}$ (1pt); Calculates the p-value (1pt)
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ and states correct conclusion in context with justification (1pt)

FRQ 3

A medical researcher wants to compare the effectiveness of two different medications, Medication X and Medication Y, for treating a certain skin condition. A randomized double-blind experiment was conducted where 150 patients received Medication X and 150 patients received Medication Y. After two weeks, 105 patients in the Medication X group showed significant improvement, while 90 patients in the Medication Y group showed significant improvement.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for a test to determine if there is a statistically significant difference in the proportions of patients who show significant improvement between the two medications. Define any parameters used.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic for this test. You do not need to calculate the p-value.
d. Based on your knowledge of significance tests and without calculating the p-value, explain how you would use the test statistic from part (c) to draw a conclusion at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct $H_0: p_X = p_Y$ and $H_a: p_X \ne p_Y$ (or $p_X > p_Y$) and defines $p_X, p_Y$ (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random assignment, $n_X\hat{p}_X \ge 10$, $n_X(1-\hat{p}_X) \ge 10$, $n_Y\hat{p}_Y \ge 10$, and $n_Y(1-\hat{p}_Y) \ge 10$ (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the pooled proportion $\hat{p}_c$ (1pt); Calculates the test statistic $z = \frac{(\hat{p}_X - \hat{p}_Y) - 0}{\sqrt{\hat{p}_c(1-\hat{p}_c)(\frac{1}{n_X} + \frac{1}{n_Y})}}$ (1pt)
  • Part d (1pt): Explains how to compare the test statistic to critical values (e.g., $z^* = \pm 1.96$) or how to use it to find a p-value to compare with $\alpha$, and states how to make a conclusion (1pt)

FRQ 4

A political campaign manager wants to estimate the proportion of registered voters in a certain district who support their candidate. An initial pilot survey of 100 randomly selected registered voters found that 40 support the candidate. The manager wants to conduct a larger survey to estimate this proportion with a margin of error of no more than 0.03 at a 95 percent confidence level.
a. Based on the initial pilot survey, what is the point estimate for the proportion of registered voters who support the candidate?
b. Explain what a margin of error of 0.03 means in the context of this problem.
c. Using the results from the pilot survey as an estimate for the true proportion, calculate the minimum sample size required to achieve a margin of error of no more than 0.03 at a 95 percent confidence level.
d. If the campaign manager did not have a pilot survey and wanted to be conservative in estimating the sample size, what value would they use for $\hat{p}$ to calculate the minimum sample size, and why?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States the point estimate $\hat{p} = 0.40$ (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that a margin of error of 0.03 means the estimate (0.40) is likely within 3 percentage points (0.03) of the true proportion of voters who support the candidate (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Sets up the margin of error formula $z^* \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}} \le 0.03$ with correct $z^* \approx 1.96$ (1pt); Solves for $n$ and rounds up (e.g., $n \ge 1024.96$, so $n=1025$) (1pt)
  • Part d (1pt): States $\hat{p} = 0.5$ and explains why this value maximizes the product $\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})$, leading to the largest possible sample size for a given margin of error (1pt)

FRQ 5

A polling organization wants to estimate the proportion of registered voters in a city who support a proposed new park. They randomly sample 250 registered voters and find that 135 of them support the new park.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the proportion of all registered voters who support the new park.
b. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the proportion of all registered voters in the city who support the new park.
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample $z$-interval for proportions (1pt); Verifies conditions (Random, Normal: $n\hat{p} \ge 10$ and $n(1-\hat{p}) \ge 10$, Independent: $N \ge 10n$) (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $\hat{p} = 135/250 = 0.54$ (0.5pt); Calculates standard error $SE = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}}$ (0.5pt); Uses correct critical value (e.g., $z^* \approx 1.96$) (0.5pt); Constructs correct interval $(0.478, 0.602)$ (0.5pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the interval in context, stating confidence level and population parameter.
