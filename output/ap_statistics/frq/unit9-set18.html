FRQ 3

A sociologist is studying the relationship between the number of years of education beyond high school ($x$) and annual income (in thousands of dollars, $y$). A random sample of 50 adults was taken. The least-squares regression line was found to be $\hat{y} = 25 + 5.5x$. A 95% confidence interval for the slope of the true regression line was calculated to be $(4.7, 6.3)$.
a. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this study.
b. Based on the 95% confidence interval, explain whether there is convincing evidence of a positive linear relationship between years of education and annual income. Justify your answer.
c. If the sociologist had instead taken a random sample of 100 adults (assuming the standard deviation of the residuals remained the same), describe how the width of the 95% confidence interval would change.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly interprets the slope $b=5.5$ in context, stating that for each additional year of education beyond high school, the predicted annual income increases by $5.5$ thousand dollars.
  • Part b (2pts): States there is convincing evidence of a positive linear relationship (1pt). Justifies by explaining that the entire interval $(4.7, 6.3)$ contains only positive values, which means we are 95% confident that the true slope is positive (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): States that the width of the confidence interval would decrease (get narrower) (1pt).

FRQ 4

A biologist is investigating the relationship between the age of a tree (in years) and its height (in meters). They collected data from 30 randomly selected trees of a certain species. A scatterplot of the data and a residual plot from the least-squares regression analysis are provided below.
a. Based on the scatterplot, identify a question suggested by the variation in the tree heights as age increases.
b. Based on the provided plots, verify the conditions for inference for the slope of the true regression line.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a question related to how the spread or pattern of tree height changes with age, e.g., "Does the variability in tree height increase as the trees get older?" or "Is the linear model equally appropriate for all ages of trees?".
  • Part b (3pts): Verifies Linearity (from scatterplot showing a linear trend or residual plot showing no pattern) (1pt). Verifies Equal Variance of residuals (from residual plot showing consistent spread/no fanning) (1pt). Verifies Independence (from stated random sample) (1pt). (Normality condition cannot be fully verified without a normal probability plot of residuals, so stating this is acceptable for full credit).

FRQ 5

A marine biologist is studying the effect of water temperature ($x$, in degrees Celsius) on the growth rate of a specific type of coral ($y$, in millimeters per month). They collected data from 18 coral samples in controlled environments. The results of a linear regression analysis yielded a sample slope ($b$) of $0.85$ mm/month/°C with a standard error ($SE_b$) of $0.20$. The mean growth rate was $5.0$ mm/month at a mean temperature of $25$ °C.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses to test if there is a positive linear relationship between water temperature and coral growth rate.
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line. Show your work.
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in the context of the study. Then, use this confidence interval to justify a conclusion for the hypothesis test stated in part (a) at a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0: \beta = 0$ and $H_a: \beta > 0$ in context.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates degrees of freedom $df=16$ and identifies correct $t^*$ for 90% confidence ($t^*=1.746$) (1pt). Correctly calculates the 90% confidence interval $0.85 \pm 1.746 \times 0.20$ and shows calculation and interval $(0.5008, 1.1992)$ (1pt).
  • Part c (3pts): Interprets the confidence interval in context (1pt). Uses the confidence interval to make a correct decision about the null hypothesis (1pt). Justifies the conclusion by explaining that since the entire interval (0.5008, 1.1992) contains only positive values, we have convincing evidence of a positive linear relationship (1pt).
