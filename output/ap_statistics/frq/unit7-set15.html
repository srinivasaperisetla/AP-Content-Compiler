AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 7: Inference for Quantitative Data: Means (Set 15)

FRQ 1

A company manufactures a new type of LED lightbulb and wants to estimate its average lifespan. A random sample of 30 lightbulbs was selected and tested. The sample mean lifespan was $\bar{x} = 25,000$ hours with a sample standard deviation of $s = 1,500$ hours.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the true mean lifespan of these lightbulbs.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true mean lifespan of these lightbulbs.
c. Interpret the calculated confidence interval in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample t-interval (1pt), verifies conditions (random sample, $n \ge 30$ allowing for approximately normal sampling distribution) (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct standard error and critical t-value (1pt), calculates the correct confidence interval (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets the confidence interval correctly in context (1pt)

FRQ 2

A local restaurant advertises that its average wait time for a table is 15 minutes. A regular customer believes the average wait time is actually longer than 15 minutes. To investigate, she collects data from a random sample of 40 recent wait times. The sample mean wait time was $\bar{x} = 16.8$ minutes with a sample standard deviation of $s = 5.2$ minutes.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test and identify the appropriate test procedure.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value, what conclusion should the customer make at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States correct null and alternative hypotheses ($H_0: \mu = 15$ minutes, $H_a: \mu > 15$ minutes) (1pt), identifies one-sample t-test for a mean (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions (random sample, $n \ge 30$ allowing for approximately normal sampling distribution) (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates correct test statistic $t = \frac{16.8 - 15}{5.2/\sqrt{40}} \approx 2.19$ (1pt), calculates correct p-value (df=39, p-value $\approx 0.017$) (1pt)
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ and makes a correct conclusion to reject $H_0$ and justifies in context (1pt)

FRQ 3

A agricultural researcher wants to compare the effectiveness of two different fertilizers, Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B, on crop yield. She decides to use a paired design by applying both fertilizers to adjacent plots within 12 different farms. The researcher records the yield (in bushels per acre) for each fertilizer on each farm. For each farm, the difference in yield, Fertilizer A - Fertilizer B, was calculated. The mean difference was $\bar{x}_d = 3.5$ bushels per acre with a standard deviation of the differences $s_d = 2.1$ bushels per acre. Assume the differences are approximately normally distributed.
a. Explain why a paired design is an appropriate choice for this study.
b. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test to determine if there is a significant difference in mean crop yield between the two fertilizers.
c. Construct a 90% confidence interval for the true mean difference in crop yield (Fertilizer A - Fertilizer B).

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Explains that pairing helps control for farm-to-farm variability, increasing power/precision (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses ($H_0: \mu_d = 0$, $H_a: \mu_d \ne 0$) (1pt)
  • Part c (3pts): Verifies conditions (random sample of farms; normality assumed) (1pt), calculates correct standard error $\frac{s_d}{\sqrt{n}}$ and critical t-value ($t^* = 1.796$ for df=11, 90% CI) (1pt), calculates correct confidence interval ($3.5 \pm 1.796 \times (2.1/\sqrt{12}) \Rightarrow (2.41, 4.59)$) (1pt)

FRQ 4

A school district wants to compare the effectiveness of two different online learning platforms, Platform X and Platform Y, for teaching calculus. A random sample of 50 students used Platform X, and their average exam score was $\bar{x}_X = 82$ with a standard deviation $s_X = 7$. A separate random sample of 60 students used Platform Y, and their average exam score was $\bar{x}_Y = 79$ with a standard deviation $s_Y = 8$. Assume all conditions for inference are met.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to determine if there is a significant difference in the mean calculus exam scores between the two platforms. Identify the appropriate statistical test.
b. The test statistic for this comparison was calculated to be $t \approx 2.37$, and the resulting p-value was approximately $0.02$. Interpret this p-value in the context of the study.
c. Based on the p-value, what conclusion should the school district make at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States correct null and alternative hypotheses ($H_0: \mu_X = \mu_Y$ or $H_0: \mu_X - \mu_Y = 0$, $H_a: \mu_X \ne \mu_Y$ or $H_a: \mu_X - \mu_Y \ne 0$) (1pt), identifies two-sample t-test for a difference of means (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly interprets the p-value as the probability of observing a test statistic as extreme or more extreme than $2.37$ (or a difference in means as extreme or more extreme than observed) assuming no true difference in mean scores (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ and makes correct conclusion to reject $H_0$ (1pt), justifies conclusion in context (1pt)

FRQ 5

An independent consumer research organization wants to estimate the difference in average annual repair costs between two luxury car brands, Brand A and Brand B. They collect random samples of repair records for 35 cars from Brand A and 40 cars from Brand B. The sample mean repair cost for Brand A was $\bar{x}_A = \$850$ with a standard deviation $s_A = \$150$. For Brand B, the sample mean was $\bar{x}_B = \$780$ with a standard deviation $s_B = \$120$. Assume all conditions for inference are met.
a. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the true difference in mean annual repair costs (Brand A - Brand B).
b. Based on this confidence interval, does there appear to be a significant difference in the average annual repair costs between Brand A and Brand B at the 5% significance level? Justify your answer.
c. If the sample sizes were increased to 100 cars for Brand A and 120 cars for Brand B, but the sample means and standard deviations remained the same, how would this affect the width of the confidence interval constructed in part (a)? Explain your reasoning.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates correct standard error $\sqrt{\frac{s_A^2}{n_A} + \frac{s_B^2}{n_B}}$ and critical t-value (1pt), calculates the correct confidence interval (e.g., $(7.08, 132.92)$) (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): States whether zero is included in the interval and makes a correct conclusion about a significant difference (1pt), justifies the conclusion in context by explaining what the interval means relative to zero (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that increasing sample sizes would decrease the standard error and thus decrease the width of the confidence interval, leading to a more precise estimate (1pt)
