FRQ 1

A company claims that its new line of colorful candies has a specific distribution of colors: 30% red, 20% blue, 20% green, 15% yellow, and 15% orange. A random sample of 200 candies is taken from a production batch, and the observed counts are recorded.
ColorObserved Count
Red52
Blue38
Green35
Yellow30
Orange45
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for an appropriate test to determine if the observed distribution of colors differs significantly from the company's claimed distribution.
b. Verify the conditions for conducting this significance test.
c. Calculate the expected count for yellow candies and the chi-square test statistic. Show your work.
d. Based on the test statistic, what conclusion should the company make at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0: p_{red}=0.30, p_{blue}=0.20, p_{green}=0.20, p_{yellow}=0.15, p_{orange}=0.15$ and $H_a:$ At least one proportion differs from the claimed distribution.
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies Random condition (random sample mentioned). Verifies Large Counts condition (all expected counts $\ge 5$).
  • Part c (2pts): Correct calculation of expected count for yellow ($200 \times 0.15 = 30$). Correct calculation of chi-square test statistic (work shown: $\chi^2 = 9.2917$).
  • Part d (2pts): States correct p-value ($\approx 0.0541$). States correct conclusion (Fail to reject $H_0$) in context. Justifies conclusion by comparing p-value to $\alpha$ and stating there is not convincing evidence that the color distribution differs.

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0:$ The distribution of brand loyalty is the same across the three regions. And $H_a:$ The distribution of brand loyalty is not the same across the three regions.

FRQ 4

A school principal wants to know if student absences are equally distributed across the days of the week (Monday-Friday). They select a random sample of 100 absences from the past semester and record the day of the week for each absence. The observed counts are: Monday: 25, Tuesday: 18, Wednesday: 15, Thursday: 22, Friday: 20. The principal runs a chi-square goodness-of-fit test and finds a test statistic of $\chi^2 = 5.6$ with 4 degrees of freedom and a p-value of $0.23$.
a. Based on the principal's findings, and using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the principal draw? Justify your answer.
b. A student questions whether the conditions for inference were met. Describe the conditions that must be checked for this test, and verify whether they are met in this specific scenario.
c. After reviewing the data, the principal notices that Monday has the highest number of absences. Describe a question suggested by the variation between the observed and expected counts that further investigation could address.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States correct conclusion (Fail to reject $H_0$) in context. Justifies conclusion by comparing p-value ($0.23$) to $\alpha = 0.05$ and stating there is not convincing evidence that student absences are not equally distributed across the days of the week.
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the Random condition (random sample of 100 absences is stated). Describes the Large Counts condition (all expected counts $\ge 5$). Verifies expected counts: Expected count for each day is $100/5 = 20$, which is $\ge 5$.
  • Part c (1pt): Describes a relevant question suggested by the variation, e.g., "Why does Monday have the highest observed number of absences, despite the overall distribution not being significantly different from equal?" or "Are there specific factors contributing to higher Monday absences that might warrant further study?"

FRQ 5

Two different scenarios are described below. For each scenario, state the most appropriate chi-square test, its null and alternative hypotheses, and the degrees of freedom.\nScenario 1: A veterinarian wants to know if the distribution of weight categories (small, medium, large) is the same for three popular dog breeds: Golden Retrievers, Poodles, and Beagles. She randomly selects 100 dogs from each of these three breeds and records their weight category.\nScenario 2: A researcher hypothesizes that the proportion of students who prefer online learning, hybrid learning, or in-person learning is 40%, 30%, and 30% respectively at a large university. A random sample of 200 students is surveyed.
a. For Scenario 1: Identify the appropriate chi-square test, state the null and alternative hypotheses, and calculate the degrees of freedom.
b. For Scenario 2: Identify the appropriate chi-square test, state the null and alternative hypotheses, and calculate the degrees of freedom.
c. Describe two key characteristics of a chi-square distribution.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies "Chi-Square Test for Homogeneity." Correctly states $H_0:$ The distribution of weight categories is the same for Golden Retrievers, Poodles, and Beagles. And $H_a:$ The distribution of weight categories is not the same for Golden Retrievers, Poodles, and Beagles. Correctly calculates $df = (3-1)(3-1) = 4$.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly identifies "Chi-Square Test for Goodness-of-Fit." Correctly states $H_0: p_{online}=0.40, p_{hybrid}=0.30, p_{in-person}=0.30$ and $H_a:$ At least one proportion differs from the hypothesized distribution. Correctly calculates $df = (3-1) = 2$.
  • Part c (1pt): Describes two characteristics of a chi-square distribution (e.g., is skewed right; values are non-negative; shape is determined by degrees of freedom; approaches normal as df increases).
