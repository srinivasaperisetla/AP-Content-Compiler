AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 4: Probability, Random Variables, and Probability Distributions (Set 11)

FRQ 1

A local high school surveyed its 800 students to gather data on whether they participate in extracurricular activities (E) or have a part-time job (J). The survey revealed that 450 students participate in extracurricular activities, 300 students have a part-time job, and 150 students do both.
a. Construct a Venn diagram or a two-way table to display the given information.
b. What is the probability that a randomly selected student participates in extracurricular activities but does NOT have a part-time job?
c. Given that a student has a part-time job, what is the probability that they also participate in extracurricular activities?
d. Are participating in extracurricular activities and having a part-time job independent events? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly constructs a two-way table or Venn diagram with all counts correct.
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates $P(E \text{ and not } J) = \frac{300}{800} = 0.375$.
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates $P(E
  • J) = \frac{P(E \text{ and } J)}{P(J)} = \frac{150/800}{300/800} = \frac{150}{300} = 0.5$.
  • Part d (2pts): Calculates $P(E) = \frac{450}{800} = 0.5625$ and $P(E
  • J) = 0.5$. Compares $P(E)$ and $P(E
  • J)$ (or $P(E)$ and $P(E \text{ and } J) / P(J)$ or $P(E \text{ and } J)$ and $P(E)P(J)$) and concludes they are NOT independent because $0.5625 \ne 0.5$.

FRQ 2

A small coffee shop observes the number of specialty coffee drinks (X) sold per hour during its peak morning hours. The probability distribution for X is given in the table below.

10.25

30.20

a. What is the probability that the coffee shop sells at least 3 specialty coffee drinks in a randomly selected peak hour?
b. Calculate the expected number of specialty coffee drinks sold per peak hour. Show your work.
c. Calculate the standard deviation of the number of specialty coffee drinks sold per peak hour. Show your work.
d. Interpret the expected value calculated in part (b) in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates $P(X \ge 3) = P(X=3) + P(X=4) + P(X=5) = 0.20 + 0.15 + 0.05 = 0.40$.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $E(X) = \sum x \cdot P(X=x) = (0 \cdot 0.10) + (1 \cdot 0.25) + (2 \cdot 0.30) + (3 \cdot 0.20) + (4 \cdot 0.15) + (5 \cdot 0.05) = 0 + 0.25 + 0.60 + 0.60 + 0.60 + 0.25 = 2.3$. Shows work for summation.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $Var(X) = \sum (x - E(X))^2 \cdot P(X=x) = (0-2.3)^2(0.10) + (1-2.3)^2(0.25) + (2-2.3)^2(0.30) + (3-2.3)^2(0.20) + (4-2.3)^2(0.15) + (5-2.3)^2(0.05) = 0.529 + 0.845 + 0.027 + 0.098 + 0.4335 + 0.3645 = 2.297$. Calculates $SD(X) = \sqrt{2.297} \approx 1.5156$. Shows work.
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets $E(X)=2.3$ as the long-run average number of specialty coffee drinks sold per peak hour if this process were repeated many times.

FRQ 3

A medical researcher believes that a new treatment for a certain allergy is effective in 80% of cases. To test this, a random sample of 15 patients with the allergy are given the new treatment. Assume the researcher's belief is correct and that the outcomes for each patient are independent.
a. Verify that the conditions for a binomial distribution are met for the number of patients for whom the treatment is effective in this sample.
b. Calculate the probability that exactly 12 patients in the sample have the treatment effective. Show your work.
c. Calculate the probability that fewer than 10 patients in the sample have the treatment effective. Show your work.
d. What are the mean and standard deviation of the number of patients for whom the treatment is effective in such samples of 15 patients?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Verifies conditions: fixed number of trials ($n=15$), two outcomes (effective/not effective), probability of success ($p=0.8$) is constant, and trials are independent (stated in problem).
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates $P(X=12) = \binom{15}{12} (0.8)^{12} (0.2)^3 = 455 \cdot 0.068719 \cdot 0.008 = 0.2501$. Shows formula or calculator notation.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $P(X < 10) = P(X \le 9)$. Shows use of binomial cumulative probability (e.g., binomcdf(15, 0.8, 9)) or summation $P(X \le 9) = \sum_{k=0}^9 \binom{15}{k} (0.8)^k (0.2)^{15-k} \approx 0.0611$.
  • Part d (1pt): Calculates mean $\mu = np = 15 \cdot 0.8 = 12$ and standard deviation $\sigma = \sqrt{np(1-p)} = \sqrt{15 \cdot 0.8 \cdot 0.2} = \sqrt{2.4} \approx 1.549$.

FRQ 4

A company manufactures computer chips, and historically, 3% of the chips produced are defective. A quality control inspector tests chips one by one until a defective chip is found. Assume that the chips are independent and the probability of a defective chip remains constant.
a. What is the probability that the first defective chip is found on the 5th chip tested? Show your work.
b. What is the expected number of chips the inspector must test until the first defective chip is found?
c. Describe how you would set up a simulation to estimate the probability that the first defective chip is found within the first 3 chips tested. You do NOT need to perform the simulation.
d. If the inspector finds the first defective chip on the 20th chip tested, explain what this result might suggest about the historical defect rate of 3%.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates $P(X=5) = (1-0.03)^4 \cdot (0.03) = (0.97)^4 \cdot (0.03) \approx 0.0265$. Shows formula or calculator notation.
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates $E(X) = \frac{1}{p} = \frac{1}{0.03} \approx 33.33$ chips.
  • Part c (2pts): Explains setting up a simulation using random numbers. Assigns 01-03 as a defective chip and 04-100 as a non-defective chip. Generates random numbers until a defective chip is found, records the number of non-defective chips before the first defective. Repeats many times. States to count how often the number of non-defective chips (or total chips) is less than or equal to 3. Alternatively, assigns 1-3 as defective and 4-100 as non-defective, generates random integers from 1 to 100, and records the trial number where the first defective (1-3) occurs. Repeats many times, then calculates the proportion of trials where the first defective occurred on trial 1, 2, or 3.
  • Part d (2pts): Calculates $P(X \ge 20) = (1-0.03)^{19} = (0.97)^{19} \approx 0.547$. Explains that finding the first defective chip on the 20th chip tested is not unusual (since $P(X \ge 20)$ is high), so this result does not necessarily suggest that the historical defect rate of 3% is incorrect. (Alternatively, $P(X=20) = (0.97)^{19}(0.03) \approx 0.0164$, which is small, but cumulative probability is better for "suggests".)

FRQ 5

A small business sells two types of custom-made shirts, Basic and Premium. The daily profit (in dollars) from selling Basic shirts, $B$, is a random variable with a mean of $\mu_B = 150$ and a standard deviation of $\sigma_B = 20$. The daily profit (in dollars) from selling Premium shirts, $P$, is a random variable with a mean of $\mu_P = 250$ and a standard deviation of $\sigma_P = 40$. The daily profits for Basic and Premium shirts are independent.
a. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the total daily profit from selling both types of shirts. Show your work.
b. If the business has a fixed daily operating cost of $C = 50, and they decide to offer a 10% discount on all sales for a promotion, let $T$ be the new total daily profit. The new total daily profit can be represented as $T = 0.90(B+P) - C$. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of $T$. Show your work.
c. Describe how the mean and standard deviation of the total daily profit would be affected if the daily profits for Basic and Premium shirts were NOT independent, but positively correlated.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates mean $\mu_{B+P} = \mu_B + \mu_P = 150 + 250 = 400$. Calculates variance $\sigma_{B+P}^2 = \sigma_B^2 + \sigma_P^2 = 20^2 + 40^2 = 400 + 1600 = 2000$. Calculates standard deviation $\sigma_{B+P} = \sqrt{2000} \approx 44.72$. Shows work and assumes independence for standard deviation.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates mean $E(T) = E(0.90(B+P) - C) = 0.90 E(B+P) - C = 0.90(400) - 50 = 360 - 50 = 310$. Calculates variance $Var(T) = Var(0.90(B+P) - C) = (0.90)^2 Var(B+P) = (0.81)(2000) = 1620$. Calculates standard deviation $SD(T) = \sqrt{1620} \approx 40.25$. Shows work.
  • Part c (2pts): Explains that the mean would remain the same, as the linearity property of expectation does not depend on independence: $E(B+P) = E(B) + E(P)$. Explains that the standard deviation (or variance) would increase if they are positively correlated, because $Var(B+P) = Var(B) + Var(P) + 2Cov(B,P)$, and $Cov(B,P) > 0$ for positive correlation.
