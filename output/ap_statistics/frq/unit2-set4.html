AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 2: Exploring Two-Variable Data (Set 4)

FRQ 1

A study investigated the relationship between the amount of sleep a student gets (in hours) and their score on a standardized math test (out of 100). The least-squares regression line calculated from the data is $\hat{y} = 45.2 + 6.8x$, where $x$ is the number of hours of sleep and $\hat{y}$ is the predicted math test score. The coefficient of determination is $r^2 = 0.7225$.
a. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this study.
b. Interpret the $y$-intercept of the least-squares regression line in the context of this study. If appropriate, explain any limitations.
c. Predict the math test score for a student who gets 7.5 hours of sleep.
d. Interpret the value of $r^2$ in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Interprets the slope as an estimated increase of 6.8 points in math test score for each additional hour of sleep.
  • Part b (1pt): Interprets the $y$-intercept as a predicted math test score of 45.2 for a student who gets 0 hours of sleep, and notes that this might not be meaningful as 0 hours of sleep is outside the typical range of observed data.
  • Part c (1pt): Shows calculation $\hat{y} = 45.2 + 6.8(7.5) = 96.2$ and states the predicted score is 96.2.
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets $r^2 = 0.7225$ as approximately 72.25% of the variation in math test scores can be explained by the linear relationship with hours of sleep.
