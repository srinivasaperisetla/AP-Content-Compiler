AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 5: Sampling Distributions (Set 19)

FRQ 1

The weights of apples harvested from an orchard are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 200$ grams and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 15$ grams.
a. Calculate the probability that a randomly selected apple weighs more than 220 grams.
b. Suppose a farmer is packaging apples into bags. Explain why it would be appropriate to use the normal distribution to approximate the distribution of the weight of a single apple from this orchard.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correct calculation of z-score $Z = \frac{220-200}{15} = 1.33$ and probability $P(X > 220) = P(Z > 1.33) \approx 0.0918$ (or similar, using tables or calculator).
  • Part b (2pts): Explanation that the problem states the population weights are *approximately normally distributed*, making the normal distribution appropriate without needing the Central Limit Theorem.

FRQ 2

A recent study claims that 60% of high school students in a large city regularly use social media. A statistics student takes a random sample of 150 high school students from this city to investigate this claim.
a. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the sample proportion of high school students who regularly use social media.
b. Justify whether the sampling distribution of the sample proportion can be approximated by a normal distribution.
c. Interpret the probability that a random sample of 150 students would yield a sample proportion of less than 0.55. (Do not calculate the probability.)

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Mean $\mu_{\hat{p}} = p = 0.60$. Standard deviation $\sigma_{\hat{p}} = \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.60(0.40)}{150}} \approx 0.04$.
  • Part b (2pts): Conditions $np = 150(0.60) = 90 \ge 10$ and $n(1-p) = 150(0.40) = 60 \ge 10$. Both conditions are met, so the sampling distribution can be approximated by a normal distribution.
  • Part c (1pt): A correct interpretation in context, e.g., "This probability represents the likelihood that, if the true proportion of students using social media is 60%, a random sample of 150 students would result in fewer than 55% of them using social media."

FRQ 3

The amount of cereal in a box is normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 368$ grams and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 5$ grams. A quality control manager selects a random sample of 25 boxes of cereal.
a. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the sample mean amount of cereal in a box.
b. Determine whether the sampling distribution of the sample mean can be described as approximately normal.
c. Calculate the probability that the sample mean amount of cereal in 25 boxes is less than 367 grams.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Mean $\mu_{\bar{x}} = \mu = 368$ grams. Standard deviation $\sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{5}{\sqrt{25}} = 1$ gram.
  • Part b (1pt): Yes, because the population distribution of the amount of cereal in a box is stated to be normally distributed.
  • Part c (2pts): Correct calculation of z-score $Z = \frac{367-368}{1} = -1.00$ and probability $P(\bar{X} < 367) = P(Z < -1.00) \approx 0.1587$ (or similar, using tables or calculator).

FRQ 4

A researcher wants to estimate the true mean weight of adult elephants. They collect a random sample of 10 elephants and measure their weights. The sample mean weight is $\bar{x} = 5500$ kg.
a. Identify the appropriate point estimate for the true mean weight of adult elephants.
b. Explain what it means for an estimator to be unbiased in the context of estimating the true mean weight of adult elephants.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): The sample mean $\bar{x} = 5500$ kg is the appropriate point estimate for the population mean $\mu$.
  • Part b (2pts): An estimator is unbiased if the mean of its sampling distribution is equal to the true value of the population parameter being estimated. In this context, it means that if we were to take many random samples of adult elephants and calculate the sample mean weight for each, the average of all these sample means would be equal to the true mean weight of all adult elephants.

FRQ 5

Two different regions, Region A and Region B, are being compared regarding the proportion of residents who have access to high-speed internet. It is known that 75% of residents in Region A have access and 60% of residents in Region B have access. A random sample of 120 residents is taken from Region A, and an independent random sample of 100 residents is taken from Region B.
a. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions ($\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$).
b. Justify whether the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions can be approximated by a normal distribution.
c. Interpret the value of the standard deviation calculated in part (a) in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Mean $\mu_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = p_A - p_B = 0.75 - 0.60 = 0.15$. Standard deviation $\sigma_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = \sqrt{\frac{p_A(1-p_A)}{n_A} + \frac{p_B(1-p_B)}{n_B}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.75(0.25)}{120} + \frac{0.60(0.40)}{100}} \approx \sqrt{0.0015625 + 0.0024} = \sqrt{0.0039625} \approx 0.0629$.
  • Part b (2pts): Check conditions: $n_A p_A = 120(0.75) = 90 \ge 10$, $n_A (1-p_A) = 120(0.25) = 30 \ge 10$, $n_B p_B = 100(0.60) = 60 \ge 10$, $n_B (1-p_B) = 100(0.40) = 40 \ge 10$. Since all conditions are met, the sampling distribution can be approximated by a normal distribution.
  • Part c (1pt): A correct interpretation in context, e.g., "The standard deviation of approximately 0.0629 describes the typical variability or spread of the differences in sample proportions ($\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$) from repeated random samples of 120 residents from Region A and 100 residents from Region B, around the true difference in population proportions of 0.15."
