AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 5: Sampling Distributions (Set 10)

FRQ 1

A large company claims that 80% of its customers are satisfied with their service. A consumer advocacy group plans to take a random sample of 150 customers to investigate this claim.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of the sample proportion of satisfied customers, $\hat{p}$, if the company's claim is true. Be sure to calculate the mean and standard deviation of $\hat{p}$.
b. Justify whether it is appropriate to use a normal distribution to model the sampling distribution of $\hat{p}$.
c. Calculate the probability that in a random sample of 150 customers, less than 75% are satisfied, assuming the company's claim is true.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies $\mu_{\hat{p}} = p = 0.80$ (1pt) and calculates $\sigma_{\hat{p}} = \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.80(0.20)}{150}} \approx 0.03266$ (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Checks conditions: $n \cdot p = 150 \cdot 0.80 = 120 \ge 10$ and $n \cdot (1-p) = 150 \cdot 0.20 = 30 \ge 10$. States that since both are $\ge 10$, the sampling distribution is approximately normal (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the $z$-score $z = \frac{0.75 - 0.80}{0.03266} \approx -1.53$ (1pt) and calculates the probability $P(\hat{p} < 0.75) \approx 0.0630$ (1pt).

FRQ 2

The amount of cereal in a box is normally distributed with a mean of 368 grams and a standard deviation of 5 grams. A quality control manager takes a random sample of 40 boxes of cereal.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of the sample mean amount of cereal, $\bar{x}$, in the 40 boxes. Be sure to calculate its mean and standard deviation.
b. Justify whether it is appropriate to use a normal distribution to model the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}$.
c. Calculate the probability that the mean amount of cereal in a random sample of 40 boxes is less than 367 grams.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies $\mu_{\bar{x}} = \mu = 368$ grams (1pt) and calculates $\sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{5}{\sqrt{40}} \approx 0.7906$ grams (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): States that since the population distribution of cereal amounts is normally distributed, the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}$ is also normally distributed (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the $z$-score $z = \frac{367 - 368}{0.7906} \approx -1.26$ (1pt) and calculates the probability $P(\bar{x} < 367) \approx 0.1038$ (1pt).

FRQ 3

A company operates two large production lines, Line A and Line B. Historically, 92% of items produced on Line A are defect-free, while 85% of items produced on Line B are defect-free. A quality control engineer takes a random sample of 200 items from Line A and a random sample of 150 items from Line B. Let $\hat{p}_A$ be the sample proportion of defect-free items from Line A and $\hat{p}_B$ be the sample proportion from Line B.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions, $\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$. Include the mean and standard deviation of this difference.
b. Justify whether the sampling distribution of $\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$ can be approximated by a normal distribution.
c. Calculate the probability that the sample proportion of defect-free items from Line A is more than 10 percentage points higher than that from Line B.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies $\mu_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = p_A - p_B = 0.92 - 0.85 = 0.07$ (1pt) and calculates $\sigma_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = \sqrt{\frac{p_A(1-p_A)}{n_A} + \frac{p_B(1-p_B)}{n_B}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.92(0.08)}{200} + \frac{0.85(0.15)}{150}} \approx 0.0349$ (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Checks all four conditions: $n_A p_A = 184 \ge 10$, $n_A (1-p_A) = 16 \ge 10$, $n_B p_B = 127.5 \ge 10$, $n_B (1-p_B) = 22.5 \ge 10$. States that since all are $\ge 10$, the sampling distribution is approximately normal (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $z = \frac{0.10 - 0.07}{0.0349} \approx 0.86$ (1pt) and calculates probability $P(\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B > 0.10) \approx 0.1949$ (1pt).

FRQ 4

A researcher is interested in estimating the average weight of a certain species of fish in a large lake. She takes a random sample of 10 fish. Their weights (in pounds) are: 7.2, 8.5, 6.9, 9.1, 7.8, 8.3, 7.5, 9.0, 8.1, 7.6.
a. Calculate the point estimate for the population mean weight of the fish using the provided sample data.
b. Explain what it means for an estimator to be unbiased. Based on your knowledge of sampling distributions, is the estimator used in part (a) unbiased? Justify your answer.
c. Describe how a simulation could be used to estimate the sampling distribution of the sample mean weight of fish for samples of size 10.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): The point estimate for the population mean is the sample mean $\bar{x} = \frac{7.2+8.5+6.9+9.1+7.8+8.3+7.5+9.0+8.1+7.6}{10} = 7.9$ pounds (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): An estimator is unbiased if the mean of its sampling distribution is equal to the true value of the population parameter being estimated (1pt). Yes, the sample mean ($\bar{x}$) is an unbiased estimator for the population mean ($\mu$), because $E(\bar{x}) = \mu$ (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): To estimate the sampling distribution, one would repeatedly take many random samples of size 10 from the population. For each sample, calculate the sample mean $\bar{x}$. Then, create a plot (like a histogram or dot plot) of these many sample means to visualize the shape, center, and spread of the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}$ (2pts).

FRQ 5

The weights of adult males in a certain city are approximately normally distributed with a mean of 185 pounds and a standard deviation of 25 pounds.
a. Calculate the probability that a randomly selected adult male from this city weighs more than 210 pounds.
b. A random sample of 36 adult males is selected. Calculate the probability that the sample mean weight for this sample is more than 190 pounds.
c. Explain why it is appropriate to use the normal distribution to calculate the probability in part (b), even if the population distribution of weights was not perfectly normal.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates $z = \frac{210 - 185}{25} = 1.00$ and $P(X > 210) = P(Z > 1.00) \approx 0.1587$ (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the standard deviation of the sample mean $\sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{25}{\sqrt{36}} = \frac{25}{6} \approx 4.167$ pounds (1pt). Calculates $z = \frac{190 - 185}{4.167} \approx 1.20$ and $P(\bar{x} > 190) = P(Z > 1.20) \approx 0.1151$ (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Because the sample size ($n=36$) is greater than or equal to 30, the Central Limit Theorem (CLT) applies, which states that the sampling distribution of the sample mean will be approximately normal (1pt).
