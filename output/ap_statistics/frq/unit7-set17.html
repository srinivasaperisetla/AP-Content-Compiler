AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 7: Inference for Quantitative Data: Means (Set 17)

FRQ 1

A company advertises that its new energy-efficient light bulbs have an average lifespan of 8,000 hours. A consumer advocacy group wants to estimate the true average lifespan. They randomly selected 30 bulbs and found a sample mean lifespan of $\bar{x} = 7920$ hours with a sample standard deviation of $s = 250$ hours.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the true average lifespan of the light bulbs.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true average lifespan of the light bulbs.
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in context.
d. Based on this confidence interval, does the data provide convincing evidence that the true average lifespan is different from 8,000 hours? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample $t$-interval for a mean (1pt); Verifies conditions (Random, Independent, Normal/Large Sample) (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates degrees of freedom and critical $t$-value (0.5pt); Correctly calculates the 95% confidence interval for $\mu$ (1.5pts).
  • Part c (1pt): Correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly answers the question with justification based on the confidence interval (1pt).

FRQ 2

A machine is designed to fill cereal boxes with an average of 450 grams of cereal. A quality control inspector suspects the machine is underfilling the boxes. A random sample of 25 boxes is taken, and their contents are weighed. The sample mean is $\bar{x} = 448.5$ grams, and the sample standard deviation is $s = 5.2$ grams.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a test to determine if the machine is underfilling the boxes. Define any parameters you use.
b. Identify the appropriate inference method and verify the conditions for carrying out this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Interpret the p-value in context and state your conclusion based on a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$. Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct $H_0: \mu = 450$ and $H_a: \mu < 450$, defining $\mu$ as the true average weight of cereal in boxes (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Identifies one-sample $t$-test for a mean (1pt); Verifies conditions (Random sample, Independence, Normality/Large sample justification - e.g., "assume population is normal since $n < 30$") (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates correct test statistic $t$ (1pt); Calculates correct p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Correct interpretation of the p-value in context (0.5pt); States correct conclusion (reject $H_0$) and justifies based on p-value $< \alpha$ (0.5pt).

FRQ 4

An automotive engineer wants to compare the fuel efficiency of two new engine designs, Design A and Design B. They randomly select 35 cars with Design A engines and 40 cars with Design B engines. The sample data are summarized below: Design A: $n_A = 35$, $\bar{x}_A = 32.5$ mpg, $s_A = 2.8$ mpg. Design B: $n_B = 40$, $\bar{x}_B = 30.1$ mpg, $s_B = 3.1$ mpg.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the difference in true average fuel efficiencies ($\mu_A - \mu_B$).
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in true average fuel efficiencies.
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in context and justify whether there appears to be a statistically significant difference in the true average fuel efficiencies of the two engine designs.
d. If the engineer wanted to halve the margin of error of the confidence interval in part (b) while maintaining the same confidence level, describe the impact on the required sample sizes, assuming similar standard deviations.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies two-sample $t$-interval for the difference of means (1pt); Verifies conditions (Random samples, Independent samples, Large sample sizes, or assumes normality) (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates degrees of freedom (0.5pt); Correctly calculates the 90% confidence interval for $\mu_A - \mu_B$ (1.5pts).
  • Part c (1pt): Correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context (0.5pt); Justifies statistical significance based on whether the interval contains 0 (0.5pt).
  • Part d (1pt): States that the sample sizes would need to be quadrupled (0.5pt); Explains this is because the margin of error is proportional to $\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}$ (0.5pt).

FRQ 5

A health researcher wants to investigate if there is a difference in the average daily screen time (in hours) between teenagers who participate in extracurricular sports and those who do not. A random sample of 50 teenagers involved in sports showed a mean screen time of $\bar{x}_S = 4.8$ hours with a standard deviation of $s_S = 1.5$ hours. A random sample of 45 teenagers not involved in sports showed a mean screen time of $\bar{x}_{NS} = 6.2$ hours with a standard deviation of $s_{NS} = 2.1$ hours.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this investigation. Define any parameters you use.
b. Identify the appropriate inference method and verify the conditions for carrying out this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic for this test.
d. The p-value for this test is approximately $0.0001$. Interpret this p-value in context.
e. Based on the p-value from part (d) and using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what conclusion should the researcher make? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct $H_0: \mu_S = \mu_{NS}$ and $H_a: \mu_S \ne \mu_{NS}$, defining parameters $\mu_S$ and $\mu_{NS}$ (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Identifies two-sample $t$-test for the difference of means (1pt); Verifies conditions (Random samples, Independent samples, Large sample sizes) (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates correct test statistic $t$ (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Correct interpretation of the p-value in context (1pt).
  • Part e (1pt): States correct conclusion (reject $H_0$) and justifies based on p-value $< \alpha$ (1pt).
