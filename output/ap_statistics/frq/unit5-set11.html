AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 5: Sampling Distributions (Set 11)

FRQ 1

A recent study claims that $30\%$ of adult Americans regularly consume organic foods. A large grocery chain plans to conduct a survey of 200 randomly selected adult Americans to verify this claim.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of the sample proportion of adult Americans who regularly consume organic foods, assuming the claim is true. Be sure to address its shape, center, and spread.
b. What is the probability that the survey will result in a sample proportion of less than $25\%$ if the claim is true? Show your work.
c. Based on your calculation in part (b), would a sample proportion of $25\%$ provide convincing evidence that the true proportion of adult Americans who regularly consume organic foods is actually less than $30\%$? Explain your reasoning.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Shape is approximately normal (condition $n\hat{p} \ge 10$ and $n(1-\hat{p}) \ge 10$ shown/stated), center is $\mu_{\hat{p}} = p = 0.30$, spread is $\sigma_{\hat{p}} = \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.30(0.70)}{200}} \approx 0.0324$.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $Z$-score for $\hat{p}=0.25$ and finds the correct probability (showing work). $Z = \frac{0.25 - 0.30}{0.0324} \approx -1.54$, $P(\hat{p} < 0.25) \approx 0.0618$.
  • Part c (1pt): Compares the probability to a common significance level (e.g., $0.05$) and concludes whether it provides convincing evidence, relating to context.

FRQ 2

A manufacturer produces bags of pretzels. The label on each bag states that the net weight is 10 ounces. The quality control department regularly selects a random sample of bags and weighs their contents.
a. Let $X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n$ represent the net weights of $n$ randomly selected bags. Is the sample mean, $\bar{X}$, an unbiased estimator for the true mean net weight, $\mu$? Explain your reasoning.
b. Let $S^2$ be the sample variance. Is $S^2$ an unbiased estimator for the population variance, $\sigma^2$? Explain your reasoning.
c. If the true mean net weight is $\mu = 10$ ounces with a standard deviation of $\sigma = 0.5$ ounces, what are the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the sample mean for samples of size $n=25$?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States "Yes" and explains that the expected value of the sample mean is equal to the population mean ($\text{E}[\bar{X}] = \mu$).
  • Part b (1pt): States "Yes" and explains that the expected value of the sample variance is equal to the population variance ($\text{E}[S^2] = \sigma^2$).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the mean of the sampling distribution of the sample mean as $\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu = 10$ ounces and the standard deviation as $\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{0.5}{\sqrt{25}} = 0.1$ ounces.

FRQ 3

A researcher is comparing the effectiveness of two different educational apps, App A and App B, designed to improve math skills. A random sample of 150 students used App A, and a separate random sample of 120 students used App B. After a month, $70\%$ of students using App A showed significant improvement, while $60\%$ of students using App B showed significant improvement.
a. Let $p_A$ be the true proportion of students who show significant improvement using App A, and $p_B$ be the true proportion for App B. Assuming $p_A = 0.70$ and $p_B = 0.60$, determine the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions ($\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$).
b. Justify whether the sampling distribution of ($\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$) can be approximated by a normal distribution.
c. Calculate the probability that the observed difference ($\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$) would be $0.10$ or greater, assuming the true proportions are $p_A = 0.65$ and $p_B = 0.65$ (i.e., there is no true difference in effectiveness). Show your work.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates $\mu_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = p_A - p_B = 0.70 - 0.60 = 0.10$ and $\sigma_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = \sqrt{\frac{p_A(1-p_A)}{n_A} + \frac{p_B(1-p_B)}{n_B}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.70(0.30)}{150} + \frac{0.60(0.40)}{120}} \approx 0.0542$.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions for normality: $n_A p_A \ge 10$, $n_A (1-p_A) \ge 10$, $n_B p_B \ge 10$, $n_B (1-p_B) \ge 10$. ($150 \times 0.7 = 105$, $150 \times 0.3 = 45$, $120 \times 0.6 = 72$, $120 \times 0.4 = 48$; all are $\ge 10$).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $\mu_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = 0.65 - 0.65 = 0$. Calculates $\sigma_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = \sqrt{\frac{0.65(0.35)}{150} + \frac{0.65(0.35)}{120}} \approx 0.0617$. Calculates $Z = \frac{0.10 - 0}{0.0617} \approx 1.62$. Finds $P(\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B \ge 0.10) \approx 0.0526$.

FRQ 4

The average wait time at a popular restaurant follows a right-skewed distribution with a mean of $\mu = 25$ minutes and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 12$ minutes. A random sample of 40 customers is observed.
a. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the sampling distribution of the sample mean wait time for samples of size 40.
b. What is the probability that the average wait time for a random sample of 40 customers is less than 22 minutes? Show your work.
c. If a random sample of only 5 customers were taken, would the sampling distribution of the sample mean wait time be approximately normal? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Shape is approximately normal due to the Central Limit Theorem ($n \ge 30$). Center is $\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu = 25$ minutes. Spread is $\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{12}{\sqrt{40}} \approx 1.897$ minutes.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $Z$-score for $\bar{x}=22$ and finds the correct probability (showing work). $Z = \frac{22 - 25}{1.897} \approx -1.58$. $P(\bar{X} < 22) \approx 0.057$.
  • Part c (1pt): States "No" because the population distribution is skewed and the sample size ($n=5$) is not large enough for the Central Limit Theorem to apply.
