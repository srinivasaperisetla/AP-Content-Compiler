AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 7: Inference for Quantitative Data: Means (Set 11)

FRQ 1

A health organization wants to estimate the mean cholesterol level of adult males in a particular city. They randomly select 30 adult males from the city and measure their cholesterol levels. The sample mean is $\bar{x} = 210$ mg/dL and the sample standard deviation is $s = 25$ mg/dL.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the mean cholesterol level.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true mean cholesterol level of adult males in this city.
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in context and explain what a 95% confidence level means.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a one-sample t-interval for means (1pt); Verifies random sample, independence ($10n < N$), and nearly normal condition ($n=30$ large enough for Central Limit Theorem) (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct standard error and degrees of freedom (1pt); Computes the correct confidence interval (e.g., $(200.41, 219.59)$ mg/dL) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the confidence interval in context (1pt); Explains the meaning of 95% confidence level (1pt).

FRQ 2

A company manufactures light bulbs, and the average lifespan is advertised as 1000 hours. A consumer advocacy group suspects that the true average lifespan is less than 1000 hours. They randomly select 15 light bulbs, and their observed lifespans (in hours) are: 980, 1010, 975, 990, 1005, 960, 995, 1020, 985, 970, 1000, 990, 975, 980, 995. The sample mean is $\bar{x} = 988.67$ hours and the sample standard deviation is $s = 18.07$ hours.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this investigation and identify the appropriate inference procedure.
b. Verify the conditions for conducting this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your results, what conclusion should the consumer advocacy group make at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States $H_0: \mu = 1000$ and $H_a: \mu < 1000$ (where $\mu$ is the true mean lifespan of light bulbs) and identifies a one-sample t-test for means (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies random sample and independence ($10n < N$ assumption) (1pt); Discusses the nearly normal condition (e.g., by creating a graphical display of the data to assess for strong skewness or outliers) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the test statistic $t = \frac{988.67 - 1000}{18.07/\sqrt{15}} \approx -2.435$ with $df = 14$ (1pt); Finds the correct p-value (e.g., $P(t < -2.435) \approx 0.0142$) (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ ($0.0142 < 0.05$) and states to reject $H_0$. Concludes there is convincing evidence that the true mean lifespan of light bulbs is less than 1000 hours (1pt).

FRQ 4

A pharmaceutical company wants to compare the effectiveness of two different pain relievers, Drug A and Drug B. They randomly assign 40 patients with similar chronic pain conditions to receive Drug A and another 45 patients to receive Drug B. After one month, they measure the pain reduction score (on a scale of 0 to 100, higher scores indicate more reduction). Summary statistics are: Drug A: $n_A = 40$, $\bar{x}_A = 65$, $s_A = 12$. Drug B: $n_B = 45$, $\bar{x}_B = 60$, $s_B = 10$.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the difference in mean pain reduction scores between Drug A and Drug B.
b. Calculate a 99% confidence interval for the true difference in mean pain reduction scores ($\mu_A - \mu_B$).
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in context. Based on this interval, can the company claim that Drug A is significantly more effective than Drug B? Justify your answer.
d. Suppose the sample sizes for both drugs were increased, while the sample means and standard deviations remained approximately the same. Describe the effect this would have on the width of the confidence interval in part (b).

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a two-sample t-interval for the difference of means (1pt); Verifies conditions: two independent random samples, independence ($10n < N$ for both groups), and large sample sizes ($n_A \ge 30, n_B \ge 30$) for approximate normality (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the standard error for the difference of means (1pt); Computes the correct 99% confidence interval (e.g., using software $df \approx 78$, $t^* \approx 2.64$; CI: $(5 - 2.64 \times \sqrt{\frac{12^2}{40} + \frac{10^2}{45}}, 5 + 2.64 \times \sqrt{\frac{12^2}{40} + \frac{10^2}{45}}) = (-0.91, 10.91)$) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the confidence interval in context (1pt); Justifies that the company cannot claim Drug A is significantly more effective because the interval contains 0 (and negative values), meaning that $\mu_A$ could be less than or equal to $\mu_B$ (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Explains that increasing sample sizes would decrease the standard error, leading to a narrower confidence interval, assuming the confidence level remains the same (1pt).
