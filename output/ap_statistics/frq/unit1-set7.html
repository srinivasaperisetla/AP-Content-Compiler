AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 7)

FRQ 1

A survey asked 50 randomly selected high school students about their primary mode of transportation to school. The results were: Car (25 students), Bus (15 students), Walk (8 students), and Bike (2 students).
a. Construct a relative frequency table for these data.
b. Create a bar chart to display the distribution of transportation modes.
c. Describe what the bar chart reveals about the students' primary mode of transportation to school.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates and presents relative frequencies for all categories (1 pt) and labels the table appropriately (1 pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly constructs a bar chart with labeled axes and appropriate scale (1 pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Describes the most frequent mode of transport and the least frequent mode of transport, referencing the chart (1 pt).

FRQ 2

A group of 20 college students was asked to record the number of hours they spent studying for their final exams during the week leading up to the exams. The data collected were: 15, 22, 18, 20, 25, 12, 17, 21, 23, 19, 16, 24, 20, 18, 26, 14, 22, 19, 21, 17.
a. Construct a stemplot of the study hours data.
b. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of study hours.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly constructs a stemplot with appropriate stems, leaves, and a key (2 pts).
  • Part b (3pts): Describes the shape of the distribution (1 pt), identifies a reasonable measure of center (1 pt), and describes the spread (1 pt).

FRQ 3

A small online bookstore records the number of books sold each day for a random sample of 10 days: 12, 15, 10, 8, 14, 11, 25, 9, 13, 10.
a. Calculate the mean, median, and standard deviation for the number of books sold.
b. Based on your calculations, explain which measure of center and which measure of variability would be more appropriate to describe these data. Justify your explanation.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (3pts): Correctly calculates the mean (1 pt), median (1 pt), and standard deviation (1 pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Identifies the median as the more appropriate measure of center and explains why (1 pt), and justifies by noting the presence of an outlier or skewness (1 pt).

FRQ 4

Two different types of fertilizer, Type A and Type B, were tested on corn plants to see their effect on yield (in bushels per acre). The yields from 20 plots treated with Type A and 20 plots treated with Type B are summarized in the parallel boxplots provided.
a. Compare the distributions of corn yield for Type A and Type B fertilizers. Your comparison should include shape, center, spread, and any unusual features.
b. Based on the comparison, which fertilizer type appears to be more effective? Justify your choice.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (4pts): Compares shape for both distributions (1 pt), compares center for both distributions (1 pt), compares spread for both distributions (1 pt), and discusses any outliers or unusual features (1 pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Identifies the more effective fertilizer (1 pt) and justifies with statistical reasoning based on the comparison (1 pt).

FRQ 5

The heights of adult males in a certain country are approximately normally distributed with a mean $\mu = 175$ cm and a standard deviation $\sigma = 7$ cm.
a. Calculate the $z$-score for an adult male who is 185 cm tall. Interpret this $z$-score in context.
b. What proportion of adult males in this country are taller than 185 cm? Show your work.
c. Suppose a separate study found that the distribution of heights for professional basketball players is strongly skewed to the right. Explain why the normal distribution would not be an appropriate model for the heights of professional basketball players.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates $z$-score (1 pt) and correctly interprets it in context (1 pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the proportion (1 pt) and shows work (e.g., $P(Z > z)$ notation) (1 pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that the normal distribution is symmetric and unimodal, so it is not appropriate for a skewed distribution (1 pt).
