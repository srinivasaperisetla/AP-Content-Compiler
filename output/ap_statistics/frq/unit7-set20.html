AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 7: Inference for Quantitative Data: Means (Set 20)

FRQ 1

A farmer claims that a new organic fertilizer increases the average yield of corn per acre beyond the current average of 120 bushels. To test this claim, the farmer applies the new fertilizer to a simple random sample of 30 acres of corn. The average yield for these 30 acres was $\bar{x} = 124.5$ bushels with a standard deviation of $s = 10.3$ bushels.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this test.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test. Show your work.
d. Based on the p-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the farmer make? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States $H_0: \mu = 120$ and $H_a: \mu > 120$ in context (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies conditions: Random sample (1pt), $n < 10\%$ of population (1pt), and normal/large sample ($n=30 \ge 30$) or normal population (1pt). (Maximum 2 points for Part b)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the test statistic $t = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{s/\sqrt{n}} = \frac{124.5 - 120}{10.3/\sqrt{30}} \approx 2.383$ (1pt); Calculates the p-value using $df = 29$, $P(t > 2.383) \approx 0.012$ (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value ($0.012$) to $\alpha$ ($0.05$). Since $0.012 < 0.05$, rejects $H_0$. Concludes there is convincing evidence that the new fertilizer increases the average yield beyond 120 bushels (1pt).

FRQ 3

A manufacturer wants to estimate the true mean lifespan (in hours) of a new type of light bulb with a 95% confidence interval.
a. Describe how increasing the sample size, while keeping the confidence level and sample standard deviation constant, would affect the width of the confidence interval.
b. Describe how increasing the confidence level from 90% to 99%, while keeping the sample size and sample standard deviation constant, would affect the width of the confidence interval.
c. If the manufacturer desires a narrower confidence interval without changing the confidence level, identify two ways this could be achieved.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States that increasing sample size decreases the standard error ($\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$) in the margin of error, thus decreasing the width of the confidence interval (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): States that increasing the confidence level requires a larger critical value ($t^*$), which increases the margin of error and thus increases the width of the confidence interval (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Identifies two ways: 1) Increase the sample size ($n$) (1pt); 2) Decrease the variability (sample standard deviation, $s$) in the population (1pt).

FRQ 4

A coffee company wants to estimate the true mean amount of coffee dispensed into their 12-ounce cups. A random sample of 25 cups is selected, and the mean amount dispensed is $\bar{x} = 11.8$ ounces with a standard deviation of $s = 0.5$ ounces.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure for this situation and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true mean amount of coffee dispensed into 12-ounce cups.
c. Interpret the confidence interval calculated in part (b) in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a one-sample t-interval for a population mean (1pt). Verifies conditions: Random sample (given), $n=25 < 30$ but assume population is approximately normal (central limit theorem condition for $t$-interval needs sample size $\ge 30$ or normal population, with $n=25$ it is usually assumed the population is normal, or a graph of the sample data shows no strong skewness/outliers) (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct degrees of freedom ($df=24$) and critical t-value (e.g., $t^* \approx 2.064$) (1pt). Calculates the interval $11.8 \pm 2.064 \left(\frac{0.5}{\sqrt{25}}\right) = 11.8 \pm 0.2064$, resulting in $(11.5936, 12.0064)$ (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the interval: "We are 95% confident that the true mean amount of coffee dispensed into 12-ounce cups is between $11.5936$ ounces and $12.0064$ ounces."

FRQ 5

A factory claims that the mean weight of their potato chip bags is 14 ounces. A quality control manager suspects the true mean weight is less than 14 ounces. She takes a random sample of 30 bags and finds a mean weight of $\bar{x} = 13.8$ ounces with a standard deviation of $s = 0.4$ ounces.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test and identify the appropriate test procedure.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and $p$-value for this test. Based on a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the quality control manager make? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States $H_0: \mu = 14$ and $H_a: \mu < 14$ (1pt). Identifies a one-sample t-test for a population mean (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions: Random sample (given), $n=30 \ge 30$ (sufficiently large for CLT for t-test) (1pt).
  • Part c (3pts): Calculates test statistic $t = \frac{13.8 - 14}{0.4/\sqrt{30}} \approx -2.739$ with $df=29$ (1pt). Calculates $p$-value $P(t < -2.739) \approx 0.0051$ (1pt). Compares $p$-value to $\alpha$ ($0.0051 < 0.05$) and concludes to reject $H_0$. Justifies that there is significant evidence that the true mean weight of potato chip bags is less than 14 ounces (1pt).
