AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 8: Inference for Categorical Data: Chi-Square (Set 4)

FRQ 1

A candy company claims that its bags of fruit-flavored candies contain 30% cherry, 20% orange, 20% lemon, 15% grape, and 15% lime. A random sample of 200 candies is taken from a newly manufactured batch, and the observed counts are shown in the table below.
FlavorObserved Count
Cherry70
Orange35
Lemon30
Grape30
Lime35
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for a test to determine if the distribution of candy flavors in this batch differs from the company's claim.
b. Verify the conditions for performing a chi-square goodness-of-fit test.
c. Calculate the expected count for cherry-flavored candies and then the chi-square test statistic for this sample.
d. Based on your results, justify a conclusion, in context, about the company's claim at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states null and alternative hypotheses in context.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies all conditions (random sample, expected counts $\ge 5$, independence) and describes how they are met.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct expected count for cherry-flavored candies ($200 \times 0.30 = 60$) AND correctly calculates the chi-square test statistic ($X^2 \approx 9.79$).
  • Part d (2pts): States a correct p-value (or range, e.g., $0.025 < p < 0.05$ or $p \approx 0.044$), makes a correct decision to reject $H_0$, and justifies the decision with statistical reasoning in context.

FRQ 2

A school administrator wants to investigate if the distribution of preferred study locations (library, home, coffee shop) is different for high school students and college students. Independent random samples of 150 high school students and 180 college students were surveyed, and their preferred study locations were recorded. The results are summarized in the two-way table below.
Study LocationHigh SchoolCollegeTotal
Library5060110
Home6570135
Coffee Shop355085
Total150180330
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for an appropriate chi-square test.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this test.
c. Calculate the expected count for college students who prefer studying at home.
d. The chi-square test statistic for this data is $X^2 = 7.85$ with a p-value of $0.02$. Based on these results, justify a conclusion, in context, about the preferred study locations for the two groups at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses for homogeneity in context ($H_0:$ The distribution of preferred study locations is the same for high school and college students. $H_a:$ The distribution of preferred study locations is different for high school and college students).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies all conditions (independent random samples, expected counts $\ge 5$, independence/10% condition) and describes how they are met.
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates the correct expected count for college students who prefer studying at home ($\frac{135}{330} \times 180 \approx 73.64$).
  • Part d (2pts): Makes a correct decision to reject $H_0$ (since $0.02 < 0.05$) and justifies the decision with statistical reasoning in context, concluding there is significant evidence of a difference in distributions.

FRQ 3

A random sample of 400 adults was selected, and their smoking status and respiratory illness status were recorded. The results are summarized in the two-way table below.
Illness PresentIllness AbsentTotal

a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for a chi-square test to investigate if there is an association between smoking status and respiratory illness.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this chi-square test for independence.

FRQ 4

A company that produces fruit snacks claims that its large bags contain an equal proportion of five different fruit flavors: cherry, orange, lemon, lime, and grape. A consumer group suspects that the proportions are not equal. To test this, they randomly selected a large bag of fruit snacks and counted the number of each flavor. The observed counts were: Cherry (58), Orange (42), Lemon (47), Lime (65), Grape (38). The total number of fruit snacks in the bag was 250.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for an appropriate chi-square test.
b. Calculate the expected count for each flavor if the company's claim is true.
c. Verify the conditions for performing this chi-square test.
d. Calculate the chi-square test statistic and the associated p-value.
e. Based on the p-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the consumer group draw about the company's claim? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0: p_{cherry} = p_{orange} = p_{lemon} = p_{lime} = p_{grape} = 0.20$ and $H_a:$ At least one proportion is different from 0.20.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates expected counts for each flavor as $250 \times 0.20 = 50$.
  • Part c (1pt): Verifies Random sample (stated), Independence (implied by random sample from large population), and Expected counts are all $\ge 5$ (all are 50).
  • Part d (1pt): Calculates $\chi^2 = \frac{(58-50)^2}{50} + \frac{(42-50)^2}{50} + \frac{(47-50)^2}{50} + \frac{(65-50)^2}{50} + \frac{(38-50)^2}{50} = 10.96$. Degrees of freedom $df = 5-1 = 4$. Finds p-value using $\chi^2=10.96$ with $df=4$, $p \approx 0.027$.
  • Part e (1pt): Compares $p$-value ($0.027$) to $\alpha$ ($0.05$). Since $p < \alpha$, rejects $H_0$. Concludes there is sufficient evidence that the proportions of fruit snack flavors are not equal.

FRQ 5

A large company wants to determine if job satisfaction differs among employees in three different departments: Sales, Marketing, and Operations. A random sample of 100 employees from each department was surveyed. Each employee reported their job satisfaction as "Satisfied," "Neutral," or "Dissatisfied." The results are summarized in the table below.
SatisfactionSalesMarketingOperationsTotal
Satisfied657060195
Neutral20202565
Dissatisfied15101540
Total100100100300
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for an appropriate chi-square test.
b. Identify the name of the appropriate chi-square test and justify your choice.
c. Calculate the expected number of Sales employees who are "Neutral" if there is no difference in job satisfaction among departments.
d. Verify the conditions for performing this chi-square test.
e. The calculated chi-square test statistic is $15.20$ with a p-value of $0.004$. Based on these results and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what conclusion should the company draw? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0:$ There is no difference in the distribution of job satisfaction among the Sales, Marketing, and Operations departments. AND $H_a:$ There is a difference in the distribution of job satisfaction among the Sales, Marketing, and Operations departments.
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies "Chi-square test for homogeneity." Justification: Compares the distribution of a categorical variable (job satisfaction) across independent samples from multiple populations (departments).
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates the total number of neutral employees ($20+20+25=65$). Total surveyed is 300. Expected count for Sales-Neutral is $\frac{100 \times 65}{300} \approx 21.67$.
  • Part d (1pt): Verifies conditions: Random samples (stated), Independence (employees within departments are independent; samples from departments are independent), Expected counts are all $\ge 5$ (all expected counts are checked and are $\ge 5$).
  • Part e (1pt): Compares $p$-value ($0.004$) to $\alpha$ ($0.01$). Since $p < \alpha$, rejects $H_0$. Concludes there is sufficient evidence to suggest that there is a difference in job satisfaction among the Sales, Marketing, and Operations departments.
