FRQ 3

A study investigated the relationship between the average daily temperature (in degrees Fahrenheit) and the number of ice cream cones sold at a local shop. For a sample of 10 days, the following summary statistics were calculated: $\bar{x} = 75^\circ F$, $s_x = 8^\circ F$, $\bar{y} = 150$ cones, $s_y = 30$ cones. The sum of the products of the standardized values is $\sum z_x z_y = 7.2$.
a. Calculate the correlation coefficient, $r$, between the average daily temperature and the number of ice cream cones sold.
b. Interpret the meaning of this correlation coefficient in the context of the problem.
c. Would it be appropriate to use the correlation coefficient to describe the relationship if a scatterplot revealed a strong curved pattern? Explain why or why not.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates $r = \frac{\sum z_x z_y}{n-1} = \frac{7.2}{10-1} = \frac{7.2}{9} = 0.8$.
  • Part b (2pts): Interprets $r=0.8$ as a strong, positive linear association between average daily temperature and the number of ice cream cones sold.
  • Part c (1pt): States that it would not be appropriate, and explains that correlation only measures the strength and direction of *linear* relationships, and would not accurately describe a curved pattern.

FRQ 4

A marine biologist is studying the relationship between the length (in cm) and weight (in grams) of a certain species of fish. For a sample of 12 fish with lengths ranging from 40 cm to 70 cm, the least-squares regression line was determined to be $\hat{y} = -240 + 5.5x$, where $x$ is the length in cm and $\hat{y}$ is the predicted weight in grams. The correlation coefficient was found to be $r = 0.92$.
a. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
b. Predict the weight of a fish that is 60 cm long using the given regression line.
c. Is it appropriate to use this regression model to predict the weight of a fish that is 10 cm long? Explain your reasoning.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Interprets the slope ($5.5$) as: for every 1 cm increase in fish length, the predicted weight increases by $5.5$ grams, on average.
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates the predicted weight: $\hat{y} = -240 + 5.5(60) = -240 + 330 = 90$ grams.
  • Part c (2pts): States that it is not appropriate (or likely not appropriate) because 10 cm is outside the range of observed lengths used to build the model (extrapolation), which might lead to unreliable or inaccurate predictions.

FRQ 5

A company analyzed the relationship between the amount of money spent on advertising (in thousands of dollars) and the resulting sales (in millions of dollars) for their new product over 10 months. The least-squares regression line was calculated as $\hat{S} = 0.5 + 2.0A$, where $A$ is advertising spending and $S$ is sales.
a. For one month, the company spent $A = \$3$ thousand on advertising and had actual sales of $S = \$7.2$ million. Calculate the residual for this month.
b. A residual plot for the 10 months showed a distinct U-shaped pattern. What does this pattern suggest about the appropriateness of a linear model for these data?
c. If a specific month's data point ($A=8, S=25$) was found to significantly increase the slope of the regression line and reduce the $R^2$ value when included in the analysis, how would you classify this point? Justify your classification.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates the predicted sales $\hat{S} = 0.5 + 2.0(3) = 6.5$ million dollars. Then calculates the residual $e = S - \hat{S} = 7.2 - 6.5 = 0.7$ million dollars.
  • Part b (2pts): Explains that a U-shaped pattern in the residual plot indicates that a linear model is not appropriate, as there is a non-linear pattern remaining in the residuals, suggesting a curved relationship between advertising and sales.
  • Part c (2pts): Classifies the point as an influential point. Justifies by explaining that its inclusion significantly changes the slope of the regression line and affects the overall fit ($R^2$), which are characteristics of influential points.
