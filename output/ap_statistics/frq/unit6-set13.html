AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 13)

FRQ 1

A marketing firm is testing two different designs for a new advertisement (Design A and Design B) to see which one leads to a higher proportion of potential customers remembering the product. They randomly assign 300 people to view Design A and 350 people to view Design B. After viewing, participants are asked if they remember the product. The results are summarized in the table below.
DesignNumber of ViewersNumber Remembering Product
A300200
B350210
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions of potential customers who remember the product (Design A - Design B).
c. Interpret the confidence interval you calculated in part (b) in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies two-sample z-interval for proportions (1pt) and verifies conditions (random, independence, Large Counts for both groups) (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates the correct 90% confidence interval (approx. (0.005, 0.129)) (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets the interval in context (1pt)

FRQ 2

A polling agency wants to estimate the proportion of registered voters in a city who support a proposed new city park. They take a simple random sample of 500 registered voters and find that 280 of them support the new park.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the population proportion.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of registered voters who support the new city park.
c. Interpret the 95% confidence interval from part (b) in the context of the problem.
d. Explain how the margin of error would change if the polling agency used a larger sample size, assuming the sample proportion and confidence level remained the same.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample z-interval for proportion and verifies Random, Normal (n*p_hat >= 10, n*(1-p_hat) >= 10), and Independence (10% condition) conditions.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the 95% confidence interval (shows formula, calculations, and interval).
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context, stating it's for the true proportion and mentioning confidence level.
  • Part d (1pt): Explains that a larger sample size would decrease the margin of error.

FRQ 3

A company claims that 80% of its customers are satisfied with their service. A consumer watchdog group wants to investigate this claim because they suspect the true proportion is lower. They take a simple random sample of 150 customers and find that 105 of them are satisfied.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this investigation.
b. Verify the conditions for performing a significance test for a population proportion.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value calculated in part (c) and a significance level of α = 0.05, what conclusion should the consumer watchdog group make regarding the company's claim? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states null (p=0.80) and alternative (p<0.80) hypotheses with parameter defined.
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies Random, Normal (n*p_0 >= 10, n*(1-p_0) >= 10), and Independence (10% condition) conditions.
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the z-test statistic and the corresponding p-value.
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to alpha, states whether to reject/fail to reject H_0, and draws a correct conclusion in context.

FRQ 4

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug for a common ailment. The null hypothesis is that the drug has no effect (proportion of patients who improve is p=0.50), and the alternative hypothesis is that the drug is effective (proportion of patients who improve is p>0.50). The company performs a significance test.
a. Describe a Type I error in this context and explain a potential consequence of making this error for the pharmaceutical company.
b. Describe a Type II error in this context and explain a potential consequence of making this error for the pharmaceutical company.
c. Explain how increasing the sample size, while keeping the significance level (α) constant, would affect the probability of a Type II error.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes Type I error (reject H_0 when H_0 is true) in context and states a reasonable consequence.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly describes Type II error (fail to reject H_0 when H_a is true) in context and states a reasonable consequence.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that increasing sample size would decrease the probability of a Type II error because the test has more power to detect a true effect.

FRQ 5

Researchers conducted an experiment to compare the effectiveness of two different types of plant fertilizers, Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B, on a particular crop. They randomly assigned 150 plants to receive Fertilizer A and 130 plants to receive Fertilizer B. After a growing season, they recorded the number of plants that successfully grew (produced a harvestable crop) for each fertilizer. The results are shown in the table below.
FertilizerPlants GrownTotal Plants
A120150
B95130
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the difference between the true proportions of plants that successfully grow using Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B (p_A - p_B).
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions (p_A - p_B).
c. Interpret the 90% confidence interval from part (b) in context and state what it suggests about the effectiveness of the two fertilizers.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies two-sample z-interval for proportions and verifies Random assignment, Normal (n*p_hat >= 10, n*(1-p_hat) >= 10 for both groups), and Independence (within groups and between groups) conditions.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the 90% confidence interval (shows formula, calculations, and interval).
  • Part c (2pts): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context, explaining it's for the true difference and mentioning confidence level, and states whether there is evidence of a difference in effectiveness based on the interval containing or not containing 0.
