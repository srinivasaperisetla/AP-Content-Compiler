AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 15)

FRQ 1

A large fast-food chain wants to estimate the proportion of customers who prefer their new plant-based burger over their traditional beef burger. They conduct a random sample of 250 customers, and 145 indicate a preference for the plant-based burger.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure for constructing a confidence interval for the true proportion of customers who prefer the plant-based burger, and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 95 percent confidence interval for the true proportion of customers who prefer the plant-based burger.
c. Interpret the calculated 95 percent confidence interval in the context of the problem.
d. The marketing department claims that more than half of all customers prefer the new plant-based burger. Based on your confidence interval, does this claim appear plausible? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a one-sample z-interval for a population proportion AND verifies conditions (randomization, $n\hat{p} \ge 10$, $n(1-\hat{p}) \ge 10$, 10% condition).
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates the correct sample proportion ($\hat{p} = 145/250 = 0.58$), standard error, and confidence interval $(0.519, 0.641)$ with appropriate work shown.
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context (e.g., "We are 95% confident that the true proportion of all customers who prefer the plant-based burger is between 0.519 and 0.641.").
  • Part d (1pt): States that the claim is plausible and justifies by noting that 0.50 is below the lower bound of the interval.

FRQ 2

A manufacturer claims that no more than 10 percent of their newly produced electronic components are defective. A quality control manager suspects the defect rate is actually higher. To test this, she selects a random sample of 300 components and finds 39 defective components.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for the manager's suspicion. Identify the appropriate inference method to test these hypotheses.
b. Verify the conditions for conducting this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the quality control manager draw? Justify your answer in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States $H_0: p = 0.10$ and $H_a: p > 0.10$ (or equivalent) AND identifies a one-sample z-test for a population proportion.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions: Random sample (given), $np_0 = 300(0.10)=30 \ge 10$, $n(1-p_0) = 300(0.90)=270 \ge 10$, and 10% condition (assuming a very large production).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the sample proportion ($\hat{p} = 39/300 = 0.13$). Calculates the test statistic $z = \frac{0.13 - 0.10}{\sqrt{\frac{0.10(0.90)}{300}}} \approx 1.732$. Calculates the p-value $P(Z > 1.732) \approx 0.0416$.
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ ($0.0416 < 0.05$). Concludes to reject $H_0$ and justifies in context (e.g., "There is sufficient evidence to conclude that the true proportion of defective components is greater than 10 percent.").

FRQ 3

A medical researcher wants to compare the effectiveness of two different medications, Medication X and Medication Y, in preventing migraines. A random sample of 200 patients received Medication X, and 120 of them reported a significant reduction in migraine frequency. Another independent random sample of 180 patients received Medication Y, and 90 of them reported a significant reduction. The researcher wants to determine if Medication X is more effective than Medication Y.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to investigate the researcher's claim. Identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for conducting this significance test for the difference between two population proportions.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test. Assume Medication X is population 1 and Medication Y is population 2.
d. Based on the p-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what conclusion should the researcher draw? Justify your answer in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States $H_0: p_X - p_Y = 0$ (or $p_X = p_Y$) and $H_a: p_X - p_Y > 0$ (or $p_X > p_Y$) AND identifies a two-sample z-test for a difference in population proportions.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions for both samples: Random samples (given), independence (given/implied), and large counts for both success and failure for each group ($n_X \hat{p}_X = 120 \ge 10, n_X(1-\hat{p}_X) = 80 \ge 10$; $n_Y \hat{p}_Y = 90 \ge 10, n_Y(1-\hat{p}_Y) = 90 \ge 10$). Also mentions 10% condition for populations if applicable.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates sample proportions ($\hat{p}_X = 120/200 = 0.60$, $\hat{p}_Y = 90/180 = 0.50$). Calculates pooled proportion $\hat{p}_c = (120+90)/(200+180) = 210/380 \approx 0.5526$. Calculates test statistic $z = \frac{(0.60 - 0.50)}{\sqrt{0.5526(0.4474)(\frac{1}{200} + \frac{1}{180})}} \approx 1.96$. Calculates p-value $P(Z > 1.96) \approx 0.025$.
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ ($0.025 > 0.01$). Concludes to fail to reject $H_0$ and justifies in context (e.g., "Since the p-value (0.025) is greater than $\alpha$ (0.01), we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is not sufficient evidence to conclude that Medication X is more effective than Medication Y in preventing migraines.").

FRQ 4

A manufacturer of cell phone batteries claims that no more than 5 percent of their batteries are defective. A consumer watchdog group believes the proportion of defective batteries is higher. They plan to conduct a significance test with $H_0: p = 0.05$ versus $H_a: p > 0.05$ at a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$.
a. Describe a Type I error and a Type II error in the context of this problem and the potential consequences of each.
b. The group decides to reduce the significance level to $\alpha = 0.01$. Explain how this change would affect the probability of a Type I error and the probability of a Type II error.
c. Besides changing the significance level, identify one other factor that would increase the power of the test to detect a higher defect rate, and explain why it has this effect.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes Type I error (rejecting $H_0$ when $H_0$ is true: concluding defect rate is higher than 5% when it's not) and its consequence (e.g., unfairly damages manufacturer's reputation, costly recall). Correctly describes Type II error (failing to reject $H_0$ when $H_a$ is true: concluding defect rate is not higher than 5% when it is) and its consequence (e.g., consumers get defective batteries, loss of consumer trust, safety issues).
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that reducing $\alpha$ to 0.01 would decrease the probability of a Type I error (because it requires stronger evidence to reject $H_0$). Explains that this would increase the probability of a Type II error (as it's harder to reject $H_0$ even when $H_a$ is true).
  • Part c (1pt): Identifies an increased sample size (e.g., sampling more batteries) AND explains that a larger sample size reduces the standard error, making the test more sensitive to detect a true difference, thus increasing power.

FRQ 5

A research study investigated the effectiveness of two different fertilizers, Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B, on the germination rate of a particular type of seed. Two independent random samples of 200 seeds each were treated with one of the fertilizers. The results are summarized in the table below.
FertilizerNumber of Seeds GerminatedTotal Seeds
A172200
B158200
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses to investigate if there is a significant difference in the germination rates between Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B.
b. Verify the conditions for performing a two-sample $z$-test for a difference in population proportions.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your results and using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what conclusion should the researchers make? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Let $p_A$ be the true germination rate for Fertilizer A and $p_B$ be the true germination rate for Fertilizer B. $H_0: p_A - p_B = 0$ (or $p_A = p_B$) and $H_a: p_A - p_B \ne 0$ (or $p_A \ne p_B$).
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies independent random samples. Checks large counts for both groups: $n_A\hat{p}_A = 172 \ge 10$, $n_A(1-\hat{p}_A) = 28 \ge 10$, $n_B\hat{p}_B = 158 \ge 10$, $n_B(1-\hat{p}_B) = 42 \ge 10$. Verifies 10% condition for both samples (sample sizes 200 are less than 10% of all possible seeds for each fertilizer).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $\hat{p}_A = 172/200 = 0.86$, $\hat{p}_B = 158/200 = 0.79$. Calculates pooled proportion $\hat{p}_c = (172+158)/(200+200) = 330/400 = 0.825$. Test statistic $z = \frac{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B}{\sqrt{\hat{p}_c(1-\hat{p}_c)(\frac{1}{n_A} + \frac{1}{n_B})}} = \frac{0.86 - 0.79}{\sqrt{0.825(0.175)(\frac{1}{200} + \frac{1}{200})}} \approx 1.84$. P-value for two-sided test $2 \times P(Z > 1.84) \approx 2 \times 0.0329 = 0.0658$.
  • Part d (1pt): Concludes that since the p-value $0.0658 > \alpha = 0.01$, there is not sufficient evidence to reject the null hypothesis. The researchers should conclude that there is not convincing evidence of a significant difference in the germination rates between Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B.
