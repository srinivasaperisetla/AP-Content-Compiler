FRQ 2

A recent study investigated the relationship between the daily average temperature (in degrees Celsius) and the number of glasses of iced tea sold at a local cafe. The correlation coefficient between these two variables was calculated as $r = 0.82$.
a. Interpret the value of the correlation coefficient in the context of this study.
b. If the temperature were measured in degrees Fahrenheit instead of degrees Celsius, what would be the value of the correlation coefficient? Explain your reasoning.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States there is a strong, positive linear relationship (1pt); States the relationship is between daily average temperature and number of glasses of iced tea sold (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): States the correlation coefficient would remain $0.82$ (1pt); Explains that correlation is not affected by changes in units of measurement or linear transformations (1pt).

FRQ 3

A forest ranger collected data on the height (in meters) and the diameter at breast height (DBH, in centimeters) of 15 trees of a certain species. A least-squares regression analysis was performed, yielding the following results:$$\hat{\text{Height}} = 0.52 + 0.65 \times \text{DBH}$$The standard deviation of the residuals was $s = 1.2$ meters. The correlation coefficient was $r = 0.88$.
a. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
b. Predict the height of a tree with a DBH of $20$ centimeters.
c. Interpret the value of $r^2$ for this model.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Explains that for every additional $1$ cm increase in DBH, the predicted tree height increases by $0.65$ meters (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Shows calculation $\hat{\text{Height}} = 0.52 + 0.65(20) = 13.52$ meters and states the predicted height (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): States $r^2 = (0.88)^2 = 0.7744$ or $77.44\%$ (1pt); Interprets that $77.44\%$ of the variation in tree height can be explained by the linear relationship with DBH (1pt).

FRQ 4

An ecologist studied the relationship between the biomass (in kg) of a certain plant species and the soil phosphorus content (in mg/kg) in 12 different plots. The scatterplot revealed a strong curved relationship. To linearize the relationship, the ecologist transformed the biomass data using a natural logarithm. The new linear regression model is:$$\widehat{\ln(\text{Biomass})} = 1.8 + 0.05 \times \text{Phosphorus}$$The original scatterplot also showed one point that appeared to deviate significantly from the general pattern.
a. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of the transformed variables.
b. Predict the biomass for a plot with $50$ mg/kg of soil phosphorus content. Show your work.
c. Suppose that an additional plot was found with extremely high soil phosphorus content ($150$ mg/kg) but a relatively low plant biomass. Would this point likely be an influential point? Explain why or why not.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Explains that for every $1$ mg/kg increase in soil phosphorus, the predicted natural logarithm of biomass increases by $0.05$ (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $\widehat{\ln(\text{Biomass})} = 1.8 + 0.05(50) = 4.3$ (1pt); Calculates predicted biomass as $e^{4.3} \approx 73.7$ kg (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): States that the point would likely be influential (1pt); Explains that influential points are outliers in the x-direction and pull the regression line towards them, affecting the slope and/or y-intercept (1pt).

FRQ 5

A researcher is studying the relationship between the number of hours students spend studying for a test and their score on the test. Data for 10 students are shown in the scatterplot.
a. Describe the relationship between study hours and test scores shown in the scatterplot in terms of direction, form, and strength.
b. Based on the scatterplot, estimate the correlation coefficient, $r$, for these data. Justify your estimate.
c. Interpret the meaning of the correlation coefficient in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (moderately strong or strong).
  • Part b (1pt): Estimates $r$ between 0.6 and 0.9 (inclusive) with a reasonable justification (e.g., "points generally follow a line but with some scatter").
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets $r$ as the strength and direction of the linear association between study hours and test scores.
