AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data: Slopes (Set 19)

FRQ 1

A company produces a new brand of energy-efficient light bulbs. Researchers want to investigate the relationship between the number of hours a bulb is used per day and its lifespan in months. They collected data from a random sample of 25 bulbs and fit a linear regression model. The regression output reported a sample slope of $\hat{b} = -0.55$ months per hour, with a standard error of $SE_{\hat{b}} = 0.12$ months per hour. The average daily usage ranged from 2 to 10 hours. A scatterplot of the data showed a linear pattern, and a residuals plot showed no obvious pattern.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure for this situation and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line relating daily usage to lifespan. Show your work.
c. Interpret the confidence interval calculated in part (b) in the context of the problem.
d. Based on this confidence interval, does the data provide convincing evidence that for each additional hour of daily use, the expected lifespan of the bulb decreases by more than half a month? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies t-interval for slope (1pt). Verifies conditions for inference for the slope of a regression model (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates degrees of freedom and identifies correct $t^*$ value (1pt). Correctly calculates the 95% confidence interval (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Compares the value $-0.5$ (or equivalent to "more than half a month decrease") to the confidence interval and justifies the conclusion (1pt).

FRQ 2

A health researcher is studying the relationship between the amount of sleep (in hours) a student gets per night and their performance on a standardized test (score out of 100). A random sample of 30 students was selected. The least-squares regression line for predicting test score from sleep hours was found to be $\hat{y} = 25.1 + 7.3x$, where $x$ is sleep hours. The standard error of the slope coefficient was $SE_{\hat{b}} = 1.8$.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a test to determine if there is a positive linear relationship between sleep hours and test scores. Identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for the hypotheses stated in part (a). Show your work.
d. Based on the p-value from part (c), what conclusion should the researcher make about the relationship between sleep hours and test scores? Use a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States correct null hypothesis ($H_0: \beta = 0$) and alternative hypothesis ($H_a: \beta > 0$) (1pt). Identifies the appropriate inference method (t-test for slope) (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies all four conditions for inference (Linearity, Independence, Normal residuals, Equal variance) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic (1pt). Finds or approximates the correct p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Compares the p-value to $\alpha = 0.05$ and states a correct conclusion in context (1pt).

FRQ 3

A study investigated the effect of temperature (in degrees Celsius) on the growth rate of a specific type of plant (in mm/day). Data from a controlled experiment resulted in a linear regression model for predicting growth rate from temperature. The estimated slope coefficient was $\hat{b} = 0.35$ mm/day per degree Celsius, with a standard error $SE_{\hat{b}} = 0.08$. The sample size was $n=22$.
a. Calculate a 99% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line. Show your work.
b. Interpret the confidence interval from part (a) in the context of this study.
c. A plant biologist claims that for every 1-degree Celsius increase in temperature, the plant's growth rate increases by more than 0.2 mm/day. Based on your confidence interval, is there convincing evidence to support this claim? Justify your answer.
d. Briefly describe how you would verify the condition of "equal variance of residuals" for this regression model if you had access to the full dataset.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly determines $df = n-2 = 20$ and finds the appropriate $t^*$ value (1pt). Calculates the 99% confidence interval correctly (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Compares the value 0.2 to the confidence interval and justifies the conclusion based on whether 0.2 is in or out of the interval (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Describes examining a residuals plot for a relatively constant spread of points around zero (1pt).

FRQ 4

A forester is studying the relationship between the age of a certain type of tree (in years) and its diameter at breast height (DBH, in inches). Data were collected from 20 trees. The least-squares regression line for predicting DBH from age is $\widehat{DBH} = 2.5 + 0.6(\text{Age})$. The 95% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line is $(0.48, 0.72)$. A scatterplot of the data shows a clear positive linear association, but with increasing variability in DBH as age increases.
a. Based on the description, describe what the scatterplot suggests about the relationship between tree age and DBH.
b. Interpret the 95% confidence interval for the slope in the context of this problem.
c. A claim is made that, on average, for every 10-year increase in age, the DBH of this type of tree increases by at least 6 inches. Based on the confidence interval, justify whether this claim is supported.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Describes a positive linear association between tree age and DBH, and notes increasing variability in DBH for older trees (heteroscedasticity) (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): States "We are 95% confident that for every one-year increase in a tree's age, the true mean DBH increases by between 0.48 inches and 0.72 inches." (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): For every 10-year increase, the expected increase in DBH is between $10 \times 0.48 = 4.8$ inches and $10 \times 0.72 = 7.2$ inches (1pt). Since the entire interval (4.8 to 7.2 inches) includes values less than 6 inches, the claim that the DBH increases by *at least* 6 inches is not fully supported, because it is plausible for the increase to be less than 6 inches (1pt).

FRQ 5

A researcher wants to study the relationship between the amount of fertilizer (in grams) applied to a plant and its subsequent growth (in centimeters) over a month. They randomly select 25 plants and apply varying amounts of fertilizer. The least-squares regression line relating plant growth ($y$) to fertilizer amount ($x$) is $\hat{y} = 5.2 + 0.8x$. The standard error of the slope coefficient is $SE_b = 0.15$. Assume all conditions for inference are met for parts (c) and (d).
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure to construct a confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line.
b. State the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the slope of a regression model. Are these conditions met for parts (c) and (d)?
c. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line.
d. Interpret the calculated confidence interval in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a t-interval for the slope of a regression line (or a linear regression t-interval).
  • Part b (2pts): States the 5 conditions (Linearity, Independent observations, Normal residuals, Equal standard deviation of residuals, Random sample/assignment) and confirms they are met as stated in the prompt for parts (c) and (d).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the confidence interval using $b \pm t^*SE_b$. Degrees of freedom $df = n-2 = 23$. For 95% confidence, $t^*_{23} \approx 2.069$. Interval: $0.8 \pm 2.069(0.15) = 0.8 \pm 0.31035 = (0.48965, 1.11035)$.
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets the interval as: "We are 95% confident that for every additional gram of fertilizer applied, the true mean plant growth will increase by between 0.49 cm and 1.11 cm."
