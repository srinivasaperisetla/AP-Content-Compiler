AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 11)

FRQ 1

A survey asked 200 students at a high school about their preferred after-school activity. The results are summarized in the table below.
ActivityNumber of Students
Sports70
Arts & Crafts35
Video Games55
Volunteering20
Other20
a. Construct a relative frequency table for the preferred after-school activities.
b. Create a bar chart to display the distribution of preferred after-school activities.
c. Suppose the same survey was conducted at a middle school and showed a much higher preference for "Video Games" (60%) compared to the high school. Compare the distributions of preferred after-school activities between the high school and the middle school.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates and presents relative frequencies in a table for all categories.
  • Part b (2pts): Creates a correctly labeled bar chart with appropriate scales and bars corresponding to the frequencies or relative frequencies.
  • Part c (2pts): Compares the distributions by noting the higher proportion for "Video Games" at the middle school and at least one other difference (e.g., lower proportion for other activities, or identifying the most preferred activity at each school).

FRQ 2

A researcher recorded the number of minutes 25 randomly selected customers spent waiting in line at a grocery store during peak hours. The data are as follows: $2, 5, 1, 7, 3, 4, 6, 2, 8, 5, 1, 9, 3, 6, 4, 7, 2, 5, 1, 10, 3, 6, 8, 4, 7$.
a. Construct a histogram to display the distribution of waiting times. Use bin widths of 2 minutes, starting at 0 minutes.
b. Describe the shape, center, and variability of the distribution of waiting times.
c. Based on your description in part (b), explain whether the mean or the median would be a more appropriate measure of the typical waiting time for these customers.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Constructs a correctly labeled histogram with appropriate scales and correct frequencies for each bin. (1pt for correct bins/frequencies, 1pt for labels/scales).
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the shape (e.g., skewed right, roughly symmetric), center (approximate median/mean), and variability (range or spread) in context.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that the median is more appropriate if the distribution is skewed or has outliers, or the mean is appropriate if it's roughly symmetric, justifying the choice based on the shape identified in part (b).

FRQ 4

A real estate agent recorded the selling prices (in thousands of dollars) of 10 houses sold in a particular neighborhood during the last month: $210, 225, 230, 240, 250, 260, 275, 280, 290, 750$.
a. Calculate the mean and median selling price for these 10 houses.
b. Determine if there are any outliers in this data set using the $1.5 \times IQR$ rule. Show your calculations.
c. Explain which measure of center (mean or median) would be a more appropriate representation of the typical selling price of houses in this neighborhood, and justify your choice.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates both the mean and the median selling price.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates Q1, Q3, and IQR, sets up the outlier fences (Lower Fence = $Q_1 - 1.5 \times IQR$, Upper Fence = $Q_3 + 1.5 \times IQR$), and correctly identifies any outliers with justification.
  • Part c (2pts): States which measure of center is more appropriate and justifies the choice by explaining the effect of the outlier(s) on the mean versus the median.

FRQ 5

The weights of adult females in a certain country are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 145$ pounds and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 20$ pounds.
a. Calculate the $z$-score for an adult female weighing 170 pounds. Interpret this $z$-score in the context of the problem.
b. Determine the proportion of adult females in this country who weigh between 130 pounds and 160 pounds.
c. If a sample of 100 adult females from this country showed a distribution of weights that was noticeably skewed to the right, describe how this sample distribution would differ from the assumed normal distribution in terms of its shape and relative position of mean and median.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the $z$-score ($z = \frac{170-145}{20} = 1.25$) and provides a correct interpretation (e.g., 170 pounds is 1.25 standard deviations above the mean weight).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the $z$-scores for both 130 and 160 pounds, and determines the correct proportion using a normal probability calculation (e.g., using a calculator or table).
  • Part c (2pts): Describes that a right-skewed distribution has a longer tail to the right and that the mean would be greater than the median for a right-skewed distribution, differentiating it from a symmetric normal distribution where mean $\approx$ median.
