AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data: Slopes (Set 5)

FRQ 1

A researcher wants to investigate the relationship between the number of hours students spend studying per week and their final exam scores in a challenging course. A random sample of 25 students from a large university was selected, and data on their study hours and exam scores were collected. A linear regression model was fitted to the data, and the least-squares regression line was $\hat{y} = 55 + 2.5x$, where $x$ is the study hours and $y$ is the exam score. The standard error of the slope, $SE_b$, was calculated to be $0.65$. The conditions for inference were checked and found to be met.
a. Identify the appropriate inference method to construct a confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line.
c. Interpret the calculated confidence interval in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a t-interval for the slope of a regression line (or a t-interval for regression coefficients).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct degrees of freedom ($df=25-2=23$) and critical t-value (approx. $t^*=2.069$). Correctly calculates the confidence interval $2.5 \pm 2.069(0.65)$, which is $(1.15, 3.85)$.
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the interval: We are 95% confident that for each additional hour a student studies per week, the mean final exam score increases by between 1.15 and 3.85 points.

FRQ 2

A horticulturalist is investigating whether the amount of a new organic fertilizer applied to tomato plants affects their yield. They apply varying amounts of fertilizer to 30 randomly selected tomato plants and record the total weight of tomatoes produced by each plant. A linear relationship is suspected.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for a test to determine if there is a linear relationship between fertilizer amount and tomato yield.
b. Identify the appropriate inference method for carrying out this test.
c. Describe the conditions that must be met to perform this inference method.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States $H_0: \beta = 0$ (no linear relationship) and $H_a: \beta \ne 0$ (a linear relationship exists), where $\beta$ is the true slope of the population regression line relating fertilizer amount to tomato yield.
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies a t-test for the slope of a regression line (or a t-test for regression coefficients).
  • Part c (2pts): Describes the four conditions: Linear (true relationship is linear), Independent (observations are independent, often random sample/assignment), Normal (residuals are normally distributed), Equal variance (variance of residuals is constant across $x$ values).

FRQ 3

A study investigated the relationship between the number of minutes spent exercising daily and a person's systolic blood pressure reduction over a month. Data from 40 participants yielded a least-squares regression line of $\hat{y} = 10 - 0.25x$, where $x$ is daily exercise minutes and $y$ is blood pressure reduction. The standard error of the slope was $SE_b = 0.1$. The degrees of freedom for the test were $df=38$.
a. Calculate the test statistic for a significance test to determine if there is a negative linear association between daily exercise minutes and blood pressure reduction.
b. Assuming the p-value for the test is $0.009$, interpret this p-value in context.
c. Based on the p-value from part (b) and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion can be drawn about the relationship between daily exercise minutes and blood pressure reduction? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates the test statistic: $t = \frac{b - \beta_0}{SE_b} = \frac{-0.25 - 0}{0.1} = -2.5$.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly interprets the p-value: If there were truly no linear relationship between daily exercise minutes and blood pressure reduction, there would be a 0.9% chance of observing a sample slope as extreme as or more extreme than -0.25 (or a test statistic as extreme as or more extreme than -2.5) by random chance.
  • Part c (2pts): Since the p-value ($0.009$) is less than $\alpha = 0.05$, we reject the null hypothesis. We have convincing evidence to conclude that there is a negative linear association between daily exercise minutes and blood pressure reduction.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): The confidence interval for Data Set 2 (sample size 50) is narrower than for Data Set 1 (sample size 20). This is because a larger sample size generally leads to a smaller standard error of the slope, resulting in a narrower confidence interval.
  • Part b (2pts): The 95% confidence interval for the true slope from Data Set 2 is $(0.64, 0.96)$. Since all values in this interval are greater than 0.5, we have convincing evidence at the 95% confidence level that the true mean increase in sales for every additional $1,000$ spent on advertising is greater than 0.5 thousand units. Thus, the company's claim is supported.

a. Describe the overall pattern of the relationship between hours of sleep and reaction time shown in the scatterplot.
b. Identify a question suggested by the variation in the scatterplot that could be investigated with further statistical analysis.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): There appears to be a negative, somewhat linear relationship, meaning as hours of sleep increase, reaction time tends to decrease. The strength appears moderate.
