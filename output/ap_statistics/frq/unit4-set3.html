AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 4: Probability, Random Variables, and Probability Distributions (Set 3)

FRQ 1

A local bakery claims that 80% of its customers purchase their popular sourdough bread. To verify this claim, a statistician decides to simulate the purchases of 10 customers.
a. Describe a simulation process using a random number generator to estimate the probability that exactly 8 out of 10 customers purchase sourdough bread, assuming the bakery's claim is true.
b. Using the random digits below, perform two trials of your simulation process from part (a) and report the number of customers who purchase sourdough bread in each trial. Random digits: $37059 \ 08200 \ 19301 \ 82436 \ 02947 \ 42686 \ 20689 \ 12693$.
c. Based on your two trials, estimate the probability that exactly 8 out of 10 customers purchase sourdough bread.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Assigns digits for sourdough purchase and non-purchase (e.g., 00-79 for purchase, 80-99 for non-purchase) (1pt); Describes how to simulate 10 customers and record success (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly identifies 8 sourdough purchases in the first trial (1pt); Correctly identifies 6 sourdough purchases in the second trial (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates the estimated probability as $\frac{1}{2}$ based on the two trials (1pt)

FRQ 2

A game involves rolling a fair six-sided die. If you roll a 1 or a 2, you win \$5. If you roll a 3 or a 4, you lose \$2. If you roll a 5 or a 6, you win \$1. Let $X$ be the random variable representing the net winnings for one roll of the die.
a. Construct the probability distribution of $X$.
b. Calculate the expected net winnings for one roll of the die. Show your work.
c. Interpret the expected net winnings from part (b) in the context of the game.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies all possible values of $X$ (1pt); Correctly assigns probabilities to each value (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct calculation for expected value (e.g., $E(X) = 5(\frac{2}{6}) + (-2)(\frac{2}{6}) + 1(\frac{2}{6})$) (1pt); Calculates the correct expected value of $E(X) = \frac{8}{6} = \frac{4}{3} \approx \$1.33$ (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the expected value in context, referring to the long-run average net winnings per roll (1pt)

FRQ 3

A recent study claims that 15% of high school students use a specific social media platform for more than 3 hours a day. A principal wants to investigate this claim by randomly selecting 20 students from her school.
a. Verify that the conditions for a binomial distribution are met for the number of students who use the platform for more than 3 hours a day in this sample of 20.
b. Calculate the probability that exactly 3 students in the sample use the platform for more than 3 hours a day, assuming the claim is true.
c. Calculate the mean and standard deviation for the number of students in a sample of 20 who use the platform for more than 3 hours a day.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Checks and states fixed number of trials, $n=20$ (1pt); Checks and states independent trials, success/failure outcomes, and constant probability of success ($p=0.15$) (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct setup for binomial probability (e.g., $\binom{20}{3}(0.15)^3(0.85)^{17}$) (1pt); Calculates the correct probability (approximately 0.2428) (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the mean $\mu = np = 20(0.15) = 3$ (1pt); Calculates the standard deviation $\sigma = \sqrt{np(1-p)} = \sqrt{20(0.15)(0.85)} \approx 1.59687$ (1pt)

FRQ 4

A telemarketing company has a 10% success rate of getting a customer to sign up for a new service on any given call. Assume that each call is independent. A new telemarketer is making calls.
a. What is the probability that the first customer to sign up for the new service is the 5th customer the telemarketer calls?
b. What is the probability that it takes more than 3 calls to get the first customer to sign up for the new service?
c. Calculate the expected number of calls until the first customer signs up for the new service and interpret this value.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates the probability $P(X=5) = (0.90)^4(0.10) \approx 0.06561$ (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Sets up the probability $P(X > 3) = (0.90)^3$ or $1 - P(X \le 3)$ (1pt); Calculates the correct probability (0.729) (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the expected number of calls $E(X) = \frac{1}{p} = \frac{1}{0.10} = 10$ calls (1pt); Interprets this value as the long-run average number of calls needed to get the first sign-up (1pt)

FRQ 5

A company produces electronic components. Historically, 8% of the components are defective. Also, 60% of the defective components are produced by Machine A. Overall, 30% of all components are produced by Machine A.
a. What is the probability that a randomly selected component is defective AND was produced by Machine A?
b. What is the probability that a randomly selected component is defective, GIVEN that it was produced by Machine A? Show your work.
c. Are the events "component is defective" and "component was produced by Machine A" independent? Justify your answer using probabilities.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates $P(\text{Defective} \cap \text{Machine A}) = P(\text{Defective}) \times P(\text{Machine A}
  • \text{Defective}) = 0.08 \times 0.60 = 0.048$ (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct setup for conditional probability $P(\text{Defective}
  • \text{Machine A}) = \frac{P(\text{Defective} \cap \text{Machine A})}{P(\text{Machine A})}$ (1pt); Calculates the correct probability $\frac{0.048}{0.30} = 0.16$ (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Compares $P(\text{Defective}
  • \text{Machine A})$ to $P(\text{Defective})$ or $P(\text{Defective} \cap \text{Machine A})$ to $P(\text{Defective})P(\text{Machine A})$ (1pt); Concludes they are NOT independent because $0.16 \ne 0.08$ (or $0.048 \ne 0.08 \times 0.30 = 0.024$) (1pt)
