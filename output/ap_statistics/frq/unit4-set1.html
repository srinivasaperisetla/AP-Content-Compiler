FRQ 1

A company produces two types of electronic components: Type A and Type B. Historically, 60% of components produced are Type A, and 40% are Type B. It is known that 3% of Type A components are defective and 5% of Type B components are defective.
a. What is the probability that a randomly selected component is Type A AND defective?
b. What is the probability that a randomly selected component is defective? Show your work.
c. Given that a randomly selected component is defective, what is the probability that it is a Type B component? Show your work.
d. Are the events "being Type A" and "being defective" mutually exclusive? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates $P(\text{Type A and Defective}) = P(\text{Defective}
  • \text{Type A}) \times P(\text{Type A}) = 0.03 \times 0.60 = 0.018$.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $P(\text{Defective}) = P(\text{Defective}
  • \text{Type A})P(\text{Type A}) + P(\text{Defective}
  • \text{Type B})P(\text{Type B}) = (0.03 \times 0.60) + (0.05 \times 0.40) = 0.018 + 0.020 = 0.038$. Requires showing work for both components and sum.
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates $P(\text{Type B}
  • \text{Defective}) = \frac{P(\text{Type B and Defective})}{P(\text{Defective})} = \frac{0.05 \times 0.40}{0.038} = \frac{0.020}{0.038} \approx 0.5263$. Requires showing the conditional probability formula and calculations.
  • Part d (1pt): States that the events are not mutually exclusive because $P(\text{Type A and Defective}) = 0.018 \ne 0$.

FRQ 2

A telemarketing company finds that 15% of the calls made to potential customers result in a sale. Assume that calls are independent. A manager randomly selects 10 calls from a telemarketer's daily record.
a. Verify that this scenario meets the conditions for a binomial distribution. If not, state which condition(s) are not met.
b. Calculate the probability that exactly 3 of the 10 calls result in a sale. Show your work.
c. Calculate the probability that at least 2 of the 10 calls result in a sale. Show your work.
d. What are the mean and standard deviation of the number of sales in 10 calls? Interpret the mean in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Verifies the BINS conditions: Binary (sale/no sale), Independent (stated in prompt), Number of trials fixed ($n=10$), Success probability fixed ($p=0.15$).
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates $P(X=3) = \binom{10}{3} (0.15)^3 (0.85)^7 \approx 0.1298$. Requires formula or calculator notation with correct values.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $P(X \ge 2) = 1 - P(X < 2) = 1 - (P(X=0) + P(X=1))$. Shows work for $P(X=0)$ and $P(X=1)$ and the final subtraction. $P(X=0) \approx 0.1969$, $P(X=1) \approx 0.3474$. So $P(X \ge 2) = 1 - (0.1969 + 0.3474) = 1 - 0.5443 = 0.4557$.
  • Part d (2pts): Calculates mean $\mu_X = np = 10 \times 0.15 = 1.5$. Calculates standard deviation $\sigma_X = \sqrt{np(1-p)} = \sqrt{10 \times 0.15 \times 0.85} = \sqrt{1.275} \approx 1.129$. Interprets the mean: On average, if many sets of 10 calls are made, approximately 1.5 sales would be expected per set.

FRQ 3

An online survey company estimates that 12% of people respond to a particular type of marketing email. Assume that responses are independent. A researcher sends out these emails sequentially until the first response is received.
a. What is the probability that the first response is received on the 4th email sent? Show your work.
b. What is the probability that the researcher needs to send more than 5 emails to get the first response? Show your work.
c. Calculate the mean and standard deviation for the number of emails that need to be sent until the first response. Interpret the mean in context.
d. Are the conditions for a geometric distribution met in this scenario? Explain your reasoning.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates $P(X=4) = (1-0.12)^{4-1} \times 0.12 = (0.88)^3 \times 0.12 \approx 0.0823$. Requires formula or calculator notation with correct values.
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates $P(X > 5) = (1-0.12)^5 = (0.88)^5 \approx 0.5277$. Requires formula or calculator notation with correct values.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates mean $\mu_X = \frac{1}{p} = \frac{1}{0.12} \approx 8.333$. Calculates standard deviation $\sigma_X = \sqrt{\frac{1-p}{p^2}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.88}{(0.12)^2}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.88}{0.0144}} \approx \sqrt{61.11} \approx 7.817$. Interprets the mean: On average, approximately 8.33 emails would need to be sent until the first response is received.
  • Part d (1pt): Verifies the BITS conditions for Geometric: Binary (response/no response), Independent (stated in prompt), Trials until first Success, Success probability fixed ($p=0.12$). Explanation needs to address each condition.

FRQ 5

A factory produces electronic components. Past data indicates that 8% of components have a manufacturing defect (event M), and 5% of components have a shipping defect (event S). It is known that $P(M \text{ and } S) = 0.02$.
a. Calculate the probability that a randomly selected component has a manufacturing defect or a shipping defect.
b. \t b. Given that a randomly selected component has a manufacturing defect, calculate the probability that it also has a shipping defect.
c. \t c. Determine whether the events "has a manufacturing defect" and "has a shipping defect" are mutually exclusive. Justify your answer.
d. \t d. Determine whether the events "has a manufacturing defect" and "has a shipping defect" are independent. Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correct calculation of $P(M \cup S) = P(M) + P(S) - P(M \cap S) = 0.08 + 0.05 - 0.02 = 0.11$.
  • Part b (1pt): Correct calculation of $P(S
  • M) = \frac{P(M \cap S)}{P(M)} = \frac{0.02}{0.08} = 0.25$.
  • Part c (1pt): States that the events are not mutually exclusive because $P(M \cap S) = 0.02 \ne 0$.
  • Part d (1pt): States that the events are not independent. Justification could be $P(M \cap S) \ne P(M)P(S)$ ($0.02 \ne 0.08 \times 0.05 = 0.004$) OR $P(S
  • M) \ne P(S)$ ($0.25 \ne 0.05$).
