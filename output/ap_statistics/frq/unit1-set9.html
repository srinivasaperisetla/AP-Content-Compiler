FRQ 1

A survey asked 150 high school students about their preferred mode of transportation to school. The results are summarized in the following frequency table:
ModeFrequency
Bus60
Car45
Walk30
Bike15
a. Construct a relative frequency table for these data.
b. Construct a bar chart to represent the distribution of preferred transportation modes.
c. Describe one advantage of using a relative frequency table over a frequency table when comparing distributions of different sample sizes.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly constructs a relative frequency table with proportions or percentages.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly constructs a bar chart with labeled axes, appropriate scale, and correct bar heights for each category.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that relative frequencies allow for direct comparison of proportions/percentages, making comparisons between groups of different sizes more meaningful.

FRQ 2

A botanist measured the growth (in millimeters) of 20 seedlings over a two-week period. The measurements are as follows: 22, 28, 30, 31, 32, 33, 34, 34, 35, 36, 37, 38, 39, 40, 42, 45, 48, 50, 52, 55.
a. Construct a stemplot of the growth measurements.
b. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of seedling growth measurements.
c. Are there any potential outliers in this distribution? Justify your answer using the $1.5 \times IQR$ rule.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly constructs a stemplot with a clear stem, leaves, and a key.
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the shape (e.g., skewed right, roughly symmetric), center (e.g., median), and spread (e.g., range or IQR) in context.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the IQR, identifies potential outliers using the $1.5 \times IQR$ rule, and justifies the conclusion based on the calculations.

FRQ 3

A health clinic collected data on the body mass index (BMI) of 100 adult patients. The five-number summary for the BMI data is Min = 18.5, Q1 = 22.0, Median = 25.5, Q3 = 29.0, Max = 35.0.
a. Calculate the interquartile range (IQR) for the BMI data.
b. Based on the provided five-number summary, describe the characteristics of the distribution of BMI for these patients.
c. If a new patient has a BMI of 33.0, explain how this patient's BMI compares to the rest of the distribution.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates $IQR = Q_3 - Q_1 = 29.0 - 22.0 = 7.0$.
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the center (median), spread (IQR/range), and discusses potential skewness based on quartiles/median position, in context.
  • Part c (2pts): Explains that a BMI of 33.0 is above $Q_3$, indicating it is in the upper 25% of the distribution, and likely in the upper range of normal or overweight category, relative to the data.

FRQ 4

The weights of adult golden retrievers are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 70$ pounds and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 5$ pounds.
a. A golden retriever is considered overweight if its weight is above 80 pounds. Calculate the proportion of adult golden retrievers that are overweight.
b. What is the weight (in pounds) that separates the lightest 15% of adult golden retrievers from the rest? Show your work.
c. Suppose a particular golden retriever weighs 72 pounds. Compare its relative position in the distribution to a golden retriever weighing 68 pounds.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates the z-score for 80 pounds and uses it to find the correct proportion (or uses calculator function) and states the proportion. $Z = \frac{80-70}{5} = 2$. Proportion = $P(Z>2) = 0.0228$.
  • Part b (2pts): Finds the z-score corresponding to the 15th percentile (approximately -1.04) and uses it to calculate the weight. $X = \mu + Z\sigma = 70 + (-1.04)(5) = 64.8$ pounds. Shows work.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the z-scores for both weights and compares them, concluding that 72 pounds is above the mean, and 68 pounds is below the mean (or equidistant from mean but on opposite sides). A 72lb retriever is $Z = (72-70)/5 = 0.4$ std devs above the mean. A 68lb retriever is $Z = (68-70)/5 = -0.4$ std devs below the mean.

FRQ 5

A researcher collected data on the number of hours of sleep for $15$ randomly selected high school students: $6, 7, 7, 7, 8, 8, 8, 8, 9, 9, 9, 10, 10, 11, 12$.
a. Calculate the median and the interquartile range (IQR) for this data set.
b. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of sleep hours.
c. Would the mean or median be a more appropriate measure of center for this data? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Median is $8$ (1pt). IQR calculation: $Q1 = 7$, $Q3 = 10$, so $IQR = 10 - 7 = 3$ (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Shape: Roughly symmetric to slightly skewed right (1pt). Center: Median is $8$ hours (1pt). Spread: IQR is $3$ hours (or range is $6$ hours) (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): The median would be more appropriate (1pt) because the distribution is slightly skewed, and the median is resistant to outliers/skewness.
