AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 6)

FRQ 1

A politician wants to estimate the proportion of registered voters in her district who support a new environmental bill. She commissions a random sample of 500 registered voters, and 280 of them indicate support for the bill.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure for estimating the population proportion of support for the bill.
b. Verify the conditions for constructing this confidence interval.
c. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the proportion of registered voters who support the bill.
d. Interpret the calculated confidence interval in context.
e. Based on this confidence interval, can the politician claim that more than 50% of registered voters support the bill? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly identifies a one-sample z-interval for a population proportion.
  • Part b (1pt): Checks random condition (random sample stated), normal condition (np-hat = 280 >= 10, n(1-p-hat) = 220 >= 10), and independence condition (sample of 500 < 10% of all registered voters).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates p-hat = 280/500 = 0.56. Calculates the confidence interval using the formula p-hat +/- z*sqrt(p-hat(1-p-hat)/n) = 0.56 +/- 1.96*sqrt(0.56*0.44/500) = 0.56 +/- 0.0438, resulting in (0.5162, 0.6038).
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets the interval as being 95% confident that the true proportion of registered voters who support the bill is between 0.5162 and 0.6038.
  • Part e (1pt): States that the politician can claim more than 50% support because the entire interval (0.5162, 0.6038) is above 0.50.

FRQ 2

A company claims that 80% of its new light bulbs last longer than 1,000 hours. A consumer advocacy group suspects this claim is too high. They test a random sample of 150 new light bulbs and find that 110 of them last longer than 1,000 hours.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for a test to investigate the advocacy group's suspicion.
b. Identify the appropriate inference method for this test. Verify that the conditions for this method are met.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Interpret the p-value in the context of this study.
e. Based on the p-value and a significance level of alpha = 0.05, what conclusion should the advocacy group make? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states H0: p = 0.80 and Ha: p < 0.80, where p is the true proportion of light bulbs lasting longer than 1,000 hours.
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies a one-sample z-test for a population proportion. Verifies random sample (given), normal condition (np0 = 150*0.80 = 120 >= 10, n(1-p0) = 150*0.20 = 30 >= 10), and independence (sample of 150 < 10% of all light bulbs).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates p-hat = 110/150 = 0.733. Calculates test statistic z = (0.733 - 0.80) / sqrt(0.80*0.20/150) = -0.067 / sqrt(0.16/150) = -0.067 / sqrt(0.001067) = -0.067 / 0.03266 = -2.05 (approx) and finds p-value (approx 0.0202 using z = -2.05).
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets p-value as the probability of observing a sample proportion as low as 0.733 or lower, assuming the true proportion of light bulbs lasting over 1,000 hours is 0.80.
  • Part e (1pt): Concludes to reject H0 because p-value (0.0202) < alpha (0.05). Provides evidence that the true proportion of light bulbs lasting longer than 1,000 hours is less than 0.80.

FRQ 3

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug designed to reduce the proportion of patients experiencing a certain side effect. The standard drug has a 20% side effect rate. The company wants to test if the new drug has a side effect rate less than 20% at a significance level of alpha = 0.05.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this study and its potential consequence for the company.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this study and its potential consequence for patients.
c. Identify one factor that would increase the power of this test.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Type I error occurs if the company concludes the new drug has a lower side effect rate when it actually doesn't (rejecting H0 when H0 is true). Consequence: Company invests in a drug that isn't better, potentially wasting resources and misleading patients.
  • Part b (1pt): Type II error occurs if the company fails to conclude the new drug has a lower side effect rate when it actually does (failing to reject H0 when Ha is true). Consequence: A potentially effective drug is not marketed, and patients continue with the standard drug or miss out on a better option.
  • Part c (1pt): Identifies increasing the sample size or increasing the significance level (alpha) as factors that would increase the power of this test.

FRQ 4

A medical researcher wants to compare the effectiveness of two different flu vaccines, Vaccine A and Vaccine B, in preventing flu symptoms. A random sample of 300 people received Vaccine A, and 45 of them developed flu symptoms. Another random sample of 250 people received Vaccine B, and 25 of them developed flu symptoms.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure for estimating the difference in proportions of people who develop flu symptoms for Vaccine A and Vaccine B.
b. Verify the conditions for constructing this confidence interval.
c. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the proportion of people developing flu symptoms (Vaccine A - Vaccine B).
d. Interpret the calculated confidence interval in context.
e. Based on this confidence interval, is there convincing evidence of a difference in the effectiveness of the two vaccines? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly identifies a two-sample z-interval for a difference of population proportions.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random samples (given), normal condition (all n*p-hat and n*(1-p-hat) >= 10 for both groups: A: 45, 255; B: 25, 225), and independence (samples < 10% population for both).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates p-hat_A = 45/300 = 0.15, p-hat_B = 25/250 = 0.10. Calculates the confidence interval (p-hatA - p-hatB) +/- z*sqrt(p-hatA(1-p-hatA)/nA + p-hatB(1-p-hatB)/nB) = (0.15 - 0.10) +/- 1.645*sqrt(0.15*0.85/300 + 0.10*0.90/250) = 0.05 +/- 1.645*sqrt(0.000425 + 0.00036) = 0.05 +/- 1.645*0.028 = 0.05 +/- 0.046, resulting in (0.004, 0.096).
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets the interval as being 90% confident that the true difference in proportions of people developing flu symptoms (Vaccine A - Vaccine B) is between 0.004 and 0.096.
  • Part e (1pt): Concludes there is convincing evidence of a difference because the interval (0.004, 0.096) does not contain 0, and all values are positive, suggesting Vaccine A has a higher proportion of flu symptoms (or Vaccine B is more effective) in the population.

FRQ 5

A new teaching method is being compared to a traditional method to see if it increases the proportion of students who pass a standardized exam. A random sample of 120 students was taught using the new method, and 96 of them passed the exam. Another random sample of 100 students was taught using the traditional method, and 70 of them passed the exam.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for a test to determine if the new teaching method is more effective.
b. Identify the appropriate inference method for this test. Verify that the conditions for this method are met.
c. Calculate the test statistic for this test. (You are not required to calculate the p-value.)
d. Based on a p-value of 0.018 and a significance level of alpha = 0.05, interpret the p-value in context.
e. Based on your interpretation of the p-value in part (d) and the given significance level, what conclusion should be drawn? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states H0: p_new = p_trad (or p_new - p_trad = 0) and Ha: p_new > p_trad (or p_new - p_trad > 0), where p_new and p_trad are the true proportions of students passing with the new and traditional methods, respectively.
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies a two-sample z-test for a difference of population proportions. Verifies random samples (given), normal condition (all pooled n*p-hat and n*(1-p-hat) >= 10 for both groups: New: 96, 24; Trad: 70, 30), and independence (samples < 10% of all students for both).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates p-hat_new = 96/120 = 0.80, p-hat_trad = 70/100 = 0.70. Pooled proportion p_c = (96+70)/(120+100) = 166/220 = 0.7545. Calculates test statistic z = (0.80 - 0.70) / sqrt(0.7545*0.2455/120 + 0.7545*0.2455/100) = 0.10 / sqrt(0.001545 + 0.001854) = 0.10 / sqrt(0.003399) = 0.10 / 0.0583 = 1.715 (approx).
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets the p-value (0.018) as the probability of observing a difference in sample proportions (p-hat_new - p-hat_trad) as large as or larger than 0.10, assuming there is no true difference in effectiveness between the teaching methods.
  • Part e (1pt): Concludes to reject H0 because p-value (0.018) < alpha (0.05). Provides convincing evidence that the new teaching method is more effective than the traditional method.
