AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data: Slopes (Set 3)

FRQ 1

A company is interested in the relationship between its monthly advertising budget (in thousands of dollars) and its monthly sales (in thousands of dollars). Data was collected for 15 months. A linear regression analysis resulted in the regression equation $\hat{\text{Sales}} = 12.5 + 3.2 \times \text{Advertising}$, with a standard error of the residuals $S = 8.7$ and $R^2 = 0.85$. A residual plot is provided.
a. Interpret the estimated slope coefficient (3.2) in the context of this problem.
b. Describe what the value of $S = 8.7$ (standard error of the residuals) represents in the context of this problem.
c. Based on the residual plot, evaluate whether the conditions for inference for the slope of a regression model, specifically linearity and constant variance, are met. Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly interprets the slope (e.g., for every increase of $1,000 in advertising budget, sales are predicted to increase by $3,200) (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly describes $S$ as the typical distance/error between observed sales and predicted sales for a given advertising budget (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Evaluates linearity (no pattern in residuals) and constant variance (no fanning/funneling) based on the residual plot (1pt for each condition evaluation with justification) (2pts total).

FRQ 2

A study investigated the relationship between the outdoor temperature in degrees Fahrenheit ($x$) and the amount of electricity consumed (in kWh, $y$) by homes in a particular region. Data was collected for 50 randomly selected days. A 90% confidence interval for the slope of the regression line ($\beta$) was calculated as $(-0.85, -0.45)$.
a. Interpret the 90% confidence interval in the context of this study. \n b. A homeowner claims that for every 1-degree Fahrenheit increase in temperature, electricity consumption decreases by exactly 0.5 kWh. Based on the confidence interval, does this claim seem plausible? Justify your answer. \n c. If the researchers were to collect data from 100 randomly selected days instead of 50, how would this likely affect the width of the confidence interval, assuming other factors remain similar?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): We are 90% confident that for each additional degree Fahrenheit increase in outdoor temperature, the mean electricity consumption decreases by between 0.45 kWh and 0.85 kWh. (1pt for correct interpretation with context). \n Part b (2pts): The claim is plausible (1pt) because 0.5 kWh is within the calculated 90% confidence interval $(-0.85, -0.45)$ (1pt for justification). \n Part c (1pt): Increasing the sample size from 50 to 100 would likely decrease the width of the confidence interval (1pt). This is because a larger sample size generally leads to a smaller standard error for the slope estimate ($SE_b$), resulting in a narrower interval.

FRQ 3

A large car dealership is interested in the relationship between the advertised price of a used car (in thousands of dollars, $x$) and the number of days it stays on the lot until sold ($y$). A random sample of 40 used cars was selected, and a linear regression was performed. The $p$-value for the test of $H_0: \beta = 0$ versus $H_a: \beta \ne 0$ was found to be $0.082$. The estimated slope was $0.75$ days per thousand dollars.
a. Interpret the $p$-value in the context of this problem. \n b. Based on the $p$-value, what conclusion can be drawn at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level regarding the relationship between advertised price and days on the lot? Justify your answer. \n c. Suppose a colleague claims that there is no linear relationship between advertised price and days on the lot. Would your conclusion in part (b) support or contradict this claim? Explain your reasoning.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Assuming there is no linear relationship between car price and days on the lot, there is an 8.2% chance of observing a sample slope as extreme as or more extreme than $0.75$ (or $-0.75$) due to random sampling variability alone. (1pt for correct interpretation with context). \n Part b (2pts): Since the $p$-value ($0.082$) $> \alpha$ ($0.05$), we fail to reject $H_0$ (1pt). There is not sufficient evidence to conclude a significant linear relationship between the advertised price of a used car and the number of days it stays on the lot (1pt for conclusion in context). \n Part c (1pt): My conclusion in part (b) would support the colleague's claim (0.5pt) because failing to reject the null hypothesis ($H_0: \beta = 0$) means we do not have enough evidence to say there *is* a linear relationship. (0.5pt for explanation).

FRQ 4

A health researcher is studying the relationship between a person's daily intake of Vitamin C (in mg, $x$) and their white blood cell count (in thousands per microliter, $y$). Data was collected from a random sample of 20 individuals. The scatterplot of the data and the residual plot from the least-squares regression analysis are provided in the stimulus.
a. Based on the scatterplot (top plot in stimulus), does a linear model appear appropriate for these data? Explain why or why not. \n b. Use the residual plot (bottom plot in stimulus) to assess whether the condition of constant variance is met. \n c. Explain why it is important for the residuals to be approximately normally distributed when performing inference for the slope of the regression line.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): A linear model appears appropriate because the scatterplot shows a generally positive linear trend with no strong curvature (1pt for explanation based on scatterplot). \n Part b (1pt): The residual plot shows no clear fanning-out or fanning-in pattern and points are randomly scattered around zero, indicating that the variance of the residuals appears to be relatively constant across the range of Vitamin C intake, thus meeting the constant variance condition (1pt for explanation based on residual plot). \n Part c (1pt): The assumption of approximately normally distributed residuals is important because it ensures that the sampling distribution of the slope estimate is approximately a $t$-distribution, which is used to construct confidence intervals and perform hypothesis tests for the slope. If residuals are not normal, the $t$-procedures may not be valid, especially for small sample sizes. (1pt for explanation).

FRQ 5

A researcher wants to investigate the relationship between the number of hours a student studies per week ($x$) and their score on a 100-point statistics exam ($y$). A random sample of 30 students was selected, and the following summary statistics were obtained for a linear regression model: $\hat{y} = 65.2 + 2.1x$, standard error of the slope $s_b = 0.45$, and residual standard error $s_e = 7.8$.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure to construct a 95% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line relating study hours to exam scores.
b. Verify the conditions necessary for constructing this confidence interval.
c. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line.
d. Interpret the 95% confidence interval for the true slope in the context of this problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a t-interval for the slope of a regression line (or t-interval for $\beta$).
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies all 5 conditions (Linearity: scatterplot shows no obvious pattern; Independent: scores for each student are independent, and sample is less than 10% of population; Normal: residuals plot shows no obvious pattern or histogram of residuals is roughly symmetric/unimodal; Equal Variance: residuals plot shows constant variability; Random: stated as a random sample).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the confidence interval using the formula $b \pm t^* s_b$. Shows correct $t^*$ for $df=28$ ($t^*=2.048$) and computes the interval $2.1 \pm 2.048(0.45) = (1.1784, 3.0216)$.
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly interprets the interval as "We are 95% confident that for each additional hour a student studies per week, the true mean exam score increases by an amount between 1.1784 and 3.0216 points."
