FRQ 1

A real estate agent wants to predict the selling price (in thousands of dollars) of a house based on its size (in hundreds of square feet). Data from 20 recent sales were collected and a linear regression model was fitted. The computer output is provided below.
PredictorCoefSE CoefTP
Constant10.525.102.060.054
Size (100 sq ft)5.210.4511.580.000
$S = 25.5$$R-Sq = 89.2\%$$R-Sq(adj) = 88.6\%$
a. Write the equation of the least-squares regression line for predicting selling price from house size.
b. Interpret the slope of the regression line in the context of this problem.
c. Interpret the value of $s = 25.5$ in the context of this problem.
d. Predict the selling price of a house that is 2200 square feet.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly writes the regression equation using appropriate variable names, e.g., $\hat{\text{Price}} = 10.52 + 5.21(\text{Size})$.
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that for each additional 100 square feet in house size, the predicted selling price increases by \$5,210.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that the typical distance (or average residual size) between the actual selling prices and the predicted selling prices is \$25,500.
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly substitutes Size = 22 and calculates $\hat{\text{Price}} = 10.52 + 5.21(22) = 125.14$ (or \$125,140).

FRQ 3

A researcher is investigating the relationship between the number of hours a student spends studying for a statistics exam and their score on the exam. Data was collected from 10 students and plotted in the scatterplot shown.
a. Describe the direction, form, and strength of the relationship between study hours and exam scores as shown in the scatterplot.
b. Based on the scatterplot, would you expect the correlation coefficient $r$ to be positive, negative, or close to zero? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (moderately strong or strong).
  • Part b (1pt): States $r$ would be positive AND provides a correct justification based on the upward trend.

Age GroupOnlineTVPrintTotal

b. Calculate the conditional proportion of adults aged 56+ who prefer online news.

FRQ 5

A company wants to understand the relationship between its advertising expenditure (in thousands of dollars) and its monthly sales (in thousands of dollars). A linear regression model was fitted to historical data, resulting in the equation $\widehat{\text{Sales}} = 12.5 + 2.3(\text{Advertising})$.
a. Interpret the slope of the regression line in the context of this problem.
b. Predict the monthly sales if the company spends $10$ thousand dollars on advertising.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States that for every additional thousand dollars spent on advertising, the predicted monthly sales increase by $2.3$ thousand dollars.
  • Part b (1pt): Correct calculation $12.5 + 2.3(10) = 35.5$ thousand dollars.
