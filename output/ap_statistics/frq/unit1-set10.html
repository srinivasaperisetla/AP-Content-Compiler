AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 10)

FRQ 1

A survey was conducted among 20 high school students asking for their favorite color. The results are as follows: Blue, Red, Green, Blue, Yellow, Red, Blue, Green, Blue, Red, Blue, Yellow, Green, Red, Blue, Blue, Red, Green, Yellow, Blue.
a. Construct a frequency table and a relative frequency table for the favorite colors.
b. Construct a bar chart to represent the distribution of favorite colors. Ensure proper labels and scales.
c. Describe the distribution of favorite colors.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correct frequency table (1pt); Correct relative frequency table (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Bar chart with correct categories, heights, and labels (1pt); Appropriate scale on y-axis (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Description of the most frequent (modal) category and overall pattern (1pt)

FRQ 2

A statistics teacher recorded the number of minutes it took each of 25 students to complete a homework assignment: 15, 20, 18, 22, 25, 17, 19, 21, 23, 20, 16, 24, 28, 19, 21, 20, 26, 18, 22, 24, 20, 23, 17, 25, 29.
a. Classify the variable "number of minutes to complete a homework assignment" as categorical or quantitative. If quantitative, specify if it is discrete or continuous.
b. Construct a stem-and-leaf plot for the distribution of homework completion times. Be sure to include a key.
c. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of homework completion times. Identify any potential outliers.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly classifies as quantitative and continuous (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Correct stem-and-leaf plot with correct stems, leaves, and a key (1pt); Correct ordering of leaves (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Description of shape (e.g., roughly symmetric, skewed) (1pt); Description of center (median or mean estimate) and spread (range or IQR estimate) (0.5pt); Identification of potential outliers (0.5pt)

FRQ 3

The hourly wages (in dollars) for a sample of 10 employees at a small company are: 15, 18, 16, 20, 14, 100, 17, 19, 16, 15.
a. Calculate the mean and median hourly wage for this sample.
b. Calculate the standard deviation and the interquartile range (IQR) for the hourly wages.
c. Explain which measure of center (mean or median) and which measure of variability (standard deviation or IQR) would be more appropriate to describe the hourly wages for this sample, and justify your choice.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1.5pts): Correctly calculates the mean ($\bar{x} = \$26$) (0.5pt); Correctly calculates the median ($\$16.50$) (1pt)
  • Part b (1.5pts): Correctly calculates the standard deviation ($s \approx \$26.68$) (0.5pt); Correctly calculates the IQR ($IQR = \$3.50$) (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): States that the median and IQR are more appropriate (1pt); Justifies the choice by noting the presence of an outlier ($\$100$) or skewness (1pt)

FRQ 5

The heights of adult males in a certain city are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 69$ inches and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 2.5$ inches. The heights of adult females in the same city are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 64$ inches and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 2.4$ inches.
a. Calculate the Z-score for an adult male who is 73 inches tall and interpret this Z-score in context.
b. What proportion of adult males in this city are taller than 73 inches? Show your work.
c. An adult male is 70 inches tall, and an adult female is 65 inches tall. Compare their relative positions within their respective height distributions.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates $Z = \frac{73-69}{2.5} = 1.6$ (1pt); Correctly interprets the Z-score (e.g., 1.6 standard deviations above the mean) (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Uses Z-score from part (a) or recalculates it (0.5pt); Uses normalcdf or equivalent to find proportion (0.5pt); Correctly states proportion (e.g., $P(Z > 1.6) \approx 0.0548$) (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates Z-score for male ($Z_M = \frac{70-69}{2.5} = 0.4$) (0.5pt); Calculates Z-score for female ($Z_F = \frac{65-64}{2.4} \approx 0.417$) (0.5pt); Compares Z-scores and concludes the female's relative position is slightly higher than the male's (1pt)
