AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 17)

FRQ 1

A candidate for mayor wants to estimate the proportion of voters who support her. In a random sample of 500 likely voters, 260 indicated they would vote for her.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the proportion of all likely voters who support the candidate.
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in the context of the problem.
d. Calculate the margin of error for the 95% confidence interval constructed in part (b).

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies a one-sample z-interval for proportions and states conditions (random, 10% condition, success/failure) and verifies them in context.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the sample proportion and standard error for the interval (1pt) and computes the correct 95% confidence interval (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the confidence interval in context, including the confidence level and the parameter being estimated.
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly calculates the margin of error for the 95% confidence interval.

FRQ 2

A cereal company claims that 30% of its boxes contain a prize. A consumer protection group suspects that the proportion is actually lower. To test this, they purchase a random sample of 150 cereal boxes and find that 35 of them contain a prize.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this investigation and identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Interpret the p-value in the context of this problem and state your conclusion at the α = 0.05 significance level, providing a statistical justification.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses and identifies a one-sample z-test for proportions.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random, 10% (if applicable), and large counts (np >= 10, n(1-p) >= 10) conditions in context.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic (1pt) and the correct p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (2pts): Interprets the p-value in context (1pt) and makes a correct conclusion based on the p-value and α, with proper justification (1pt).

FRQ 3

A new medical test is being developed to detect a rare disease that affects 1% of the population. The null hypothesis is that a person does NOT have the disease, and the alternative hypothesis is that the person DOES have the disease.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this medical test.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this medical test.
c. Explain how changing the significance level (α) would affect the probability of a Type I error and subsequently the probability of a Type II error for this test.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly describes a Type I error as rejecting H0 when H0 is true, in the context of the disease.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly describes a Type II error as failing to reject H0 when H0 is false, in the context of the disease.
  • Part c (2pts): Explains that decreasing α decreases the probability of a Type I error (1pt) and explains that this would increase the probability of a Type II error (1pt).

B35070
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure for comparing the effectiveness of the two strategies and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions of customers who would purchase the product after viewing Strategy A versus Strategy B (p_A - p_B).
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in context and use it to justify whether there is a statistically significant difference in the effectiveness of the two advertising strategies.

  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the confidence interval in context (1pt) and justifies whether there is a statistically significant difference based on whether the interval contains 0 (1pt).
