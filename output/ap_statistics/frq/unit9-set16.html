AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data: Slopes (Set 16)

FRQ 1

A biologist is studying the growth of a certain plant species. She measured the height (in cm) of 25 plants over a period of 10 days and recorded the number of days since planting ($x$) and the average height ($y$). The scatterplot of the data and the corresponding residual plot are shown below.
a. Based on the scatterplot, describe the direction, form, and strength of the relationship between the number of days since planting and the average plant height. Does the scatterplot suggest a linear relationship is appropriate for modeling the data?
b. Use the provided graphs to verify the conditions necessary for performing inference for the slope of the regression line.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Describes the direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (strong) of the relationship (1pt). States that a linear relationship is appropriate based on the scatterplot's appearance (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies linearity condition using the scatterplot (no obvious curve) and residual plot (no pattern) (1pt). Verifies equal variance condition using the residual plot (no fanning/funneling) (1pt).

FRQ 2

A researcher is studying the relationship between the number of hours students spend studying per week and their GPA. A random sample of 30 students was selected, and data on their weekly study hours ($x$) and GPA ($y$) were collected. A linear regression analysis was performed, yielding the following results: $\hat{y} = 2.5 + 0.05x$, standard error of the slope $SE_b = 0.012$, and degrees of freedom $df = 28$.
a. Identify the appropriate inference method to construct a confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line relating weekly study hours to GPA.
b. Verify the conditions for constructing this confidence interval. Assume a residual plot shows no clear pattern, and a histogram of residuals shows no strong skewness or outliers.
c. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line.
d. Interpret the calculated confidence interval in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly identifies a t-interval for the slope of a regression line.
  • Part b (1pt): States and verifies (or acknowledges assumption of) linearity, independence, normality of residuals, equal variance of residuals, and randomness of data collection.
  • Part c (2pts): Shows correct calculation using $b \pm t^* SE_b$. For $df=28$, $t^*=2.048$. Interval: $0.05 \pm 2.048(0.012) = (0.025424, 0.074576)$.
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly interprets the interval as "We are 95% confident that for each additional hour a student studies per week, their GPA will increase by an amount between 0.0254 and 0.0746, on average."

FRQ 3

A company wants to determine if there is a linear relationship between the age of an employee (in years) and their annual sick days taken. A random sample of 25 employees was taken. The regression analysis yielded a sample slope of $b = 0.25$ sick days per year, with a standard error of the slope $SE_b = 0.10$.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test if there is a positive linear relationship between employee age and annual sick days.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test. Assume a scatterplot shows a linear pattern, and residuals are approximately normally distributed with constant variance.
c. Calculate the test statistic for the slope.
d. Based on a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion can be made regarding the relationship between employee age and annual sick days? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0: \beta = 0$ and $H_a: \beta > 0$.
  • Part b (1pt): States and verifies (or acknowledges assumption of) linearity, independence, normality of residuals, equal variance of residuals, and randomness of data collection.
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates test statistic $t = \frac{b - \beta_0}{SE_b} = \frac{0.25 - 0}{0.10} = 2.5$.
  • Part d (2pts): For $df = 25-2=23$, $t=2.5$. P-value (one-sided) is between 0.005 and 0.01. Since p-value $< 0.05$, reject $H_0$. Concludes there is convincing evidence of a positive linear relationship between employee age and annual sick days.

FRQ 4

A marketing analyst constructed a 90% confidence interval for the slope of the regression line relating advertising spending (in thousands of dollars) to monthly sales (in thousands of dollars). The calculated interval was $(0.85, 1.25)$.
a. Interpret this 90% confidence interval in the context of the problem.
b. A company claims that for every additional $\$1000$ spent on advertising, monthly sales increase by more than $\$750$. Based on this confidence interval, does this claim appear plausible? Justify your answer.
c. If the marketing analyst wanted to construct a narrower 90% confidence interval for the slope, what could be done, assuming all other factors remain constant?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): We are 90% confident that for every additional $\$1000$ spent on advertising, the average monthly sales will increase by an amount between $\$850$ and $\$1250$.
  • Part b (2pts): The claim is plausible because the entire interval $(0.85, 1.25)$ is above $0.75$. Since all values in the interval are greater than $0.75$, it is plausible that the true increase is more than $\$750$ per $\$1000$ advertising.
  • Part c (1pt): To make the interval narrower, the sample size could be increased.

FRQ 5

A biologist is studying the growth of a certain species of tree. She collects data on the age (in years) and height (in meters) for a random sample of 15 trees. The scatterplot shows the relationship between age and height. The linear regression analysis yields a slope of $b = 0.75$ meters/year and a standard error of the slope $SE_b = 0.12$.
a. Describe the relationship between tree age and height as shown in the scatterplot.
b. Identify the appropriate inference method to construct a 90% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line.
c. State and verify the conditions for constructing this confidence interval. Assume residual plots show no clear pattern and residuals are roughly normal.
d. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line.
e. Interpret the calculated confidence interval in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Describes a positive, linear, moderately strong relationship with no obvious outliers between tree age and height from the provided scatterplot.
  • Part b (1pt): A t-interval for the slope of a regression line.
  • Part c (1pt): Conditions are: Linearity (scatterplot appears linear, residual plot shows no pattern), Independence (random sample, 15 < 10% of population), Normality of residuals (histogram of residuals approximately normal), Equal variance of residuals (residual plot shows no fanning), Randomness (random sample).
  • Part d (2pts): For $df = 15-2=13$, $t^*=1.771$. Interval: $0.75 \pm 1.771(0.12) = (0.53748, 0.96252)$.
  • Part e (1pt): We are 90% confident that for each additional year of age, the average height of this tree species increases by an amount between 0.5375 and 0.9625 meters.
