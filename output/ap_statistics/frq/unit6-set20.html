AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 20)

FRQ 1

A online streaming service wants to estimate the proportion of its subscribers who watch content daily. A random sample of $300$ subscribers was selected, and $186$ of them reported watching content daily.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the true proportion of subscribers who watch content daily.
b. Calculate a $95\%$ confidence interval for the true proportion of subscribers who watch content daily.
c. Interpret the $95\%$ confidence interval in the context of the problem.
d. The streaming service management claims that more than $60\%$ of its subscribers watch content daily. Based on your interval, does this claim appear to be supported? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample $z$-interval for proportions (1pt); Verifies conditions: Random sampling (1pt), Large Counts ($n\hat{p} \ge 10$ and $n(1-\hat{p}) \ge 10$) (1pt - combined for both, deduct if one is missing).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the $95\%$ confidence interval for the proportion.
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets the confidence interval in context (e.g., "We are $95\%$ confident that the true proportion of subscribers who watch content daily is between X and Y").
  • Part d (1pt): States whether the claim is supported and provides a correct justification based on the confidence interval (e.g., if $0.60$ is contained or not).

FRQ 2

A university claims that $80\%$ of its first-year students continue to their second year. A statistics professor wants to investigate if this proportion has decreased. She takes a random sample of $250$ first-year students from the most recent cohort and finds that $185$ of them continued to their second year.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for the professor's investigation. Identify the appropriate inference method to test these hypotheses.
b. Verify that the conditions for this inference method are met.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your p-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the professor make? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States $H_0: p = 0.80$ and $H_a: p < 0.80$ (1pt); Identifies a one-sample $z$-test for proportions (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions: Random sampling (1pt), Large Counts ($np_0 \ge 10$ and $n(1-p_0) \ge 10$) (1pt - combined for both, deduct if one is missing).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic ($z$-score) (1pt); Calculates the correct p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (2pts): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ and states a correct conclusion (reject/fail to reject $H_0$) (1pt); Justifies conclusion in context (1pt).

FRQ 3

A medical device company manufactures sterile syringes. They conduct a quality control test to ensure that the proportion of contaminated syringes in a batch does not exceed $0.01$. The null hypothesis is $H_0: p = 0.01$ and the alternative hypothesis is $H_a: p > 0.01$, where $p$ is the true proportion of contaminated syringes in a batch.
a. Describe a Type I error and a Type II error in the context of this problem.
b. Describe a potential consequence for the company of making a Type I error and a potential consequence of making a Type II error.
c. Explain how increasing the sample size used in the quality control test (while keeping the significance level $\alpha$ fixed) would affect the probability of a Type II error.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes Type I error in context (1pt); Correctly describes Type II error in context (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Describes a plausible consequence for a Type I error (1pt); Describes a plausible consequence for a Type II error (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that increasing sample size would decrease the probability of a Type II error, with a brief justification (e.g., greater power, better detection of true alternative).

FRQ 5

A pharmaceutical company developed a new drug to treat a rare skin condition. In a double-blind, randomized clinical trial, $150$ patients received the new drug and $150$ patients received a placebo. After six months, $96$ of the patients who received the new drug showed significant improvement, while $75$ of the patients who received the placebo showed significant improvement. The company wants to determine if there is convincing evidence that the new drug is more effective than the placebo.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this investigation. Identify the appropriate inference method to test these hypotheses.
b. Verify that the conditions for this inference method are met.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your p-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what conclusion should the company make? Justify your conclusion in context regarding the effectiveness of the new drug.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States $H_0: p_D = p_P$ (or $p_D - p_P = 0$) and $H_a: p_D > p_P$ (or $p_D - p_P > 0$) (1pt); Identifies a two-sample $z$-test for a difference in proportions (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions: Random assignment/independent groups (1pt), Large Counts for both groups (pooled counts used for expected values: $n_D\hat{p}_c \ge 10$, $n_D(1-\hat{p}_c) \ge 10$, $n_P\hat{p}_c \ge 10$, $n_P(1-\hat{p}_c) \ge 10$) (1pt - combined for all, deduct if any are missing).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic ($z$-score) (1pt); Calculates the correct p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ and states a correct conclusion (reject/fail to reject $H_0$) and justifies it in context.
