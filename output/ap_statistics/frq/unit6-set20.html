AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 20)

FRQ 1

A local animal shelter claims that 75% of adopted dogs are still in their new homes one year later. A researcher wants to estimate the true proportion of adopted dogs that remain in their homes after one year. A random sample of 120 adoption records from the past year shows that 84 dogs were still in their adopted homes.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of adopted dogs that are still in their new homes one year later.
c. Based on your confidence interval in part (b), does the data provide convincing evidence that the true proportion is different from 75%? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample z-interval for proportions and checks random, normal (\(np \ge 10\), \(n(1-p) \ge 10\)), and independence conditions.
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct formula and calculation of the confidence interval, leading to (0.627, 0.773) or similar.
  • Part c (1pt): States that 75% (0.75) is within the interval, so there is no convincing evidence that the true proportion is different from 75%, with justification.

FRQ 2

A manufacturer of light bulbs claims that no more than 5% of their bulbs are defective. A consumer watchdog group suspects that the proportion of defective bulbs is higher than 5%. They take a random sample of 200 light bulbs and find that 17 are defective.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test and identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your results from part (c), what conclusion should the consumer watchdog group make at the \(\alpha = 0.05\) significance level? Justify your conclusion.
e. Describe a Type II error in the context of this problem and state a consequence of making this error.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States H0: p \(\le\) 0.05, Ha: p > 0.05 (or H0: p=0.05) and identifies one-sample z-test for a proportion.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random sample, normal (\(np_0 \ge 10\), \(n(1-p_0) \ge 10\)), and independence conditions.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates test statistic z = (0.085-0.05)/sqrt(0.05*0.95/200) = 2.27 and p-value P(Z>2.27) = 0.0116.
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to alpha (0.0116 < 0.05) and concludes to reject H0, stating there is convincing evidence that the proportion of defective bulbs is greater than 5%.
  • Part e (1pt): Describes Type II error as failing to reject H0 when the true proportion of defective bulbs is actually greater than 5%, and a consequence (e.g., consumers continue to buy defective bulbs, manufacturer not forced to improve quality).

FRQ 3

A local school district is considering two different online learning platforms, Platform A and Platform B, for their high school students. To compare student satisfaction, a random sample of 150 students using Platform A were surveyed, and 108 reported being satisfied. Separately, a random sample of 120 students using Platform B were surveyed, and 78 reported being satisfied.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the proportion of satisfied students (Platform A - Platform B).
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies two-sample z-interval for proportions and checks random samples (independent), normal (\(n_1\hat{p}_1 \ge 10\), \(n_1(1-\hat{p}_1) \ge 10\), \(n_2\hat{p}_2 \ge 10\), \(n_2(1-\hat{p}_2) \ge 10\)), and independence conditions.
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct formula and calculation for CI, leading to (0.021, 0.199) or similar.
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets the interval as being 90% confident that the true difference in proportions of satisfied students (Platform A - Platform B) is between 0.021 and 0.199. Since 0 is not in the interval, there is evidence Platform A has a higher proportion of satisfied students.

FRQ 4

A medical researcher wants to investigate if there is a difference in the proportion of patients who experience a side effect when taking two different dosages of a new medication. In a randomized controlled trial, 180 patients were given a low dose, and 27 of them experienced a side effect. Another 200 patients were given a high dose, and 44 of them experienced a side effect.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test and identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your results from part (c), what conclusion should the researcher make at the \(\alpha = 0.05\) significance level? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States H0: pL = pH, Ha: pL \(\neq\) pH and identifies two-sample z-test for difference in proportions.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random assignment, normal (pooled counts \(np_c \ge 10\), \(n(1-p_c) \ge 10\) for both groups), and independence conditions.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates pooled proportion \(p_c = (27+44)/(180+200) = 71/380 \approx 0.1868\). Test statistic z = (0.15 - 0.22) / sqrt(0.1868*(1-0.1868)*(1/180 + 1/200)) \(\approx -1.86\). P-value 2*P(Z < -1.86) \(\approx 2*0.0314 = 0.0628\).
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to alpha (0.0628 > 0.05) and concludes to fail to reject H0, stating there is not convincing evidence of a difference in the proportion of patients experiencing side effects between the two dosages.

FRQ 5

A local election for mayor is approaching, and a pollster wants to estimate the proportion of voters who support Candidate X. In a recent survey of 400 randomly selected likely voters, 210 indicated they would vote for Candidate X.
a. A hypothesis test was conducted to determine if the true proportion of voters for Candidate X is greater than 0.50. The p-value for this test was 0.02. Interpret this p-value in the context of the problem.
b. Describe how increasing the sample size, while keeping the confidence level the same, would affect the width of a confidence interval for the proportion of voters for Candidate X. Explain why this effect occurs.
c. If the pollster fails to reject the null hypothesis that the true proportion of voters for Candidate X is 0.50 when, in reality, it is 0.55, what type of error has occurred? Describe a consequence of this error in the context of the election.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Interprets p-value as the probability of observing a sample proportion as extreme as or more extreme than what was observed (210/400 = 0.525), assuming the true proportion of voters for Candidate X is 0.50 (or less than or equal to 0.50).
  • Part b (2pts): States that increasing the sample size would decrease the width of the confidence interval. Explains that a larger sample size leads to a smaller standard error (denominator of margin of error), thus reducing the margin of error and the interval width.
  • Part c (1pt): Identifies this as a Type II error. Describes a consequence, such as Candidate X's campaign might scale back efforts believing they don't have enough support, or news outlets might report the election is too close to call when Candidate X is actually favored.
