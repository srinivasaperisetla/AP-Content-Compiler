AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 18)

FRQ 1

A local school conducted a survey to determine the favorite color among its kindergarten students. The results from a random sample of 25 students are summarized below:
ColorNumber of Students
Red8
Blue6
Green4
Yellow3
Purple4
a. Create a frequency distribution table and a relative frequency distribution table for the favorite colors.
b. Construct a bar chart to graphically represent the distribution of favorite colors.
c. Describe the distribution of favorite colors among these kindergarten students based on your bar chart.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly constructs a frequency table (1 pt) and a relative frequency table (1 pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Constructs a correct bar chart with labeled axes and categories.
  • Part c (1pt): Describes the most popular color and least popular color or general trend.

FRQ 2

A group of environmental scientists is monitoring the dissolved oxygen levels (in mg/L) in a local river at 15 different sites. The measurements are as follows: $6.8, 7.2, 7.0, 6.9, 7.1, 8.5, 7.0, 6.7, 7.3, 7.1, 6.9, 7.2, 7.0, 6.8, 7.1$.
a. Construct a stem-and-leaf plot for the dissolved oxygen levels.
b. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of dissolved oxygen levels.
c. Calculate the median and the interquartile range (IQR) for these data.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Constructs a correct stem-and-leaf plot with a key (1 pt for plot, 1 pt for key).
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the shape (approximately symmetric/unimodal or slightly skewed), center (estimates around 7.0-7.1), and spread (range of values) (0.5 pt for shape, 0.5 pt for center, 0.5 pt for spread, 0.5 pt for context).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct median (1 pt) and IQR (1 pt).

FRQ 3

The weights of adult female grizzly bears in a certain region are approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 190$ kg and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 25$ kg.
a. Calculate the probability that a randomly selected adult female grizzly bear weighs less than $170$ kg.
b. A grizzly bear is considered unusually heavy if its weight is in the top 5% of all adult female grizzly bears. Calculate the minimum weight (in kg) for an unusually heavy bear.
c. An environmental scientist observes two bears, Bear X weighing $205$ kg and Bear Y weighing $180$ kg. Calculate the $z$-score for each bear and use them to compare the relative positions of their weights within the distribution.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates the correct probability $P(X < 170)$ (showing $z$-score and probability).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct $z$-score for the 95th percentile (1 pt) and converts it to the correct weight (1 pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct $z$-score for Bear X (0.5 pt) and Bear Y (0.5 pt) and correctly compares their relative positions based on the $z$-scores (1 pt).

FRQ 4

A researcher is studying the characteristics of undergraduate students at a large university. For a sample of students, the researcher collects data on their academic major, their current GPA, and their hometown population size.
a. For each of the three variables (academic major, current GPA, hometown population size), identify whether it is a categorical or quantitative variable.
b. The researcher constructs a histogram of the current GPAs for the sampled students, and it shows a distribution that is roughly symmetric and unimodal. Describe what this shape suggests about the academic performance of the students.
c. The researcher finds that the mean GPA is $3.25$ and the standard deviation is $0.30$. Interpret the meaning of the standard deviation of $0.30$ in the context of student GPAs.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly classifies each variable (academic major: categorical, current GPA: quantitative, hometown population size: quantitative).
  • Part b (2pts): Describes that most students have GPAs close to the center/mean, with fewer students having very high or very low GPAs, consistent with a unimodal, symmetric distribution (1 pt for description, 1 pt for linking to academic performance).
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the standard deviation as the typical distance/variation of a student's GPA from the mean GPA of $3.25$.

FRQ 5

A school surveyed 200 students about their favorite type of pet among four options: Dogs, Cats, Fish, and Birds. The results are shown in the frequency table.
Pet TypeFrequency
Dogs90
Cats60
Fish30
Birds20
a. Construct a relative frequency table for these data.
b. Describe the distribution of favorite pet types among these students.
c. Another survey of 150 teachers showed 75 preferred Dogs, 45 preferred Cats, 20 preferred Fish, and 10 preferred Birds. Compare the distribution of pet preferences for students and teachers.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates and presents relative frequencies for all four pet types in a table. Includes labels for pet type and relative frequency.
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies "Dogs" as the most preferred pet and describes the relative popularity of other pets (e.g., Cats second, Fish and Birds least preferred).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates relative frequencies for teachers' preferences. Makes at least two valid comparisons between the student and teacher distributions, addressing center (mode) and/or spread (distribution across categories) in context.
