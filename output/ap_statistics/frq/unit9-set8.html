AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data: Slopes (Set 8)

FRQ 1

A researcher wants to investigate if there is a linear relationship between the number of hours a student studies for a statistics exam and their score on the exam. A random sample of 30 students yields a least-squares regression line $\hat{y} = 45 + 5x$, where $x$ is the study hours and $y$ is the exam score. The standard error of the slope coefficient is $SE_b = 0.95$, and the standard deviation of the residuals is $s_e = 7.2$.
a. Verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the slope of the population regression line. (You may assume linearity, independence, and approximate normality of residuals based on diagnostic plots not shown.)
b. Calculate a $95\%$ confidence interval for the slope of the population regression line.
c. Interpret the calculated confidence interval in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies and verifies the equal variance condition (e.g., residual plot shows no obvious pattern or band with constant width) and identifies the random sample/independence condition.
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct calculation of the degrees of freedom ($df=28$) and the critical t-value ($t^* \approx 2.048$), then calculates the confidence interval using the formula $b \pm t^*SE_b$ (e.g., $5 \pm 2.048(0.95) \Rightarrow (3.05, 6.95)$).
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context, stating that we are $95\%$ confident that for each additional hour of study, the true mean exam score increases by an amount within the interval.

FRQ 2

An agricultural researcher wants to model the relationship between the amount of a new organic fertilizer (in kilograms per plot) and the yield of a certain crop (in bushels per plot). Data were collected from 25 randomly selected plots of land, and a least-squares regression line was calculated. The residual plot showed no apparent pattern and the residuals appeared to be normally distributed when viewed in a histogram. However, the scatterplot of yield versus fertilizer amount showed some increase in variability of yield as the amount of fertilizer increased.
a. Verify the conditions for performing a significance test for the slope of the population regression line based on the given information. Clearly state which conditions are met, which are not, and why.
b. The least-squares regression line was $\hat{y} = 50 + 2.5x$, where $x$ is the fertilizer amount and $y$ is the crop yield. The standard error of the slope coefficient was $SE_b = 0.8$. Calculate the test statistic for determining if there is a positive linear relationship between fertilizer amount and crop yield.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies and verifies the Independence (random selection) and Normality (residuals appear normal) conditions as met. Identifies and explains that the Equal Variance condition is violated due to increasing variability in the scatterplot, which makes inference less reliable, and therefore, the conditions for inference are not fully met.
  • Part b (2pts): States the null hypothesis $H_0: \beta = 0$ (or similar) and the alternative hypothesis $H_a: \beta > 0$. Shows correct calculation of the test statistic: $t = \frac{b - \beta_0}{SE_b} = \frac{2.5 - 0}{0.8} = 3.125$.

FRQ 3

A biologist is studying the relationship between the age of a certain species of tree (in years) and its height (in meters). For a random sample of 40 trees, a $95\%$ confidence interval for the slope of the population regression line relating height to age was calculated as $(0.15, 0.23)$. A colleague performs a similar study with a random sample of 100 trees and calculates a new $95\%$ confidence interval for the slope.
a. Interpret the $95\%$ confidence interval $(0.15, 0.23)$ in the context of this study.
b. Explain how increasing the sample size from 40 to 100 trees would affect the width of the $95\%$ confidence interval for the slope, assuming all other factors remain constant.
c. The biologist claims that, on average, for every additional year a tree ages, its height increases by more than $0.1$ meters. Using the confidence interval $(0.15, 0.23)$, justify whether this claim is supported.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly interprets the interval: We are $95\%$ confident that for each additional year of age, the true mean height of this tree species increases by between $0.15$ and $0.23$ meters.
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that a larger sample size ($n=100$) will lead to a smaller standard error for the slope ($SE_b$), which will result in a narrower confidence interval.
  • Part c (2pts): States that since the entire interval $(0.15, 0.23)$ is above $0.1$, there is convincing evidence to support the claim that the true mean increase in height is more than $0.1$ meters per year of age.
