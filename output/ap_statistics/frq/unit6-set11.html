AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 11)

FRQ 1

A school district implemented two different reading programs, Program A and Program B, in two different schools. To compare their effectiveness, a random sample of 200 students from School A (using Program A) and 180 students from School B (using Program B) were assessed for reading proficiency after one year. The results are summarized in the table below.
ProgramSample SizeProficient Readers
Program A200140
Program B180108
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the difference in the true proportion of proficient readers between Program A and Program B.
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the true proportion of proficient readers (Program A - Program B).
c. Interpret the 90% confidence interval in the context of the problem.
d. Based on this confidence interval, does there appear to be a significant difference in the true proportion of proficient readers between the two programs? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a two-sample z-interval for a difference in proportions and verifies conditions for both groups (random samples, 10% condition for both populations, large counts for both samples: all np̂ and n(1-p̂ ≥ 10 for both A and B).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the 90% confidence interval (e.g., (0.031, 0.169)).
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets the confidence interval in context (e.g., "We are 90% confident that the true proportion of proficient readers in Program A is between 3.1% and 16.9% higher than in Program B.").
  • Part d (1pt): States yes, and justifies with the confidence interval (e.g., "Yes, because the entire interval (0.031, 0.169) is above zero, suggesting that pA is significantly higher than pB.").

FRQ 2

A company wants to determine if there is a difference in the proportion of online purchases made by customers in two different age groups: Group 1 (18-30 years old) and Group 2 (31-45 years old). They conducted a survey of randomly selected customers from each group.
Age GroupSample SizeOnline Purchases
Group 1 (18-30)12070
Group 2 (31-45)15078
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test if there is a difference in the true proportion of online purchases between the two age groups.
b. Identify the appropriate inference method and verify the conditions for conducting this test.
c. Calculate the test statistic for this test.
d. Given that the p-value for this test is 0.082, interpret this p-value in context.
e. Based on the p-value and a significance level of α = 0.05, what conclusion should the company make? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States H0: p1 = p2 and Ha: p1 ≠ p2.
  • Part b (1pt): Identifies a two-sample z-test for a difference in proportions and verifies conditions (random samples, 10% condition for both populations, large counts for pooled data: n1p_pooled, n1(1-p_pooled), n2p_pooled, n2(1-p_pooled) all ≥ 10).
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates the correct pooled proportion (p̂_c = (70+78)/(120+150) ≈ 0.548) and test statistic (z ≈ -1.74).
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly interprets the p-value (e.g., "Assuming there is no difference in the true proportion of online purchases between the two age groups, there is an 8.2% chance of observing a sample difference as extreme as or more extreme than the one observed by random chance alone.").
  • Part e (1pt): Compares p-value to α and makes correct conclusion with justification (e.g., "Since p-value (0.082) > α (0.05), we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is not convincing evidence of a difference in the true proportion of online purchases between the two age groups.").

FRQ 3

FRQ 4

A school district wants to compare the effectiveness of two different online learning platforms, Platform X and Platform Y, for teaching calculus. A random sample of 80 students used Platform X and 100 students used Platform Y. At the end of the semester, 64 students using Platform X passed the calculus final, while 70 students using Platform Y passed. The district wants to determine if there is a significant difference in passing rates.
PlatformNumber of StudentsNumber Passed Final
X8064
Y10070
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses and identify the name of the appropriate statistical test.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your p-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what conclusion should the school district make regarding the difference in passing rates?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States correct H0 ($p_X=p_Y$) and Ha ($p_X \neq p_Y$) and identifies two-sample z-test for proportions.
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies random, normal (large counts for both groups using pooled p), and independence conditions.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates correct test statistic (z = 1.95) and p-value (0.0512 for two-sided).
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to alpha and states a correct conclusion in context (fail to reject H0, there is no convincing evidence of a difference in passing rates).

FRQ 5

A polling organization wants to estimate the proportion of registered voters in a large city who support a proposed new environmental policy. They randomly sample 500 registered voters, and 280 of them express support for the policy.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of registered voters in the city who support the policy.
c. Interpret the confidence interval calculated in part (b) in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies a one-sample z-interval for a proportion AND verifies Random, 10% condition, and Large Counts condition with calculations.
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct calculation of sample proportion, standard error, critical z-value, and interval limits.
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context.
