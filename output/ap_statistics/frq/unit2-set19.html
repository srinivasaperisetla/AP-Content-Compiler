FRQ 1

A school administration wants to investigate if there is a relationship between students' favorite subject (Math, Science, English) and their preferred study method (Group Study, Individual Study). They surveyed 200 randomly selected students and recorded the following data.
Favorite SubjectGroup StudyIndividual StudyTotal
Math304575
Science253560
English204565
Total75125200
a. Calculate the proportion of students who prefer Group Study.
b. Calculate the proportion of students who prefer Math given that they prefer Group Study.
c. Based on the data, does there appear to be an association between favorite subject and preferred study method? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates the overall proportion for Group Study ($75/200 = 0.375$).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the conditional proportion of students who prefer Math given Group Study ($30/75 = 0.40$).
  • Part c (2pts): Compares relevant conditional proportions (e.g., proportion of Group Study for Math vs. Science vs. English, or vice versa) and explains if they are similar or different, concluding about association.

FRQ 2

A car manufacturer wants to model the relationship between the weight of a car (in hundreds of pounds) and its fuel efficiency (in miles per gallon, mpg). A random sample of 15 car models was selected. The least-squares regression line for predicting fuel efficiency from car weight is given by $\hat{y} = 45.1 - 0.86x$, where $\hat{y}$ is the predicted fuel efficiency and $x$ is the car weight (in hundreds of pounds). The correlation coefficient is $r = -0.78$.
a. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
b. Interpret the $y$-intercept of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem. If appropriate, comment on whether the $y$-intercept makes sense.
c. Predict the fuel efficiency for a car that weighs 3500 pounds.
d. Describe the strength and direction of the linear relationship between car weight and fuel efficiency.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Explains that for every additional 100 pounds of car weight, the predicted fuel efficiency decreases by 0.86 mpg.
  • Part b (1pt): Interprets the y-intercept as the predicted fuel efficiency for a car with 0 weight, and states that it doesn't make sense as a car cannot have 0 weight and still function.
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates $\hat{y} = 45.1 - 0.86(35) = 15$ mpg, showing correct use of $x=35$.
  • Part d (1pt): States that there is a moderately strong, negative linear relationship between car weight and fuel efficiency.

FRQ 3

An agricultural scientist is investigating the relationship between the amount of fertilizer applied to a crop (in kg per hectare) and the resulting crop yield (in tons per hectare). Data from 12 experimental plots were collected. The initial scatterplot showed a curved relationship. To linearize the relationship, the scientist applied a base-10 logarithmic transformation to the crop yield, using $\log_{10}(\text{yield})$. The least-squares regression line for predicting $\log_{10}(\text{yield})$ from fertilizer amount ($x$) is $\widehat{\log_{10}(\text{yield})} = 0.5 + 0.02x$.
a. One data point from the original dataset was (50 kg, 0.8 tons). After transformation, this point is (50, $\log_{10}(0.8)$). Suppose this point significantly influences the regression line. Describe what "influential point" means in the context of this study.
b. If the point (50, 0.8) were removed, how would you expect the slope of the least-squares regression line to change? Justify your answer.
c. Using the given regression equation, predict the crop yield (in tons per hectare) for a fertilizer amount of 75 kg per hectare.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): An influential point is an observation that, if removed, would significantly change the slope and/or y-intercept of the least-squares regression line.
  • Part b (2pts): Explains that the point (50 kg, 0.8 tons) with $\log_{10}(0.8) \approx -0.097$ has high leverage ($x=50$ is likely a high $x$-value) and a large negative residual (observed $\log_{10}(0.8)$ is much lower than predicted $\widehat{\log_{10}(\text{yield})} = 0.5 + 0.02(50) = 1.5$). Removing this point, which pulls the line down at its high $x$-value, would cause the slope to increase (become steeper).
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates $\widehat{\log_{10}(\text{yield})} = 0.5 + 0.02(75) = 2$, and correctly transforms back to $\text{yield} = 10^2 = 100$ tons per hectare.

FRQ 4

A survey was conducted to investigate the relationship between a person's preferred method of news consumption (TV, Online, Print) and their age group (Under 30, 30-60, Over 60). The results from a random sample of 200 adults are summarized in the following table.
Preferred News MethodUnder 3030-60Over 60Total
TV10452580
Online40201070
Print10152550
Total608060200
a. Calculate the marginal distribution of preferred news consumption method.
b. Calculate the conditional distribution of preferred news consumption method for those in the "Under 30" age group.
c. Based on your calculations, describe any association between age group and preferred news consumption method.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates and lists marginal percentages for each news method (e.g., TV: $80/200=40\%$, Online: $70/200=35\%$, Print: $50/200=25\%$).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates and lists conditional percentages for Under 30 (e.g., TV: $10/60 \approx 16.7\%$, Online: $40/60 \approx 66.7\%$, Print: $10/60 \approx 16.7\%$).
  • Part c (2pts): Compares the conditional distribution from (b) to the marginal distribution or other age groups and describes a clear association, such as "Under 30 prefer online news significantly more than other age groups or the overall population."
