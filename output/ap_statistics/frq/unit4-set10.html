FRQ 1

A company manufactures computer chips. Historically, 3% of the chips produced are defective. A quality control engineer wants to understand the probability of finding defective chips in a batch.
a. Describe how you could use a simulation with a random number generator to estimate the probability that the first defective chip is found on or before the 5th chip inspected. You do not need to perform the simulation.
b. Based on the historical data, calculate the probability that exactly 2 out of the next 5 chips inspected are defective, assuming the chips are independent.
c. Interpret the probability calculated in part (b) in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Describes assigning digits for defective/non-defective chips (1pt) AND describes how to run trials until a defective is found or 5 chips are inspected, and count successes (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Identifies binomial distribution and shows correct setup (e.g., $P(X=2) = \binom{5}{2}(0.03)^2(0.97)^3$) (1pt) AND calculates the correct probability (0.0082) (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the probability in context (1pt)

FRQ 3

An online retailer ships packages using two different carriers, A and B. The weight (in pounds) of packages handled by Carrier A, $W_A$, has a mean of 15 pounds and a standard deviation of 3 pounds. The weight of packages handled by Carrier B, $W_B$, has a mean of 12 pounds and a standard deviation of 2 pounds. Assume that the weights of packages handled by Carrier A and Carrier B are independent.
a. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the total weight for a randomly selected package from Carrier A and a randomly selected package from Carrier B. Show your work.
b. The shipping cost for Carrier A is calculated as $C_A = 2W_A + 5$ dollars, and for Carrier B it is $C_B = 1.5W_B + 3$ dollars. Describe how the mean and standard deviation of the shipping cost for Carrier A would change if all weights were converted from pounds to kilograms ($1 \text{ pound} \approx 0.4536 \text{ kg}$). You do not need to calculate the new values.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (3pts): Calculates the mean of the total weight $E(W_A + W_B) = E(W_A) + E(W_B) = 15+12=27$ (1pt) AND calculates the variance of the total weight $Var(W_A + W_B) = Var(W_A) + Var(W_B) = 3^2+2^2 = 9+4=13$ (1pt) AND calculates the standard deviation of the total weight $\sigma_{W_A+W_B} = \sqrt{13} \approx 3.61$ (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): States that the mean would be multiplied by 0.4536 (or similar) (1pt) AND states that the standard deviation would be multiplied by 0.4536 (or similar) (1pt)

FRQ 4

A recent study claims that 80% of adults in a certain city own a smartphone. A market researcher randomly selects 10 adults from this city to verify the claim.
a. Verify that the conditions for a binomial distribution are met for the number of smartphone owners in this sample.
b. Calculate the probability that exactly 7 of the 10 selected adults own a smartphone. Show your work.
c. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the number of smartphone owners in a random sample of 10 adults from this city, assuming the claim is true.
d. If the researcher finds only 4 smartphone owners in the sample of 10, explain whether this finding would provide convincing evidence against the study's claim.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies fixed number of trials ($n=10$) (0.5pt), independent trials (0.5pt), two outcomes (own/don't own) (0.5pt), AND constant probability of success ($p=0.80$) (0.5pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Shows correct setup for binomial probability (e.g., $P(X=7) = \binom{10}{7}(0.80)^7(0.20)^3$) AND calculates the correct probability (0.2013) (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the mean $E(X) = np = 10(0.80) = 8$ (1pt) AND calculates the standard deviation $\sigma_X = \sqrt{np(1-p)} = \sqrt{10(0.80)(0.20)} = \sqrt{1.6} \approx 1.265$ (1pt)
  • Part d (1pt): Calculates $P(X \le 4)$ or $P(X=4)$ for context (e.g., $P(X \le 4) \approx 0.0063$) (0.5pt) AND explains that this low probability provides convincing evidence against the claim (0.5pt)

FRQ 5

a. Define a random variable and justify why a geometric distribution would be an appropriate model for the number of flights until the first instance of engine trouble occurs for this airline.
b. Calculate the probability that the first instance of engine trouble occurs on the 100th flight or later.
c. What is the expected number of flights until the first instance of engine trouble? Interpret this value in context.
d. Given that no engine trouble has occurred on the first 50 flights, what is the probability that the first instance of engine trouble occurs on the 100th flight or later?
