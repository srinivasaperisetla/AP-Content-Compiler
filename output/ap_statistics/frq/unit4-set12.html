AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 4: Probability, Random Variables, and Probability Distributions (Set 12)

FRQ 1

A company manufactures light bulbs, and 15% are defective. A quality control manager wants to estimate the probability that in a random sample of 10 bulbs, exactly 2 are defective.
a. Describe how you would set up and perform a simulation using a table of random digits to estimate the probability that exactly 2 out of 10 bulbs are defective. Clearly indicate what each digit represents and how you would count the number of defective bulbs.
b. Using the binomial probability formula, calculate the theoretical probability that exactly 2 out of 10 randomly selected bulbs are defective, assuming the company's claim is true.
c. If a simulation yielded an estimated probability of $0.20$, explain how this compares to your calculated theoretical probability from part (b).

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Assigns digits for defective/non-defective (1pt); Describes how to select 10 digits and count defective bulbs, repeating for multiple trials (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Sets up binomial probability with correct $n, k, p$ values and calculates $P(X=2) = \binom{10}{2}(0.15)^2(0.85)^8 \approx 0.2759$ (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Compares the simulated probability ($0.20$) to the theoretical probability ($0.2759$) and explains the difference (e.g., simulation is an estimate, not exact; closer with more trials) (1pt).

FRQ 2

A recent study claims that 70% of high school students use social media for more than 3 hours a day. A random sample of 15 high school students is selected. Let $X$ be the number of students in the sample who use social media for more than 3 hours a day.
a. Justify why $X$ can be modeled by a binomial distribution.
b. Calculate the probability that exactly 12 students in the sample use social media for more than 3 hours a day.
c. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of $X$.
d. Interpret the mean calculated in part (c) in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States the four binomial conditions are met: Fixed number of trials ($n=15$), two outcomes (success/failure), independent trials (random sample), constant probability of success ($p=0.70$) (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Uses binomial formula $P(X=12) = \binom{15}{12}(0.70)^{12}(0.30)^3 \approx 0.1700$ (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates mean $\mu_X = np = 15(0.70) = 10.5$ (1pt); Calculates standard deviation $\sigma_X = \sqrt{np(1-p)} = \sqrt{15(0.70)(0.30)} = \sqrt{3.15} \approx 1.7748$ (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets $\mu_X=10.5$ as the average number of students in many such samples of 15 that would use social media for more than 3 hours a day (1pt).

FRQ 3

An automobile manufacturer knows that 1 out of every 20 cars produced has a minor defect. Cars are inspected sequentially as they come off the assembly line.
a. What is the probability that the first car with a minor defect is the 5th car inspected?
b. What is the expected number of cars that need to be inspected until the first car with a minor defect is found? Interpret this value in context.
c. Given that the first 3 cars inspected had no minor defects, what is the probability that the first car with a minor defect is found on the 5th car inspected?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies this as a geometric distribution with $p = 1/20 = 0.05$ and calculates $P(X=5) = (0.95)^{4}(0.05) \approx 0.0407$ (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the expected value $E(X) = 1/p = 1/0.05 = 20$ (1pt); Interprets $E(X)=20$ as the long-run average number of cars inspected until the first defect is found (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Recognizes the memoryless property of geometric distribution or uses conditional probability directly. $P(X=5
  • X>3) = P(X=2 \text{ for new count}) = (0.95)^1(0.05) \approx 0.0475$ (1pt for correct understanding of independence/memoryless, 1pt for correct calculation) (2pts).

FRQ 4

A small coffee shop records the time (in minutes) a customer spends ordering at the counter, $C$, and the time a customer spends waiting for their drink to be prepared, $W$. Both $C$ and $W$ are independent random variables. The mean and standard deviation for each are: $E(C) = 2.5$ minutes, $\sigma_C = 0.8$ minutes; $E(W) = 3.0$ minutes, $\sigma_W = 1.2$ minutes. A customer's total time in the shop, $T$, is given by $T = C + W$. The shop also offers a premium service where the ordering time is reduced by 1 minute and then multiplied by 0.5. Let $C'$ be the ordering time for the premium service.
a. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of $C'$, the ordering time for the premium service.
b. Describe the effect of the linear transformation on the mean and standard deviation of the ordering time from $C$ to $C'$.
c. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the total time $T$ a regular customer spends in the shop.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): For $C' = 0.5(C-1)$, calculates $E(C') = 0.5(E(C)-1) = 0.5(2.5-1) = 0.5(1.5) = 0.75$ minutes (1pt); Calculates $\sigma_{C'} =
  • 0.5
  • \sigma_C = 0.5(0.8) = 0.4$ minutes (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that subtracting a constant affects only the mean (shifts it), not the standard deviation; multiplying by a constant affects both the mean (scaled by the constant) and the standard deviation (scaled by the absolute value of the constant) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $E(T) = E(C+W) = E(C) + E(W) = 2.5 + 3.0 = 5.5$ minutes (1pt); Calculates $\sigma_T = \sqrt{\sigma_C^2 + \sigma_W^2} = \sqrt{(0.8)^2 + (1.2)^2} = \sqrt{0.64 + 1.44} = \sqrt{2.08} \approx 1.4422$ minutes (1pt).
