AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 2: Exploring Two-Variable Data (Set 20)

FRQ 1

A researcher is studying the relationship between the number of hours a student spends studying for a statistics exam and the score they receive on the exam. Data from 10 students were collected and displayed in the scatterplot.
a. Describe the relationship between study hours and exam scores based on the scatterplot, including direction, form, and strength.
b. The correlation coefficient for these data is calculated to be $r = 0.88$. Interpret this value in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (strong).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly interprets $r=0.88$ as a strong positive linear association between study hours and exam scores.

FRQ 2

A scientist is studying the growth of a certain plant species. They recorded the height of plants (in cm) after a certain number of days since germination. A linear model was fit to the data, and a residual plot was generated.
a. The observed height of a plant after 10 days was $18$ cm. The linear model predicted a height of $16.5$ cm for a plant after 10 days. Calculate the residual for this observation.
b. Based on the residual plot provided, describe whether a linear model is appropriate for these data. Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculates residual as $18 - 16.5 = 1.5$ cm.
  • Part b (2pts): States that a linear model is NOT appropriate AND justifies by describing a clear pattern (e.g., curved pattern, U-shape) in the residual plot.

FRQ 4

A human resources department is studying the relationship between an employee's years of experience and their annual salary (in thousands of dollars). The least-squares regression line for this relationship is given by $\hat{\text{Salary}} = 45 + 2.5 \times \text{Years of Experience}$.
a. Predict the annual salary for an employee with 10 years of experience.
b. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
c. Interpret the $y$-intercept of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem. Does this interpretation make practical sense?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Calculation: $\hat{\text{Salary}} = 45 + 2.5(10) = 45 + 25 = 70$. The predicted salary is \$70,000.
  • Part b (2pts): Interpretation: For each additional year of experience, the predicted annual salary increases by \$2,500.
  • Part c (2pts): Interpretation: The $y$-intercept of 45 means that an employee with 0 years of experience is predicted to have an annual salary of \$45,000. This might make practical sense as an entry-level salary.
