AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 7: Inference for Quantitative Data: Means (Set 14)

FRQ 1

A consumer advocacy group wants to estimate the average battery life (in hours) of a new smartphone model. They randomly select 30 phones and test them under identical conditions. The sample mean battery life is $\bar{x} = 26.5$ hours with a sample standard deviation of $s = 3.2$ hours.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the true mean battery life of this smartphone model.
b. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the true mean battery life of this smartphone model.
c. Interpret the 95% confidence interval in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample t-interval (1pt) and verifies conditions (1pt for Random sample, Normal/Large sample, Independent).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the t-interval using $\bar{x} \pm t^* \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$ (1pt for correct values, 1pt for correct interval).
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context.

FRQ 2

A company claims that its new energy-efficient light bulbs have an average lifespan of more than 10,000 hours. A consumer watchdog organization wants to test this claim. They obtain a random sample of 25 bulbs and find their average lifespan to be $\bar{x} = 10,250$ hours with a standard deviation of $s = 500$ hours. Assume the organization wants to test this claim at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for this test.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. State the conclusion of the test in context, based on the p-value and the significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0: \mu = 10000$ and $H_a: \mu > 10000$.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions: Random sample, $n < 30$ but assume normal (or state need to check normality from sample data, given sample size is 25, population assumed normal or sample nearly normal), Independence ($N \ge 10n$).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates $t = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{s/\sqrt{n}}$ (1pt) and finds the correct p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ and states a correct conclusion in context (e.g., "Since p-value > $\alpha$, we fail to reject $H_0$. There is not sufficient evidence...").

FRQ 3

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug designed to lower cholesterol. They recruit 12 patients and measure their cholesterol levels before and after taking the drug for one month. The differences in cholesterol levels (Before - After) for the 12 patients are summarized as follows: mean difference $\bar{d} = 15$ mg/dL, and standard deviation of differences $s_d = 8$ mg/dL. The company wants to determine if the drug significantly reduces cholesterol levels.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for a test to determine if the drug significantly reduces cholesterol levels.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this matched-pairs t-test.
d. Based on the p-value, what is your conclusion regarding the drug's effectiveness in reducing cholesterol levels? Use a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0: \mu_d = 0$ and $H_a: \mu_d > 0$, where $\mu_d$ is the true mean difference (Before - After).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions: Random assignment/sampling for patients, Normal/Large sample (given $n=12$, state need to check normality of differences), Independence ($N \ge 10n$).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates $t = \frac{\bar{d} - 0}{s_d/\sqrt{n}}$ (1pt) and finds the correct p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ and states a correct conclusion in context (e.g., "Since p-value < $\alpha$, we reject $H_0$. There is sufficient evidence...").

FRQ 4

A researcher wants to compare the average weekly study hours of students at two different universities, University A and University B. A random sample of 35 students from University A reported a mean of $\bar{x}_A = 22.5$ hours with a standard deviation of $s_A = 5.8$ hours. A random sample of 40 students from University B reported a mean of $\bar{x}_B = 20.0$ hours with a standard deviation of $s_B = 6.3$ hours.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the difference in the true mean weekly study hours between students at University A and University B.
b. Construct a 90% confidence interval for the difference in true mean weekly study hours $(\mu_A - \mu_B)$.
c. Interpret the 90% confidence interval in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies two-sample t-interval (1pt) and verifies conditions (1pt for two independent random samples, Large samples (both $n \ge 30$), Independence within populations).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the two-sample t-interval using $(\bar{x}_A - \bar{x}_B) \pm t^* \sqrt{\frac{s_A^2}{n_A} + \frac{s_B^2}{n_B}}$ (1pt for correct values, 1pt for correct interval).
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context.

FRQ 5

A school district implements a new curriculum (Method X) in one high school and continues with the traditional curriculum (Method Y) in another comparable high school. At the end of the year, a random sample of 50 students from Method X school had an average math test score of $\bar{x}_X = 82$ with a standard deviation of $s_X = 7$. A random sample of 45 students from Method Y school had an average math test score of $\bar{x}_Y = 78$ with a standard deviation of $s_Y = 9$. The district wants to determine if Method X significantly improves math test scores compared to Method Y. Use a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for this investigation.
b. Verify the conditions for performing a significance test for the difference in mean test scores.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value and the significance level, what conclusion can be made regarding the new curriculum's effectiveness?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0: \mu_X - \mu_Y = 0$ (or $\mu_X = \mu_Y$) and $H_a: \mu_X - \mu_Y > 0$ (or $\mu_X > \mu_Y$).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions: Two independent random samples, Large samples (both $n \ge 30$), Independence within populations ($N \ge 10n$).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates $t = \frac{(\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y) - 0}{\sqrt{\frac{s_X^2}{n_X} + \frac{s_Y^2}{n_Y}}}$ (1pt) and finds the correct p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value to $\alpha$ and states a correct conclusion in context (e.g., "Since p-value < $\alpha$, we reject $H_0$. There is sufficient evidence...").
