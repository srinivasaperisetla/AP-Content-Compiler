AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 5: Sampling Distributions (Set 14)

FRQ 1

A company produces a certain type of battery. Historically, 15% of these batteries are defective. A quality control manager takes a random sample of 100 batteries from a large production batch.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of the sample proportion of defective batteries by calculating its mean and standard deviation.
b. Verify that the conditions for using a normal distribution to approximate the sampling distribution of the sample proportion are met.
c. What is the probability that the sample of 100 batteries will contain 10% or fewer defective batteries?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates mean $\mu_{\hat{p}} = 0.15$ (1pt) and standard deviation $\sigma_{\hat{p}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.15(0.85)}{100}} \approx 0.0357$ (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Checks $n p = 100(0.15) = 15 \ge 10$ and $n(1-p) = 100(0.85) = 85 \ge 10$ (1pt). States that because conditions are met, the sampling distribution is approximately normal (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the $z$-score $z = \frac{0.10 - 0.15}{0.0357} \approx -1.40$ (1pt). Uses the $z$-score to find $P(\hat{p} \le 0.10) \approx 0.0808$ (1pt).

FRQ 2

The weight of adult male grizzly bears in a certain region follows a distribution with a mean of $\mu = 500$ pounds and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 75$ pounds. A researcher captures and weighs a random sample of 36 adult male grizzly bears.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of the sample mean weight of the grizzly bears by calculating its mean and standard deviation.
b. Explain why the sampling distribution of the sample mean can be approximated by a normal distribution, even if the population distribution of bear weights is not known to be normal.
c. What is the probability that the sample mean weight of the 36 grizzly bears will be greater than 525 pounds?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates mean $\mu_{\bar{x}} = 500$ pounds (1pt) and standard deviation $\sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{75}{\sqrt{36}} = 12.5$ pounds (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that because the sample size $n=36$ is large (typically $n \ge 30$), the Central Limit Theorem (CLT) applies, allowing the sampling distribution of the sample mean to be approximated by a normal distribution (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the $z$-score $z = \frac{525 - 500}{12.5} = 2.00$ (1pt). Uses the $z$-score to find $P(\bar{x} > 525) \approx 0.0228$ (1pt).

FRQ 3

A pharmaceutical company is testing two new allergy medications, Medication A and Medication B. In a large clinical trial, 25% of patients taking Medication A reported drowsiness, while 20% of patients taking Medication B reported drowsiness. Researchers select a random sample of 100 patients who took Medication A and an independent random sample of 120 patients who took Medication B.
a. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution for the difference in sample proportions ($\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$).
b. Verify that the conditions are met for the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions to be approximately normal.
c. What is the probability that the sample proportion of patients reporting drowsiness for Medication A is at least 10 percentage points higher than for Medication B?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates mean $\mu_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = 0.25 - 0.20 = 0.05$ (1pt). Calculates standard deviation $\sigma_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = \sqrt{\frac{0.25(0.75)}{100} + \frac{0.20(0.80)}{120}} = \sqrt{0.001875 + 0.001333} \approx \sqrt{0.003208} \approx 0.0566$ (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies conditions for both samples: For A: $n_A p_A = 100(0.25)=25 \ge 10$, $n_A(1-p_A) = 100(0.75)=75 \ge 10$. For B: $n_B p_B = 120(0.20)=24 \ge 10$, $n_B(1-p_B) = 120(0.80)=96 \ge 10$ (1pt for all four checks). States that because all conditions are met and samples are independent, the sampling distribution of the difference is approximately normal (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the $z$-score for a difference of $0.10$: $z = \frac{0.10 - 0.05}{0.0566} \approx 0.88$ (1pt). Uses the $z$-score to find $P(\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B \ge 0.10) \approx P(Z \ge 0.88) \approx 0.1894$ (1pt).

FRQ 4

A consumer advocacy group wants to compare the average fuel efficiency of two car models, Model X and Model Y. Fuel efficiency for Model X cars has a mean of $\mu_X = 35$ mpg and a standard deviation of $\sigma_X = 3.5$ mpg. For Model Y cars, the mean is $\mu_Y = 32$ mpg and the standard deviation is $\sigma_Y = 4.0$ mpg. The group takes a random sample of 40 Model X cars and an independent random sample of 50 Model Y cars.
a. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution for the difference in sample means ($\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y$).
b. Explain why the sampling distribution for the difference in sample means can be approximated by a normal distribution.
c. What is the probability that the sample mean fuel efficiency of Model X cars is less than the sample mean fuel efficiency of Model Y cars?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates mean $\mu_{\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y} = 35 - 32 = 3$ mpg (1pt). Calculates standard deviation $\sigma_{\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y} = \sqrt{\frac{3.5^2}{40} + \frac{4.0^2}{50}} = \sqrt{\frac{12.25}{40} + \frac{16}{50}} = \sqrt{0.30625 + 0.32} = \sqrt{0.62625} \approx 0.7913$ mpg (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that because both sample sizes ($n_X=40$ and $n_Y=50$) are large (both $\ge 30$), the Central Limit Theorem (CLT) applies to both sample means, and thus to their difference, allowing the sampling distribution of the difference in sample means to be approximated by a normal distribution (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the $z$-score for a difference of $0$: $z = \frac{0 - 3}{0.7913} \approx -3.79$ (1pt). Uses the $z$-score to find $P(\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y < 0) \approx P(Z < -3.79) \approx 0.0001$ (1pt).

FRQ 5

A town planning committee wants to estimate the proportion of households that support building a new community center. They decide to survey a random sample of 50 households. The true proportion of households supporting the center in the town is $p=0.6$.
a. Define what it means for an estimator to be unbiased in the context of estimating the population proportion of households supporting the center. Is the sample proportion $\hat{p}$ an unbiased estimator of $p$? Justify your answer.
b. The committee conducts the survey and finds 28 households support the center. Calculate the point estimate for the proportion of households supporting the center.
c. Describe how you would use a simulation with random numbers to estimate the sampling distribution of the sample proportion $\hat{p}$ for samples of size 50 if the true proportion is $p=0.6$. Outline the steps clearly, assuming you have access to a random number generator.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Defines an unbiased estimator as one whose sampling distribution has a mean equal to the true population parameter (1pt). States that $\hat{p}$ is an unbiased estimator of $p$ because $\mu_{\hat{p}} = p$ (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates the point estimate $\hat{p} = \frac{28}{50} = 0.56$ (1pt).
  • Part c (3pts): Step 1: Assign digits (e.g., 0-5 for support, 6-9 for not support, or similar split for 60% and 40%) (1pt). Step 2: Generate 50 random digits (representing 50 households) and count the number of 'support' outcomes, then calculate the sample proportion for this sample (1pt). Step 3: Repeat steps 1 and 2 many times (e.g., hundreds or thousands of times) to create a distribution of sample proportions (1pt).
