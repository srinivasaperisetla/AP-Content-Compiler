AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 4)

FRQ 1

A recent survey of 800 randomly selected U.S. adults found that 360 of them indicated they regularly use public transportation. Construct and interpret a 95 percent confidence interval for the proportion of all U.S. adults who regularly use public transportation.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure by name and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate the 95 percent confidence interval for the population proportion of U.S. adults who regularly use public transportation.
c. Interpret the confidence interval in context. Based on this interval, can it be concluded that more than 40 percent of U.S. adults regularly use public transportation? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a one-sample z-interval for a proportion (1pt); Verifies conditions (Random, Normal, Independent) (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates the correct confidence interval (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the interval in context (1pt); Justifies conclusion about 40% based on interval (1pt)

FRQ 2

A company claims that 75% of its new product line passes a quality control test. A consumer advocacy group suspects the proportion is lower. They test a random sample of 200 products and find that 138 pass the test.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for this investigation. Identify the appropriate inference method and verify the conditions for performing this test.
b. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
c. Based on the p-value, what conclusion should the consumer advocacy group make at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level? Interpret the p-value in the context of this problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States $H_0: p = 0.75$ and $H_a: p < 0.75$ (1pt); Identifies one-sample z-test for proportion and verifies conditions (1pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic $z$ (1pt); Calculates the correct p-value (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Makes a correct conclusion based on the p-value and $\alpha$ (1pt); Interprets the p-value in context (1pt)

FRQ 3

They conduct a hypothesis test where the null hypothesis is $H_0: p = 0.60$ and the alternative hypothesis is $H_a: p > 0.60$, where $p$ is the true proportion of patients for whom the drug reduces blood pressure.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this problem.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this problem.
c. Suppose the company decides to increase the sample size for their clinical trial. Explain how this change would affect the probability of making a Type II error, assuming the significance level $\alpha$ remains the same.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part b (1pt): Correctly describes Type II error in context (1pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that increasing sample size decreases the probability of a Type II error (1pt)

FRQ 4

A medical researcher wants to compare the effectiveness of two different treatments for a certain skin condition. Treatment A was administered to 120 randomly selected patients, and 96 showed improvement. Treatment B was administered to 100 randomly selected patients, and 70 showed improvement.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use to construct a confidence interval for the difference between the true proportions of patients who show improvement for Treatment A and Treatment B ($p_A - p_B$).
b. Calculate a 90 percent confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions of patients who show improvement for Treatment A and Treatment B.
c. Interpret the 90 percent confidence interval in context. Does the interval provide convincing evidence that Treatment A is more effective than Treatment B? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a two-sample z-interval for proportions (1pt); Verifies conditions for both samples (Random, Normal, Independent) (1pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates the correct confidence interval (1pt)
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the interval in context (1pt); Justifies conclusion about effectiveness based on interval (1pt)

FRQ 5

A company claims that 80% of its customers are satisfied with their new product. To verify this claim, an independent consumer group surveyed a random sample of 250 customers and found that 185 reported being satisfied.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the true proportion of satisfied customers.
b. Construct a 90% confidence interval for the true proportion of satisfied customers.
c. Interpret the confidence interval in context.
d. Based on this confidence interval, does the company's claim of 80% customer satisfaction seem plausible? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample $z$-interval for proportion (1pt). Verifies conditions: Random sample (given), $n\hat{p} = 250(185/250) = 185 \ge 10$ and $n(1-\hat{p}) = 250(65/250) = 65 \ge 10$ (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates $\hat{p} = 185/250 = 0.74$. Calculates $SE = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.74(0.26)}{250}} \approx 0.0276$. Finds $z^* = 1.645$. Interval is $0.74 \pm 1.645(0.0276) = 0.74 \pm 0.0454$ which is $(0.6946, 0.7854)$ (2pts for correct interval).
  • Part c (1pt): We are 90% confident that the true proportion of all satisfied customers is between $0.6946$ and $0.7854$. (1pt for correct interpretation in context).
  • Part d (1pt): The claim of 80% (or $0.80$) does not seem plausible because $0.80$ is not contained within the 90% confidence interval $(0.6946, 0.7854)$. (1pt for correct justification).
