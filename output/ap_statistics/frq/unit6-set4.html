AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 4)

FRQ 1

A recent poll of 800 registered voters found that 456 plan to vote in favor of a new school bond. Construct and interpret a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of all registered voters who plan to vote in favor of the new school bond.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the population proportion.
b. Calculate the 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of voters who plan to vote in favor of the new school bond.
c. Interpret the confidence interval in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies one-sample Z-interval for proportion and verifies random sample, 10% condition, and Large Counts condition.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the confidence interval showing formula or calculator input.
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context.

FRQ 2

A company claims that no more than 15% of its new product line has a minor defect. A consumer advocacy group randomly sampled 200 products and found 38 with minor defects. Is there convincing statistical evidence at the significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$ that the company's claim is false?
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses and verify the conditions for performing a significance test.
b. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
c. Interpret the p-value in the context of the problem and make a conclusion regarding the company's claim using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States correct hypotheses (H0: p ≤ 0.15, Ha: p > 0.15) and verifies random sample, 10% condition, and Large Counts condition.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic (z-score) and corresponding p-value.
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the p-value in context and makes a correct conclusion based on comparing p-value to alpha, linking back to the alternative hypothesis.

FRQ 3

A researcher wants to compare the effectiveness of two different exercise programs (Program A and Program B) for reducing cholesterol. 150 participants were randomly assigned to Program A, and 120 participants were randomly assigned to Program B. After six months, 90 participants in Program A showed a significant reduction in cholesterol, while 60 participants in Program B showed a significant reduction. Construct and interpret a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the true proportions of participants who show a significant cholesterol reduction for the two programs (Program A minus Program B).
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the difference of two population proportions.
b. Calculate the 90% confidence interval for the difference in proportions (Program A minus Program B).
c. Interpret the confidence interval in context and explain what it suggests about the difference in effectiveness between the two programs.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies two-sample Z-interval for proportions and verifies random assignment, 10% condition for each sample, and Large Counts condition for each sample.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the confidence interval showing formula or calculator input.
  • Part c (2pts): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context and draws a conclusion about the difference (e.g., if zero is included/excluded).

FRQ 4

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug for treating a certain condition. In a double-blind experiment, 300 patients were randomly assigned to the new drug group, and 250 patients were randomly assigned to a placebo group. After three months, 210 patients in the new drug group showed improvement, while 150 patients in the placebo group showed improvement. Does this study provide convincing statistical evidence at the $\alpha = 0.01$ significance level that the new drug is more effective than the placebo?
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to address the research question and verify the conditions for performing a significance test for the difference of two population proportions.
b. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
c. Interpret the p-value in the context of the problem and make a conclusion regarding the effectiveness of the new drug compared to the placebo, using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States correct hypotheses (H0: p_drug = p_placebo, Ha: p_drug > p_placebo) and verifies random assignment, 10% condition for each sample, and Large Counts condition for pooled samples.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct pooled test statistic (z-score) and corresponding p-value.
  • Part c (2pts): Interprets the p-value in context and makes a correct conclusion based on comparing p-value to alpha, linking back to the alternative hypothesis.

FRQ 5

A local bakery claims that 80% of its customers purchase a coffee with their pastry. A new manager wants to test this claim. They decide to take a random sample of customers to see if the true proportion is different from 80%.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this scenario.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this scenario.
c. If the manager wants to estimate the true proportion of customers who purchase coffee with their pastry with 95% confidence and a margin of error of no more than 3 percentage points, calculate the minimum sample size required.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly describes Type I error (rejecting true null) in the context of the bakery's claim.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly describes Type II error (failing to reject false null) in the context of the bakery's claim.
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the minimum sample size using the margin of error formula, showing work and rounding up to the nearest whole number, using 0.8 for p-hat.
