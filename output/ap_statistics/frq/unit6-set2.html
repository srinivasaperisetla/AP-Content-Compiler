AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 2)

FRQ 1

A nationwide survey of 1,500 randomly selected adults found that 930 reported regularly drinking coffee. The researchers want to estimate the true proportion of all adults who regularly drink coffee with a 95% confidence interval.
a. Identify the appropriate inference method and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for a population proportion.
b. Calculate the 95% confidence interval for the proportion of adults who regularly drink coffee.
c. Interpret the confidence interval in the context of this problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a one-sample z-interval for a proportion (1pt) and verifies conditions (random sample, $n\hat{p} \ge 10$, $n(1-\hat{p}) \ge 10$, and $N \ge 10n$) (1pt). For conditions: $1500(0.62)=930 \ge 10$, $1500(0.38)=570 \ge 10$, $1500 \le 10\%$ of all adults (assumed).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates $\hat{p} = 930/1500 = 0.62$ and the interval $0.62 \pm 1.96 \sqrt{\frac{0.62(0.38)}{1500}}$ leading to $(0.595, 0.645)$ (2pts for correct interval, 1pt for correct $\hat{p}$ and standard error).
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation, stating "We are 95% confident that the true proportion of all adults who regularly drink coffee is between 0.595 and 0.645."

FRQ 2

A company claims that 90% of its new light bulbs last at least 1,000 hours. A consumer advocacy group suspects that the true proportion is lower. They test a random sample of 200 light bulbs and find that 170 lasted at least 1,000 hours.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a test of the company's claim, and identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value, what conclusion should the consumer advocacy group make at a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States $H_0: p = 0.90$ and $H_a: p < 0.90$ (1pt) and identifies a one-sample z-test for a population proportion (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies conditions: Random sample (given), Normal condition $np_0 = 200(0.90) = 180 \ge 10$ and $n(1-p_0) = 200(0.10) = 20 \ge 10$ (1pt), and Independence (sample size $\le 10\%$ of population of light bulbs) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $\hat{p} = 170/200 = 0.85$. Test statistic $z = \frac{0.85 - 0.90}{\sqrt{\frac{0.90(0.10)}{200}}} = \frac{-0.05}{0.0212} \approx -2.358$ (1pt). P-value $P(Z < -2.358) \approx 0.0092$ (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Since the p-value (0.0092) is less than $\alpha = 0.05$, reject $H_0$. Conclude there is significant evidence that the true proportion of light bulbs lasting at least 1,000 hours is less than 90%.

FRQ 3

A pharmaceutical company wants to compare the effectiveness of two different medications, Drug X and Drug Y, in reducing allergy symptoms. In a randomized controlled trial, 120 patients received Drug X, and 90 reported significant relief. Another 100 patients received Drug Y, and 65 reported significant relief. Test if there is a significant difference in the proportions of patients who experience relief for the two drugs at a 5% significance level.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses, and identify the appropriate inference method for this situation.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on the p-value, what conclusion should the company make about the effectiveness of Drug X versus Drug Y? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): States $H_0: p_X = p_Y$ or $H_0: p_X - p_Y = 0$ and $H_a: p_X \ne p_Y$ or $H_a: p_X - p_Y \ne 0$ (1pt). Identifies a two-sample z-test for a difference in population proportions (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Verifies conditions: Random assignment/Independent random samples (given), Large Counts ($n_X \hat{p}_X = 90 \ge 10$, $n_X(1-\hat{p}_X) = 30 \ge 10$, $n_Y \hat{p}_Y = 65 \ge 10$, $n_Y(1-\hat{p}_Y) = 35 \ge 10$) (1pt). Independence within each sample ($N_X \ge 10n_X$, $N_Y \ge 10n_Y$) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $\hat{p}_X = 90/120 = 0.75$, $\hat{p}_Y = 65/100 = 0.65$. Calculates pooled proportion $\hat{p}_c = (90+65)/(120+100) = 155/220 \approx 0.7045$. Test statistic $z = \frac{0.75 - 0.65}{\sqrt{0.7045(1-0.7045)(\frac{1}{120} + \frac{1}{100})}} = \frac{0.10}{\sqrt{0.7045(0.2955)(0.00833+0.01)}} \approx \frac{0.10}{0.066} \approx 1.515$ (1pt). P-value $2 \times P(Z > 1.515) \approx 2 \times 0.0649 \approx 0.1298$ (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Since the p-value (0.1298) is greater than $\alpha = 0.05$, fail to reject $H_0$. Conclude there is not sufficient evidence to suggest a significant difference in the proportions of patients experiencing relief between Drug X and Drug Y.

FRQ 4

A manufacturer claims that less than 5% of their product is defective. An independent testing agency conducts a hypothesis test to check this claim, using $H_0: p=0.05$ versus $H_a: p<0.05$ at a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this problem.
b. What is the probability of committing a Type I error for this test?
c. Describe a Type II error in the context of this problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): A Type I error would occur if the agency concludes that the true proportion of defective products is less than 5% (rejects $H_0$), when in reality, the true proportion of defective products is 5% or more (the null hypothesis is true).
  • Part b (1pt): The probability of a Type I error is equal to the significance level, $\alpha = 0.01$.
  • Part c (1pt): A Type II error would occur if the agency fails to conclude that the true proportion of defective products is less than 5% (fails to reject $H_0$), when in reality, the true proportion of defective products actually is less than 5% (the alternative hypothesis is true).

FRQ 5

A political pollster wants to estimate the proportion of voters who support a particular candidate. They want to construct a 95% confidence interval with a margin of error of no more than 3 percentage points (0.03).
a. Calculate the minimum sample size required for this poll. Assume no prior estimate for the proportion is available.
b. If the pollster decides to use a 99% confidence level instead of 95%, describe how this change would affect the required sample size, assuming the same margin of error is desired. Explain your reasoning.
c. Suppose the pollster conducts the survey with $n=1000$ voters and constructs a 95% confidence interval. How would doubling the sample size to $n=2000$ affect the width of the confidence interval, assuming the same confidence level and sample proportion?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Uses the formula $ME = z^* \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}}$. For maximum sample size, use $\hat{p} = 0.5$. $0.03 = 1.96 \sqrt{\frac{0.5(0.5)}{n}}$ (1pt). Solves for $n$: $n = \left(\frac{1.96 \times 0.5}{0.03}\right)^2 \approx (32.666)^2 \approx 1067.11$. So, a minimum sample size of $n=1068$ is required (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Increasing the confidence level from 95% to 99% (which means increasing $z^*$) would increase the required sample size to achieve the same margin of error. This is because a higher confidence level demands a larger interval, and to keep the margin of error constant, the sample size must increase to reduce the standard error.
  • Part c (1pt): Doubling the sample size from $n=1000$ to $n=2000$ would decrease the width of the confidence interval by a factor of $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$. The width is proportional to $\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}$, so multiplying $n$ by 2 means the width is multiplied by $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$.
