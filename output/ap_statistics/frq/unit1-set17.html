AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data (Set 17)

FRQ 1

A local ice cream shop surveyed 150 customers about their favorite flavor from a selection of four popular options. The results are summarized in the table below.
FlavorNumber of Customers
Vanilla45
Chocolate60
Strawberry25
Mint Chip20
a. Construct a relative frequency table for the given data.
b. Create a bar chart to display the distribution of favorite ice cream flavors.
c. Describe what the bar chart from part (b) reveals about customer preferences.
d. The shop owner is considering adding a new flavor if no single flavor dominates preference (i.e., no flavor has more than 40% of the vote). Based on your chart, should the owner proceed with adding a new flavor? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly constructs a relative frequency table with flavors and their corresponding relative frequencies (proportions or percentages).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly constructs a bar chart with labeled axes, appropriate scale, and bars of correct height, representing the favorite ice cream flavors.
  • Part c (1pt): Describes the most and least preferred flavors and potentially mentions the overall spread of preferences among the categories, in context.
  • Part d (2pts): Calculates the proportion for the dominant flavor, compares it to 40%, and provides a correct justification based on this comparison.

FRQ 2

A statistics class collected data on the number of text messages sent by 25 randomly selected students in a single day. The data are as follows: 8, 15, 22, 25, 30, 31, 32, 35, 38, 40, 42, 45, 48, 50, 50, 52, 55, 58, 60, 62, 65, 70, 75, 80, 120
a. Is the variable "number of text messages sent" categorical or quantitative? Justify your answer.
b. Construct a stemplot of the data. Be sure to include a key.
c. Based on the stemplot, describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of text messages sent. Are there any unusual features or outliers?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies the variable as quantitative and provides a correct justification (e.g., values are numerical measurements for which arithmetic operations make sense).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly constructs a stemplot, showing all stems and leaves, with a correct key.
  • Part c (2pts): Describes the shape (e.g., skewed right, unimodal), estimates the center, describes the spread, and identifies any potential outliers, all in context.

FRQ 3

A small company recorded the salaries (in thousands of dollars) of its 10 employees: 40, 42, 45, 48, 50, 50, 55, 60, 65, 120
a. Calculate the mean and median salary for these employees.
b. Calculate the interquartile range (IQR) for the salaries.
c. Based on the calculated statistics, which measure of center (mean or median) would be more appropriate to describe the typical salary for these employees? Justify your answer.
d. Construct a boxplot for the salaries.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates both the mean ($\bar{x} = 57.5$ thousand dollars) and median ($52.5$ thousand dollars) salaries.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the IQR ($Q_3 - Q_1 = 62.5 - 45 = 17.5$ thousand dollars).
  • Part c (1pt): States that the median is more appropriate and provides a correct justification (e.g., the presence of an outlier like $120$ thousand dollars, or strong skewness, pulls the mean away from the center).
  • Part d (2pts): Correctly constructs a boxplot, showing minimum, $Q_1$, median, $Q_3$, maximum, and any outliers, with a labeled axis.

FRQ 4

The heights of adult males in a certain city are approximately normally distributed with a mean of 69 inches and a standard deviation of 2.5 inches.
a. What proportion of adult males in this city are taller than 72 inches? Show your work.
b. An adult male is considered "exceptionally tall" if his height is in the top 5% of the distribution. What is the minimum height, in inches, for an exceptionally tall adult male in this city?
c. A male from another city has a height of 70 inches. If the heights of adult males in that city are normally distributed with a mean of 68 inches and a standard deviation of 2 inches, compare his relative standing to an adult male of 70 inches from the first city.
d. Describe a graphical method that could be used to assess whether the heights of adult males in a sample are approximately normally distributed.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates the $z$-score ($z = \frac{72 - 69}{2.5} = 1.2$) and uses it to find the proportion ($P(Z > 1.2) \approx 0.1151$). Shows calculation or uses appropriate technology output.
  • Part b (2pts): Determines the $z$-score corresponding to the 95th percentile (approximately $z = 1.645$) and uses it to calculate the minimum height ($x = 69 + 1.645 \times 2.5 \approx 73.11$ inches). Shows calculation or uses appropriate technology output.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $z$-scores for both males ($z_1 = \frac{70 - 69}{2.5} = 0.4$, $z_2 = \frac{70 - 68}{2} = 1.0$) and compares their relative standings, concluding the male from the second city is relatively taller because his $z$-score is higher.
  • Part d (1pt): Describes a normal probability plot (or Q-Q plot), explaining that if the plot is roughly linear, the data are approximately normally distributed.

FRQ 5

A survey was conducted among 200 high school students about their preferred mode of transportation to school. The results are summarized in the table below.
Transportation ModeFrequencyPercent
Bus8040%
Car6030%
Walk4020%
Bicycle2010%
a. Construct a bar chart to display the distribution of preferred transportation modes.
b. Describe the distribution of preferred transportation modes based on your chart.
c. If 50 additional students were surveyed, all preferring "Walk," describe how the relative frequency distribution would change for "Bus" and "Walk."

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly labeled bar chart with appropriate scales and bars representing frequencies or relative frequencies
  • Part b (1pt): Describes the mode (Bus) and acknowledges other categories/relative frequencies
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that the relative frequency for "Walk" would increase significantly (from $20\%$ to $\frac{40+50}{200+50} = 36\%$) and "Bus" would decrease (from $40\%$ to $\frac{80}{250} = 32\%$) relative to the new total.
