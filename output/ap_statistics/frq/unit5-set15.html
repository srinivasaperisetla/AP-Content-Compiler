AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 5: Sampling Distributions (Set 15)

FRQ 1

A large online retailer claims that 80% of its customers are satisfied with their recent purchases. To investigate this claim, a consumer advocacy group plans to take a random sample of 200 customers. Let $\hat{p}$ be the proportion of satisfied customers in the sample.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of $\hat{p}$ by determining its mean and standard deviation.
b. Verify that the conditions for using a normal distribution to approximate the sampling distribution of $\hat{p}$ are met.
c. Calculate the probability that less than 75% of the customers in the sample are satisfied, assuming the retailer's claim is true.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Mean of $\hat{p}$ correctly identified as $p=0.80$ (1pt). Standard deviation of $\hat{p}$ correctly calculated as $\sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.8(0.2)}{200}} \approx 0.0283$ (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Large counts condition met: $n p = 200(0.8) = 160 \ge 10$ and $n(1-p) = 200(0.2) = 40 \ge 10$ (1pt). $10\%$ condition met: $200$ is less than $10\%$ of all customers (assuming a large population) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates Z-score $Z = \frac{0.75 - 0.80}{0.0283} \approx -1.767$ (1pt). Correctly calculates probability $P(\hat{p} < 0.75) \approx P(Z < -1.767) \approx 0.0385$ (1pt).

FRQ 2

The weight of adult male grizzly bears in a certain region is approximately normally distributed with a mean of $\mu = 300$ kg and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 40$ kg. Researchers plan to capture and weigh a random sample of 25 adult male grizzly bears. Let $\bar{x}$ be the mean weight of the bears in the sample.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}$ by determining its mean and standard deviation.
b. Verify that the conditions for using a normal distribution to model the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}$ are met.
c. Calculate the probability that the mean weight of the sample of 25 bears is greater than 310 kg.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Mean of $\bar{x}$ correctly identified as $\mu_{\bar{x}} = \mu = 300$ kg (1pt). Standard deviation of $\bar{x}$ correctly calculated as $\sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{40}{\sqrt{25}} = 8$ kg (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Random sample condition met (given) (1pt). Normality condition met because the population distribution of bear weights is approximately normally distributed (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates Z-score $Z = \frac{310 - 300}{8} = 1.25$ (1pt). Correctly calculates probability $P(\bar{x} > 310) \approx P(Z > 1.25) \approx 0.1056$ (1pt).

FRQ 3

A quality control manager is interested in estimating the average lifespan of a certain brand of light bulbs. Two different methods are proposed. Method A involves selecting a random sample of 50 bulbs and calculating their sample mean lifespan, $\bar{x}$. Method B involves selecting a random sample of 50 bulbs and calculating the median lifespan of these bulbs. The true population mean lifespan is $\mu$.
a. Suppose a random sample of 50 bulbs yielded a mean lifespan of $\bar{x} = 980$ hours and a median lifespan of 970 hours. Calculate the estimate for the population mean lifespan using Method A and using Method B.
b. Explain which of the two estimators (sample mean or sample median) is generally considered an unbiased estimator for the population mean.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Estimate for population mean using Method A is $\bar{x} = 980$ hours (1pt). Estimate for population mean using Method B is the median, which is 970 hours (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): The sample mean ($\bar{x}$) is generally an unbiased estimator for the population mean ($\mu$) (1pt). This means that, on average, the sample mean will equal the population mean over many samples (1pt).

FRQ 4

A company develops a new type of battery (Type A) and compares its defect rate to their older model (Type B). From a large production run, a random sample of 500 Type A batteries had 15 defects, while a random sample of 400 Type B batteries had 20 defects. Let $\hat{p}_A$ be the sample proportion of defects for Type A and $\hat{p}_B$ for Type B.
a. Determine the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions ($\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$), assuming the true defect rates are $p_A = 0.03$ and $p_B = 0.05$.
b. Verify that the conditions for using a normal distribution to approximate the sampling distribution of $\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$ are met.
c. Calculate the probability that the sample proportion of defects for Type A batteries is greater than that for Type B batteries, i.e., $P(\hat{p}_A > \hat{p}_B)$, assuming the true defect rates are $p_A = 0.03$ and $p_B = 0.05$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Mean of $\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$ is $p_A - p_B = 0.03 - 0.05 = -0.02$ (1pt). Standard deviation of $\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$ is $\sqrt{\frac{p_A(1-p_A)}{n_A} + \frac{p_B(1-p_B)}{n_B}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.03(0.97)}{500} + \frac{0.05(0.95)}{400}} \approx 0.01330$ (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Large counts condition met for both samples: $n_A p_A = 15 \ge 10$, $n_A(1-p_A) = 485 \ge 10$, $n_B p_B = 20 \ge 10$, $n_B(1-p_B) = 380 \ge 10$ (1pt). $10\%$ condition met for both samples, assuming large populations (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly identifies the event as $P(\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B > 0)$ and calculates Z-score $Z = \frac{0 - (-0.02)}{0.01330} \approx 1.50$ (1pt). Correctly calculates probability $P(Z > 1.50) \approx 0.0668$ (1pt).

FRQ 5

Two different fertilizers, Fertilizer X and Fertilizer Y, are tested on tomato plants. The yield of tomatoes (in kg) per plant for Fertilizer X is known to have a mean of $\mu_X = 5.2$ kg with a standard deviation of $\sigma_X = 1.0$ kg. For Fertilizer Y, the mean yield is $\mu_Y = 4.8$ kg with a standard deviation of $\sigma_Y = 1.2$ kg. Assume the population distributions are approximately normal. A random sample of 30 plants is treated with Fertilizer X, and an independent random sample of 35 plants is treated with Fertilizer Y. Let $\bar{x}_X$ and $\bar{x}_Y$ be the sample mean yields.
a. Determine the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the difference in sample means ($\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y$).
b. Verify that the conditions for using a normal distribution to model the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y$ are met.
c. Interpret the probability $P(\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y > 1)$ in the context of this study. You do not need to calculate this probability.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Mean of $\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y$ is $\mu_X - \mu_Y = 5.2 - 4.8 = 0.4$ kg (1pt). Standard deviation of $\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y$ is $\sqrt{\frac{\sigma_X^2}{n_X} + \frac{\sigma_Y^2}{n_Y}} = \sqrt{\frac{1.0^2}{30} + \frac{1.2^2}{35}} \approx 0.2729$ kg (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Random samples and independence are met (given) (1pt). Normality condition met because both population distributions are approximately normal (given) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): The probability $P(\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y > 1)$ represents the chance that the sample mean yield for Fertilizer X is more than 1 kg greater than the sample mean yield for Fertilizer Y (1pt). If this probability is high, it would suggest that observing such a large difference in sample means, favoring Fertilizer X, is common when the true mean difference is 0.4 kg (1pt).
