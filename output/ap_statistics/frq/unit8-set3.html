AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 8: Inference for Categorical Data: Chi-Square (Set 3)

FRQ 1

A company claims the distribution of colors in their large bags of candies is 30% red, 20% green, 20% yellow, 15% blue, and 15% orange. A statistics student wants to test this claim. They open a random bag containing 500 candies. The observed counts are provided in the table.
ColorObserved Count
Red135
Green90
Yellow80
Blue95
Orange100
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for an appropriate test.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this test.
c. Calculate the expected number of yellow candies and the chi-square test statistic for this sample. Show your work.
d. The $p$-value for the test is 0.038. Using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the student make about the company's claim? Justify your answer in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0$ and $H_a$ for goodness of fit test.
  • Part b (1pt): Checks random sample and expected counts condition ($n p_i \ge 5$ for all categories).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates expected yellow candies ($500 \times 0.20 = 100$). Calculates the chi-square test statistic using $\sum \frac{(\text{Observed} - \text{Expected})^2}{\text{Expected}}$.
  • Part d (2pts): Compares $p$-value to $\alpha$, states correct conclusion (reject $H_0$), and justifies in context.

FRQ 4

A medical researcher is investigating whether a new drug treatment causes a different distribution of side effects compared to a placebo. They collected data from a randomized experiment. After conducting a chi-square test, they obtained a chi-square test statistic of $\chi^2 = 1.25$ with 3 degrees of freedom and a $p$-value of 0.74.
a. Describe the general shape of a chi-square distribution with 3 degrees of freedom. Is it symmetric or skewed?
b. Interpret the $p$-value of 0.74 in the context of this study.
c. Suppose the observed counts for one specific side effect category were much higher than their corresponding expected counts, leading to a large contribution to the chi-square statistic for that category. What question might this observation suggest to the researcher?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Describes chi-square distribution as skewed right (or positively skewed) for 3 degrees of freedom.
  • Part b (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the $p$-value in context, relating it to the probability of observing such results if the null hypothesis were true.
  • Part c (1pt): Identifies a question suggested by the large variation (e.g., "Is this specific side effect more prevalent with the drug than expected?").

FRQ 5

A quality control manager at a factory inspects components for defects. Historically, 6% of components have a minor defect, 3% have a major defect, and 91% have no defects. A recent random sample of 200 components showed the following: 14 minor defects, 6 major defects, and 180 no defects. The manager wants to determine if the defect distribution has changed.
a. What type of chi-square test is appropriate for this situation? Identify the null and alternative hypotheses for this test.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this test.
c. Calculate the expected number of components with minor defects and major defects, assuming the historical distribution is still true. Show your work.
d. The chi-square test statistic for this sample is $\chi^2 = 3.90$ with 2 degrees of freedom. The $p$-value is 0.142. Based on these results, using $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the manager draw? Justify your answer in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies Chi-square test for Goodness of Fit. States correct $H_0$ and $H_a$.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions (random sample, all expected counts $\ge 5$).
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly calculates expected counts for minor ($200 \times 0.06 = 12$) and major ($200 \times 0.03 = 6$) defects.
  • Part d (2pts): Compares $p$-value to $\alpha$, states correct conclusion (fail to reject $H_0$), and justifies in context.
