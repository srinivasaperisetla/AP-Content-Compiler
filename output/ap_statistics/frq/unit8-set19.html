AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 8: Inference for Categorical Data: Chi-Square (Set 19)

FRQ 1

A snack company claims that its new "Fruity Bites" candy bags contain fruit chews with the following color distribution: 30% red, 20% green, 25% yellow, 15% orange, and 10% purple. A quality control inspector opens a random sample of 200 bags and counts the number of each color. The observed counts are shown in the table below.
ColorObserved Count
Red55
Green35
Yellow48
Orange32
Purple30
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for an appropriate test to investigate the company's claim.
b. Name the appropriate statistical test and verify the conditions for inference for this test.
c. Calculate the expected count for green fruit chews in this sample. Show your work.
d. The test statistic for this sample is $\chi^2 = 8.75$ with a p-value of $0.067$. Based on these results, what conclusion should the quality control inspector make at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null ($H_0$: The distribution of fruit chew colors matches the company's claim (30% red, 20% green, 25% yellow, 15% orange, 10% purple)) and alternative hypotheses ($H_a$: The distribution of fruit chew colors does not match the company's claim).
  • Part b (2pts): Names the chi-square goodness-of-fit test (1pt) and verifies all conditions (random sample of bags and all expected counts $\ge 5$) (1pt). Expected counts are: Red (60), Green (40), Yellow (50), Orange (30), Purple (20), all $\ge 5$.
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates the correct expected count for green fruit chews: $200 \times 0.20 = 40$.
  • Part d (2pts): Compares the p-value to $\alpha$ ($0.067 > 0.05$) (1pt) and states the correct conclusion in context (Fail to reject $H_0$; there is insufficient evidence to conclude that the distribution of fruit chew colors differs from the company's claim) (1pt).

FRQ 2

A school counselor wants to investigate if the distribution of post-graduation plans (4-year college, 2-year college, gap year, employment) is the same for students attending three different high schools (School A, School B, School C) in a district. The counselor takes a random sample of 100 graduating students from each school. The observed counts are summarized in the two-way table below.
Post-Graduation PlanSchool ASchool BSchool CTotal
4-year College605045155
2-year College15202560
Gap Year10151843
Employment15151242
Total100100100300
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for an appropriate test to investigate the counselor's question.
b. Name the appropriate statistical test and verify the conditions for inference for this test.
c. The calculated test statistic is $\chi^2 = 12.18$ with 6 degrees of freedom, and the p-value is $0.057$. Based on these results, what conclusion should the counselor make at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level? Justify your conclusion.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null ($H_0$: The distribution of post-graduation plans is the same for all three high schools) and alternative hypotheses ($H_a$: The distribution of post-graduation plans is not the same for all three high schools).
  • Part b (2pts): Names the chi-square test for homogeneity (1pt) and verifies all conditions (random samples from each population, all expected counts are $\ge 5$) (1pt). All expected counts are greater than 5, for example, the expected count for 4-year College at School A is $\frac{155 \times 100}{300} \approx 51.67$.
  • Part c (2pts): Compares the p-value to $\alpha$ ($0.057 > 0.05$) (1pt) and states the correct conclusion in context (Fail to reject $H_0$; there is not significant evidence to conclude that the distributions of post-graduation plans differ among the three high schools) (1pt).

FRQ 3

A psychologist is studying the relationship between pet ownership (Dog, Cat, None) and stress level (Low, Moderate, High) in adults. A random sample of 300 adults is surveyed, and the observed counts are summarized in the table below.
Pet OwnershipLow StressModerate StressHigh StressTotal
Dog455025120
Cat253540100
None35202580
Total10510590300
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for an appropriate test to investigate if there is an association between pet ownership and stress level.
b. Calculate the expected count for adults who own a dog and have a low stress level. Show your work.
c. The p-value for the chi-square test of independence is $0.008$. Interpret this p-value in the context of the study.
d. After conducting the test, the psychologist found that the cell for "Cat ownership" and "High stress" had a relatively large positive standardized residual. What does this suggest about the relationship between cat ownership and high stress level in this sample?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null ($H_0$: There is no association between pet ownership and stress level in adults) and alternative hypotheses ($H_a$: There is an association between pet ownership and stress level in adults).
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates the correct expected count for Dog/Low stress: $\text{Expected} = \frac{\text{Row Total} \times \text{Column Total}}{\text{Grand Total}} = \frac{120 \times 105}{300} = 42$.
  • Part c (1pt): Interprets the p-value correctly in context (Assuming there is no association between pet ownership and stress level, there is a 0.008 probability of observing data as extreme or more extreme than what was observed in this sample).
  • Part d (1pt): States that cat owners in this sample had a higher proportion of high stress levels than would be expected if there were no association between pet ownership and stress level.

FRQ 4

A car manufacturer is testing if the distribution of paint defect types (scratch, chip, dent, blemish) on new cars follows a specified pattern from historical data: 40% scratches, 25% chips, 20% dents, and 15% blemishes. A random sample of 150 defective cars is inspected, and the observed counts are recorded.
Defect TypeObserved Count
Scratch55
Chip38
Dent35
Blemish22
a. Describe the general shape of a chi-square distribution as the degrees of freedom increase.
b. Verify the conditions for making statistical inferences using a chi-square test for this scenario.
c. Calculate the chi-square test statistic. Show your work.
d. The p-value for this test is $0.012$. Interpret this p-value in the context of the car manufacturer's claim.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States that as the degrees of freedom increase, the chi-square distribution becomes more symmetric and less skewed to the right, approaching a more bell-shaped curve.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies the conditions: 1. Random sample of defective cars is given. 2. All expected counts are $\ge 5$. Expected counts: Scratch ($150 \times 0.40 = 60$), Chip ($150 \times 0.25 = 37.5$), Dent ($150 \times 0.20 = 30$), Blemish ($150 \times 0.15 = 22.5$). All are $\ge 5$.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the individual chi-square components (1pt) and sums them to get the correct test statistic (1pt). $\chi^2 = \frac{(55-60)^2}{60} + \frac{(38-37.5)^2}{37.5} + \frac{(35-30)^2}{30} + \frac{(22-22.5)^2}{22.5} = \frac{25}{60} + \frac{0.25}{37.5} + \frac{25}{30} + \frac{0.25}{22.5} \approx 0.4167 + 0.0067 + 0.8333 + 0.0111 \approx 1.2678$.
  • Part d (1pt): Interprets the p-value correctly in context (Assuming the paint defect distribution follows the specified pattern, there is a 0.012 probability of observing a sample distribution of defect types as extreme or more extreme than the one observed).

FRQ 5

A candy company claims that its bags of candies contain 20% red, 20% orange, 20% yellow, 10% green, 10% blue, and 20% brown candies. A statistics student opens a large bag containing 500 candies and counts the following distribution: 95 red, 105 orange, 88 yellow, 52 green, 55 blue, and 105 brown. The student wants to determine if the observed distribution differs significantly from the company's claimed distribution using a chi-square goodness-of-fit test.
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for an appropriate test.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this test.
c. Calculate the chi-square test statistic and the corresponding p-value. Show your work.
d. Based on the p-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the student make? Justify your answer in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly states $H_0: p_{red}=0.20, p_{orange}=0.20, p_{yellow}=0.20, p_{green}=0.10, p_{blue}=0.10, p_{brown}=0.20$ and $H_a:$ At least one proportion is different from the stated value.
  • Part b (1pt): Mentions random sample (implied by 'opens a large bag') and verifies all expected counts are $\ge 5$. Expected counts: Red=100, Orange=100, Yellow=100, Green=50, Blue=50, Brown=100. All are $\ge 5$.
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates $\chi^2 = \sum \frac{(Observed-Expected)^2}{Expected} = \frac{(95-100)^2}{100} + \frac{(105-100)^2}{100} + \frac{(88-100)^2}{100} + \frac{(52-50)^2}{50} + \frac{(55-50)^2}{50} + \frac{(105-100)^2}{100} = 0.25+0.25+1.44+0.08+0.5+0.25 = 2.77$. Degrees of freedom $df=6-1=5$. P-value (from $\chi^2=2.77, df=5$) $\approx 0.735$.
  • Part d (1pt): Compares p-value ($0.735$) to $\alpha=0.05$. Since $p > \alpha$, fails to reject $H_0$. Concludes there is not convincing evidence that the distribution of candy colors in the bag differs from the company's claim.
