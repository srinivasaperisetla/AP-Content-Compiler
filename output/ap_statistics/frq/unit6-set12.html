AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 12)

FRQ 1

A school administrator wants to estimate the proportion of students who feel prepared for college after graduating from their high school. A random sample of 150 recent graduates was surveyed, and 108 of them indicated that they felt prepared for college.
a. Verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the proportion of all graduates who feel prepared for college.
b. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the proportion of all graduates who feel prepared for college. Show your work.
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in the context of the problem.
d. The administrator wants to claim that more than 70% of their graduates feel prepared for college. Based on your confidence interval, is there convincing evidence to support this claim? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Checks random sample, 10% condition, and large counts condition for success and failure.
  • Part b (2pts): Correct formula/method, correct calculation of interval.
  • Part c (1pt): Correct interpretation of CI in context.
  • Part d (1pt): Correct justification based on the CI relative to 70%.

FRQ 2

A company manufactures light bulbs and claims that no more than 5% of their bulbs are defective. A consumer advocacy group suspects the defect rate is higher. They test a random sample of 200 bulbs and find 16 defective bulbs.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your p-value, what conclusion should the consumer advocacy group make at the significance level of α = 0.05? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correct null and alternative hypotheses defined with parameter.
  • Part b (1pt): Checks random sample, 10% condition, and large counts condition for expected successes/failures.
  • Part c (2pts): Correct calculation of z-test statistic and correct p-value.
  • Part d (1pt): Correct conclusion based on p-value and significance level, justified in context.

FRQ 3

A medical researcher is conducting a clinical trial to test a new drug that aims to reduce the proportion of patients experiencing severe allergic reactions to a common vaccine. The current proportion of patients experiencing severe reactions is known to be 0.02 (2%). The researcher wants to test if the new drug reduces this proportion.
a. Describe a Type I error in the context of this study.
b. Describe a Type II error in the context of this study.
c. If the drug company wants to minimize the risk of a Type I error, what would be a consequence for the patients? Explain one way the researcher could reduce the probability of a Type II error without changing the significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correct description of Type I error in context.
  • Part b (1pt): Correct description of Type II error in context.
  • Part c (2pts): Explains consequence of minimizing Type I error (e.g., drug not approved when effective), explains one way to reduce Type II error (e.g., increase sample size, increase alpha, stronger treatment effect).

FRQ 4

A public health organization is investigating whether a new awareness campaign has increased the proportion of people who report getting a flu shot. Before the campaign, a random sample of 300 adults found 120 reported getting a flu shot. After the campaign, a separate random sample of 350 adults found 165 reported getting a flu shot.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a significance test to determine if the campaign increased the proportion of people getting a flu shot.
b. Verify the conditions for performing this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Based on your p-value, what conclusion should the public health organization make at the significance level of α = 0.01? Justify your conclusion in context.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correct null and alternative hypotheses for difference of proportions defined with parameters (p_after - p_before).
  • Part b (1pt): Checks random samples (independent), 10% condition, and large counts condition for pooled expected successes/failures for both groups.
  • Part c (2pts): Correct calculation of z-test statistic (pooled proportion) and correct p-value.
  • Part d (1pt): Correct conclusion based on p-value and significance level, justified in context.

FRQ 5

A school administrator wants to estimate the proportion of students who support a proposed change to the school's dress code. A random sample of 150 students was surveyed, and 87 of them indicated support for the change.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure by name and verify the conditions required for its use.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of students who support the change.
c. Interpret the calculated confidence interval in the context of the problem.
d. The administrator believes that more than 50% of students support the change. Based on your confidence interval, does the data provide convincing evidence to support this belief? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies a one-sample z-interval for a proportion AND verifies Random, Normal (Large Counts), and Independent conditions.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the 95% confidence interval with work shown (e.g., formula or calculator output).
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context.
  • Part d (1pt): States whether there is convincing evidence AND justifies the conclusion by referencing the confidence interval.
