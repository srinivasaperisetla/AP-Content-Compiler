FRQ 1

Mode of TransportationFrequency

a. Construct a relative frequency table for these data. b. Create a bar chart to display the distribution of transportation modes.

FRQ 2

A statistics class recorded the number of text messages sent by 25 students in a single day. The data are as follows: 15, 22, 30, 18, 25, 35, 40, 28, 12, 50, 20, 32, 27, 45, 10, 38, 24, 29, 16, 33, 21, 42, 19, 36, 26.
a. Construct a stemplot of the number of text messages sent. b. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of text messages. c. If another group of 25 students had a median of 45 text messages and an IQR of 15 text messages, how would their distribution likely compare to the one described in part (b)?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly constructs a stemplot with appropriate stem and leaf units (1 pt) and all data points accurately represented (1 pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Describes the shape (e.g., skewed, symmetric, unimodal) (1 pt) and identifies a measure of center and spread (1 pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Compares the median (center) (1 pt) and IQR (spread) (1 pt) of the two distributions in context.

FRQ 3

The following data represent the number of hours 12 randomly selected students spent studying for a final exam: 5, 8, 10, 12, 12, 15, 16, 18, 20, 22, 25, 35.
a. Calculate the mean and the median number of hours studied. b. Calculate the interquartile range (IQR) for the number of hours studied. c. Using the $1.5 \times IQR$ rule, determine if there are any outliers in this data set. Show your work. d. Based on your findings, which measures of center and spread would be more appropriate to describe this data set, and why?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the mean (1 pt) and median (1 pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the IQR (1 pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the upper and lower fences (1 pt) and identifies any outliers, showing justification (1 pt).
  • Part d (1pt): Explains which measures (median/IQR or mean/std dev) are more appropriate based on the presence of outliers (1 pt).

FRQ 5

The weight of adult male grizzly bears in a certain region is approximately normally distributed with a mean of 600 pounds and a standard deviation of 75 pounds.
a. What proportion of adult male grizzly bears weigh less than 500 pounds? Show your work. b. What is the weight of an adult male grizzly bear that is at the 80th percentile? Show your work. c. A wildlife biologist proposes that the weights of female grizzly bears in the same region are also normally distributed with a mean of 350 pounds and a standard deviation of 40 pounds. Compare the relative position of a male bear weighing 650 pounds to a female bear weighing 380 pounds.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Calculates the z-score (1 pt) and determines the correct proportion (1 pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Uses the inverse normal function to find the z-score (1 pt) and calculates the corresponding weight (1 pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the z-score for the male bear (1 pt) and the female bear (1 pt) and correctly compares their relative positions.
