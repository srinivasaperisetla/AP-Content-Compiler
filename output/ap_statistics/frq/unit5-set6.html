AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 5: Sampling Distributions (Set 6)

FRQ 1

A battery manufacturer claims that 95% of its batteries last longer than 100 hours. A quality control manager takes a random sample of 200 batteries to test this claim. Let $\hat{p}$ be the proportion of batteries in the sample that last longer than 100 hours.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of $\hat{p}$ by determining its mean and standard deviation.
b. Verify that the sampling distribution of $\hat{p}$ can be approximated by a normal distribution.
c. Calculate the probability that in a random sample of 200 batteries, fewer than 90% last longer than 100 hours, assuming the manufacturer's claim is true.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies $\mu_{\hat{p}} = p = 0.95$ (1pt) and calculates $\sigma_{\hat{p}} = \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.95(0.05)}{200}} \approx 0.0154$ (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Shows $n p = 200(0.95) = 190 \ge 10$ AND $n(1-p) = 200(0.05) = 10 \ge 10$ (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the z-score $z = \frac{0.90 - 0.95}{0.0154} \approx -3.25$ (1pt) and finds the probability $P(\hat{p} < 0.90) \approx 0.0006$ (1pt).

FRQ 2

The time a customer waits in line at a popular coffee shop is known to have a mean of 4.5 minutes and a standard deviation of 1.8 minutes. A random sample of 50 customers is taken. Let $\bar{x}$ be the mean waiting time for this sample.
a. Determine the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}$.
b. Explain why the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}$ can be approximated by a normal distribution, even if the population distribution of individual waiting times is not normal.
c. Calculate the probability that the mean waiting time for a random sample of 50 customers is greater than 5 minutes.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies $\mu_{\bar{x}} = \mu = 4.5$ minutes (1pt) and calculates $\sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{1.8}{\sqrt{50}} \approx 0.2546$ minutes (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that since the sample size $n=50$ is sufficiently large ($n \ge 30$), the Central Limit Theorem (CLT) applies, making the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}$ approximately normal (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the z-score $z = \frac{5 - 4.5}{0.2546} \approx 1.96$ (1pt) and finds the probability $P(\bar{x} > 5) \approx 0.025$ (1pt).

FRQ 4

A pharmaceutical company is testing two new drugs, Drug A and Drug B, for treating a certain condition. Drug A was given to a random sample of 150 patients, and 120 experienced an improvement. Drug B was given to a separate random sample of 180 patients, and 135 experienced an improvement. Let $p_A$ be the true proportion of patients who improve with Drug A and $p_B$ be the true proportion for Drug B. Assume $p_A=0.8$ and $p_B=0.75$.
a. Describe the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions, $\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$, by determining its mean and standard deviation.
b. Verify that the sampling distribution of $\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$ can be approximated by a normal distribution.
c. Calculate the probability that the sample proportion of patients who improve with Drug A is at least 10 percentage points higher than the sample proportion for Drug B.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly identifies $\mu_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = p_A - p_B = 0.8 - 0.75 = 0.05$ (1pt) and calculates $\sigma_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = \sqrt{\frac{p_A(1-p_A)}{n_A} + \frac{p_B(1-p_B)}{n_B}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.8(0.2)}{150} + \frac{0.75(0.25)}{180}} \approx \sqrt{0.0010667 + 0.0010417} \approx 0.0459$ (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies all four conditions: $n_A p_A = 150(0.8) = 120 \ge 10$, $n_A (1-p_A) = 150(0.2) = 30 \ge 10$, $n_B p_B = 180(0.75) = 135 \ge 10$, $n_B (1-p_B) = 180(0.25) = 45 \ge 10$ (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Sets up $P(\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B \ge 0.10)$. Calculates the z-score $z = \frac{0.10 - 0.05}{0.0459} \approx 1.09$ (1pt) and finds the probability $P(Z \ge 1.09) \approx 0.1379$ (1pt).

FRQ 5

The scores on a nationwide standardized test are approximately normally distributed with a mean of 500 and a standard deviation of 100.
a. On the provided normal distribution curve, shade the region representing the probability that a randomly selected student scores between 450 and 650. Calculate this probability.
b. Suppose a researcher takes a random sample of 25 students. Can the normal distribution be used to describe the distribution of sample scores for these individual students? Explain your reasoning.
c. If two different random samples of 25 students were taken from this population, what question might be suggested by the variation observed in their sample means?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly shades the region between 450 and 650 on the normal curve (1pt). Calculates $P(450 < X < 650)$ using $z_1 = \frac{450-500}{100} = -0.5$ and $z_2 = \frac{650-500}{100} = 1.5$, finding the probability to be $\approx 0.6247$ (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that the normal distribution can be used to describe the distribution of individual sample scores *because the original population is normally distributed*, and not simply due to the sample size (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Identifies a question such as: "Is the difference in the two sample means due to random chance (sampling variability) or does it suggest a significant underlying difference?" or "How much variation in sample means should we expect just by chance?" (1pt).
