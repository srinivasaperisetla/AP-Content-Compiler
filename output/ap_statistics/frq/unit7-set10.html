AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 7: Inference for Quantitative Data: Means (Set 10)

FRQ 1

A cereal company wants to ensure that the mean weight of cereal in its "SuperCrunch" boxes is 450 grams. A quality control manager randomly selects 25 boxes of SuperCrunch cereal and measures their weights. The sample mean weight is $\bar{x} = 448.2$ grams with a sample standard deviation of $s = 5.1$ grams.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a 95% confidence interval for the true mean weight of SuperCrunch cereal boxes.
b. Calculate the 95% confidence interval for the true mean weight of SuperCrunch cereal boxes.
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in context.
d. Based on the confidence interval, does the company have evidence that the true mean weight of its cereal boxes is different from 450 grams? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a one-sample t-interval for a mean (1pt). Verifies conditions: Random sample (given), and since $n=25 < 30$, assumes the population of weights is approximately normal or inspects a graph of sample data (robustness of t-procedure for non-severe deviations) (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct degrees of freedom ($df=24$) (0.5pt). Correctly finds the critical t-value ($t^* \approx 2.064$) (0.5pt). Calculates the correct confidence interval using the formula $\bar{x} \pm t^* \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$ (e.g., $(446.08, 450.32)$) (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the interval in context, stating that we are 95% confident that the true mean weight of SuperCrunch cereal boxes is between the lower and upper bounds of the interval (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): States that there is (or is not) evidence that the mean weight is different from 450 grams (0.5pt). Justifies by explaining whether 450 grams is contained within the confidence interval (0.5pt).

FRQ 2

An engineering firm claims that a new composite material has a mean breaking strength of at least 250 megapascals (MPa). A government agency wants to test this claim. A random sample of 18 specimens of the material is tested, yielding a sample mean breaking strength of $\bar{x} = 247.5$ MPa and a sample standard deviation of $s = 8.3$ MPa.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for a test to investigate the firm's claim.
b. Verify the conditions for conducting a t-test for a population mean.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Interpret the p-value in the context of the problem and state the conclusion of the test using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States $H_0: \mu = 250$ MPa and $H_a: \mu < 250$ MPa (1pt). Defines $\mu$ as the true mean breaking strength of the new material (0.5pt bonus if provided).
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions: Random sample (given), and since $n=18 < 30$, assumes the population of breaking strengths is approximately normal or inspects a graph of sample data (robustness of t-test mentioned) (1pt).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the test statistic $t = \frac{247.5 - 250}{8.3/\sqrt{18}} \approx -1.278$ (1pt). Calculates the p-value using $df=17$ for a one-tailed test (e.g., $P(T < -1.278) \approx 0.109$) (1pt).
  • Part d (2pts): Interprets the p-value (e.g., "If the true mean breaking strength is 250 MPa, there is approximately a 10.9% chance of observing a sample mean of 247.5 MPa or less purely by random chance") (1pt). Compares p-value to $\alpha=0.05$ and states a correct conclusion in context (e.g., "Since p-value $0.109 > 0.05$, we fail to reject $H_0$. There is not sufficient evidence to conclude that the true mean breaking strength of the material is less than 250 MPa, thus we cannot dispute the firm's claim that it's at least 250 MPa") (1pt).

  • Part a (1.5pts): Identifies a paired t-test for a mean difference (0.5pt). States $H_0: \mu_d = 0$ and $H_a: \mu_d > 0$, where $\mu_d$ is the true mean difference (After - Before) in vertical jump height (1pt).
  • Part b (1.5pts): Verifies conditions: Random sample of players (given). The differences are assumed to be approximately normally distributed (or check a graph of differences for strong skew/outliers, or state robustness of t-procedure for $n=10$) (1.5pts).

FRQ 4

A marketing company wants to compare the effectiveness of two different advertising strategies, Strategy A and Strategy B, on customer spending. They randomly assign 20 stores to implement Strategy A and another 25 stores to implement Strategy B. After one month, the average increase in daily sales (in dollars) for the two groups of stores is recorded. Strategy A: $\bar{x}_A = 1250$, $s_A = 210$, $n_A = 20$. Strategy B: $\bar{x}_B = 1180$, $s_B = 190$, $n_B = 25$.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a 90% confidence interval for the true difference in mean daily sales increase between Strategy A and Strategy B ($\mu_A - \mu_B$).
b. Calculate the 90% confidence interval for the true difference in mean daily sales increase between Strategy A and Strategy B.
c. Interpret the confidence interval from part (b) in context.
d. Based on this confidence interval, is there convincing evidence that Strategy A leads to a higher mean increase in daily sales than Strategy B? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Identifies a two-sample t-interval for a difference of means (1pt). Verifies conditions: Two independent random samples (given), $n_A=20 < 30$ and $n_B=25 < 30$, so populations are approximately normal or graphs show no strong skew/outliers for each group (mention t-distribution robustness) (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates the correct degrees of freedom (e.g., using conservative $df = \min(n_A-1, n_B-1) = 19$ or software's more precise df $\approx 37.9$) (0.5pt). Correctly finds the critical t-value (or uses calculator) (0.5pt). Calculates the correct confidence interval using the formula $(\bar{x}_A - \bar{x}_B) \pm t^* \sqrt{\frac{s_A^2}{n_A} + \frac{s_B^2}{n_B}}$ (e.g., for $df=37.9$, $t^* \approx 1.687$, interval is $(70 \pm 88.07) \Rightarrow (-18.07, 158.07)$) (1pt).
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the interval in context, stating that we are 90% confident that the true difference in mean daily sales increase ($\mu_A - \mu_B$) is between the lower and upper bounds of the interval (1pt).
  • Part d (1pt): States whether there is convincing evidence that Strategy A leads to a higher mean increase (0.5pt). Justifies by explaining whether the entire interval is above zero (for $\mu_A - \mu_B$) or if zero is included/excluded (0.5pt).

FRQ 5

They randomly assign 30 volunteers to receive Dosage 1 and another 30 volunteers to receive Dosage 2. After administering the drug, they measure each volunteer's reaction time (in milliseconds). Dosage 1: $\bar{x}_1 = 285$ ms, $s_1 = 35$ ms, $n_1 = 30$. Dosage 2: $\bar{x}_2 = 270$ ms, $s_2 = 30$ ms, $n_2 = 30$. The company wants to determine if there is a statistically significant difference in mean reaction times between the two dosages.
