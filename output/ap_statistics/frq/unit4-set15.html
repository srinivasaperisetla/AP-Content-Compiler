FRQ 1

A game involves rolling a fair six-sided die. If you roll a 1, 2, or 3, you lose 2 points. If you roll a 4 or 5, you gain 1 point. If you roll a 6, you gain 5 points. Let $X$ be the random variable representing the points gained or lost in one roll. The probability distribution of $X$ is shown below.
$X$ (Points)Probability $P(X)$
-21/2
11/3
51/6
a. Represent the probability distribution of $X$ as a histogram.
b. Calculate the expected value of $X$, $E(X)$.
c. Interpret the expected value of $X$ in the context of playing this game many times.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly labeled histogram with $X$ values (-2, 1, 5) on x-axis and probabilities on y-axis, with correct bar heights (0.5, 0.333, 0.167).
  • Part b (2pts): $E(X) = (-2)(1/2) + (1)(1/3) + (5)(1/6) = -1 + 1/3 + 5/6 = -1 + 2/6 + 5/6 = -1 + 7/6 = 1/6 \approx 0.167$.
  • Part c (1pt): In the long run, playing this game many times, a player can expect to gain approximately $1/6$ of a point per game on average.

FRQ 2

A high school surveyed its students about their favorite subject. $60\%$ prefer Math (M), $40\%$ prefer English (E), and $20\%$ prefer both Math and English.
a. Are the events "prefer Math" and "prefer English" mutually exclusive? Justify your answer.
b. What is the probability that a randomly selected student prefers Math or English or both?
c. Are the events "prefer Math" and "prefer English" independent? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States No and provides correct justification that $P(M \cap E) = 0.20 \ne 0$.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates $P(M \cup E) = P(M) + P(E) - P(M \cap E) = 0.60 + 0.40 - 0.20 = 0.80$.
  • Part c (2pts): States No. Calculates $P(M) \times P(E) = 0.60 \times 0.40 = 0.24$. Compares $P(M \cap E) = 0.20$ with $0.24$ and concludes they are not equal, thus not independent.

FRQ 4

A small bakery records the number of cakes sold each day. The probability distribution for the number of cakes sold, $X$, is shown below.
$X$ (Number of Cakes Sold)1234
$P(X)$0.150.250.30?
a. What is the value of $P(X=4)$? Show your work.
b. Calculate the expected number of cakes sold per day, $E(X)$. Show your work.
c. Calculate the standard deviation of the number of cakes sold per day, $\sigma_X$. Show your work.
d. Interpret the expected value calculated in part (b) in the context of the problem.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates $P(X=4) = 1 - (0.15 + 0.25 + 0.30) = 1 - 0.70 = 0.30$.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates $E(X) = (1 \times 0.15) + (2 \times 0.25) + (3 \times 0.30) + (4 \times 0.30) = 0.15 + 0.50 + 0.90 + 1.20 = 2.75$ cakes.
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates variance $\sigma_X^2 = (1-2.75)^2(0.15) + (2-2.75)^2(0.25) + (3-2.75)^2(0.30) + (4-2.75)^2(0.30) = 0.459375 + 0.140625 + 0.01875 + 0.46875 = 1.0875$. Then $\sigma_X = \sqrt{1.0875} \approx 1.0428$.
  • Part d (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation in context, e.g., "On average, over many days, the bakery is expected to sell $2.75$ cakes per day."

FRQ 5

An online store sells two types of custom-made T-shirts, Basic and Premium. The profit $P_B$ for a Basic T-shirt has a mean of $\$5$ and a standard deviation of $\$1.50$. The profit $P_P$ for a Premium T-shirt has a mean of $\$10$ and a standard deviation of $\$2.50$. Assume the profits from selling Basic and Premium T-shirts are independent.
a. Calculate the expected total profit if the store sells one Basic T-shirt and one Premium T-shirt. Show your work.
b. Calculate the standard deviation of the total profit if the store sells one Basic T-shirt and one Premium T-shirt. Show your work.
c. Suppose the store offers a promotion where the profit from each Basic T-shirt sold is increased by $20\%$ and a flat bonus of $\$1$. Describe how this linear transformation affects the mean and standard deviation of the profit from a Basic T-shirt.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly calculates $E(P_B + P_P) = E(P_B) + E(P_P) = \$5 + \$10 = \$15$.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates $\text{Var}(P_B + P_P) = \text{Var}(P_B) + \text{Var}(P_P) = (1.50)^2 + (2.50)^2 = 2.25 + 6.25 = 8.50$. Then $\text{SD}(P_B + P_P) = \sqrt{8.50} \approx \$2.915$.
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly states the new mean is $E(1.20 P_B + 1) = 1.20 E(P_B) + 1 = 1.20(\$5) + \$1 = \$7$. Correctly states the new standard deviation is $\text{SD}(1.20 P_B + 1) = 1.20 \text{SD}(P_B) = 1.20(\$1.50) = \$1.80$.
