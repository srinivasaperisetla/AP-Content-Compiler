AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data: Slopes (Set 13)

FRQ 1

A student is investigating the relationship between the number of hours spent studying for an exam and the score received on the exam. They collected data from 15 students and created a scatterplot of study hours versus exam scores.
a. Based on the scatterplot, identify a question that could be investigated regarding the relationship between study hours and exam scores.
b. Describe what a positive slope in the linear regression model for this data would mean in the context of the study.
c. If the student were to collect data from a larger sample of 50 students, how would this change likely affect the width of a confidence interval for the slope, assuming all other factors remain constant?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a relevant question about the relationship (e.g., Is there a positive association? Does more studying lead to higher scores?) (1 pt)
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly describes the meaning of a positive slope in context (e.g., For each additional hour of studying, the exam score is predicted to increase by the value of the slope) (1 pt)
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that a larger sample size would generally lead to a narrower confidence interval (1 pt)

FRQ 2

A marketing analyst is investigating whether there is a linear relationship between a company's monthly advertising expenditure (in thousands of dollars) and its monthly sales revenue (in tens of thousands of dollars). They plan to collect data from a random sample of months and perform a significance test for the slope of the regression line.
a. Identify the appropriate inference method the analyst should use to test the claim that there is a linear relationship between advertising expenditure and sales revenue.
b. Describe one condition necessary for performing this inference procedure and explain how the analyst could check if this condition is met.
c. Explain what the standard error of the slope, $SE_b$, represents in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies the t-test for the slope of a regression line (1 pt)
  • Part b (2pts): Describes one of the four conditions (Linearity, Independence, Normality of Residuals, Equal Variance) and explains a method to check it (2 pts)
  • Part c (1pt): Explains $SE_b$ as the typical variation of sample slopes from the true population slope, in context (1 pt)

FRQ 3

Researchers investigated the relationship between the amount of fertilizer applied (in grams per square meter, $x$) and the yield of a certain crop (in kilograms, $y$). A random sample of $n=22$ plots of land was used. The partial computer output from a linear regression analysis is shown below.
PredictorCoefSE CoefTP
Constant12.53.14.030.000
Fertilizer0.750.155.000.000
a. Using the computer output, construct and interpret a 90% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line relating fertilizer amount to crop yield.
b. Based on your confidence interval in part (a), is there convincing evidence that increasing the amount of fertilizer applied leads to an increase in crop yield? Justify your answer.
c. If the sample size were larger, but the slope estimate and standard error remained the same, what effect would this have on the width of the confidence interval? Explain your reasoning.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the 90% confidence interval using $t$-critical value ($df=20$, $t^*=1.725$) for the slope: $0.75 \pm 1.725(0.15) \approx (0.49125, 1.00875)$. Interprets the interval in context.
  • Part b (1pt): States that since the entire interval $(0.49125, 1.00875)$ is above zero, there is convincing evidence that increasing fertilizer leads to increased yield. Justifies by explaining the range of plausible values for the slope.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that a larger sample size would lead to a larger degrees of freedom, which would result in a smaller $t$-critical value, thus narrowing the confidence interval (assuming $SE_{\hat{b}}$ remains the same due to other factors).

FRQ 4

A health researcher wanted to investigate if there is a linear relationship between a person's daily screen time (in hours, $x$) and their average daily steps ($y$). A random sample of $n=30$ adults was taken. The partial computer output from a linear regression analysis is shown below. Assume all conditions for inference were met.
PredictorCoefSE CoefTP
Constant1050050021.000.000
Screen Time-20055-3.640.001
a. State the conclusion of the test for a linear relationship between daily screen time and average daily steps, using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$.
b. Interpret the p-value from the computer output in the context of this study.
c. Justify your claim from part (a) based on the results of the significance test.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Concludes that there is convincing evidence of a negative linear relationship between daily screen time and average daily steps.
  • Part b (1pt): Interprets the p-value: If there were no linear relationship (true slope is 0), the probability of observing a sample slope as extreme or more extreme than $\hat{b}=-200$ due to random chance is approximately 0.001.
  • Part c (1pt): Justifies the claim by comparing the p-value ($0.001$) to $\alpha=0.01$. Since $0.001 < 0.01$, we reject $H_0: \beta=0$, providing evidence of a linear relationship.

FRQ 5

A researcher is studying the relationship between the number of hours a student spends studying for a statistics exam and the score they receive on the exam. Data was collected from 20 students. A linear regression analysis was performed, yielding a least-squares regression line $\hat{y} = 55.2 + 2.8x$, where $x$ is the study hours and $y$ is the exam score. The standard error of the slope coefficient was $SE_b = 0.55$.
a. Identify a statistical question that could be answered using these data regarding the relationship between study hours and exam scores.
b. Describe the meaning of the slope coefficient, $2.8$, in the context of this problem.
c. Identify what effect, if any, increasing the sample size from 20 to 50 students (while maintaining a similar scatter of data points) would likely have on the width of a confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States a question about the linear relationship between study hours and exam score in the population, such as "Is there a linear relationship between study hours and exam scores?".
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that for each additional hour a student studies, the predicted exam score increases by $2.8$ points.
  • Part c (1pt): States that increasing the sample size would likely decrease the width of a confidence interval for the true slope, because the standard error of the slope would decrease.
