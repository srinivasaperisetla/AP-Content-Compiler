AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 2: Exploring Two-Variable Data (Set 16)

FRQ 1

A research team studied the relationship between the number of hours spent studying for a final exam and the score received on the exam for a random sample of 15 students. The data is displayed in the scatterplot.
a. Describe the relationship between hours studied and exam score shown in the scatterplot.
b. Suppose the correlation coefficient for these data is $r = 0.85$. Interpret the meaning of this correlation coefficient in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Describes the direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (strong) of the relationship AND includes context. Notes any unusual features or lack thereof.
  • Part b (2pts): States that there is a strong, positive linear association between hours studied and exam score. Explains that as hours studied increase, exam scores tend to increase.

FRQ 2

A car manufacturer wants to model the relationship between a car's weight (in hundreds of pounds) and its fuel efficiency (in miles per gallon, MPG). A random sample of 12 car models was selected, and the following partial computer output for a least-squares regression analysis is provided.

S = 2.15R-Sq = 93.8%R-Sq(adj) = 93.1%
a. Write the equation of the least-squares regression line for predicting fuel efficiency from car weight.
b. Interpret the slope of the regression line in the context of this problem.
c. Predict the fuel efficiency for a car that weighs 3500 pounds.
d. Explain why it would not be appropriate to use this model to predict the fuel efficiency for a car that weighs 10000 pounds.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Correctly writes the equation $\widehat{\text{MPG}} = 45.42 - 5.56 \times \text{Weight}$.
  • Part b (1pt): Interprets the slope: For every additional 100 pounds in car weight, the predicted fuel efficiency decreases by approximately 5.56 MPG.
  • Part c (1pt): Calculates the predicted MPG: $\widehat{\text{MPG}} = 45.42 - 5.56 \times 35 = 25.96$ MPG.
  • Part d (1pt): Explains that 10000 pounds (100 hundreds of pounds) is outside the observed range of weights (extrapolation), and the linear relationship may not hold for such extreme values.

FRQ 3

A veterinarian collected data on the age (in years) and weight (in kg) of a random sample of 20 dogs of a particular breed. A least-squares regression line was fitted to the data to predict weight from age. The residual plot from this regression is shown below.
a. Describe what a residual represents in the context of this problem.
b. Based on the residual plot, is a linear model appropriate for describing the relationship between age and weight for this breed of dog? Justify your answer.
c. What does the pattern in the residual plot suggest about the model's predictions for younger versus older dogs?

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): A residual is the difference between an observed dog's weight and the weight predicted by the regression line for a dog of that age.
  • Part b (2pts): No, a linear model is not appropriate. The residual plot shows a clear curved (U-shaped) pattern, indicating that the linear model systematically overpredicts for some ages and underpredicts for others.
  • Part c (1pt): The U-shaped pattern suggests the model underpredicts for younger dogs (positive residuals), overpredicts for middle-aged dogs (negative residuals), and underpredicts again for older dogs (positive residuals).

FRQ 4

A survey was conducted to investigate the relationship between a person's preferred method of news consumption (TV, Online, Print) and their political affiliation (Democrat, Republican, Independent). The results from a random sample of 500 adults are summarized in the two-way table below.
TVOnlinePrintTotal
Democrat1008020200

a. Calculate the proportion of Republicans who prefer to consume news via Online sources.
b. Compare the proportion of Democrats who prefer TV news to the proportion of Republicans who prefer TV news.
c. Based on the data, is there an association between preferred news consumption method and political affiliation? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Shows calculation $\frac{90}{150} = 0.60$ or 60%.
  • Part b (2pts): Calculates proportion for Democrats: $\frac{100}{200} = 0.50$. Calculates proportion for Republicans: $\frac{60}{150} = 0.40$. Compares the proportions, stating that Democrats are more likely to prefer TV news than Republicans (50% vs. 40%).
  • Part c (1pt): Yes, there appears to be an association. The conditional proportions (e.g., preference for TV news differs across political affiliations) are not similar, indicating that the variables are not independent.

FRQ 5

A researcher collected data on the number of hours students spent studying for a final exam and their scores on the exam. The scatterplot shows a positive, linear association with no apparent outliers. The correlation coefficient was calculated as $r = 0.85$.
a. Describe the relationship between hours studied and exam scores based on the given information.
b. Interpret the value of the correlation coefficient $r = 0.85$ in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Description includes direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (strong).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly interprets $r=0.85$ as a strong, positive, linear association between hours studied and exam scores.
