FRQ 2

A teacher recorded the number of minutes 15 students spent studying for a statistics quiz: 15, 20, 25, 30, 30, 35, 40, 40, 45, 50, 50, 55, 60, 60, 120.
a. Construct a dot plot to display the distribution of study times.
b. Calculate the mean and median study time for these students.
c. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of study times, and identify any potential outliers.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly constructs a dot plot with a clear number line and accurately plotted points (1pt for axis/scale, 1pt for correct points).
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the mean ($\bar{x}$) and median for the study times. ($\bar{x} \approx 45.33$ minutes, Median $= 40$ minutes).
  • Part c (2pts): Describes the shape (e.g., skewed right due to 120), center (using median or mean), and spread (range or IQR, noting variability), and correctly identifies 120 as a potential outlier, all in context.

FRQ 3

The boxplots below show the distribution of daily high temperatures (in degrees Fahrenheit) for two cities, City A and City B, during the month of July.
a. Compare the medians and interquartile ranges (IQRs) of the daily high temperatures for City A and City B.
b. Describe the shapes of the distributions of daily high temperatures for both cities.
c. Based on the shapes of the distributions, explain which measures of center and spread would be more appropriate to use for describing each city's temperatures.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Compares medians (e.g., City B's median is higher than City A's) and IQRs (e.g., City B has a larger IQR than City A's) in context. (1pt for medians, 1pt for IQRs).
  • Part b (1pt): Describes the shapes (e.g., City A is approximately symmetric, City B is skewed right).
  • Part c (2pts): Explains that for City A (symmetric), mean and standard deviation are appropriate, and for City B (skewed), median and IQR are more appropriate, providing reasoning related to skewness for City B.

FRQ 4

The scores on a national standardized test are approximately normally distributed with a mean of 500 and a standard deviation of 100.
a. Calculate the $z$-score for a student who scored 650 on the test. Interpret this $z$-score in context.
b. What proportion of students scored below 450 on this test? Show your work.
c. What score would a student need to achieve to be in the 80th percentile of test takers? Show your work.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly calculates the $z$-score for a score of 650 as $z = \frac{650 - 500}{100} = 1.5$ (1pt). Interprets the $z$-score as the score being 1.5 standard deviations above the mean (1pt).
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct calculation for $P(X < 450)$ using $z = \frac{450 - 500}{100} = -0.5$ and determines the proportion (approximately 0.3085) (1pt for z-score, 1pt for proportion).
  • Part c (2pts): Determines the $z$-score corresponding to the 80th percentile (approximately $z = 0.84$) (1pt). Uses the $z$-score to calculate the raw score (e.g., $X = 500 + 0.84 \times 100 = 584$) (1pt).

FRQ 5

A researcher collected data on the number of hours spent exercising per week by a sample of adults. The histogram below displays the distribution of these exercise times.
a. Describe the shape, center, and spread of the distribution of exercise times shown in the histogram.
b. Based on the shape of the distribution, explain whether the mean would be greater than, less than, or approximately equal to the median. Justify your answer.
c. Justify which measures of center (mean or median) and spread (standard deviation or interquartile range) would be more appropriate to summarize this distribution.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Describes the shape as skewed right (1pt). Describes the approximate center (e.g., between 2-4 hours) and spread (e.g., range from 0 to about 10 hours) (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that the mean would be greater than the median because the distribution is skewed right, and the mean is pulled in the direction of the skew by higher values.
  • Part c (2pts): Justifies that the median and interquartile range (IQR) are more appropriate measures of center and spread, respectively (1pt). Provides reasoning that these measures are resistant to the influence of the skewness and potential outliers present in a skewed distribution, unlike the mean and standard deviation (1pt).
