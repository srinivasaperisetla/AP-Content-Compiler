AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data: Slopes (Set 17)

FRQ 1

Researchers are studying the relationship between the age of a tree (in years) and its diameter (in cm). They collect data from a random sample of 30 trees of a certain species. A linear regression model, $\widehat{\text{Diameter}} = a + b \cdot \text{Age}$, is fitted to predict diameter from age. The computer output is provided below.
PredictorCoefSE CoefTP
Constant5.201.802.890.007
Age1.000.137.690.000
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure for the slope of the regression line and verify the conditions for its use. Assume that a residual plot showed no obvious pattern and a histogram of residuals was approximately normal.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true slope of the regression line relating tree diameter to age. Show your work.
c. Interpret the confidence interval calculated in part (b) in the context of the problem.
d. Based on this interval, is there convincing statistical evidence that older trees tend to have larger diameters? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2 pts): Identifies a t-interval for the slope (1 pt); Verifies conditions: Random sample, linearity (from residual plot), independence (30 is less than 10% of all trees), and nearly normal residuals (from histogram) (1 pt).
  • Part b (1 pt): Calculates the correct 95% confidence interval for the slope using $\hat{b} \pm t^* \cdot SE_{\hat{b}}$ with $df=28$ and $t^* \approx 2.048$. Shows calculation $1.00 \pm 2.048 \times 0.13 = (0.73376, 1.26624)$ (1 pt).
  • Part c (1 pt): Interprets the interval in context, stating "We are 95% confident that for every additional year in age, the true mean tree diameter increases by an amount between 0.73376 cm and 1.26624 cm" (1 pt).
  • Part d (1 pt): States there is convincing evidence because the entire interval $(0.73376, 1.26624)$ contains only positive values, which indicates a positive association (1 pt).

FRQ 2

A medical researcher is investigating whether the dosage of a new pain medication (in mg) affects the time it takes for a patient to report pain relief (in minutes). A random sample of 40 patients is randomly assigned to receive different dosages. The researcher performs a linear regression analysis to predict pain relief time from dosage. Partial computer output is provided.
PredictorCoefSE CoefTP
Constant60.53.218.910.000
Dosage-0.850.18-4.720.000
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses to test if there is a *negative* linear relationship between medication dosage and pain relief time for the population of patients. Define any parameters used.
b. Verify the conditions for performing a significance test for the slope of the regression line. Assume a residual plot shows no clear pattern and a histogram of residuals is approximately normal.
c. Calculate the test statistic for the hypotheses stated in part (a). Show your work.
d. Based on the test statistic calculated in part (c), and given that the p-value for a one-sided test is $0.0001$, what conclusion can be made about the relationship between medication dosage and pain relief time at a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1 pt): States $H_0: \beta = 0$ and $H_a: \beta < 0$, where $\beta$ is the true slope of the linear relationship between medication dosage and pain relief time (1 pt).
  • Part b (1 pt): Verifies conditions: Random assignment, linearity (from residual plot), independence (40 is less than 10% of all patients), and nearly normal residuals (from histogram) (1 pt).
  • Part c (1 pt): Calculates the test statistic correctly: $t = \frac{\hat{b} - \beta_0}{SE_{\hat{b}}} = \frac{-0.85 - 0}{0.18} \approx -4.722$ (1 pt).
  • Part d (2 pts): Compares the p-value ($0.0001$) to $\alpha = 0.01$ (p-value < $\alpha$) (1 pt). Concludes to reject $H_0$ and states there is convincing evidence of a negative linear relationship between medication dosage and pain relief time (1 pt).

FRQ 3

A nutritionist is studying the relationship between the daily fat intake (in grams) and the cholesterol level (in mg/dL) for adults. A random sample of 50 adults provides the following least-squares regression output for predicting cholesterol level from fat intake.
PredictorCoefSE CoefTP
Constant150.05.030.000.000
Fat Intake1.000.119.090.000
a. State the estimated regression equation. Interpret the slope in the context of this problem.
b. Suppose a residual plot from this analysis shows no obvious pattern and a histogram of the residuals is approximately normal. Based on this information and the sample size, describe how you would check the remaining conditions for inference for the slope.
c. The 95% confidence interval for the true slope, $\beta$, is calculated as $(0.78, 1.22)$. Based on this interval, can the nutritionist conclude that there is a significant linear relationship between daily fat intake and cholesterol level at the $\alpha = 0.05$ level? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2 pts): States the regression equation $\widehat{\text{Cholesterol}} = 150 + 1.00 \cdot \text{Fat Intake}$ (1 pt). Interprets the slope: "For every additional gram of daily fat intake, the predicted cholesterol level increases by 1.00 mg/dL, on average" (1 pt).
  • Part b (1 pt): Describes checking the independence condition: "The sample of 50 adults is less than 10% of all adults in the population, so individual cholesterol levels can be considered independent." (1 pt).
  • Part c (2 pts): Compares the interval to zero (1 pt). Concludes there is a significant linear relationship because the entire interval $(0.78, 1.22)$ contains only positive values and does not contain 0 (1 pt).

FRQ 4

An environmental scientist is investigating the relationship between the amount of dissolved oxygen (in mg/L) and the water temperature (in degrees Celsius) in a particular lake. Data were collected from 60 random locations in the lake. A linear regression model was fitted to predict dissolved oxygen from water temperature. The computer output is shown below.
PredictorCoefSE CoefTP
Constant12.00.524.000.000
Temperature-0.300.04-7.500.000
a. State the null and alternative hypotheses for testing if there is a negative linear relationship between water temperature and dissolved oxygen in the lake. Define any parameters used.
b. Based on the computer output, what is the p-value for testing the hypotheses from part (a)? Interpret this p-value in the context of the problem.
c. Based on the p-value and a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what conclusion should the environmental scientist make? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1 pt): States $H_0: \beta = 0$ and $H_a: \beta < 0$, where $\beta$ is the true slope of the linear relationship between water temperature and dissolved oxygen (1 pt).
  • Part b (2 pts): Identifies the p-value from the output for a two-sided test ($0.000$). For a one-sided test, $p\text{-value} = 0.000 / 2 \approx 0$ (1 pt). Interprets: "Assuming there is no linear relationship between water temperature and dissolved oxygen (i.e., $\beta=0$), there is a $0\%$ chance of observing a sample slope as extreme or more extreme than what was observed due to random chance" (1 pt).
  • Part c (1 pt): Compares p-value ($0$) to $\alpha = 0.01$ (p-value < $\alpha$) (1 pt). Concludes to reject $H_0$ and states there is convincing evidence of a negative linear relationship between water temperature and dissolved oxygen (1 pt).

FRQ 5

A marketing team wants to study the relationship between the number of hours spent watching promotional videos for a new product and the number of units of the product purchased by customers. They collect data from a random sample of customers. A scatterplot of the data is provided.
a. Describe the relationship between hours watched and units purchased shown in the scatterplot.
b. From the scatterplot, identify one question that might be suggested by the variation in the data points, other than "Is there a linear relationship?"
c. One 95% confidence interval for the true slope, $\beta$, was calculated as $(0.15, 0.45)$ based on a sample of 50 customers. If a new study was conducted with 200 customers (assuming similar data pattern and variability), would the 95% confidence interval for the slope be wider, narrower, or about the same width? Explain your reasoning.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1 pt): Describes the relationship by mentioning direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (moderately strong to strong) (1 pt).
  • Part b (1 pt): Identifies a relevant question suggested by variation, e.g., "Are there any outliers affecting the perceived strength of the relationship?" or "Is the variability in units purchased consistent across all hours watched?" or "Is a linear model appropriate for the entire range of hours watched?" (1 pt).
  • Part c (2 pts): States the interval would be narrower (1 pt). Explains that increasing sample size ($n$) decreases the standard error of the slope ($SE_{\hat{b}}$), which in turn decreases the margin of error and thus the width of the confidence interval (1 pt).
