AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data: Proportions (Set 5)

FRQ 1

A market research firm wants to estimate the proportion of adults in a certain city who prefer to shop online for groceries. They conducted a random sample of 500 adults in the city and found that 210 of them prefer to shop online for groceries.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the proportion of adults in this city who prefer to shop online for groceries.
c. Interpret the 95% confidence interval in the context of the problem.
d. Suppose the firm wants to estimate this proportion with a margin of error of no more than 2% at a 95% confidence level. Calculate the minimum sample size required.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a one-sample z-interval for proportions and verifies random sampling, independence (10% condition), and large counts condition.
  • Part b (1pt): Correctly calculates the 95% confidence interval using the appropriate formula or technology, showing work or providing values used.
  • Part c (1pt): Provides a correct interpretation of the confidence interval in context, stating both the confidence level and the estimated range for the true proportion.
  • Part d (1pt): Correctly calculates the minimum sample size required using the appropriate formula and rounding up to the nearest whole number.

FRQ 2

A company claims that 80% of its new customers are satisfied with their service within the first month. A consumer advocacy group suspects this claim is too high and takes a random sample of 150 new customers. They find that 108 of them are satisfied with the service.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test the advocacy group's suspicion and identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for conducting this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Interpret the p-value in context and state the conclusion of the test at the alpha = 0.05 significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses in symbols and words, and identifies a one-sample z-test for proportions.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random sampling, independence (10% condition), and large counts condition for the null hypothesis proportion.
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the z-test statistic (1pt) and the corresponding p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (2pts): Provides a correct interpretation of the p-value in context (1pt) and draws a correct conclusion with justification based on the p-value and significance level (1pt).

FRQ 3

A study was conducted to compare the effectiveness of two different fertilizers, Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B, on a certain type of plant. 200 plants were randomly assigned to be treated with Fertilizer A, and 120 of them produced fruit. Another 180 plants were randomly assigned to Fertilizer B, and 90 of them produced fruit.
a. Identify the appropriate confidence interval procedure and verify the conditions for its use.
b. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the difference in the proportions of plants that produce fruit (Fertilizer A - Fertilizer B).
c. Based on your confidence interval, is there convincing evidence of a difference in the effectiveness of the two fertilizers? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a two-sample z-interval for proportions and verifies random assignment, independence within and between groups (10% condition for each sample), and large counts condition for each sample.
  • Part b (2pts): Correctly calculates the 90% confidence interval using the appropriate formula or technology, showing work or providing values used (1pt for formula/setup, 1pt for correct interval).
  • Part c (2pts): States whether there is convincing evidence (1pt) and justifies the answer by referring to the confidence interval (e.g., whether 0 is included) in context (1pt).

FRQ 4

A researcher wants to investigate if there is a difference in the proportion of male and female college students who use a certain social media platform. A random sample of 250 male students found 160 use the platform. A separate random sample of 300 female students found 180 use the platform.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to test for a difference in proportions and identify the appropriate inference method.
b. Verify the conditions for conducting this significance test.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Interpret the p-value in context and state the conclusion of the test at the alpha = 0.01 significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States correct null and alternative hypotheses in symbols and words, and identifies a two-sample z-test for proportions.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies random sampling (independent samples), independence (10% condition for each sample), and large counts condition for the pooled proportion for each sample.
  • Part c (2pts): Correctly calculates the z-test statistic (1pt) and the corresponding p-value (1pt).
  • Part d (2pts): Provides a correct interpretation of the p-value in context (1pt) and draws a correct conclusion with justification based on the p-value and significance level (1pt).

FRQ 5

A pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug designed to reduce allergic reactions. They claim the drug reduces reactions in more than 70% of patients. A new study is conducted to test this claim.
a. Describe a Type I error and a Type II error in the context of this study.
b. If the sample size for a confidence interval were increased, explain how this would affect the width of the confidence interval, assuming the confidence level remains the same.
c. If the confidence level for a confidence interval were increased (e.g., from 90% to 99%), explain how this would affect the width of the confidence interval, assuming the sample size remains the same.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Correctly describes a Type I error (1pt) and a Type II error (1pt) in the specific context of the drug study, identifying the consequence of each error.
  • Part b (1pt): Explains that increasing the sample size would decrease the width of the confidence interval because it reduces the standard error/margin of error.
  • Part c (1pt): Explains that increasing the confidence level would increase the width of the confidence interval because it requires a larger critical value.
