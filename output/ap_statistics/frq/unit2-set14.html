FRQ 1

A car manufacturer is investigating the relationship between a car's weight (in hundreds of pounds) and its fuel efficiency (in miles per gallon, MPG). A random sample of $20$ car models yielded the least-squares regression line $\widehat{\text{MPG}} = 45.1 - 0.72 \times \text{Weight}$. The coefficient of determination is $r^2 = 0.85$.
a. Interpret the slope of the least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
b. Calculate the predicted fuel efficiency for a car that weighs $3500$ pounds.
c. Interpret the value of $r^2 = 0.85$ in the context of this study.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): For every additional hundred pounds of car weight, the predicted fuel efficiency decreases by $0.72$ MPG.
  • Part b (2pts): Shows calculation: $\widehat{\text{MPG}} = 45.1 - 0.72 \times 35 = 45.1 - 25.2 = 19.9$ MPG. Correct calculation and units.
  • Part c (1pt): $85\%$ of the variation in a car's fuel efficiency (MPG) can be explained by the linear relationship with car weight.

FRQ 3

A researcher is studying the growth of a certain type of bacteria. They collect data on the time (in hours) and the number of bacteria (in thousands). A linear model for the number of bacteria versus time was found to be inappropriate due to a curved pattern in the residual plot. To address this, the researcher decided to transform the data by taking the natural logarithm of the number of bacteria, $\ln(\text{Bacteria})$. The least-squares regression line for the transformed data is $\widehat{\ln(\text{Bacteria})} = 0.5 + 0.2 \times \text{Time}$. The $R^2$ value for this transformed model is $0.98$.
a. Interpret the slope of the transformed least-squares regression line in the context of this problem.
b. Predict the number of bacteria (in thousands) after $10$ hours using the transformed model. Show your work.
c. After fitting the original linear model (Number of Bacteria vs. Time), a data point was identified as $(2, 100)$ where $2$ hours was very low compared to other observations, and $100$ thousand bacteria was unusually high for that time. Describe how this point might affect the slope and $R^2$ of the original least-squares regression line.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): For every additional hour, the natural logarithm of the number of bacteria is predicted to increase by $0.2$. OR For every one-unit increase in time, the predicted number of bacteria is multiplied by a factor of $e^{0.2} \approx 1.221$.
  • Part b (2pts): Shows calculation: $\widehat{\ln(\text{Bacteria})} = 0.5 + 0.2 \times 10 = 2.5$. Then, $\widehat{\text{Bacteria}} = e^{2.5} \approx 12.18$ thousand. Correct calculation and conversion.
  • Part c (1pt): The point $(2, 100)$ is an influential point because its $x$-value (Time=2) is relatively low, and its $y$-value (Bacteria=100) is unusually high, pulling the least squares regression line towards it. This would likely make the slope of the original regression line steeper (more positive) and decrease the $R^2$ value as it increases the overall residual sum of squares for the original linear model.

FRQ 4

A researcher studied the relationship between the number of hours students spent studying for a final exam and their scores on the exam. A scatterplot of the data from 10 randomly selected students is shown below.
a. Describe the direction, form, and strength of the relationship between study hours and exam scores shown in the scatterplot.
b. The correlation coefficient for this data is calculated to be $r \approx 0.96$. Interpret this value in the context of this study.
c. Based on the scatterplot and the correlation, would it be appropriate to use a linear model to predict exam scores from study hours? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (2pts): Describes direction (positive), form (linear), and strength (strong) (1pt). Mentions context (1pt).
  • Part b (1pt): Interprets $r=0.96$ as a strong, positive linear association between hours studied and exam scores (with context).
  • Part c (1pt): States "Yes" (0.5pt). Justifies based on strong, linear pattern in scatterplot and high $r$ value (0.5pt).
