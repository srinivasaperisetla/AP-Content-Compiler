AP Statistics FRQs — Unit 7: Inference for Quantitative Data: Means (Set 5)

FRQ 1

A company manufactures a specific type of light bulb. The production manager wants to estimate the true mean lifespan of these bulbs. A random sample of $n = 30$ bulbs is selected and tested. The sample mean lifespan is $\bar{x} = 985$ hours and the sample standard deviation is $s = 50$ hours.
a. Identify the appropriate inference procedure and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the true mean lifespan of the light bulbs.
b. Calculate a 95% confidence interval for the true mean lifespan of the light bulbs. Show all steps.
c. Interpret the 95% confidence interval in the context of this problem.
d. The company claims that the average lifespan of their light bulbs is 1000 hours. Based on your confidence interval, does this claim seem plausible? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): Identifies a one-sample t-interval for a population mean and verifies conditions (random sample, $10\%$ condition, Normal/large sample condition).
  • Part b (2pts): Shows correct formula, calculates appropriate degrees of freedom, t-critical value, margin of error, and the correct 95% confidence interval ($\bar{x} \pm t^* \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$).
  • Part c (1pt): Correctly interprets the confidence interval in context (e.g., "We are 95% confident that the true mean lifespan...").
  • Part d (1pt): States whether the claim is plausible and justifies the answer by referencing the confidence interval.

FRQ 2

A new energy drink claims to improve athletic performance. A random sample of 25 athletes is selected. Each athlete consumes the energy drink, and their performance time in a specific task is measured. The company claims the average performance time for this task is normally 15 seconds without the drink. The sample mean performance time with the drink is $\bar{x} = 14.2$ seconds with a sample standard deviation of $s = 2.5$ seconds. Assume that performance times are approximately normally distributed for this task.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses for testing if the energy drink improves performance time.
b. Verify the conditions for performing a significance test for the true mean performance time.
c. Calculate the test statistic and the p-value for this test.
d. Interpret the p-value in context and state a conclusion at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States $H_0: \mu = 15$ and $H_a: \mu < 15$, defining $\mu$ as the true mean performance time with the energy drink.
  • Part b (1pt): Verifies conditions: random sample, $10\%$ condition ($N \ge 10n$), and Normal condition (stated to be approximately normal/large enough sample).
  • Part c (2pts): Calculates the correct test statistic $t = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{s/\sqrt{n}}$ and determines the correct p-value using appropriate degrees of freedom ($df=24$).
  • Part d (2pts): Interprets the p-value in context (probability of observed results or more extreme if $H_0$ is true) and makes a correct conclusion comparing p-value to $\alpha = 0.05$, linking to the claim in context.

FRQ 5

A health organization wants to compare the effectiveness of two different types of exercise routines, Routine X and Routine Y, on reducing blood pressure. They recruit 60 volunteers with high blood pressure and randomly assign 30 to Routine X and 30 to Routine Y. After 12 weeks, the reduction in systolic blood pressure (in mmHg) is recorded for each participant. For Routine X: $\bar{x}_X = 12.5$ mmHg, $s_X = 4.2$ mmHg. For Routine Y: $\bar{x}_Y = 10.3$ mmHg, $s_Y = 3.8$ mmHg. Assume that the conditions for inference have been met.
a. State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses to investigate if there is a difference in the true mean blood pressure reduction between Routine X and Routine Y. Define any parameters you use.
b. Calculate the test statistic for this hypothesis test.
c. Determine the p-value for the test.
d. Interpret the p-value in context and state a conclusion at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part a (1pt): States $H_0: \mu_X - \mu_Y = 0$ (or $\mu_X = \mu_Y$) and $H_a: \mu_X - \mu_Y \ne 0$ (or $\mu_X \ne \mu_Y$), defining $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ as the true mean blood pressure reduction for Routine X and Routine Y, respectively.
  • Part b (1pt): Calculates the correct test statistic $t = \frac{(\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y) - 0}{\sqrt{\frac{s_X^2}{n_X} + \frac{s_Y^2}{n_Y}}}$.
  • Part c (1pt): Determines the correct p-value using the test statistic and appropriate degrees of freedom.
  • Part d (2pts): Interprets the p-value in context (probability of observing a difference this extreme or more if no true difference exists) and makes a correct conclusion comparing p-value to $\alpha = 0.05$, linking to the claim in context.
