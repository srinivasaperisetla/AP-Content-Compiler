Q13. (ID: UN3_Q3_INFERENCE)
A group of researchers was interested in the effect of exercise on cognitive performance. They obtained a list of all 10,000 students at a large university and randomly selected a sample of 200 students. These 200 students were then randomly assigned to one of two groups: one group was instructed to exercise for 30 minutes a day, and the other group was instructed not to exercise. After a month, all students were given a cognitive test. The exercise group performed significantly better on the test. What is the appropriate scope of inference from this study?
  • A. The results can be generalized to the student population of the university, and a causal relationship between exercise and cognitive performance can be established.
  • B. The results cannot be generalized to the student population of the university, but a causal relationship between exercise and cognitive performance can be established.
  • C. The results can be generalized to the student population of the university, but a causal relationship between exercise and cognitive performance cannot be established.
  • D. The results cannot be generalized to the student population of the university, and a causal relationship between exercise and cognitive performance cannot be established.
