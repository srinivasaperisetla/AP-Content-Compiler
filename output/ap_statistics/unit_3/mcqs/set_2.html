Q2. (ID: U3_Q2_BiasTypes)
A state's department of motor vehicles (DMV) wants to estimate the proportion of registered drivers who wear their seatbelts regularly. A survey is mailed to a random sample of 5,000 registered drivers in the state. The survey asks, 'As a safe and responsible driver, do you always wear your seatbelt?' Of the 1,200 surveys returned, 95% of respondents indicated that they always wear their seatbelt. Which of the following is a potential source of bias that is NOT accounted for by the randomization?
  • A. Undercoverage bias, because the sampling frame might not include all drivers.
  • B. Response bias, because the wording of the question may influence drivers to answer that they wear their seatbelt.
  • C. onvenience sampling bias, because the sample was not selected randomly.
  • D. There is no bias because the sample was selected randomly.
Q3. (ID: U3_Q3_ExperimentVsObservational)
Researchers wanted to investigate whether there is an association between regular exercise and stress levels in adults. They surveyed a random sample of 500 adults, asking them to report the number of hours they exercise per week and to rate their typical stress level on a scale from 1 to 10. The researchers found that adults who exercised more tended to have lower stress levels. Which of the following is a correct statement?
  • A. This is a completely randomized experiment because a random sample of adults was selected.
  • B. This is an observational study, and a causal link between exercise and stress cannot be established from this study.
  • C. This is a matched-pairs experiment because it compares exercise habits and stress levels for each person.
  • D. This is an observational study, and the results can be generalized to the entire adult population.

The change in each volunteer's blood pressure is measured after 8 weeks. What is the primary purpose of having a placebo group?

  • B. To ensure that all volunteers have a similar initial blood pressure.
  • C. To act as a baseline for comparison, helping to control for the effects of other factors, such as the placebo effect.
  • D. To reduce the amount of variation in blood pressure measurements within each treatment group.

Which of the following is the most appropriate conclusion?

  • B. The new fertilizer causes a higher yield, but this result cannot be generalized beyond the 30 plots in the study.
  • C. There is an association, but not necessarily a causal relationship, between the new fertilizer and higher yield.
  • D. No conclusion can be drawn because the study did not use a placebo.

  • B. The new medication is effective at lowering cholesterol, but the results cannot be generalized to all adults with high cholesterol.
  • C. It is not possible to conclude that the new medication is effective at lowering cholesterol, but the results can be generalized to all adults with high cholesterol.
  • D. It is not possible to conclude that the new medication is effective at lowering cholesterol, nor can the results be generalized to all adults with high cholesterol.
