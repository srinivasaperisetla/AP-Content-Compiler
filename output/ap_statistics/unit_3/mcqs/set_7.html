Q1. (ID: STAT_U3_Q1)
A state's department of education wants to survey high school students about their college and career plans. They obtain a list of all high schools in the state. They randomly select 20 high schools from the list and attempt to survey every student at these 20 schools. Which of the following best describes this sampling method?
  • A. Simple random sample
  • B. Stratified random sample
  • C. luster sample
  • D. Systematic random sample
Q2. (ID: STAT_U3_Q2)
A medical researcher wants to investigate if a new drug reduces cholesterol levels. The researcher recruits 100 volunteers with high cholesterol. Fifty of the volunteers are randomly assigned to receive the new drug, and the other fifty are assigned a placebo. After three months, the change in cholesterol level is measured for each volunteer. What is the primary purpose of randomly assigning the volunteers to the two groups?
  • A. To ensure that the results of the study can be generalized to the entire population of people with high cholesterol.
  • B. To produce two groups that are, on average, similar in all respects before the treatment begins.
  • C. To ensure that the volunteers do not know whether they are receiving the new drug or the placebo.
  • D. To reduce the amount of variation in cholesterol levels within each group.
Q3. (ID: STAT_U3_Q3)
A television station wants to gauge public opinion on a local political issue. It invites viewers to call a telephone number to record their vote as either 'in favor' or 'opposed'. Over 10,000 people call in. Which of the following is the most significant source of bias in this poll?
  • A. Nonresponse bias, because many people who were called did not answer.
  • B. Response bias, because the question may have been worded in a confusing way.
  • C. Undercoverage, because people without televisions could not participate.
  • D. Voluntary response bias, because people who chose to participate may have stronger opinions than those who did not.
Q4. (ID: STAT_U3_Q4)
An experiment was conducted to determine whether a new type of soil additive improves the growth of pepper plants. An agricultural company recruited 80 volunteers who grow pepper plants in their home gardens. The volunteers were randomly assigned to two groups: one that used the new additive and one that did not. The study found a statistically significant increase in pepper plant growth for the group using the additive. Which of the following is an appropriate conclusion?
  • A. We can conclude that the new additive causes an increase in pepper plant growth, and the results can be generalized to all pepper plants.
  • B. We can conclude that the new additive causes an increase in pepper plant growth for plants similar to those in the study, but we cannot generalize the results to all pepper plants.
  • C. We cannot conclude that the new additive causes an increase in pepper plant growth, but we can generalize the results to all pepper plants.
  • D. We cannot conclude that the new additive causes an increase in pepper plant growth, nor can we generalize the results to all pepper plants.

All plants are grown in the same soil, receive the same amount of sunlight and water, and are otherwise treated identically.   • C. We cannot generalize these results to any population because the participants were not randomly assigned to exercise groups.
  • D. For adults in this city, there is no evidence of an association between exercise and stress levels.
