Q1. (ID: STAT_U3_Q1)
A state's department of education wants to survey high school students about their post-graduation plans. They obtain a list of all high school districts in the state. They randomly select 20 districts and survey every high school student in those 20 districts. Which of the following best describes this sampling method?
  • A. Simple random sampling
  • B. Stratified random sampling
  • C. luster sampling
  • D. Systematic random sampling
Q2. (ID: STAT_U3_Q2)
A nutritionist is investigating the effect of a new dietary supplement on weight loss. Sixty volunteers who want to lose weight are recruited. The nutritionist randomly assigns 30 volunteers to receive the supplement and 30 volunteers to receive a placebo. The volunteers do not know which treatment they are receiving. After three months, the amount of weight lost by each volunteer is recorded. Which of the following is the primary purpose of random assignment in this study?
  • A. To allow the results to be generalized to a larger population.
  • B. To minimize the effects of nonresponse bias.
  • C. To create treatment groups that are as similar as possible at the beginning of the study.
  • D. To ensure that the study is a double-blind experiment.
Q3. (ID: STAT_U3_Q3)
Researchers conducted a study to investigate the relationship between regular exercise and blood pressure in adults. They recruited 500 adult volunteers and asked them to report the average number of hours they exercise per week. They also measured each volunteer's blood pressure. The study found that adults who exercised more tended to have lower blood pressure. Which of the following is a valid conclusion from this study?
  • A. Increased exercise causes lower blood pressure in adults.
  • B. There is an association between exercise and blood pressure, but this study does not provide evidence that exercise causes lower blood pressure.
  • C. This result can be generalized to all adults because the sample size of 500 is large.
  • D. Lower blood pressure causes adults to exercise more.

  • B. The new drug causes a reduction in cholesterol, and this result can be generalized to all people with high cholesterol.
  • C. The new drug causes a reduction in cholesterol for people similar to the volunteers in this study.
  • D. There is an association, but not necessarily a causal relationship, between taking the new drug and lower cholesterol.

Q14. (ID: UN3_Q4)
A biologist is studying the effect of two different soil additives (A and B) on the growth of a particular plant species. They have 60 plots of land available for the experiment. The plots are located in two distinct areas: 30 plots in a sunny, dry area and 30 plots in a shady, moist area. The biologist suspects that the location will affect plant growth. Which of the following experimental designs would be most appropriate?
  • A. completely randomized design, where the 60 plots are randomly assigned to receive Additive A or Additive B.
  • B. matched pairs design, where plots are paired based on their initial soil quality.
  • C. randomized block design, using the location (sunny/dry vs. shady/moist) as blocks.
  • D. n observational study, where the biologist observes existing plants in areas where the additives have been used naturally.

What is the primary reason for designing the experiment this way?

  • B. To reduce the effect of confounding from the living environment.
  • C. To ensure the number of participants in each group is equal.
  • D. To allow for generalization of the results to all allergy sufferers.
