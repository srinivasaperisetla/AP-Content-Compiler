FRQ 1

  • Part (d): 1 point for correctly identifying the most and least common modes of transportation (Bus and Bike, respectively) with reference to their frequencies or relative frequencies.

FRQ 3

c. The mean length of the sharks in the sample is 104.53 cm and the standard deviation is 7.22 cm. If the shark with length 125.7 cm were removed from the data, how would the mean and standard deviation change? Explain your reasoning without performing any calculations.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part (a): Requires ordered data: 96.5, 97.1, 98.8, 99.4, 100.3, 101.7, 102.1, 103.6, 104.9, 105.5, 106.9, 107.3, 108.0, 110.2, 125.7. 1 point for correct min (96.5) and max (125.7). 1 point for correct median (103.6). 1 point for correct quartiles (Q1=99.4, Q3=107.3).

The standard deviation would decrease because a value far from the mean is being removed, thus reducing the overall variability of the data.

FRQ 4

The weights of bags of premium coffee beans are approximately normally distributed with a mean of 340 grams and a standard deviation of 8 grams.
a. A bag is randomly selected. What is the probability that the bag weighs less than 330 grams?
b. According to the empirical rule, approximately 95% of the bags will have weights between which two values? Explain your reasoning.
c. The coffee company wants to label the top 5% of bags as 'Extra Full'. What is the minimum weight a bag must have to receive this label?
d. A customer buys one bag of these premium coffee beans and one bag of specialty tea leaves. The weights of the tea bags are approximately normally distributed with a mean of 120 grams and a standard deviation of 5 grams. The customer's coffee bag weighs 352 grams and the tea bag weighs 128 grams. Relative to their respective products, which bag is heavier? Justify your answer.

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part (a): 1 point for calculating the correct z-score for 330 grams (z = (330-340)/8 = -1.25). 1 point for finding the correct probability from the z-score (approx. 0.1056).
  • Part (b): 1 point for identifying the correct interval (324 grams to 356 grams). 1 point for the explanation that the empirical rule states 95% of data in a normal distribution falls within 2 standard deviations of the mean (340 ± 2*8).
  • Part (c): 1 point for identifying the correct z-score corresponding to the top 5% (the 95th percentile), which is approximately 1.645. 1 point for using the z-score to calculate the correct weight (340 + 1.645*8 ≈ 353.16 grams).
  • Part (d): 1 point for calculating the z-score for both bags (Coffee: z = (352-340)/8 = 1.5; Tea: z = (128-120)/5 = 1.6). 1 point for the correct conclusion that the tea bag is relatively heavier because it has a larger z-score, indicating it is more standard deviations above its mean weight.

FRQ 9

The weights of bags of premium coffee beans are approximately normally distributed with a mean of 340 grams and a standard deviation of 5 grams.
a. A bag is randomly selected. What is the probability that the bag weighs more than 350 grams?
b. The coffee company wants to label the lightest 10 percent of bags as 'underfilled' for quality control checks. What is the maximum weight a bag can have to be considered underfilled?
c. A different brand of coffee has weights that are approximately normally distributed with a mean of 12 ounces and a standard deviation of 0.2 ounces. A customer buys one bag of the premium coffee that weighs 348 grams and one bag of the different brand that weighs 12.3 ounces. Which bag's weight is more unusual relative to its own brand's distribution? Justify your answer. (1 ounce ≈ 28.35 grams)

Scoring Guidelines

  • Part (a): 1 point for correctly calculating the z-score: z = (350-340)/5 = 2. 1 point for finding the correct probability from the z-score, P(Z > 2) ≈ 0.0228. (Showing calculator command with correct parameters is also acceptable).
  • Part (b): 1 point for identifying the z-score corresponding to the 10th percentile (approx -1.28). 1 point for correctly calculating the weight: X = 340 + (-1.28)*5 = 333.6 grams. (Showing calculator command with correct parameters is also acceptable).
  • Part (c): 1 point for calculating the z-score for the premium bag: z = (348-340)/5 = 1.6. 1 point for calculating the z-score for the other brand: z = (12.3-12)/0.2 = 1.5. 1 point for the correct conclusion that the premium bag (348g) is more unusual because its z-score of 1.6 is larger in magnitude (further from the mean) than the z-score of 1.5 for the other bag.

A score of 122 would be an acceptable answer. Partially correct (P) if the correct z-score is found but the final test score is incorrect, or vice-versa.
  • Part (c) is essentially correct (E) if the student calculates the z-score for both tests (Creativity: z = 1.67 from part a; Analytical: z = (600-500)/80 = 1.25), correctly compares the two z-scores, and concludes that the student performed better on the creativity test because they have a higher standardized score.
