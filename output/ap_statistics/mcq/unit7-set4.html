Q6. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S4Q6)
A quality control manager wants to determine if the average fill volume of juice boxes is less than the advertised 12 ounces. A random sample of 36 boxes yields a mean fill volume of 11.8 ounces and a standard deviation of 0.5 ounces. A significance test at $\alpha = 0.05$ produces a p-value of $0.012$. Based on these results, what should the manager conclude?
  • A. The manager should not reject the null hypothesis because the p-value is greater than $\alpha$.
  • B. The manager should reject the null hypothesis because the p-value is less than $\alpha$. There is convincing evidence that the true average fill volume is less than 12 ounces.
  • C. The manager should reject the null hypothesis because the sample mean is 11.8 ounces, which is less than 12 ounces.
  • D. The manager should not reject the null hypothesis because a sample mean of 11.8 ounces is not far enough below 12 ounces to be statistically significant.

  • A. There is a 6% chance that Fertilizer A and Fertilizer B have the same effect on plant growth.
  • B. If there is no difference in the true mean plant growth between the two fertilizers, there is a 6% chance of observing a difference in sample means as extreme as or more extreme than the one obtained, by random chance.
  • C. There is a 6% probability that the alternative hypothesis is false.
  • D. There is a 6% chance that a Type I error was made.
Q8. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S4Q8)

Their reaction times are measured. A two-sample t-test for the difference in mean reaction times (Energy Drink - Placebo) yields a p-value of $0.008$. If the significance level is $\alpha = 0.01$, what should the researchers conclude?
  • A. There is no significant difference in mean reaction times between the energy drink and placebo groups.
  • B. There is convincing evidence that the energy drink significantly improves reaction time (reduces it), as $p < \alpha$.
  • C. The p-value is close to $\alpha$, so the results are inconclusive.
  • D. Since $0.008 < 0.01$, we fail to reject the null hypothesis.
