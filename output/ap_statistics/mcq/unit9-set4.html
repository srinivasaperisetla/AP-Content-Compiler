Q1. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U9S4Q1)
A researcher collects data on the number of hours students spent studying for a final exam and their scores on the exam. A scatterplot shows a generally positive, linear relationship between study hours and exam scores, but there is noticeable variation around the regression line. Which of the following questions is most directly suggested by the variation observed in the scatterplot?
  • A. What is the average study time for all students?
  • B. What is the exact value of the correlation coefficient between study hours and exam scores?
  • C. How would the mean exam score change if all students studied one hour less?
  • D. What other factors might explain the variability in exam scores not accounted for by study time?
