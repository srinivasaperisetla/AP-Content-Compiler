Q2. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U6S3Q2)

Only 90 report being satisfied. A significance test for $H_0: p = 0.75$ versus $H_a: p < 0.75$ yields a p-value of 0.003. Which of the following is the correct interpretation of this p-value?
  • A. The probability that the true proportion of satisfied customers is 0.75 is 0.003.
  • B. There is a 0.003 probability that a random sample of 150 customers would result in 90 or fewer satisfied customers, assuming the true proportion is 0.75.
  • C. There is a 0.003 probability that the consumer group's claim is wrong.
  • D. If the true proportion of satisfied customers is less than 0.75, the probability of observing 90 or fewer satisfied customers is 0.003.
Q3. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U6S3Q3)
A school administrator claims that the proportion of students who walk to school is 40%. A random sample of 100 students shows that 30 walk to school. A hypothesis test ($H_0: p=0.40$ vs. $H_a: p \ne 0.40$) is conducted, resulting in a test statistic $z = -2.04$ and a p-value of 0.041. Using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion can be drawn?
  • A. Since the p-value (0.041) is less than $\alpha$ (0.05), we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is not enough evidence to conclude the proportion is different from 40%.
  • B. Since the p-value (0.041) is less than $\alpha$ (0.05), we reject the null hypothesis. There is convincing evidence that the proportion of students who walk to school is different from 40%.
  • C. Since the p-value (0.041) is less than $\alpha$ (0.05), we reject the alternative hypothesis. There is convincing evidence that the proportion of students who walk to school is 40%.
  • D. Since the p-value (0.041) is not much less than $\alpha$ (0.05), we fail to reject the null hypothesis. The observed difference is likely due to chance.

Q10. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U6S3Q10)
Researchers are investigating whether the proportion of males who prefer brand X soda is different from the proportion of females. A random sample of 300 males found 180 preferred brand X, while a random sample of 250 females found 120 preferred brand X. A hypothesis test ($H_0: p_M = p_F$ vs. $H_a: p_M \ne p_F$) results in a p-value of 0.005. At a significance level of $\alpha = 0.01$, what is the appropriate conclusion?
  • A. Since the p-value (0.005) is less than $\alpha$ (0.01), we fail to reject $H_0$. There is not sufficient evidence to conclude a difference in proportions.
  • B. Since the p-value (0.005) is less than $\alpha$ (0.01), we reject $H_0$. There is convincing evidence that the proportion of males who prefer brand X is different from the proportion of females.
  • C. Since the p-value (0.005) is greater than $\alpha$ (0.01), we fail to reject $H_0$. The observed difference is likely due to chance.
  • D. Since the p-value (0.005) is less than $\alpha$ (0.01), we accept $H_a$. The observed difference is statistically significant.
