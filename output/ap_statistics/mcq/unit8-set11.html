A political scientist wants to know if there's an association between political affiliation (Democrat, Republican, Independent) and opinion on a new policy (Favor, Oppose). She samples 100 people and obtains the following results:\n| | Favor | Oppose | Total |\n| --- | --- | --- | --- |\n| Democrat | 30 | 10 | 40 |\n| Republican | 10 | 25 | 35 |\n| Independent | 15 | 10 | 25 |\n| Total | 55 | 45 | 100 |\nCalculate the chi-square test statistic.
  • A. $ \sum \frac{(\text{observed} - \text{expected})^2}{\text{expected}} = \frac{(30-22)^2}{22} + \frac{(10-18)^2}{18} + \frac{(10-19.25)^2}{19.25} + \frac{(25-15.75)^2}{15.75} + \frac{(15-13.75)^2}{13.75} + \frac{(10-11.25)^2}{11.25} \approx 16.60 $
  • B. $ \sum \frac{(\text{observed} - \text{expected})^2}{\text{observed}} = \frac{(30-22)^2}{30} + \frac{(10-18)^2}{10} + \dots $
  • C. $\frac{(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - 0}{\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}}$
  • D. $\frac{\hat{p}_1 - \hat{p}_2}{\sqrt{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})(\frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2})}}$
