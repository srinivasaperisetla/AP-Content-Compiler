Q1. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S17Q1)
A medical researcher is conducting a study to determine if a new drug significantly lowers cholesterol levels. A random sample of 50 patients was given the drug, and their cholesterol levels were measured before and after treatment. A paired t-test was performed, resulting in a test statistic of t = -2.85 and a p-value of 0.003. What is the correct interpretation of the p-value in this context?
  • A. The probability that the drug does not lower cholesterol is 0.003.
  • B. Assuming the drug has no effect on cholesterol levels, there is a 0.003 probability of observing a sample mean reduction in cholesterol as extreme as or more extreme than the one observed.
  • C. There is a 0.3% chance that the researcher made a Type I error.
  • D. The probability that the drug is effective is 0.003.

Q3. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S17Q3)
A company claims that its new energy-efficient light bulbs have an average lifespan of more than 10,000 hours. A consumer advocacy group tests a random sample of 30 bulbs and finds a sample mean lifespan of 10,250 hours with a standard deviation of 800 hours. A t-test yields a p-value of 0.035. At a significance level of α = 0.05, what conclusion should the group draw?
  • A. Fail to reject the null hypothesis, as the sample mean is close to 10,000 hours.
  • B. Reject the null hypothesis, as 10,250 hours is greater than 10,000 hours.
  • C. Reject the null hypothesis because the p-value (0.035) is less than α (0.05), providing significant evidence that the average lifespan is greater than 10,000 hours.
  • D. Fail to reject the null hypothesis because the p-value (0.035) is less than α (0.05), indicating insufficient evidence.

The test results in a p-value of 0.002. If the company wants to minimize the risk of falsely claiming the drug is effective when it is not, what decision should be made at α = 0.01 and what type of error is being primarily considered?

  • C. Reject H0, thereby risking a Type II error.
