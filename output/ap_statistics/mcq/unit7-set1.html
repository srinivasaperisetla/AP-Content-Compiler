  • C. The population distribution is approximately normal or the sample size is sufficiently large ($n \ge 30$).

  • C. Since the p-value ($0.012$) is greater than $\alpha = 0.05$, we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is no convincing evidence that the true mean reduction in blood pressure is different from 10 mmHg.
  • D. Since the p-value ($0.012$) is greater than $\alpha = 0.05$, we reject the null hypothesis. There is convincing evidence that the true mean reduction in blood pressure is different from 10 mmHg.
