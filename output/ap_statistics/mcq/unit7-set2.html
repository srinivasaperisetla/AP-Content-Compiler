Q1. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S2Q1)
A hospital administrator conducted a significance test to see if the average waiting time for patients in the emergency room was less than 45 minutes. A random sample of 30 patients yielded a mean waiting time of 42 minutes with a standard deviation of 8 minutes. The test resulted in a p-value of 0.035. Which of the following is a correct interpretation of this p-value?
  • A. There is a 3.5% chance that the true average waiting time is 45 minutes.
  • B. There is a 3.5% chance of observing a sample mean waiting time of 42 minutes or less, assuming the true average waiting time is 45 minutes.
  • C. There is a 3.5% chance that the hospital administrator made a Type I error.
  • D. There is a 3.5% chance that the alternative hypothesis is true.
Q2. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S2Q2)
A researcher is investigating whether a new fertilizer increases the average yield of corn. The average yield using the old fertilizer is 120 bushels per acre. A random sample of 50 plots treated with the new fertilizer had an average yield of 125 bushels per acre with a standard deviation of 15 bushels per acre. A significance test for $H_0: \mu = 120$ versus $H_a: \mu > 120$ resulted in a p-value of 0.012. Using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what conclusion should the researcher make?
  • A. There is insufficient evidence to conclude that the new fertilizer increases the average yield, because $0.012 < 0.05$.
  • B. There is sufficient evidence to conclude that the new fertilizer increases the average yield, because $0.012 < 0.05$.
  • C. There is sufficient evidence to conclude that the new fertilizer increases the average yield, because $125 > 120$.
  • D. There is insufficient evidence to conclude that the new fertilizer increases the average yield, because the standard deviation is large.

  • A. There is a 2.8% chance that the true mean scores for Method A and Method B are equal.
  • B. Assuming there is no difference in the true mean scores between the two methods, there is a 2.8% chance of observing a sample mean difference of 4 or greater.
  • C. There is a 2.8% chance that Method A is more effective than Method B.
  • D. There is a 2.8% chance that the null hypothesis is true.
