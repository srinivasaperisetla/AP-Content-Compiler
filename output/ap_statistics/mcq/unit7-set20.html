Q1. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S20Q1)
A city planning office conducted a study to determine if the average commute time for residents is less than 30 minutes. What is the correct interpretation of this p-value?

  • B. There is a 3.5% chance of observing a sample mean of 28.5 minutes or less, assuming the true average commute time is 30 minutes.
  • C. There is a 3.5% chance that the null hypothesis is true.
  • D. The probability that the true average commute time is less than 30 minutes is 0.035.

Q3. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S20Q3)

Random samples of 30 students using Method A and 32 students using Method B were selected. A two-sample t-test for the difference in means yielded a p-value of 0.012. The alternative hypothesis was $H_a: \mu_A \ne \mu_B$. Which of the following is the correct interpretation of this p-value?
  • A. The probability that the true average test scores for the two methods are different is 0.012.
  • B. Assuming no difference in the true average test scores between the methods, there is a 1.2% chance of observing a difference in sample means as extreme as or more extreme than the one observed.
  • C. There is a 1.2% chance that Method A is more effective than Method B.
  • D. There is a 1.2% chance of making a Type I error.
