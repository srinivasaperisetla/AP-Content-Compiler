Q4. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S3Q4)
A botanist conducted a hypothesis test to determine if a new fertilizer increases the average height of a particular plant species, which historically has an average height of 20 cm. The null hypothesis was H0: μ = 20 cm, and the alternative was Ha: μ > 20 cm. A sample of 25 plants treated with the fertilizer yielded a p-value of 0.035. Which of the following is a correct interpretation of this p-value?
  • A. There is a 3.5% chance that the null hypothesis is true.
  • B. There is a 3.5% chance of observing a sample mean height as extreme as, or more extreme than, the one observed, assuming the true average height is 20 cm.
  • C. There is a 3.5% chance that the fertilizer has no effect on plant height.
  • D. There is a 3.5% chance that the true average height of plants with the new fertilizer is greater than 20 cm.

  • C. There is a 1.2% chance that the new drug is more effective than the placebo.
  • D. The probability that the observed difference is due to chance is 1.2%.
