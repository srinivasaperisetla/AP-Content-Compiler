Q7. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S3Q7)
A company implemented a new training program to improve employee productivity. The productivity scores of 15 employees were measured before and after the training. A matched-pairs t-test was performed, and the p-value was found to be 0.012. The alternative hypothesis was that productivity increased after training ($H_a: \mu_d > 0$, where $d = \text{after - before}$). Using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what is the appropriate conclusion?
  • A. Since the p-value (0.012) is less than $\alpha$ (0.05), we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is not enough evidence to conclude that the training program improved productivity.
  • B. Since the p-value (0.012) is less than $\alpha$ (0.05), we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is enough evidence to conclude that the training program improved productivity.
  • C. Since the p-value (0.012) is less than $\alpha$ (0.05), we reject the null hypothesis. There is convincing evidence that the training program improved employee productivity.
  • D. Since the p-value (0.012) is greater than $\alpha$ (0.05), we reject the null hypothesis. There is convincing evidence that the training program improved employee productivity.
