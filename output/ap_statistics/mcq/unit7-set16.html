Q1. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S16Q1)
A researcher performs a hypothesis test to determine if the mean weight of a new potato variety, $\mu$, is greater than the standard mean weight of 120 grams. The null hypothesis is $H_0: \mu = 120$ and the alternative is $H_a: \mu > 120$. A random sample of 30 potatoes yields a test statistic of $t = 2.15$ and a p-value of 0.018. What is the correct interpretation of this p-value?
  • A. There is a 1.8% chance that the true mean weight is 120 grams.
  • B. Assuming the true mean weight is 120 grams, there is a 1.8% chance of observing a sample mean as extreme as or more extreme than the one observed.
  • C. There is a 1.8% chance of making a Type I error.
  • D. There is a 1.8% chance that the alternative hypothesis is true.
