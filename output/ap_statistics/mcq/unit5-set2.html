A marketing firm compares the proportion of online shoppers who prefer Brand A to Brand B. Suppose 60% of consumers prefer Brand A and 40% prefer Brand B. A random sample of 100 Brand A consumers and a random sample of 150 Brand B consumers are taken. What are the mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions ($\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B$)?
  • A. A: $\mu_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = 0.20, \sigma_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = \sqrt{\frac{0.6 \times 0.4}{100} + \frac{0.4 \times 0.6}{150}}$
  • B. B: $\mu_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = 0.20, \sigma_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = \frac{0.6}{100} - \frac{0.4}{150}$
  • C. C: $\mu_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = 0.60 - 0.40, \sigma_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = \sqrt{\frac{0.6^2}{100} + \frac{0.4^2}{150}}$
  • D. D: $\mu_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = 0.60 - 0.40, \sigma_{\hat{p}_A - \hat{p}_B} = \sqrt{\frac{0.60 \times 0.40}{100} + \frac{0.40 \times 0.60}{150}}$

  • B. B: $\mu_{\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y} = -5, \sigma_{\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y} = \sqrt{\frac{10^2}{50} - \frac{12^2}{60}}$
  • C. C: $\mu_{\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y} = 35 - 40, \sigma_{\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y} = \sqrt{\frac{10^2}{50} + \frac{12^2}{60}}$
  • D. D: $\mu_{\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y} = 35 - 40, \sigma_{\bar{x}_X - \bar{x}_Y} = \frac{10^2}{50} + \frac{12^2}{60}$
