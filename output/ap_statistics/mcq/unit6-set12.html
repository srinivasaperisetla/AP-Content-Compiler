Q11. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U6S12Q11)

What is the correct interpretation of this p-value?
  • A. There is a 15.8% chance that the true proportion of consumers who prefer the new product is 0.60.
  • B. There is a 15.8% chance that the null hypothesis is true.
  • C. If the true proportion of consumers who prefer the new product is 0.60, there is a 15.8% chance that another random sample of 100 consumers would result in 65 or more preferring the product.
  • D. Assuming the true proportion of consumers who prefer the new product is 0.60, there is a 15.8% probability of observing a sample proportion as extreme as or more extreme than 0.65 by random chance.
