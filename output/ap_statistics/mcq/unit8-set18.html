Q1. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U8S18Q1)
A company claims that its new product color preferences are 30% red, 40% blue, and 30% green. A market researcher wants to test this claim. What are the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses?
  • A. $H_0: p_{red}=0.3, p_{blue}=0.4, p_{green}=0.3$; $H_a:$ At least one proportion is different from the claimed value.
  • B. $H_0:$ The observed proportions are different from the claimed proportions; $H_a:$ The observed proportions are the same as the claimed proportions.
  • C. $H_0: p_{red}=0.3, p_{blue}=0.4, p_{green}=0.3$; $H_a: p_{red} \ne 0.3 \text{ or } p_{blue} \ne 0.4 \text{ or } p_{green} \ne 0.3$.
  • D. $H_0:$ The observed counts fit the expected counts; $H_a:$ The observed counts do not fit the expected counts.
