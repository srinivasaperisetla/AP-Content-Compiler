Q7. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U5S10Q7)
Two independent random samples are collected. Sample 1 has $n_1=50$ and true proportion $p_1=0.2$. Sample 2 has $n_2=60$ and true proportion $p_2=0.1$. Can the sampling distribution of the difference in sample proportions ($\hat{p}_1 - \hat{p}_2$) be approximated by a normal distribution?
  • A. No, because $n_1 p_1 = 50 \times 0.2 = 10$, but $n_2 p_2 = 60 \times 0.1 = 6 < 10$.
  • B. Yes, because $n_1 p_1 = 10 \ge 10$, $n_1 (1-p_1) = 40 \ge 10$, $n_2 p_2 = 6 \ge 5$, and $n_2 (1-p_2) = 54 \ge 5$.
  • C. No, because $n_1(1-p_1) = 50 \times 0.8 = 40$, but $n_2(1-p_2) = 60 \times 0.9 = 54$, which are both large enough.
  • D. Yes, because all four conditions ($n_1 p_1 \ge 10, n_1(1-p_1) \ge 10, n_2 p_2 \ge 10, n_2(1-p_2) \ge 10$) are met.
