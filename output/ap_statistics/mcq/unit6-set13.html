  • A. Since the p-value ($0.008$) is less than $\alpha$ ($0.05$), we reject $H_0$. There is convincing evidence that more than 50% of voters support the candidate.
  • B. Since the p-value ($0.008$) is less than $\alpha$ ($0.05$), we fail to reject $H_0$. There is not convincing evidence that more than 50% of voters support the candidate.
  • C. Since the p-value ($0.008$) is less than $\alpha$ ($0.05$), we reject $H_0$. There is convincing evidence that exactly 50% of voters support the candidate.
  • D. Since the p-value ($0.008$) is greater than $\alpha$ ($0.05$), we fail to reject $H_0$. There is not convincing evidence that more than 50% of voters support the candidate.
