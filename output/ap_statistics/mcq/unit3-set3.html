AP Statistics — Unit 3: Collecting Data

Q1. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S3Q1)
A local newspaper wants to determine the average age of its readers. Which of the following is the most appropriate question to ask before collecting data?
  • A. How will we define "reader" and measure age?
  • B. What is the average income of our readers?
  • C. How many articles do readers typically read per day?
  • D. What is the most popular section of the newspaper?
Q2. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S3Q2)
A pharmaceutical company plans a study to assess the effectiveness of a new drug for blood pressure. Which of the following questions should they address before designing the study?
  • A. What is the company's annual profit margin?
  • B. How will "effectiveness" be defined and measured?
  • C. What is the historical stock price of the company?
  • D. How many employees does the research department have?
Q3. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S3Q3)

  • A. How many students are enrolled in the school?
  • B. What is the average teacher salary?
  • C. How will "improvement" be measured and compared between methods?
  • D. Are there enough textbooks for every student?

Q7. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S3Q7)
An observational study found that people who drink coffee regularly tend to have lower rates of Parkinson's disease. Which of the following is a valid conclusion?
  • A. Coffee consumption causes a reduction in Parkinson's disease.
  • B. There is an association between coffee consumption and Parkinson's disease rates.
  • C. Drinking coffee is a guaranteed way to prevent Parkinson's disease.
  • D. People who do not drink coffee will definitely develop Parkinson's disease.

  • A. Using a private tutor causes higher grades.
  • B. There is an association between using a private tutor and higher grades, but causation cannot be established.
  • C. Students who use tutors are smarter than those who don't.
  • D. Schools should mandate private tutoring for all students to improve grades.
Q9. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S3Q9)
An observational study revealed that individuals who eat a high-fiber diet have a lower risk of heart disease. Which conclusion can be drawn from this study?
  • A. A high-fiber diet causes a lower risk of heart disease.
  • B. There is an association between a high-fiber diet and a lower risk of heart disease.
  • C. Eating a high-fiber diet is the only way to prevent heart disease.
  • D. Individuals with a lower risk of heart disease must eat a high-fiber diet.

  • C. Basketball players are not real adults.

  • A. It is a convenience sample and may not represent the opinions of all students.
  • B. It is a simple random sample and is appropriate.
  • C. It is a systematic sample, which introduces bias.
  • D. It is a stratified sample, which is not suitable for this question.
