AP Statistics — Unit 3: Collecting Data

Q1. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S7Q1)
A group of researchers wants to investigate the effect of a new fertilizer on crop yield. They randomly assign 50 plots of land to receive the new fertilizer and 50 plots to receive a standard fertilizer. All other conditions are kept as similar as possible. At the end of the growing season, the crop yield from each plot is measured. What type of study is this?
  • A. An observational study
  • B. An experiment
  • C. A survey
  • D. A census
Q2. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S7Q2)
A psychologist is interested in the relationship between screen time and academic performance among high school students. They select a random sample of 200 high school students and collect data on their average daily screen time and their current GPA. The psychologist then analyzes the association between these two variables. What type of study is this?
  • A. An observational study
  • B. A controlled experiment
  • C. A randomized block design
  • D. A matched-pairs experiment
Q3. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S7Q3)
A medical researcher is investigating whether a new drug causes a reduction in cholesterol levels. They recruit 100 participants with high cholesterol and randomly assign 50 to receive the new drug and 50 to receive a placebo. Both groups are monitored, and their cholesterol levels are measured after 3 months. Based on this description, which of the following is the most appropriate classification for this study?
  • A. An observational study because the researcher does not control cholesterol levels.
  • B. A sample survey because data is collected from a subset of a larger population.
  • C. A census because all individuals with high cholesterol could potentially be included.
  • D. An experiment because treatments are imposed on randomly assigned groups.
Q4. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S7Q4)

  • A. Exercising more causes increased life satisfaction.
  • B. Increased life satisfaction causes people to exercise more.
  • C. There is an association between exercise and life satisfaction, but causation cannot be determined.
  • D. The study proves that exercise is beneficial for mental health.
Q5. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S7Q5)
A researcher conducts a study on a sample of 200 college students at a large state university and finds a statistically significant association between eating breakfast daily and higher GPA. Which of the following conclusions is appropriate?
  • A. Eating breakfast daily causes a higher GPA for all college students.
  • B. There is an association between eating breakfast daily and higher GPA for college students similar to those in the study, but causation cannot be inferred.
  • C. Eating breakfast daily causes a higher GPA for the 200 students in the study.
  • D. The study proves that if a student starts eating breakfast daily, their GPA will increase.
Q6. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S7Q6)
An observational study examined the relationship between caffeine consumption and sleep quality among adults. Researchers surveyed 1,000 randomly selected adults about their daily caffeine intake and self-reported sleep quality. They found that adults who consumed more caffeine tended to report lower sleep quality. What is a valid conclusion from this study?
  • A. Reducing caffeine intake will improve sleep quality.
  • B. Poor sleep quality leads to increased caffeine consumption.
  • C. Caffeine consumption is the sole cause of poor sleep quality.
  • D. There is an association between caffeine consumption and sleep quality, but other factors might also influence this relationship.

Q11. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U3S7Q11)

Why might this method, despite random selection, be problematic for drawing conclusions about all customers?

  • D. Email surveys always lead to response bias because customers cannot be anonymous.
