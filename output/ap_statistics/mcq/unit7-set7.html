Q1. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S7Q1)
A researcher conducts a one-sample t-test to determine if the mean caffeine content of a certain energy drink differs from $100 \text{ mg}$. The p-value for the test is $0.035$. Which of the following is the most appropriate interpretation of this p-value?
  • A. There is a $3.5\%$ chance that the null hypothesis is true.
  • B. Assuming the true mean caffeine content is $100 \text{ mg}$, there is a $3.5\%$ chance of observing a sample mean as extreme as, or more extreme than, the one obtained.
  • C. There is a $3.5\%$ chance that the sample mean is equal to $100 \text{ mg}$.
  • D. The probability that the alternative hypothesis is false is $0.035$.
Q2. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S7Q2)
A manager claims that the average time employees spend on a particular task is less than $30$ minutes. A random sample of $25$ employees yields a sample mean of $28.5$ minutes and a p-value of $0.012$ for the test of $H_0: \mu = 30$ versus $H_a: \mu < 30$. Using a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what is the appropriate conclusion?
  • A. Reject $H_0$. There is convincing statistical evidence that the average time is less than $30$ minutes.
  • B. Fail to reject $H_0$. There is convincing statistical evidence that the average time is $30$ minutes.
  • C. Reject $H_0$. There is not convincing statistical evidence that the average time is less than $30$ minutes.
  • D. Fail to reject $H_0$. There is not convincing statistical evidence that the average time is $30$ minutes.
Q3. (ID: ap_statistics_MCQ_U7S7Q3)

  • A. There is an $0.8\%$ probability that there is no true difference in average reaction times between the two devices.
  • B. There is an $0.8\%$ chance that the difference in sample means is due to sampling variability.
  • C. Assuming there is no true difference in average reaction times, there is an $0.8\%$ chance of observing a difference in sample means as extreme as, or more extreme than, the one observed.
  • D. The probability that the handheld device leads to slower reaction times is $0.008$.

At a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$, what is the appropriate conclusion?
  • A. Fail to reject $H_0$. There is no convincing evidence that fertilizer A leads to a higher mean yield than fertilizer B.
  • B. Reject $H_0$. There is convincing evidence that fertilizer A leads to a higher mean yield than fertilizer B.
  • C. Reject $H_0$. There is convincing evidence that fertilizer B leads to a higher mean yield than fertilizer A.
  • D. Fail to reject $H_0$. There is convincing evidence that fertilizer A does not lead to a higher mean yield than fertilizer B.
