Q1. (ID: APSTATS-U9-001)
A biologist collected data on the height (in cm) and weight (in kg) of a random sample of 50 black bears. A 95 percent confidence interval for the slope of the population regression line of weight on height was found to be (1.2, 2.5). Which of the following is a correct interpretation of this interval?
  • A. We are 95% confident that for a one cm increase in a specific bear's height, the bear's weight will increase by an amount between 1.2 kg and 2.5 kg.
  • B. We are 95% confident that the average increase in weight for a one cm increase in height for all black bears is between 1.2 kg and 2.5 kg.
  • C. There is a 95% probability that the true slope of the population regression line is between 1.2 kg and 2.5 kg.
  • D. For 95% of all black bears, a one cm increase in height is associated with an increase in weight between 1.2 kg and 2.5 kg.
