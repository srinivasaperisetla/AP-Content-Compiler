A sociologist wants to determine if there is a statistically significant linear relationship between years of education and annual income. Using data from a random sample of 50 adults, a hypothesis test is conducted on the slope of the population regression line. The null hypothesis H₀: β = 0 is tested against the alternative Hₐ: β ≠ 0. The test yields a p-value of 0.028. Assuming the conditions for inference are met, which of the following is the most appropriate conclusion at a significance level of α = 0.05?
  • A. Since the p-value (0.028) > α (0.05), we fail to reject H₀. There is not convincing evidence of a linear relationship between years of education and annual income.
  • B. Since the p-value (0.028) < α (0.05), we reject H₀. There is convincing evidence of a linear relationship between years of education and annual income.
  • C. Since the p-value (0.028) < α (0.05), we accept H₀. There is convincing evidence that there is no linear relationship between years of education and annual income.
  • D. Since the p-value (0.028) is small, we reject H₀. We have proven that more years of education cause an increase in annual income.
